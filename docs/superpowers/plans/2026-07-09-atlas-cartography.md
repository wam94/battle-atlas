## Design decisions (recommended + justified)

### D1 — Draping technique: segmented ribbon meshes built on the CPU, not decals, not biased quads

Per unit, build a grid mesh conforming to the terrain: N columns along the frontage × M rows along the symbol depth (N from frontage at ~5 m per column, clamped [8, 64]; M = 4), each vertex's Y sampled from `terrain.SampleHeight` + a small display-meter lift (≈0.4 m) so the ribbon hugs but never z-fights the ground. Draw with `Graphics.RenderMesh` — one call per rendered unit, ≤190 total, trivial on Apple Silicon Metal (the project already issues hundreds of instanced calls for trees/fences/crops).

- **Why not URP/HDRP decal projectors:** 190 projectors is real per-decal + DBuffer cost; the render pipeline is mid-bake-off (both URP and HDRP 17.4.0 are installed, Phase 3/4 will pick one), and decal support/behavior differs across them — a CPU mesh is pipeline-portable through the migration. Decals also stretch on steep slopes (projection along one axis) — exactly where Culp's Hill hurts today — and crisp echelon borders/hatching per unit would need an atlas texture pipeline this plan doesn't otherwise want.
- **Why not flat quads with depth bias:** that IS the current failure mode, minus the height. A rigid plane cannot lie on ~20 m of relief; bias fights z at grazing theater angles.
- **Cost honesty:** rebuild only when dirty. Most of the 190 units are static most of the battle — the dirty predicate is pure and tested (Task 2): position/facing moved past epsilon (reuse the `IsMovingAt` idiom), strength crossed a thickness-quantization step, formation changed, tier changed, or selection changed. A moving unit rebuilds ≤ 64×4 = 256 verts + skirt into preallocated arrays via `Mesh.SetVertices/SetUVs` — no allocation, no `RecalculateNormals` (normals are constant up; lighting is flat by design, this is a MAP symbol).

### D2 — Symbol grammar

- **Type (shape class, not shade):**
  - *Infantry* — solid rectangular bar (the tabletop line), square ends.
  - *Cavalry* — parallelogram: both ends sheared ~30° (the map-symbol slash made structural, readable at any zoom without a glyph texture).
  - *Artillery (battery/battalion)* — a row of discrete gun-dots (small squares) spaced along the frontage instead of a continuous bar; dot count = `clamp(round(strength / 20), 2, 8)` ≈ guns per battery, deterministic from strength.
  - *Artillery Reserve park / trains* (`us-arty-reserve-park`, prefix `us-arty-`) — hollow rectangle outline with an inset diagonal cross, NO gun-dots, fill permanently at the inactive desaturation: the honest non-combat symbol the user asked for. A park that an authored event names still brightens its outline (events remain the activity truth), but it never reads as a firing line.
  - *Formation states:* scattered/routed render the bar with fragmented fill (deterministic gap pattern via `FormationLayout.Jitter`) and dashed border — a dissolving unit must not read as an ordered one (same honesty rule as today's sub-block refusal); skirmish renders a thin dotted line spanning frontage×1.2 (matches `FillSkirmish`'s spread).
- **Echelon (border, not size alone):** brigade (has `regiments` roster, has children, or parentless infantry default at macro grain) = double border (outer heavy + inner pinstripe); regiment (has `parent`) = single thin border; battery = the gun-dot grammar itself IS the echelon mark (no bar = no confusion with a regiment). This adapts the military I/II/X convention to what actually survives 4 km of zoom: notch ticks don't, border weight and symbol class do. Echelon classification is pure (`EchelonOf(hasRoster, hasChildren, hasParent, kind)`), tested.
- **Strength → size, reconciled with attested frontage:** frontage is DATA (`frontage_m`, cited) and stays the symbol's length, exactly as authored. Strength becomes the symbol's THICKNESS: `displayDepth = clamp(strength × AreaPerEffective / frontage_m, MinDepth, MaxDepth)` with AreaPerEffective ≈ 6 m², MinDepth 8 m, MaxDepth 48 m — area-proportional to effectives wherever the clamp doesn't bite, so a 300-man regiment is visibly a sliver next to a 1,700-man brigade, and a brigade THINS as its keyframed strength falls through the charge (casualties become visible, deterministically, at every scrub position). The attested `depth_m` stops driving the symbol tiers (it never encoded anything a viewer could read) but still drives figure layout at the Soldiers tier, unchanged. Thickness quantizes to 0.5 m steps so interpolating strength doesn't dirty the mesh every frame.
- **Side color:** `SideColor` unchanged (deep blue / muted red). Kind SHADE multipliers retire — shape replaced them.
- **Activity salience:** absorbed as-is — `IsActiveAt` + the latch decide full-saturation fill vs `InactiveColor` fill (border keeps side hue so the ring never vanishes). Same rewrite-only-on-flip MPB discipline.
- **Confidence styling (USER RULING 2026-07-09 — two states):** documented = solid fill; inferred = diagonal-hatched fill (UV-space hatch in the shader, deterministic, no texture asset); the format's `unknown` RENDERS AS inferred (hatched) — "anything unknown can be considered inferred for this project." The mapping lives in ONE function (StyleOf) with the ruling documented at it; the format vocabulary itself is unchanged. A symbol carries its bracketing START keyframe's confidence (same rule as formation). "Contested" is V2-bundle vocabulary — the shader reserves a third style slot (cross-hatch) for Phase 11 proper; this plan styles the two states the ruling names.

### D3 — Labels: world-space TextMeshPro, pooled, pure-math declutter

World-space TMP (available via `com.unity.ugui` 2.0.0 — TMP is merged into uGUI in Unity 6; no new package), billboarded to the camera, distance-scaled to constant screen size. NOT screen-space UI: screen-space labels belong to the retained-mode UI-Toolkit migration deferred to Phase 11 proper, and world-space labels survive that migration untouched. Which units label at which zoom: Block tier — brigades/parents and the park (batteries only when active or selected); Regiments tier — the rendering family members (children or roster names on the brigade line) within the label budget; Soldiers tier — selected unit only (close zoom is deliberate attention; text spam would fight the figures). Declutter is a pure static function (`LabelLayout.Declutter`): deterministic priority order (selected > active brigades > inactive brigades > active regiments/batteries > rest, FNV tie-break), greedy screen-space AABB overlap rejection into caller-owned preallocated arrays, with a per-label sticky bonus so labels don't flicker in/out at the margin (the hysteresis idiom applied to text). Pool of TMP components created once in Start (budget 48), enabled/disabled per frame, zero allocation steady-state.

### D4 — LOD integration

Symbols replace the Block tier slab AND the Regiments tier sub-block boxes: Block tier = one brigade-grain ribbon per family representative; Regiments tier = one ribbon per rendering member (children of an expanded family) or per roster slot via the existing `FormationLayout.RegimentSlots` frontage partition (roster ribbons get regiment echelon borders and strength = parent strength / roster count, labeled as display-inferred). Figures stay <1500 m exactly as today; families collapse/expand atomically on the parent latch exactly as today (`RendersAtTier` truth table untouched, its test stays green). `MarkerHeight`, `MaxBlockHeight`, `BlockCenterAndHeight`, kind height/shade multipliers, and the cube markers themselves are deleted in Task 4, along with their pinned tests (counted below).

### D5 — Test strategy

Pure math TDD via CLI for everything that can lie: taxonomy, dimensions, ribbon vertices (fed a synthetic `Func<float,float,float>` height field — the `BuildMatrices(groundY)` pattern already proven in `UnitFormationRendererTests`), dirty predicate, declutter, picking. Shader/material correctness pinned the `HdrpMigrationTests` way (asset exists, wired, instancing/property flags). Visual verdicts — does Culp's Hill read, does the park read as non-combat, does thickness read as strength — ride the owner's editor session (Task 7); no screenshot automation in this plan.

## Risks

- **Vertical-scale tension (flagged, not solved here):** symbols sample the displayed terrain, so they are correct under today's 2.5× and under a future 1.0× — but the LIFT and label-anchor constants are display-meter tunings reviewed at 2.5×; the eventual Atlas true-scale decision (V2 DoD) re-tunes them. This plan deliberately does NOT change terrain scale.
- **Render-pipeline flux:** the symbol shader is written unlit/portable, but Phase 3/4's HDRP decision may still require a conversion pass — it is one shader by design, and it's on Phase 4's conversion list by note.
- **Roster-partition strengths are invented at display grain** (parent strength / roster count): labeled inferred, consistent with the roster's display-convention status in battle-format.md — but a reviewer may want per-regiment strengths promoted to child units instead; that's an authoring decision, out of scope.
- **190 unique dynamic meshes** is new memory territory (~190 × ≤300 verts ≈ trivial, <1 MB) but `Mesh` object count is worth watching in the owner's session frame-rate check; fallback is merging static units' ribbons into spatial-cell batches (SpatialBatcher precedent) — only if measured, not preemptively.
- **Declutter is per-frame CPU** over ≤190 candidates with a 48 budget — O(n·budget) AABB tests, negligible, but the sticky bonus must be verified against orbit flicker in the session (test pins two-frame stability only).
- **BattleLoaderScaleTests drives Start headlessly** and may pin cube-marker specifics; Task 4 budgets for adjusting it, but if it pins more slab behavior than expected the −5 retirement count grows — re-anchor arithmetic at the task, never skip the suite.
- **Editor is open now:** all CLI verifies queue for owner sessions; the plan's per-task green-suite discipline degrades to compile-clean + owner-batch verification. Report BLOCKED honestly rather than improvising Unity CLI runs.
