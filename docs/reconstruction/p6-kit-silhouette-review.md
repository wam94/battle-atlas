# Phase 6 kit — uniform and weapon silhouette review

Plan §12 Phase 6 checklist item ("review uniform and weapon silhouette
against research references"). Executor review, 2026-07-09; owner may
overrule at the gate. References: US Army 1861 uniform regulations and
standard secondary treatments (Echoes of Glory: Arms & Equipment of the
Union/Confederacy; Time-Life), plus the project's Pickett's-division
research notes (`docs/research/2026-06-13-july3-source-library*.md`).

## What the kit asserts (and why)

**Union (union_a/b/c)** — dark navy wool sack coat (4-button fatigue
blouse silhouette: loose, mid-thigh skirt), sky-blue kersey trousers,
kepi/forage cap (one slouch variant — common on campaign by 1863),
blackened-leather accoutrements: cartridge box on the RIGHT hip slung
from the left shoulder, cap pouch on the belt right of the plate,
bayonet scabbard/haversack/canteen on the LEFT. One variant carries a
blanket roll (Army of the Potomac veterans frequently marched without
knapsacks in the Gettysburg campaign).

**Confederate (csa_a/b/c)** — the variation is RESTRAINED per plan §7.1:
cadet-gray shell jacket (waist-length), one butternut jean-cloth jacket,
gray-brown jean trousers, slouch hats with one kepi, russet leather,
bedrolls standard, mixed haversack presence. No random earth-tone
costumes: two cloth families (gray wool, butternut jean) only, matching
the mixed-issue reality of Pickett's division in 1863 without inventing
rags-and-motley.

**Weapon** — project-owned Springfield Model 1861 rifle-musket from
public-domain dimensions (56 in overall, 40 in barrel, three barrel
bands, right-side percussion lock, steel ramrod in the under-barrel
channel). At hero distance the three-band full-stock silhouette and the
high-vertical shoulder-arms carry are the identity cues; both read.

## Known deviations (accepted for Gate P6, owed to Phase 7)

- Garments drape via the scripted smoothed-normal offset: the sack coat
  reads closer to a fitted blouse than a loose fatigue coat; no button
  placket/cuff detail.
- Equipment is primitive-geometry (box/sphere): correct place and size,
  no flap/buckle detail; no visible sling on the musket.
- Kepi crown slouch is approximated; hat brims are rigid.
- No rank/branch trim, no unit distinctions — correct default for a
  crowd of 1863 privates, revisit for any named-unit hero framing.
- Skin tone is a single constant; body variation is build-only
  (height/weight/muscle/age macros per variant).
