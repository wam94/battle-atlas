## 3. Best finds (the Spangler's Meadow verdict FIRST)

1. **THE SPANGLER'S MEADOW VERDICT: the dispute is real, primary,
   and TWO-POLE — the cheap fetches did NOT pin the time, and the
   Pfanz precondition STANDS.** What the test found:
   - **The early pole is PRIMARY, not just the Stone Sentinels
     outlier**: Morse (2nd MA, No. 288): "At daylight, July 3, our
     skirmishers, Company E, Captain Robeson, became engaged. Firing
     was kept up until 5.30 o'clock, when the regiment was ordered
     to charge the woods in front of us." Fesler (27th IN, No. 287):
     works occupied "between 5 and 6 o'clock", then "I was then
     ordered by you to charge their works" in one narrative motion.
     The 27th IN farthest-advance marker INSCRIBES the clock: "its
     charge at 6 a.m. July 3d. 1863."
   - **The late pole is the ORDER CHAIN's primary**: Ruger (division):
     "This state of things continued until about 10 a. m.… At this
     time I received orders to try the enemy… with two regiments…
     I sent orders by a staff officer to Colonel Colgrove" — and his
     narrative welds the charge into the fight-ending sequence (the
     123rd NY works reoccupation, CA-J3M-4). **Bachelder's timed
     sheets adjudicate the same way**: j3-01 (4–8 a.m.) draws
     Colgrove's brigade IN POSITION south of the meadow; j3-02
     (8–11 a.m.) draws "2 Mass" and "27 Ind" bars ADVANCED at the
     works' foot (59/69 m from the veteran stones — label-class
     agreement).
   - **Colgrove himself is clock-silent** (verified): "The general
     directs that you advance your line immediately" (verbal, via
     Lieut. Snow) — vs Ruger's skirmishers-first stated intent and
     his own "mistake of the staff officer, or misunderstanding on
     the part of Colonel Colgrove": the order-corruption pair at
     both echelons, verbatim.
   - **The conflation risk is now explained**: Lockwood's ~6 a.m.
     woods-deployment order (1st MD PHB, the meadow's NW woods —
     104 casualties) puts a REAL ~06:00 action on the same flank;
     the early-pole clocks and the Stone Sentinels 06:00 have a
     concrete adjacent event to have fused with — OR the regimental
     reports are right and Ruger's 10 a.m. describes the order's
     issue, not its origin. **Not decidable from these sources** —
     hence ED-64 (§5) records the two-pole structure and CA-J3M-3's
     ~10:00 ruling direction stays PROVISIONAL behind the Pfanz
     gate.
2. **CA-J3M-1 LANDED ON ITS AUTHOR (the tablet ladder's primary
   text)**: Muhlenberg — "At 4.30 a. m. the two rifle batteries (ten
   guns) and the two light 12-pounder batteries (ten guns) opened…
   fired for fifteen minutes without intermission at a range of from
   600 to 800 yards… Commenced at 5.30 a. m., and continued firing
   at intervals until 10 a. m." — with A. S. Williams's corps report
   carrying the same program at verbatim class ("continued by
   arrangement for fifteen minutes") and Ruger's 4.30 concurring.
   The anchor's basis is now issuer + executor + inheritor (tablet).
   TWO author-vs-tablet conflicts surfaced and carried: **20 guns
   (report) vs 26 (tablet)** — Rigby's A/1st MD (Powers Hill,
   registered) is the flagged reconciliation candidate — and **"until
   10 a. m." (report) vs "10.30 A.M." (tablet)**, with Jacobs and
   the Johnson tablet siding 10.30.
3. **THE OPENING IS A CLUSTER, NOT A POINT — and the infantry fire
   preceded the guns**: Cobham "At 3 o'clock next morning, July 3,
   the enemy's skirmishers commenced firing on us, and by 4 o'clock
