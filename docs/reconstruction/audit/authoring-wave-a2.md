   post-bombardment leg to the (already-moved) park.
3. **The Ziegler's Grove convergence** (cut 1) is ready-made decomposition
   -wave content: four dossiers, one ground, a repulse trigger, cold
   muzzles — cheap to author the moment new units are allowed.
4. **Window/day-model observations from working the format** (for day
   expansion):
   - The single `t=0..endTime` window forces every per-day fact into
     "pre-window ⇒ subtract at t=0" arithmetic (Taft's 9/10 A.M. pins,
     Hill's Braddock, A1's 8th Ohio ED-50). A day model wants per-phase
     `startTime` segments so morning pins land as keyframes, not as t=0
     strength surgery.
   - Day-scoped casualty columns (tablets) vs battle-scoped returns vs
     campaign supersets (Torbert's 17) all collide at the window edge;
     the ED-49/ED-52 scope discipline held, but a day model should carry
     loss records as per-day ledgers on the unit, not only as end-strength
     deltas.
   - Events at the window edge (Taft's marker "until 4 p.m." = t=10800
     exactly; McCartney's 4 P.M. arrival; Wheeler's 10680 arrival) show
     the 16:00 cut is slicing through attested activity — the day
     expansion should widen July 3 to at least sunset (19:29 LMT, ED-31)
     before decomposing further.
   - Morning-only fire records (Culp's Hill batteries, Taft's 8 A.M.)
     are already sitting in citations as documented silence — they
     become events for free once the July 3 morning phase opens.
5. The benchmark harness needed no changes this wave (`-benchmarkTimes`
   from A1 reused).
