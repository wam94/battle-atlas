## 3. Best finds

1. **THE ARTILLERY RESERVE'S OWN REPORTING CULTURE, a structural
   finding.** Of the four Artillery Reserve volunteer-brigade
   commanders researched this pass (each doubling as his own
   battery's captain), THREE filed no separate brigade-command
   report at all: Ransom's slot is followed directly by his own
   former battery's (Weir's) report; Taft's and Huntington's "reports"
   are scoped entirely to their own batteries. Only Fitzhugh's is a
   genuine brigade-wide narrative — and it happens to be this pass's
   richest single Artillery Reserve source, directly narrating the
   Webb's-fence crisis-reinforcement action from the commanding
   officer's own pen. This is not a research failure but a confirmed,
   page-checked pattern across a whole command class.
2. **THE BARTLETT/NEVIN/MCCANDLESS THREE-DOSSIER RESOLUTION.**
   Nevin's own dossier (pass 7) flagged a "triple command succession"
   (Wheaton→Nevin→Bartlett-attached) as a "rendering-metadata hazard"
   without a date or cause. Bartlett's own report, read for the first
   time this pass, resolves it precisely: Sedgwick gave Bartlett
   temporary command of Nevin's brigade for the JULY 3 Crawford-
   support sweep through the Wheatfield — the same action
   McCandless's own dossier (pass 7) already documents from the V
   Corps side (matching prisoner count, matching captured colors).
   Three previously-unconnected dossiers now read as one event.
3. **SHALER'S BRIGADE CLOSES A STANDING VERIFICATION FLAG.** The VI
   Corps HQ dossier (pass 12) explicitly flagged Shaler's Culp's Hill
   movement as "attributed-grade only... NOT independently verified"
   and named it a pass-13 target (missed that pass, picked up this
   one). Shaler's own TWO reports — a battle narrative and a
   November 1863 letter correcting a published misattribution to
   "Wheaton's brigade" — plus the brigade tablet close the flag with
   a genuinely engaged unit (9-11 A.M., Culp's Hill, under Geary) and
   a sub-hour regimental ladder feeding the CA-J3M chain.
4. **THE SOUTH CAVALRY FIELD SHEET-CROP EXERCISE, the first find of
   its own kind.** Bachelder sheet No. 8 (1-5 P.M. July 3, manifest id
   `j3-03`) draws "FARNSWORTH'S BRIGADE," Merritt's regulars, and
   Robertson's Elder's battery all within ~350 m of each other — the
   sheet pass 13 explicitly left unfetched. A second sheet (No. 9, 5
   P.M.) draws NO troop blocks in the same sector, an honest negative
   consistent with the ~17:30 close of the action.
5. **AN INCIDENTAL CONFLICT: "HART'S S.C." AT SOUTH CAVALRY FIELD.**
   While reading the same crop, a hand-lettered "HART'S S.C." block
   appeared directly on the South Cavalry Field ground — but
   Beckham's own Stuart Horse Artillery battalion marker states
   Hart's battery was off-ground guarding the army trains the whole
   battle. Recorded as a first-class ED-73-class conflict on
   `csa-cav-bn-ha-beckham.md`, not investigated further (out of this
   pass's assigned scope) but flagged for whichever future pass
   authors Hart's battery its own dossier.
6. **RUSSELL'S BRIGADE CONTRADICTS ITS OWN TABLET.** "There were no
   casualties in this command during any of the engagements on July
   2, 3, and 4" (Russell's own report, verbatim) directly disagrees
   with his brigade's own tablet ("Wounded 2 Men"). A small,
   honestly-carried conflict — the VI Corps batch's cleanest example
   of report-vs-tablet disagreement on an otherwise wholly
   attested-static brigade.
