## 2. Best finds

1. **THE STATIC-PARK TRIAGE WAS WRONG FOR 11 OF 13 BATTERIES, this
   pass's headline structural finding.** The pass-12 register triage
   classified thirteen Artillery Reserve batteries `static-park` — the
   negative-evidence placeholder class the task brief itself described
   as implying "casualties ~0 per return." Direct verification this
   pass found only TWO (Brooker's and Pratt's, both Connecticut Heavy
   batteries left at Westminster, Md., because siege ordnance was
   judged unsuited to rapid movement) actually fit that description.
   The other eleven were detached and fought: Turnbull's lost and
   recaptured four guns; Evan Thomas's battery took a direct-hit
   caisson explosion during the pre-charge bombardment that drew "a
   cheer from Confederate lines" (tablet, verbatim); Eakin's/Mason's
   battery took ten casualties on Cemetery Hill across two days;
   Dow's took thirteen wounded in the cannonade preceding Pickett's
   Charge; Norton's battery relieved a "badly shot up" battery in
   place; Hill's battery has two named fatalities dated to different
   days. This was not a research failure by pass 12 — it was an
   honest, explicitly-labeled placeholder ("a coverage-honesty
   placeholder, NOT a claim the battery never fired," per the
   register's own JSON text) that this pass's dedicated verification
   sweep was specifically commissioned to resolve, per the pass-13
   census's own recommendation to do this "deliberately... now that
   all... parents are dossiered."
2. **TWO BATTERIES' OWN COMMANDERS' REPORTS WERE ALREADY THIS CORPUS'S
   PRIMARY SOURCES BEFORE THIS PASS BEGAN — Taft's (5th NY) and
   Edgell's (1st NH).** Both batteries sat classified `static-park`
   while their own commanders' OR reports were already the pass-14
   parent brigade dossiers' ENTIRE evidentiary basis (Taft doubles as
   the 2nd Volunteer Brigade's commander; Huntington's own brigade
   report doesn't exist, so Edgell's battery report IS the 3rd
   Volunteer Brigade dossier's primary text). This is the pass's
   single largest triage/research mismatch — richly-sourced units
   (four individually-timed casualties, itemized rounds by projectile
   type, direct reporting to Howard at East Cemetery Hill) sitting
   behind a "stayed in the park" placeholder purely because no one had
   yet created their OWN battery-grain register row's dossier file.
3. **THE ZIEGLER'S GROVE CONVERGENCE, a new three-battery Union
   reinforcement fact.** Three VI Corps regular batteries (Williston's
   D/2US, Butler's G/2US, Leonard Martin's F/5US) independently
   tablet-attest moving to Ziegler's Grove, rear of Hays's Division,
   specifically AFTER the Pickett's Charge repulse — a post-crisis
   consolidation this corpus's Angle-front dossiers had not
   previously named from the VI Corps side. A fourth battery (Harn's,
   3rd NY) was independently ordered to support Hays's Division the
   same afternoon. Williston's own individual page conflicts with the
   convergence claim (it says "Not engaged," Taneytown Road only) —
   recorded as an open cross-source tension, not silently resolved.
4. **THE OPENING-GUN BATTERY GETS ITS OWN DOSSIER.** Calef's Battery A,
   2nd U.S. — "The first Union gun of the battle was fired from the
   right section" (tablet, verbatim) — now has its own register row
   at battery grain rather than only a cross-reference on Gamble's
   brigade dossier.
5. **A CLEAN REPORT-VS-TABLET CASUALTY CONFLICT ON SMITH'S BRIGADE**:
   every single category disagrees (killed 51 vs 66, wounded 278 vs
   259, missing 19 vs 26) despite near-identical grand totals (348 vs
   345) — recorded per this corpus's standing discipline as an open
   conflict, a candidate for a future ED-71/ED-74-class treatment if a
   corroborating return is located.
6. **A BAD SEARCH-STAGE CASUALTY FIGURE CAUGHT AND REJECTED.** An
   initial search-stage summary offered "43 total casualties" for
   Gibbs's Battery L (1st Ohio); a direct page fetch corrected this to
   "2 men wounded." The error and its correction are both recorded on
   the dossier, per this corpus's honesty discipline — wrong numbers

7. **AN INDIVIDUAL BATTERY DOSSIER UPGRADES ITS OWN PARENT BRIGADE
   DOSSIER.** Heaton's battery (Batteries B&L, 2nd U.S.) was

   activity statement beyond... 'In Reserve.'" This pass's individual

With the register's coverage-gap classes cleared to zero, the campaign
has three plausible next directions, each requiring a different kind
of work:

truth-spec's own scope ruling separates "research stays upfront,

units (per the pass-14 register stats) and many more not-yet-cast. A

and which units within it), the existing claims-compiler tooling

prioritize.

**Option B — Day expansion (open a new battle phase's authoring, not
just research).** Every phase's chain is now executor-substantiated
at dossier grain (§8.1) — the remaining work to bring, say, July 1
morning or July 2 evening to the SAME claim-compiled standard as the
July 3 Angle window is now purely an authoring-and-compilation
question, not a research one. **Needs**: the same claims-compiler
tooling as Option A, applied to a specific day/phase, likely the most
resource-intensive of the three options since it touches the actual
in-build battle file.

**Option C — Cartography (the spatial layer).** The Bachelder
sheet-crop exercise has been used opportunistically (pass 2's first
use, pass 9/10's j1/j2 sheet exercises, pass 12/13's ECF/SCF sheets)
but never systematically — many of this pass's 37 new dossiers have
NO EC3 sheet-crop corroboration at all (only tablet/report position
statements). A dedicated cartography pass could raise EC3 confidence
across the whole corpus without touching claims/keyframes. **Needs**:
the existing `docs/reconstruction/audit/spatial-evidence.md` apparatus
and a defined sheet/sector priority list (this pass's own Ziegler's
Grove convergence and the VI Corps Artillery Brigade's positions would
be a natural, cheap first target, since they involve several units
sharing one small sector).

**This report's own recommendation**: given the register clearance
now removes the "which units need dossiers" question entirely, Option
A (authoring wave A2) is the most natural next step — it directly
answers "what was all this research FOR," per the unit-truth-spec's
