# Order of Battle — Engaged Strengths, Pickett-Pettigrew-Trimble Assault (July 3, 1863)

**Status:** Follow-up pass closing Gap A flagged in [source library pass 1](2026-06-13-july3-source-library.md) and [pass 2](2026-06-13-july3-source-library-pass2.md). Two parallel research passes (Confederate assault brigades; Union defenders) fetched Stone Sentinels brigade/regiment/monument pages, NPS pages, Encyclopedia Virginia, Wikipedia (checked for direct Busey & Martin citations — none found), and OR Series I Vol. 27. URLs live-fetched 2026-07-01.

**Governing rule for this document, per task instructions: figures are never averaged or reconciled.** Where sources disagree — including two independent extractions of the *same* Stone Sentinels page disagreeing with each other — every figure is kept and attributed. Disagreement is data for the app's provenance system.

**A systemic caveat that applies to most Stone Sentinels "Present" figures below:** several brigade pages (Pettigrew's/Marshall's, Davis's, and implicitly Brockenbrough's) label their strength figure "present on the first day" — i.e., July 1 pre-battle strength, not July 3 assault strength. Heth's/Pender's-division brigades took heavy July 1 losses before the July 3 assault; their "Present" figures likely overstate actual charge-day numbers. Pickett's fresh division did not fight July 1, so its "Present" figures are more directly comparable to a July 3 assault strength. This is flagged per-brigade below, not corrected.

## 1. Confederate assault column

### Pickett's Division

#### Garnett's Brigade
- **Commander/fate:** Brig. Gen. Richard B. Garnett — killed. Too unwell to walk (fever, recent kick from a horse), rode his horse against orders, shot in the head roughly 20 yards from the Angle, fell dead. *(Stone Sentinels narrative)*
- **Regiments:** 8th, 18th, 19th, 28th, 56th Virginia Infantry.
- **Engaged-strength figures:**
  - Stone Sentinels — Present **1,480**; Killed 78, Wounded 324, Missing 539, Total losses 941. https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/confederate-headquarters/garnetts-brigade/
  - Encyclopedia Virginia / NPS: no brigade-specific strength figure located.

#### Kemper's Brigade
- **Commander/fate:** Brig. Gen. James L. Kemper — badly wounded, briefly captured, rescued by Sgt. Leigh Blanton (1st VA). Col. Joseph Mayo Jr. (3rd VA) assumed command of survivors. *(Stone Sentinels narrative)*
- **Regiments:** 1st, 3rd, 7th, 11th, 24th Virginia Infantry.
- **Engaged-strength figures:**
  - Stone Sentinels — Present **1,575**; Killed 56, Wounded 356, Missing 317, Total losses 731. https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/confederate-headquarters/kempers-brigade/
  - Encyclopedia Virginia / NPS: not located.

#### Armistead's Brigade
- **Commander/fate:** Brig. Gen. Lewis A. Armistead — mortally wounded and captured at the stone wall (hat borne aloft on his sword point); died July 5. *(Stone Sentinels; corroborated by Rawley Martin's SHSP account in the source library pass 2 — "I was disabled at Armistead's side a moment after he had fallen.")*
- **Regiments:** 9th, 14th, 38th, 53rd, 57th Virginia Infantry.
- **Engaged-strength figures:**
  - Stone Sentinels — Present **1,650**; Killed 88, Wounded 460, Missing 643, Total losses 1,191. https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/confederate-headquarters/armisteads-brigade/
  - Encyclopedia Virginia, citing Col. W.R. Aylett's July 12, 1863 report (Museum of the Confederacy) — **1,191 total casualties** (matches Stone Sentinels' total exactly; no separate "present" figure given). https://encyclopediavirginia.org/1117hpr-5342776bbcb9068/
  - ⚠️ A WebSearch summary (not a direct page fetch) separately surfaced "strength of 1,950" for this brigade. The direct WebFetch of the Stone Sentinels page returned 1,650. Treat 1,650 as the reliable figure; 1,950 is flagged as a likely search-summary paraphrase artifact and should not be cited.

#### Pickett's Division, column-level (not brigade-specific, retained from source library pass 1/2)
  - Longstreet (1896, p. 315, verbatim): "only four thousand seven hundred under Pickett were fresh."
  - Busey & Martin (via verified secondary reproductions, book not free online): Pickett's division **~5,820 engaged**.
  - Encyclopedia Virginia "Numbers at Pickett's Charge": Pickett's Division **"5,800–6,200 depending on source."** https://encyclopediavirginia.org/entries/numbers-at-picketts-charge/

### Pettigrew's Division (Heth's)

#### Fry's Brigade (formerly Archer's)
- **Commander/fate:** Brig. Gen. James J. Archer captured July 1 (before this action). Col. Birkett D. Fry (13th AL) commanded July 3 — wounded and captured. Lt. Col. Samuel G. Shepard (7th TN) then took over. *(Stone Sentinels; Fry's own SHSP account, source library pass 2, confirms he was "down wounded before the end.")*
- **Regiments:** 5th Alabama Battalion, 13th Alabama, 1st Tennessee (Provisional Army), 7th Tennessee, 14th Tennessee.
- **Engaged-strength figures:**
  - Stone Sentinels — Present **1,048**; Killed and wounded 160, Missing 517, Total losses 677. Narrative text on the same page separately states "less than 400 men remained at the end of July 3rd." https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/confederate-headquarters/archers-brigade/
  - ⚠️ A "~1,200 men (Busey & Martin)" figure surfaced only in an unverified WebSearch summary snippet — never confirmed via direct source fetch. Not citable; flagged as unreliable.

#### Marshall's Brigade (Pettigrew's own)
- **Commander/fate:** Col. James K. Marshall (52nd NC) — killed. (Pettigrew himself moved up to division command; wounded in the hand — confirmed separately by Encyclopedia Virginia.) Maj. John T. Jones, "the only surviving field officer," assumed command. *(Stone Sentinels)*
- **Regiments:** 11th, 26th, 47th, 52nd North Carolina Infantry.
- **Engaged-strength figures:**
  - Stone Sentinels — "Present on the first day about **2,000**" (whole-battle/July-1 figure, not July-3-specific per se — see systemic caveat above); Killed 190, Wounded 915, Missing ~300, Total losses 1,405. https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/confederate-headquarters/pettigrews-brigade/
  - Wikipedia / Encyclopedia Virginia: only the divisional aggregate is given (Pettigrew's division ~2,700 casualties, 62%) — no brigade breakdown.

#### Davis's Brigade
- **Commander/fate:** Brig. Gen. Joseph R. Davis — survived July 3 (became too ill to remain in the field shortly after the battle; not reported wounded on July 3). Col. Hugh R. Miller (42nd MS) mortally wounded/captured. *(Stone Sentinels)*
- **Regiments:** 2nd, 11th, 42nd Mississippi Infantry; 55th North Carolina Infantry.
- **Engaged-strength figures:**
  - Stone Sentinels — "Present on the first day about **2,000**" (see systemic caveat); Killed 180, Wounded 717, Missing ~500, Total losses 1,397. https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/confederate-headquarters/davis-brigade/
  - Encyclopedia Virginia / NPS: no brigade-specific figure.

#### Brockenbrough's Brigade — weakest data of the eleven assault brigades
- **Commander/fate:** Col. John M. Brockenbrough (40th VA) — apparently survived July 3, but was replaced as brigade commander immediately after the battle. *(Consistent across sources; no wound reported.)*
- **Regiments:** 40th, 47th, 55th Virginia Infantry; 22nd Virginia Infantry Battalion.
- **Engaged-strength figures:**
  - ⚠️ Stone Sentinels — direct WebFetch of https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/confederate-headquarters/brockenbroughs-brigade/ returned figures **identical** to Davis's Brigade page ("Present on the first day about 2,000 / Killed 180 Wounded 717 Missing about 500 Total 1,397"), reproduced on two separate fetch attempts. This is almost certainly a scraping/extraction error — Brockenbrough's was the smallest brigade in the division, and identical casualty returns for two different brigades is not credible. **Do not use this figure.**
  - Secondary/period-report figures, via a WebSearch snippet citing an NPS Gettysburg symposium essay (npshistory.com/series/symposia/gettysburg_seminars/12/essay5.pdf — not independently WebFetched, so treat with the same caution as any unverified snippet):
    - Col. R.M. Mayo's official report (Aug. 13, 1863): brigade began the battle with **"800 muskets" (~880 officers and men)**.
    - Don Ernsberger (2011 study): **"just under 1,000 officers and men."**
    - Dr. Bradley M. Gottfried, *Gettysburg Magazine* Issue 23: **"about a thousand muskets" (~1,100 officers and men)** — the essay's own author characterizes this as likely an overestimate.
  - No reliable casualty figure located for this brigade specifically.

### Trimble's Command (Pender's Division)

#### Lane's Brigade
- **Commander/fate:** Brig. Gen. James H. Lane — survived unharmed; took over division command after Trimble was wounded. *(Stone Sentinels; Encyclopedia Virginia confirms Trimble wounded.)*
- **Regiments:** 7th, 18th, 28th, 33rd, 37th North Carolina Infantry.
- **Engaged-strength figures:**
  - Stone Sentinels (direct fetch, quoting monument inscription) — Present **1,355**; Killed 41, Wounded 348, Missing 271, Total losses 660. https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/confederate-headquarters/lanes-brigade/
  - ⚠️ A separate WebSearch summary of the *same* Stone Sentinels page returned a conflicting set: "strength of 1,730 men... 180 killed, 375 wounded, 240 missing, 795 total." Both figures are reported here since they trace to the same nominal source but disagree; the direct fetch (quoting monument text explicitly) is judged more reliable, but the conflict itself is flagged, not resolved.
  - Narrative text on the same Stone Sentinels page states Lane's and Lowrance's/Scales's brigades **combined** "could field no more than 800 men for the assault on July 3rd" — a combined, not per-brigade, figure, far below either "Present" number. This suggests the "Present" figures reflect pre-July-1 strength rather than actual July 3 assault strength (see systemic caveat).

#### Lowrance's Brigade (formerly Scales's)
- **Commander/fate:** Col. William Lee Jackson Lowrance (34th NC) commanded July 3 — wounded during the charge. Brig. Gen. Alfred Scales had been badly wounded in the leg on July 1 and did not command on July 3. *(Stone Sentinels; Encyclopedia Virginia confirms Lowrance wounded.)*
- **Regiments:** 13th, 16th, 22nd, 34th, 38th North Carolina Infantry.
- **Engaged-strength figures:**
  - Stone Sentinels — "Present about **1,250**"; Killed 102, Wounded 381, Missing 116, Total losses 599. https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/confederate-headquarters/scales-brigade/
  - Wikipedia: no absolute figure, only that the brigade "had lost almost two-thirds of their men on July 1" (proportional, not a number).
  - Encyclopedia Virginia: only the combined Trimble's-command figure of 885 casualties (52%), not split by brigade.

### Supporting/echelon brigades (Anderson's Division)

#### Wilcox's Brigade
- **Commander/fate:** Brig. Gen. Cadmus M. Wilcox — survived unharmed; later promoted to major general. *(Stone Sentinels)*
- **Regiments:** 8th, 9th, 10th, 11th, 14th Alabama Infantry.
- **Engaged-strength figures:**
  - Stone Sentinels (direct fetch, monument inscription) — Present **1,777**; Killed 51, Wounded 469, Missing 261, Total losses 781. https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/confederate-headquarters/wilcoxs-brigade/
  - ⚠️ A WebSearch summary of the same page returned a conflicting set: "strength of 1,725 men... 80 killed, 440 wounded, 260 missing, total 780." Both reported; smaller gap than Lane's Brigade's equivalent conflict, but the same extraction-inconsistency pattern.
  - Wikipedia "Pickett's Charge" (uncited): "Wilcox's brigade reported losses of 200" — inconsistent with Stone Sentinels' 781/780 totals; likely reflects only losses during the actual advance on July 3, distinct from the brigade's cumulative multi-day monument total.

#### Lang's Brigade (Florida Brigade, under Perry's name)
- **Commander/fate:** Col. David Lang commanded in place of Brig. Gen. Edward Perry (absent, typhoid fever). Lang's personal fate on July 3 is **not located** in any source checked — not guessed at.
- **Regiments:** 2nd, 5th, 8th Florida Infantry.
- **Engaged-strength figures:**
  - Stone Sentinels — Present **700**; Killed 33, Wounded 217, Missing 205, Total losses 455. https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/confederate-headquarters/perrys-brigade/
  - Wikipedia "Pickett's Charge" (uncited): "Lang's about 400" (casualties only) — conflicts with Stone Sentinels' 455 total, though closer than most other brigades' discrepancies; may again reflect a July-3-only vs. whole-battle framing difference.

### Column-level context (not brigade-specific — retained for cross-reference)

- **Longstreet** (1896, p. 315, verbatim): "the entire force of these divisions was only fifteen thousand strong... lost about sixty per cent."
- **Busey & Martin** (via verified secondary reproductions): column totals reproduced at **11,484–12,500**.
- **Hess (2001):** column **~12,000–12,500** (in-copyright, no free full text; citable via library access only).
- **Stewart (1957):** ~**10,500** (via Encyclopedia Virginia's historiography summary).
- **Encyclopedia Virginia "Numbers at Pickett's Charge"** (https://encyclopediavirginia.org/entries/numbers-at-picketts-charge/): total Confederate force **"between 12,000–13,000,"** revising a traditional 15,000 estimate; frames scholarly consensus as "surprisingly hard to reach."
- **Wikipedia "Pickett's Charge" infobox:** "12,500 infantry in 9–11 brigades" — cited to Hess 2001 pp. 333–35 and Sears 2003 p. 467.
- **Wikipedia casualty breakdown by division** (same article, same citations): Pickett's Division 2,655 casualties (498 killed, 643 wounded, 833 wounded-and-captured, 681 captured unwounded); Pettigrew's ~2,700 (470 killed, 1,893 wounded, 337 captured); Trimble's two brigades 885 (155 killed, 650 wounded, 80 captured); Wilcox's brigade "losses of 200"; Lang's "about 400"; stated total "6,555."
  - ⚠️ **Internal arithmetic error in the source, reported as-is, not corrected:** 2,655 + 2,700 + 885 + 200 + 400 = 6,840, not the stated 6,555.
- **Explicit non-finding:** despite the task brief's expectation, no Wikipedia article checked ("Pickett's Charge," "Battle of Gettysburg, Third Day," "Battle of Gettysburg order of battle: Union," or individual commander pages) cites Busey & Martin by name or page number for any unit, Union or Confederate. Treat "Wikipedia reproduces B&M figures" as not confirmed in this pass.
- **OR Series I Vol. 27 Part 2** (Confederate reports, Texas ARK https://texashistory.unt.edu/ark:/67531/metapth154598/): confirmed infeasible to mine for per-brigade numeric returns via automated fetch — the scan has OCR text, but the full-text search interface is bot-gated. No numbers extracted from this route in this pass.

### Gibbon's Division (2nd Division, II Corps)

#### Webb's Brigade (Philadelphia Brigade)
- **Commander/fate:** Brig. Gen. Alexander S. Webb — wounded (thigh and groin), remained in command. Medal of Honor citation confirms "wounded." https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_S._Webb · https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/monuments-to-individuals/alexander-webb/
- **Regiments:** 69th, 71st, 72nd, 106th Pennsylvania Infantry.
- **Strength figures:**
  - Stone Sentinels summary table: **1,250** engaged.
  - Wikipedia "Pickett's Charge": "two guns and **940 men**" defending the immediate stone-wall position — a narrower scope (position, not full brigade), not necessarily contradictory.
  - 69th PA (Stone Sentinels monument page): **258 men** at the Angle.
  - 71st PA (Stone Sentinels): **331 men** brought to the field.
  - 72nd PA (Stone Sentinels): **458** "present at Gettysburg" vs. **473** "present for duty" — the page explicitly distinguishes these two terms, a useful illustration of the engaged-vs-present distinction.
  - 106th PA (Stone Sentinels): **335 total** (23 officers, 312 men) per one account on the page; a second account on the same page gives **312 men** alone.
  - Not located: Busey & Martin citation; NPS order-of-battle strength figures.

#### Hall's Brigade
- **Commander/fate:** Col. Norman J. Hall — no wound reported July 3; continued in command (inferred from absence of any "(w)"/"(k)" annotation on the Wikipedia Union OOB page, unlike Webb/Gibbon). https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norman_J._Hall
- **Regiments:** 19th Massachusetts, 20th Massachusetts, 7th Michigan, 42nd New York, 59th New York (4 companies).
- **Strength figures:**
  - Stone Sentinels summary table: **920** engaged.
  - 19th MA (Stone Sentinels): **231 men** brought to the field.
  - 7th MI (Stone Sentinels): **165 total** (14 officers, 151 men present for duty).
  - 42nd NY (Stone Sentinels): **197 men** brought to the field.
  - 20th MA, 59th NY: not located.
  - Gibbon's whole 2nd Division (Harrow + Hall + Webb combined): **3,773 men**, per William Harrow's official report as reproduced on Wikipedia's "William Harrow" page — division-level, not brigade-specific, but useful as a cross-check ceiling.

#### Harrow's Brigade
- **Commander/fate:** Brig. Gen. William Harrow — no wound reported; took over division command from wounded Gibbon on July 3. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Harrow
- **Regiments:** 19th Maine, 15th Massachusetts, 1st Minnesota, 82nd New York.
- **Strength figures:**
  - Stone Sentinels summary table: **1,410** engaged.
  - Wikipedia "William Harrow": brigade "suffered as many as **768** casualties" (cited to a War Department/NPS marker) vs. "**Harrow reported 722 losses**" in his own Official Report (via Civilwarhome.com reproduction) — two different casualty totals from two source lineages; no single brigade strength figure given, casualties only.
  - 19th ME (Stone Sentinels): "Effective strength, July 2d: **405**" — explicitly labeled effective strength.
  - 15th MA (Stone Sentinels): **304 men** brought to the field.
  - 1st MN (Stone Sentinels): **420** total regiment; **262 men** (eight companies) in the July 2 charge specifically; a secondary monument states **330 engaged** — three figures at three different scopes, all reported, none reconciled. This is the widest internal spread found for any single regiment in this pass.
  - 82nd NY (Stone Sentinels): **394 men** brought to the field.
  - Not located: Busey & Martin citation.

### Hays's Division (3rd Division, II Corps)

#### Smyth's Brigade
- **Commander/fate:** Col. Thomas A. Smyth — **wounded July 3** (face, struck by a shell/shrapnel fragment), relinquished brigade command briefly that day; not disabling long-term. He was later killed at Farmville, VA, in April 1865 — a separate, later event; do not conflate. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_A._Smyth
  - A lower-confidence, not-independently-verified web synthesis additionally describes Smyth as hit in the face and continuing to direct ~290 skirmishers at the Bliss farm — flagged as unverified detail, not used as a citable fact.
- **Regiments:** 14th Connecticut, 1st Delaware, 12th New Jersey, 10th New York Battalion, 108th New York.
- **Strength figures:**
  - Stone Sentinels summary table: **1,190** engaged.
  - 14th CT (Stone Sentinels): **160 men** in action.
  - 1st DE (Stone Sentinels): **288 men** brought to Gettysburg.
  - 12th NJ (Stone Sentinels): **532 men** brought to the field.
  - 10th NY Battalion, 108th NY: not located.
  - Not located: Busey & Martin citation.

#### Sherrill's/Willard's Brigade
- **Commanders/fates:**
  - Col. George L. Willard — **killed in action, July 2** (shell fragment to the head, during withdrawal after the brigade's counterattack on Barksdale's brigade). Not a July-3 fatality — noted for clarity since the brigade fought under his successor on July 3. https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/monuments-to-individuals/george-willard/
  - Col. Eliakim Sherrill — took command July 2; **mortally wounded July 3** during the repulse of Pickett's Charge; died July 4, 1863. https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/union-headquarters/3rd-brigade-3rd-division-2nd-corps/
  - Lt. Col. James M. Bull then assumed command for the remainder of the battle.
- **Regiments:** 39th New York (4 companies), 111th New York, 125th New York, 126th New York.
- **Strength figures:**
  - Stone Sentinels summary table: **1,510** engaged — the largest of the II Corps brigade figures on that table.
  - 111th NY (Stone Sentinels): **390 men engaged** (8 companies).
  - 125th NY (Stone Sentinels, monument rear tablet): "Number engaged, **500**."
  - 126th NY (Stone Sentinels): **455 total engaged** (30 officers, 425 enlisted).
  - 39th NY (4 companies): not located.
  - Not located: Busey & Martin citation.

### Stannard's 2nd Vermont Brigade (3rd Brigade, 3rd Division, I Corps)

- **Commander/fate:** Brig. Gen. George J. Stannard — **wounded (thigh) July 3** while directing the flank attack; remained on the field until the battle's end. https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/union-headquarters/3rd-brigade-3rd-division-1st-corps/
- **Regiments (full brigade roster):** 12th, 13th, 14th, 15th, 16th Vermont. **Only the 13th, 14th, and 16th** were engaged in the July 3 flank attack (12th and 15th were on detached/guard duty elsewhere per Stone Sentinels' action narrative).
- **Strength figures:**
  - Stone Sentinels summary table: **1,950** — note the table's own row label reads "3rd Brigade, 1st Division, I Corps," an apparent division-number mislabeling (Stannard's brigade was 3rd Division), but the strength figure itself is presented as the brigade total.
  - 13th VT (Stone Sentinels monument): "Officers and men engaged **480**."
  - 14th VT (Stone Sentinels): "**647 men** brought to the field" — described as "one of the largest regiments in the battle."
  - 16th VT (Stone Sentinels): "**661 men** brought to the field."
  - ⚠️ Sum of the three engaged regiments (480+647+661 = **1,788**) does not match the brigade-level **1,950** figure — a ~160-man gap, flagged not reconciled; likely explained by the 1,950 figure including non-engaged 12th/15th VT companies or a different counting basis.
  - Not located: Busey & Martin citation.

### Batteries (II Corps Artillery Brigade, Hazard commanding)

Aggregate for the whole brigade, Stone Sentinels summary table: **600 men**, 150 total losses (35%) — **not broken out by individual battery** on that page. Per-battery figures below come from each battery's own monument/unit page.

#### Cushing's Battery A, 4th US Artillery
- **Commander/fate:** 1st Lt. Alonzo Cushing — **killed in action** at the Angle, July 3. Three wounds are documented: a non-fatal shell fragment, then a shell fragment that "tore open his stomach and groin" (likely mortal alone), then a bullet through the mouth that killed him instantly at roughly 100 yards' range as the Confederates closed. Sources agree the popular "fired the last shot" legend is inaccurate — Sgt. Frederick Fuger fired that shot after Cushing had already fallen. https://www.army.mil/medalofhonor/cushing/ · https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/monuments-to-individuals/alonzo-cushing/ · https://www.nps.gov/articles/000/alonzo-cushing-gettysburg.htm
  - Note: this resolves the head-vs-mouth wound question flagged as a discrepancy in source library pass 1 (Haskell says "shot through the head"; Fuger's account says the bullet entered his mouth) — modern reconstructions describe multiple wounds, of which the fatal one was through the mouth. Both period accounts may be describing different wounds in the same sequence rather than contradicting each other; not fully reconciled here, but the additional detail is recorded.
- **Guns:** six 3-inch Ordnance Rifles.
- **Strength:**
  - US Army Medal of Honor page: **"126 men and six cannons."**
  - Stone Sentinels battery page: no strength figure given; casualties only (1 officer + 5 men killed; 1 officer + 31 men wounded = 38 total).

#### Rorty's Battery B, 1st New York Light Artillery
- **Commander/fate:** Capt. James McKay Rorty — **killed in action, July 3**, when Kemper's Virginians briefly overran the battery; killed alongside 9 other men in hand-to-hand fighting. Lt. Albert S. Sheldon was wounded in the same action. https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/union-monuments/new-york/new-york-artillery-and-engineers/1st-new-york-battery-b/
- **Guns:** four 10-pounder Parrott Rifles (reduced from a larger complement by July 2 losses).
- **Strength:** Stone Sentinels — **114 men** brought to the field; casualties 10 killed, 16 wounded.

#### Arnold's Battery A, 1st Rhode Island Light Artillery
- **Commander/fate:** Capt. William A. Arnold — **survived unwounded**. Hazard's official report (reproduced by Iron Brigader) lists him among "Surviving Officers," "gallantly fighting his own battery and saving it." https://ironbrigader.com/2021/06/26/captain-john-g-hazards-report-on-the-union-2nd-corps-artillery-brigade-at-gettysburg/
- **Guns:** six 3-inch Ordnance Rifles.
- **Strength:** Stone Sentinels — **139 men** brought to the field; casualties 4 killed, 24 wounded. Civilwarintheeast.com independently states the same 139/4/24 figures — likely derivative of the same underlying source, so treated as low-independence corroboration rather than a second data point.

#### Cowan's 1st New York Independent Battery
- **Commander/fate:** Capt. Andrew Cowan — **survived unwounded**; his only war wound came later, at Third Winchester (1864). Known for the "double canister at ten yards" repulse at the Angle. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andrew_Cowan_(soldier) · https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/union-monuments/new-york/new-york-artillery-and-engineers/1st-new-york-independent-battery/
- **Guns:** six Ordnance Rifles.
- **Strength:** Stone Sentinels — **113 men** brought to the field; casualties 4 killed, 8 wounded (plus 14 horses killed).

#### Woodruff's Battery I, 1st US Artillery
- **Commander/fate:** 1st Lt. George A. Woodruff — **mortally wounded July 3, died July 4, 1863**. Hazard's report (Iron Brigader reproduction) states he "fell, mortally wounded" and "expired on July 4." Command passed to Lt. Tully McCrea. A lower-confidence web synthesis (not independently re-verified) adds he was hit by a conical ball through the body, died at the Granite Schoolhouse hospital, and his body was later retrieved by his father and buried at Oakridge Cemetery, Marshall, Michigan — flagged as unverified detail. https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/us-regulars/us-artillery/1st-us-artillery-battery-i/ · https://ironbrigader.com/2021/06/26/captain-john-g-hazards-report-on-the-union-2nd-corps-artillery-brigade-at-gettysburg/
  - Note: Woodruff was positioned at Ziegler's Grove, not the Angle proper — consistent with the note already recorded in source library pass 1.
- **Guns:** six 12-pounder Napoleons.
- **Strength:** Stone Sentinels — **120 men** brought to the field. ⚠️ Casualty figures show an internal discrepancy between two fetch passes of the same/related source: one gave "1 killed, 1 officer + 28 men wounded" (30 total), another gave "1 killed, 1 officer + 23 men wounded" (25 total) — both reported, not reconciled.

### Units/data points with zero sourced strength figures ("not located")

## 3. Confidence guidance — mapping figures to the format's provenance vocabulary

Per `docs/format/battle-format.md`, keyframe confidence is `documented` | `inferred` | `unknown`, and `documented` requires a non-empty citation.

### Should be tagged `documented` (period-record or official-report-derived, with a specific citation)
- Longstreet's 4,700/15,000 figures — a named participant's own 1896 memoir, page-cited (p. 315).
- Encyclopedia Virginia's Armistead brigade casualty figure of 1,191, explicitly sourced to Col. W.R. Aylett's July 12, 1863 official report (Museum of the Confederacy).
- Col. R.M. Mayo's Aug. 13, 1863 official report figure for Brockenbrough's brigade ("800 muskets," ~880 officers and men) — a period after-action report, though the report itself was only accessed via a secondary NPS-essay citation, not directly fetched; if used, cite through that essay and flag the secondary-hop.
- William Harrow's official report figure for Gibbon's division (3,773 men) and his brigade's own-reported losses (722) — official report, reproduced by a secondary site (Civilwarhome.com/Wikipedia); citable as documented with the report as the primary citation and the reproduction noted.
- Hazard's official report on the II Corps Artillery Brigade (via Iron Brigader reproduction) for battery commander fates (Arnold survived, Woodruff mortally wounded) — an official after-action report is the citation basis.
- US Army Medal of Honor citation for Cushing (126 men, six cannon) and for Webb (wounded) — an official commendation record, though compiled well after 1863; treat as documented for the *fact* (strength/wound), with the caveat that Medal of Honor citations are administrative documents, not battlefield returns.

### Should be tagged `inferred` (modern reconstruction, monument-committee tallies, or historiographic synthesis)
- All Stone Sentinels "Present"/"engaged" brigade and regiment figures. These derive from GNMP monument inscriptions and modern compilations of OR data (the page's own disclaimer: "in disagreement between regimental and brigade records") — useful, multiply-corroborating, but not itself a period document. This is the majority of the strength figures in this file.
- Busey & Martin's reproduced totals (11,484–12,500 column; ~5,820 Pickett's division) — a modern (1986) scholarly reconstruction from OR and other returns, the standard reference in the field, but a secondary synthesis, not a primary return.
- Hess (2001) ~12,000–12,500, Stewart (1957) ~10,500 — modern historiographic estimates, explicitly presented by Encyclopedia Virginia as a range reflecting scholarly disagreement.
- Encyclopedia Virginia's "12,000–13,000" total and "5,800–6,200" Pickett's division range — an encyclopedia-level historiographic synthesis.
- Wikipedia's casualty breakdown by division (cited to Hess 2001 and Sears 2003) — secondary synthesis; also carries a known internal arithmetic inconsistency (6,840 vs. stated 6,555), which should itself be noted if this figure is ever surfaced in-app.
- All per-regiment "brought to the field"/"present for duty" Stone Sentinels figures for Union units.

### Should be tagged `unknown` or omitted (no reliable record located)
- Brockenbrough's brigade's Stone Sentinels "Present" figure — flagged above as a duplication artifact; do not encode any strength value for this brigade from that source. Use the Mayo/Ernsberger/Gottfried range (800–1,100) only, each tagged to its own source and none promoted to a single number.
- Lang's fate on July 3 — not located; leave the fate field `unknown` rather than inferring "survived" from silence.
- Per-regiment strengths for 20th MA, 59th NY, 10th NY Battalion, 108th NY, 39th NY (4 cos.), 12th VT, 15th VT — not located; do not backfill from brigade totals by subtraction, since brigade totals themselves are contested (see Stannard's brigade gap above).
- The "period estimate" figures already flagged in source library pass 1 (Haskell's 18,000 for the whole column) remain a flagged period overestimate — encode as `documented` for the fact "Haskell said 18,000," but do not present it as an engaged-strength estimate for the app's own numbers.

### Cases requiring dual/conflicting citation, not a single confidence tag
Several units have two conflicting figures traced to the *same* nominal source (Lane's Brigade, Wilcox's Brigade, Woodruff's Battery casualties, Stannard's brigade sum-vs-total). For these, the authoring tool should record both figures with their respective extraction context rather than picking one as "the" documented number — neither extraction is more official than the other; the disagreement is between two readings of one source page, not between two independent sources.

## Biggest disagreements found

1. **"Present" vs. actual July 3 strength (systemic, Confederate side):** Marshall's/Pettigrew's and Davis's brigades' Stone Sentinels figures are explicitly labeled "present on the first day" (~2,000 each), while narrative text for Lane's/Lowrance's brigades states the two brigades combined "could field no more than 800 men" by July 3. The Heth's/Pender's-division "Present" figures likely substantially overstate actual July 3 assault strength; Pickett's fresh-division figures do not have this problem.
2. **Brockenbrough's brigade — data corruption:** the only Stone Sentinels figure available is byte-for-byte identical to Davis's brigade figure (implausible duplication), leaving only a 800–1,100-man range from period/secondary sources with no consistent single number.
3. **Stannard's brigade — internal arithmetic gap:** brigade-level Stone Sentinels total of 1,950 vs. the sum of the three actually-engaged regiments (13th+14th+16th VT = 1,788), a ~160-man unexplained gap.
4. **Extraction inconsistency within the same source (Lane's and Wilcox's brigades):** two separate fetches of the same Stone Sentinels page returned different Present/casualty numbers for each brigade (Lane: 1,355 vs. 1,730; Wilcox: 1,777 vs. 1,725) — a data-quality issue with page-scraping this particular site rather than a genuine historical disagreement, but it must be flagged since it affects reliability of every Stone Sentinels figure in this file.
5. **Column-level strength, Confederate (retained from source library pass 2, still unresolved):** Longstreet's own ~15,000 vs. Busey & Martin's 11,484–12,500 vs. Hess's ~12,000–12,500 vs. Stewart's ~10,500 — roughly a 4,500-man (30%) spread between the highest period figure and the lowest modern reconstruction.
6. **1st Minnesota — three figures, three scopes:** 420 (whole regiment), 262 (eight companies, July 2 charge only), 330 ("engaged," secondary monument) — a caution that "strength" figures for this war are frequently scope-dependent (whole regiment vs. companies actually present vs. present-for-duty vs. engaged-in-this-specific-action) and must be captured with their scope, not just a bare number.
7. **Harrow's brigade casualties:** 768 (War Department/NPS marker) vs. 722 (Harrow's own official report) — two "official-ish" sources disagree by 46, illustrating that even documented-tier figures are not always internally consistent.
8. **Cushing's battery strength:** the Army Medal of Honor page gives 126 men; Stone Sentinels' own battery monument page gives no strength figure at all — not a numeric conflict but a coverage gap on the otherwise-most-consistent source.
