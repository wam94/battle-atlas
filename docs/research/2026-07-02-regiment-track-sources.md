## 1. Bachelder's July 3 troop-position maps — the PD backbone, but asymmetric

### 1.1 The 1876/1883 three-sheet "Positions of Troops" set (the map we already use)

**What it shows (VERIFIED by direct inspection of IIIF crops of the Third Day sheet, this pass):**

- **Title block:** "THIRD DAY'S BATTLE — POSITIONS OF TROOPS, compiled and added for the Government by John B. Bachelder … from the OFFICIAL REPORTS, consultations on the field, private letters, and oral explanations of the officers of both armies. Entered according to Act of Congress A.D. 1876." Scale 1:12,000.
- **Time encoding:** the legend defines **four position classes per unit** — *First Position*, *Intermediate*, *Evening*, *All Day* (distinct hatchings, blue Union / red Confederate) — plus "*Dotted lines and arrows indicate the movements of troops.*" So the sheet is **not** a single snapshot: it is a coarse 2–3-step time sequence per unit with movement vectors. It is also not clock-stamped — classes, not times. (This refines pass 1's finding: "one map per day" both overstates and understates the granularity.)
- **Echelon — the critical asymmetry (VERIFIED by inspection of the Angle/Cemetery Ridge crop):**
  - **Union: individual regiments and batteries, named.** Legible at the Angle: `8'Ohio`, `1'Del`, `12'N.J.`, `14'Ct`, `108'N.Y.`, `71'Pa`, `72'Pa`, `69'Pa`, `59'N.Y.`, `7'Mich`, `20'Ms`, `19'Me`, `15'Ms`, `1'Minn`, `82'N.Y.`, `13'Vt`, `14'Vt`, `16'Vt`, and batteries by commander (`CUSHING`, `COWAN`, `ARNOLD`, `WOODRUFF`, `BROWN`), with brigade labels (SMYTH, HAYS, HALL, STANNARD, DOUBLEDAY) overlaid. Corps-badge symbols encode corps/division/brigade ("Figures preceding Corps Badges indicate the number of the Brigade").
  - **Confederate assault column: regiment bars at the START positions, brigade labels at the forward positions.** Two independent inspections of the same sheet at full resolution resolve this precisely. In the **Seminary Ridge formation area**, the assault brigades are drawn as lines **subdivided into numbered regiment bars** — Garnett's `56'`, `28'`, `19'` (VA…); Armistead's `38'`, `57'`, `53'`; Kemper's regiments likewise — with every Confederate battery named individually (Dearing's: Stribling, Caskie, Macon, Blount; Poague, etc.). But the **forward/intermediate positions near the wall** are dashed red bars labeled at brigade level only (`GARNETT`, `ARMISTEAD`, `KEMPER`, `DAVIS`, `PETTIGREW`, `ARCHER FRY`, `SCALES`, `LANE`, `BROCKENBOROUGH`) with red advance arrows. Some movement arrows carry **clock annotations** (e.g. "…to its right at 1 P.M." near Poague's guns). Confederate regiment labels also appear in other sectors (`25'Va`, `10'Va`, `1'Md` at Culp's Hill). **Net: PD regiment-level start geometry exists for the assault column; the at-the-wall geometry is brigade-level.**
- **LOC metadata (VERIFIED via `?fo=json` fetches this pass):** 1876 first printing = item 99447491 (resources `g3824g.cw0325000a/b/c`; sheet c IIIF canvas 8315×11407 px) and the Library of Virginia copy, item lva0000067, whose catalog note states verbatim: *"A very minute analysis of the deployment of the various units of both armies, with the names of commanding officers, **period of time spent in a particular position**, and other pertinent information is given. Dotted lines and arrows indicate the movements of the troops, and **the positions at various times of the day are shown by symbols explained in the legend**."* 1883 new edition = item 99447492 (`g3824g.cw0326000a/b/c`; sheet c: JP2 8476×10489 px ~21 MB, master TIFF 266,714,848 bytes — the URLs already in pass 1).
  - https://www.loc.gov/item/99447491/ · https://www.loc.gov/item/99447492/ · https://www.loc.gov/item/lva0000067/
  - Sheet 3 (1883 ed) viewer: https://www.loc.gov/resource/g3824g.cw0326000c/ — master TIFF: https://tile.loc.gov/storage-services/master/gmd/gmd382/g3824/g3824g/cw0326000cZ.tif
  - Sheet 3 (1876 ed, LOC G&M copy) IIIF: https://tile.loc.gov/image-services/iiif/service:gmd:gmd382:g3824:g3824g:cw0325000c/info.json (8,315×11,407 px) · master TIFF: https://tile.loc.gov/storage-services/master/gmd/gmd382/g3824/g3824g/cw0325000cZ.tif (~285 MB)
  - **Highest-pixel scan** = the Library of Virginia copy, sheet 3: https://www.loc.gov/resource/glva01.lva00067c/ — JP2: https://tile.loc.gov/storage-services/service/gmd/gmdlva/glva/glva01/lva00067c.jp2 (9,608×12,001 px)
  - Same sheet also in the 1883 War Dept atlas *Military Maps of the United States*, sequence 20: https://www.loc.gov/resource/g3701sm.gcw0093000/?sp=20 (8,798×10,460)
- **Legibility:** ~300 ppi effective (74×71 cm sheet); regiment labels fully legible at IIIF `pct:50` regional crops and at full res (dozens read by eye across both inspections this pass).
- **Printed caveat on the sheet itself (VERIFIED):** "Certain lines of breastworks are shown which, however, were in many instances thrown up after the fighting ceased" — don't treat drawn works as battle-time terrain without cross-checking.
- **Provenance status: PD-traceable.** 1876/1883 US publication; LOC rights statement (VERIFIED): "The content of the Library of Congress Geography and Map Division digitized collections is free to use and reuse unless a Rights Advisory statement is present" — none is. Geometry may be traced directly.

**Bottom line for Q1:** *Yes — for both armies at the start positions; Union-only at the forward positions.* Bachelder 1876/1883 places every Union regiment and battery in our window with a coarse (first/intermediate/evening) class sequence, **and** places the Confederate assault regiments at their Seminary Ridge step-off positions — all PD, traceable geometry. What it does **not** give: clock-stamped states (classes, not times; the sheet superimposes all phases — there is no separate "cannonade" vs "repulse" panel) and Confederate regiment geometry at the wall (brigade bars only). The charge movement itself must be built from texts (§3), the 1880s timed set (§1.2), and fact-checked reconstructions (§2). Also note: "criticized for showing only one time slice" is **refuted** — the real critique in the literature is the inverse (too many time-states cluttering one sheet, which is why Bachelder himself later decomposed the days into timed maps).

Bachelder Gettysburg Map Set: A User's Guide*, Sauers with Bearss, Bandy & Heiser, 1995 — the standard reliability treatment, itself in copyright). Some sheets mirrored on Internet Archive. LOC additionally holds a reduced early-1900s reprint of one series sheet — item 99447494, "No. II … First day. General position of troops, 2 to 3.30 p.m." (VERIFIED metadata; JP2 4462×4943).

Resolve against the physical set before encoding.
  - **Access routes:** the timed maps were also reproduced as the **map supplement to *The Bachelder Papers* vol. 3** (IA metadata lists "sup. Maps" — VERIFIED) and are sold as modern reprints (Savas Beatie "The Bachelder Maps," 7 oversized sheets — SNIPPET). Originals went to the War Department in 1886 → **NARA RG 77** is the next hunt; not in LOC's digital collections (VERIFIED negative).
  - **PD status:** original 1880s sheets = PD (pre-1930 + War Department authority). Rumsey applies a CC BY-NC-SA site license *to its scans*; the underlying maps are PD — for clean provenance, prefer tracing positions as facts, or locate/scan NARA or reprint originals.
  - **Implication for us:** the **1:00–3:00 PM July 3 sheet** (if Horse Soldier's slicing is right) documents regiment-level positions of both armies during the bombardment (our window's first half), and the **5:00 PM sheet** documents post-repulse positions. ⚠️ **The charge itself (~3:00–4:00 PM) falls in the gap between sheets** on either numbering — the assault movement still has to be built from texts (§3) and fact-checked reconstructions (§2). Even so, this gives PD regiment-level bombardment-phase and end anchors for the Confederate assault column beyond the 1876 sheet's start positions.
- **Reliability caveats (encode, don't hide):**
  - **Hartwig (VERIFIED PDF, Seminar 12 essay 2, pp. 24–25, 28):** Bachelder coined "High Water Mark" (after Walter Harrison identified the copse); he "had more to do with the placement of monuments and their inscriptions than any other man"; his famous claim of interviewing "the commander of every regiment and battery" in winter 1863–64 is overstated (11th/12th Corps had gone west; Stannard's Vermonters were discharged) — some coverage was later correspondence.
  - **Desjardin, *These Honored Dead*** (via H-Net review — SNIPPET): Bachelder "first framed the general action for [veterans], then let them fill in details" — his position data partly reflect his priors fed back through witnesses.
  - **Union skew:** his account is "heavily biased toward the Union, because Confederate recollections were slow to come in"; as GBMA Superintendent of Tablets he could deny monument placements (http://civildiscourse-historyblog.com/blog/2015/3/20/the-great-battle-which-would-naturally-decide-the-contest-john-badger-bachelder-and-the-creation-of-gettysburg — VERIFIED fetch). Practical implication: his July 3 **Union front-line** positions are the best-sourced; **Confederate intermediate positions at the wall are the most interpretation-laden**.
  - **Sauers:** the standard treatment is Richard A. Sauers, "John B. Bachelder: Government Historian of the Battle of Gettysburg," *Gettysburg Magazine* 3 (July 1990), 115–127 — **not accessible this pass; its specific July 3 verdicts unverified.** Sauers also co-wrote the 1995 *User's Guide* to the map set.
  - Treat Bachelder's Union regiment geometry as `documented` (map citation), Confederate start-position regiment bars as `documented`-with-Union-skew caveat, and interpretive placements near the copse as contested.

### 1.3 The Bachelder Papers (Ladd & Ladd, Morningside, 1994–95, 3 vols. + map supplement)

- Originals: New Hampshire Historical Society (https://www.nhhistory.org/object/273079/john-b-bachelder-papers-1863-1894 — VERIFIED). The 1994 edition (transcription apparatus, annotations, indexes) is in copyright; archive.org vol. 1 is lending-only: https://archive.org/details/bachelderpapersg0001unse.
- ⚠️ **Copyright nuance stronger than previously assumed:** the underlying 19th-century letters are *not automatically PD*. Under 17 U.S.C. §303(a), works unpublished before 1978 and **first published 1978–2002** (which most of these letters were, in the 1994 edition) are protected until **Dec. 31, 2047**. Practical rule: the **facts** in the letters (unit X at position Y at time Z) are not copyrightable and fully usable for geometry/timeline; **quote sparingly** from the 1994 volumes; letters that were published in the 19th century (e.g. in SHSP or regimentals) are PD via that earlier publication. (Statutory analysis, not fetched — verify before bulk reproduction.)
- Content: veteran testimony **is** regiment-level position evidence in places — verified example: Trimble's 1883 letter placing his pre-charge position "about one and one half miles from that point on Cemetery Ridge…" (quoted in Hartwig, Seminar 12 essay 2, p. 28); also Cowan's Dec. 2, 1885 letter re Cushing (pass 2) and the 69th/71st PA testimony underlying Hartwig's reconstructions. Position-at-time support: mostly **sequence/relative**, occasionally clock-anchored.

## 3. Attested regiment-level movement facts (the keyframe candidates)

Verdict vocabulary: **timed** = clock time attested (encode `documented` keyframe at a time, ±10–20 min); **phased** = order-of-events attested, clock inferred (`documented` position + `inferred` time); **position-only** = a place, no sequence.

### The seven asked

1. **Brockenbrough's early withdrawal — phased, wing-level.** The brigade advanced in **two wings** (Col. Mayo commanding one), took the 8th Ohio's flank fire plus Osborn's Cemetery Hill guns, and broke before reaching the road — the only assault brigade that never closed. Primary: Col. R. M. Mayo's report (OR 27/2, via the Texas ARK route) and Fry's SHSP 7 account (repo-VERIFIED: http://www.gdg.org/research/SHSP/shpettig.html — "Brockenbrough's brigade genuinely broke early," pass-2 finding). Secondary: Gottfried, "Brockenbrough's Brigade," *Gettysburg Magazine* 23; GNMP Seminar 12 essay 5 (https://npshistory.com/series/symposia/gettysburg_seminars/12/essay5.pdf — cited in the OOB doc). Per-REGIMENT split not attested; **2-wing decomposition is** (40th VA + 22nd Bn / 47th + 55th VA). Time: relative to the ~3:00 step-off, broke within ~15 min — inferred.
2. **Davis's brigade at the fence / 11th Mississippi at the Brian barn — phased, one strong regiment fact.** The 11th Miss (fresh on July 3 — train guard July 1) got farthest; its Co. A ("University Greys") reached the wall by the Brian barn with the colors, ~100% casualties. VERIFIED: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_Greys · https://www.thelocalvoice.net/oxford/150-years-ago-today-the-11th-mississippi-at-the-battle-of-gettysburg/ · http://www.gdg.org/research/OOB/Confederate/July1-3/greys.html. 2nd/42nd Miss and 55th NC reports (OR 27/2) attest reaching the road cut and the slaughter beyond — phased only. Supports: a distinct 11th-Miss track (documented endpoint at the Brian wall) vs. brigade-average track for the rest.
3. **8th Ohio flank attack — the best-attested Union regiment maneuver; phased with clock anchors.** VERIFIED directly this pass from Sawyer's regimental history full text (https://archive.org/details/amilitaryhistor00groogoog; djvu.txt in scratchpad as `sawyer8oh.txt`): cannonade opened "**between twelve and one o'clock**," lasted "**nearly two hours**" while the regiment lay in the sunken Emmitsburg-road cut; as the column approached, the skirmish charge, then "**We changed our front, and taking position by a fence, facing the left flank of the advancing column of rebels, the men were ordered to fire into their flank at will**"; afterwards "advanced and cut off three regiments," capturing the colors of the **34th North Carolina** (Lowrance's) and **38th Virginia** (Armistead's). OR cross-check: Sawyer's report, OR 27/1:461–62; ABT article (VERIFIED fetch: https://www.battlefields.org/learn/articles/picket-line-against-picketts-charge — 209 men, ~200 yds in front of the main line). Supports a 3-keyframe regiment track (road cut → left wheel to fence → advance to cut off). ⚠️ His "between twelve and one" vs. the repo's 1:00/1:07 anchors — keep as disagreement. **Note: the 8th Ohio (Carroll's brigade) is not in our current 22-unit roster — it would enter as a new tracked regiment.**
4. **Stannard's 13th/16th VT wheel — phased-to-timed; the best-documented moving regiments on the field.** "Change front forward on first company" attested three independent ways: Sturtevant 1910 pp. 304–305 (repo-VERIFIED, https://archive.org/details/cu31924030916187); the 13th VT monument inscription ("changed front forward on first company, advanced 200 yards, attacking the Confederate right flank … capturing 243 prisoners" — VERIFIED: https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/union-monuments/vermont/13th-vermont/); G. G. Benedict, *Vermont in the Civil War* vol. 2 (1888), free full text VERIFIED to exist: https://archive.org/details/vermontincivilwa02bene (passage extraction still to do). Second phase: the **16th VT reversed** (joined by the 14th's fire) against Wilcox/Lang ~15 min later — Veazey's OR report + Benedict. ## 4. Company level — confirmed absent as a system; nine attested exceptions

**Systematic finding (VERIFIED negative):** no map or text source places companies systematically during our window. Bachelder maps: regiment floor. OR: regimental reports name companies only anecdotally (Smith's 71st PA report: none at all). Monumentation: regimental flank markers only in the July 3 sector. Modern cartography (ABT, Hess, Laino, Stanley): regiment floor. The one apparent exception — the Gettysburg Animated app's "detached companies" — is a modern commercial interpolation, not a source. **Model companies as frontage-derived from regiment tracks, plus these exceptions** (all verified by the dedicated survey agent unless noted):

1. **69th PA Cos. I, A, F, D at the Angle** — "the first three companies, I, A and F, were ordered to change front and face these flankers"; I and A executed; "the commander of Company F, George Thompson, being killed before he could give the command, they remained at the wall"; the gap "through which the enemy poured, enveloping the latter company"; Co. D "held the enemy at bay, using their muskets as clubs" (HIGH; McDermott 1889 verbatim, https://archive.org/details/briefhistoryof00mcde). The gold-standard fragment.
2. **Cushing's battery by section** — 2 serviceable guns at the wall vs. 4 wrecks at the crest (HIGH; NPS/Fuger). Sub-fragment: 71st PA men running one gun to the outer-angle apex (LOW — forum-grade: https://civilwartalk.com/threads/the-california-regiment-mans-cushings-guns-at-the-angle.189081/).
3. **Cowan's 5+1 gun split** — **five guns SOUTH of the copse ~100 ft from the wall; one gun (Sgt. Mullaly's) carried NORTH of the trees beside the 69th PA/Cushing** (HIGH; Brown 1985 Filson PDF, read page-by-page). ⚠️ **Corrects the task brief's "single gun on the south side" — it is the reverse.**
4. **Brown's B/1st RI as a 4-gun unit** — and note **Lt. Joseph Milne was detached from Brown's to Cushing's battery** (killed there): "Milne's section" belongs with Cushing's guns on July 3, not Brown's (HIGH; Filson + https://gettysburg.stonesentinels.com/union-monuments/rhode-island/rhode-island-battery-b/).
5. **Arnold's left-section gun at the wall** while the other four withdrew; last round double-shotted (MED-HIGH; Stone Sentinels; timing debated).
6. **106th PA Cos. A & B on the skirmish line in front of Cowan** — the other eight companies had gone to Cemetery Hill on July 2 (HIGH; Filson fn. 75 citing Ward 1906 p. 199). **Directly affects Webb-brigade decomposition: "Webb's brigade" in our window = 69th + 71st + 72nd PA + two companies of the 106th.**
7. **Vermont wheel mechanics** — "change front forward on first company" is company-resolution kinematics by definition (first company pivots, successive companies wheel in). HIGH for the maneuver; MED for per-company detail (Sturtevant 1910 not yet page-fetched).
8. **8th Ohio skirmish split** — July 2: Cos. A & I on the line (Capt. Nickerson), D in support, B in the dawn fight (VERIFIED in Sawyer's text this pass). July 3 PM company identities **not** attested — Sawyer names no companies in the charge passage. Model reserve + skirmish line, companies unnamed. ("Cos. D and B as designated skirmishers": SNIPPET only, do not cite.)
9. **University Greys (Co. A, 11th Miss) at the Brian wall** — the lone Confederate company fragment, ~31 men, 100% casualties (MED-HIGH; rooted in postwar Mississippi accounts; the field marker is regimental).

Structural, not positional: **59th NY and 39th NY were four-company battalions** (59th consolidated June 1863, 182 men; 39th consolidated to Cos. A–D May 31, 1863, 332 men — https://museum.dmna.ny.gov/unit-history/infantry/39th-infantry-regiment) — encode as short-frontage regiments, not company sets. **14th CT Cos. A/B carried Sharps rifles** (armament attribute; their distinct Bliss-farm action is morning, pre-window). Rorty's battery: gun-level anecdotes but no distinct section positions — exclude.

## 7. Disagreements to encode as data (not noise)

1. **Cannonade start:** ~1:00 (Rawley Martin, Waitt — repo) vs. "between twelve and one" (Sawyer, VERIFIED this pass) vs. 1:07 (Jacobs, pass 1). Keep all three.
2. **Who reached the wall:** Fry's "all five colors" & the NC claims vs. Virginia-centric accounts & Bachelder's High-Water-Mark framing (Hartwig, Seminar 12 essay 2). Encode both endpoints with attribution.
3. **72nd PA:** "stood at the crest / refused Webb's order" vs. the regiment's monument-litigation position that it fought at the wall. Two readings, one flagged.
4. **Arnold's withdrawal timing** (before vs. during the charge) — debated; encode two candidate timings.
5. **Cushing's death wound** (head vs. mouth) — already documented in pass 2/3; affects the section-exception narrative, not geometry.
6. **Cowan's gun split orientation** — the task brief's memory ("single gun south") is refuted by Brown 1985: five south, one north. Corrected here.
7. **ABT time slices** (charge 3:00–4:15) vs. literature clocks (step-off ~2:50–3:00, repulse ~3:30–3:50; older CWT sheets used 2:00–4:00). Never mix clock frames in one track without noting the frame.
8. **Bachelder time-classes vs. clock times** — the PD map gives sequence classes, not clocks; every clock attached to a Bachelder-derived position is an `inferred` time on a `documented` position. The format's per-keyframe confidence supports exactly this split — use it.
9. **Hess vs. others at the copse** — even top reconstructions disagree on Hall's-brigade regiment placement (20th MA / 59th NY critique — SNIPPET). Regiment tracks in the melee zone should carry wider position uncertainty than their brigade parents.
