## EC1 — Identity & command

- 9th Michigan Battery; **Capt. Jabez J. Daniels** commanding
  throughout (tablet, gettysburg.stonesentinels.com, "9th Michigan
  Battery," fetch-verified this pass). Guns: **six 3-inch Ordnance
  Rifles** (tablet, verbatim). Designated "Horse Artil'y, Cavalry
  Corps" though, per the tablet, "stationed to support the infantry of
  the 1st Corps during Pickett's Charge" — a horse-artillery unit
  fighting in an infantry-support role, not mounted cavalry
  operations.

## EC2 — Engaged strength

- **119 men** (tablet, verbatim, fetch-verified this pass).

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **The approach march** [C, report-primary, via `us-ha-1-robertson.md`
   EC3.1]: joined Robertson's column en route, June 28 – July 1.
2. **July 3, 12:30 P.M. – July 4, 7 A.M.** [C ~12:30, tablet, PRECISE]:
   "held the position... from 12:30 p.m. July 3rd, until 7 a.m. the
   following morning" (tablet, verbatim) — a rare exact-timestamped
   window for a horse-artillery battery, on Hancock Avenue 180 feet
   south of Pleasonton Avenue, across from the Pennsylvania Monument
   (i.e. Cemetery Ridge, near the II Corps line).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. [C] The approach march (EC3.1).
2. [A ~12:30 July 3] Into position on Cemetery Ridge, under Maj. Gen.
   Newton's (I Corps) direction (`us-ha-1-robertson.md` EC5.1).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **Engaged under Newton's direction** [C, report-primary]: "doing
   good execution, until the close of the battle" (Robertson's report,
   via the parent dossier).
2. **Rounds expended, tablet [E]**: "322 rounds of shot, shell and
   canister" (tablet, verbatim, fetch-verified this pass) — this
   pass's most precisely itemized horse-artillery ordnance figure.
3. **Held the position overnight** [D]: through 7 A.M. July 4 (EC3.2).
