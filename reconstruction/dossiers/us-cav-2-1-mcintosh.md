## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **The Hanover Road right** [C, tablet-adjudicated class]: the 2nd
   Division tablet's "Took position with the right on Hanover Road"
   places McIntosh's brigade on the division's right, with Custer's
   (recalled) brigade completing the line — the Rummel farm ground,
   georeferenced at **±75 m** on the ECF Bachelder sheets (two-tie
   solve, Hanover×Low Dutch + Rummel farm centroid, local coords
   (10048.3, 6822.0); East Cavalry Field sits off the main heightmap
   square, `reconstruction/spatial/bachelder-manifest.json` id
   `ecf-base`).
2. **PASS-13 SHEET-CROP PATCH (ED-73 class):** sheet `ecf-j3-mid`
   (11 a.m.–2 p.m.) draws "McINTOSH BRIGADE 1' BRIG. 2' DIV." at local
   (10,809, 5,419) — a full ~1,400 m EAST of the Rummel farm tie point,
   NOT overlapping Jenkins's/Hampton's ground on the same sheet. Two
   named sub-blocks carry their OWN drawn clock range: "1' MD… 1 to
   1.30" and "1' PA… (Anthony Howard) 1 to 1.45 PM" — both narrower
   than, and sitting inside, the tablet-adopted CA-ECF-2 envelope
   (13:30–14:45). Read via `crop_sheet.py`/`georef_maps.py`, radius
   ~77 m (sheet `estAbsUncertaintyM`). This is the first drawn-bar read
   for this dossier (pass 12 flagged none was run); it corrects the
   working mental picture from "co-located with Custer at Rummel farm"
   to "a separate, more easterly sector astride the Low Dutch/Hanover
   Road crossing," with Custer's brigade block ~1,240 m to the west
   (local 9,570, 5,333) — the two brigades are drawn as ADJACENT
   sectors of one line, not stacked on the same ground.
2. **The dismounted skirmish advance** [D]: the brigade's carbine line
   pushed toward the Rummel farm buildings and the fence lines ahead
   of the woods on Cress Ridge (Stuart's division tablet: "a ridge
   which controlled the open ground toward Hanover"), triggering the
   Confederate response.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The general engagement** [C ~14:00]: "soon engaged with Major
   Gen. Stuart's command" (the tablet's own phrase for the opening of
   the sustained fight — carbine and artillery duel, Randol's and
   Pennington's batteries named as co-engaged).
2. **The mounted repulse of Hampton and Fitzhugh Lee** [C ~15:00, tier
   tablet-adjudicated]: "About 3 P. M. Brig. Gen. Hampton's and Brig.
   Gen. Fitzhugh Lee's Brigades[,] the reserves which had been
   concealed on the Stallsmith Farm[,] emerged from the woods in front
   and charged but were repulsed with the aid of Artillery" — the
   brigade's own tablet account of what popular history calls "the
   grand charge," with the Stallsmith Farm concealment detail not
   carried on the division-level tablet.
3. No fire-window/rounds-expended primary found this pass (EC5 stays
   at tablet-narrative grain).

## Conflicts & negative evidence

1. **McIntosh's own OR report (No. 348, pp. 967-968) is
   post-battle-itinerary grade, not a battle narrative.** Both fetched
   pages (IA/civilwar.com channel, 2026-07-12) cover only the
   brigade's July 4-28 marches (Emmitsburg, Waynesborough,
   Funkstown...); no July 2-3 narrative, no clock, no casualty figure
   was found on either page. This mirrors the pattern already on
   record for Gregg's own report (below) and Custer's (see
   us-cav-3-2-custer.md) — the division's OR-report layer is thin on
   the day itself and rich on the pursuit; the tablet layer carries
   the battle narrative instead. Recorded honestly rather than
   papered over with a B&M-hop substitute for a report quote.
2. **The "1st PA Cavalry" roster-table entry** (EC2) — carried as an
   unresolved secondary-source table ambiguity, not summed into the
   brigade total.
3. **Gregg's division-level report** (or-27-1-gregg-dmm, OR No. 344,
   pp. 949-960) credits McIntosh directly: "handled his brigade with
   great skill, and deserves particular mention for his gallantry and
   untiring energy throughout the day" (p. 957, fetch-verified
   2026-07-12) — a division-command credit line, not a clock or a
   casualty figure, carried as command-record corroboration.
