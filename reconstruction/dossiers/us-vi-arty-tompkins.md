## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2, arrival** [D]: "Arrived in the afternoon and evening from
   Manchester Md." (brigade tablet, verbatim) — the same forced march
   `us-vi-hq-sedgwick.md` already documents at corps grain.
2. **South of Gettysburg, east side of Sedgwick Avenue, near the
   George Weickert farm** [E] — the brigade's static reserve ground
   (tablet, verbatim location).
3. **Individual battery positions** [E, per-battery]: McCartney's
   battery did not reach its position "near the cemetery, on the left
   and rear of Gettysburg" until ~4 P.M. July 3 — AFTER the repulse
   had already occurred (McCartney's own report, verbatim: "the enemy
   had been repulsed before the battery took position"); Butler's
   (G, 2nd U.S.) and Williston's (D, 2nd U.S.) batteries were "brought
   up to Ziegler's Grove in rear of Third Division Second Corps on the
   repulse of Longstreet's Assault" July 3, alongside Martin's (F, 5th
   U.S.) — three of this brigade's regular batteries independently
   converge on the SAME rear-of-Hays reinforcement ground the same
   afternoon (cross-reference: `us-ii-3-hq-hays.md`, `us-btty-
   woodruff.md`); Harn's battery "was ordered to support Hays's
   Division of the Second Corps" July 3 afternoon — a FOURTH battery
   on the same cross-reference.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **THE BRIGADE'S OWN SELF-CHARACTERIZATION** [D, verbatim]: "batteries
   were placed in reserve on different portions of the field so as to
   be available but with exception of 1st New York Battery [Cowan's]
   were not actively engaged" (brigade tablet) — an attested-static
   majority, matching `us-vi-hq-sedgwick.md`'s corps-grain framing
   exactly.
2. **McCartney's battery, July 3** [D]: fired "only four rounds of
   long range solid shot" after arriving post-repulse; separately
   collected "48 rounds of 3-inch projectiles, perfect" abandoned by
   the withdrawing 1st New Hampshire Battery (McCartney's own report,
   verbatim) — a genuinely negative/minimal engagement record, own-
   report sourced.
3. **Harn's, Waterman's, Adams's own reports** [D, report-primary,
   fetch-verified this pass]: all three are march-itinerary documents
   (June 12/28 – July 25) with NO stated rounds-fired or casualty
   figures for the Gettysburg days specifically — consistent with the
   tablet's "not actively engaged" line for these three; Waterman's
   states the battery "took position to left of Gettysburg, supported
   by a portion of Brigadier-General Webb's division" July 3, without
   further activity detail.
4. **The Ziegler's-Grove convergence (EC3.3)** is this dossier's best
   finding: FOUR of the brigade's eight batteries (Butler's,
   Williston's, Martin's, Harn's) were independently moved to reinforce
   the II Corps's Hays-front sector on July 3, a genuine cross-corps
   reserve-commitment pattern this corpus's II Corps dossiers do not
   currently carry from the VI Corps side.
