## EC5 — Activity record — THE STRUCTURED PARTIAL, VERBATIM

1. **The participation statement**: "The First Virginia Artillery
  [Dance's battalion] and a portion of Carter's and [William]
  Nelson's battalions engaged the enemy's batteries, **in order to
  divert their fire from our infantry**" — fire "well directed, and
  its fine effect was very noticeable", temporarily silencing
  Cemetery Hill artillery (or-27-2-jtbrown) — the wing's July 3 role
  was DIVERSIONARY counter-battery, not assault preparation.
2. **Carter's mission statement** [C]: the ten rifles fired on
  Cemetery Hill "to divert the fire of the enemy's guns from Hill's
  and Pickett's troops" and to cover Dance; his observation of the
  Union response: "**Their fire slackened, and finally ceased**"
  (or-27-2-carter) — a CSA-side sequence witness to CA-J3A-4→5 from
  the Oak Hill axis (tier D).
3. **The negatives inside the partial**: Carter's other guns (the
  smoothbore remainder) not stated as firing; Nelson only "a
  portion"; Jones absent (cavalry/train); Latimer's battalion — the
  fetched pages give NO July 3 fire record (Brown's July 3 passage
  names only Dance/Carter/Nelson) — Raine's guns carried as
  probably-limited, NOT attested-silent (honesty: absence of record,
  not record of absence).
4. **July 2 record** [C]: Latimer's Benner's Hill fight ("aided by
  Graham's battery ... and from Dance's Watson's and Smith's jr.,
  batteries ... on the right of our line"); the withdrawal of three
  batteries against "superior positioning"; Latimer mw.
