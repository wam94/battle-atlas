## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **Arrival + deployment** [C via or-27-1-dobke-45ny ~0]: arrived
   11 a.m.; "hurried to the battle-field in double-quick, to the
   right of the First Army Corps"; the 45th NY "on the immediate
   right of the First Corps, threw out four companies as skirmishers
   at 11.30 a.m.", then wholly deployed as skirmishers; **"Dilger's
   battery, in the rear, and the Sixty-first Ohio, on the right"** —
   the brigade's left-anchor geometry primary.
2. The line north of town [D]: the open-field skirmish line under
   the Oak Hill batteries' fire ("grape, canister, solid shot, and
   shell") — the enfilade position the corps' bent front forced.
3. The retreat ladder [D]: ~16:00 ordered back; **the seminary
   rally** ("ordered to halt and cover the retreat of the First and
   Eleventh Corps through Gettysburg" — a cross-CORPS cover task,
   primary); the town gauntlet (below); Cemetery Hill stone fence.
4. July 2-3 [D/E]: Cemetery Hill front; the 45th NY's July 2 night
   march to the Culp's Hill stone wall ("For a mile through the
   complete darkness… through an incessant shower of bullets") —
   the ECH/Culp's crisis-reinforcement class; July 3 under the
   cross-fire.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The 1.30 flank fire** [C via or-27-1-dobke-45ny, early-lean
   flagged]: "At about 1.30 p.m. a long line of the enemy moved on
   the extreme right of the First Corps, passing the left of the
   Forty-fifth, and offering the flank" — the left wing's fire at
   50-100 yards, "the whole of the enemy's line halted, gradually
   disappeared on the same spot where they stood, and the remainder…
   surrendered, partly to the First Corps and a great number to the
   Forty-fifth" — **the O'Neal/Iverson-front surrender event from
   the XI Corps side** (the third receiving echelon after
   Cutler/Baxter). The 1.30 reading rides the ED-69 candidate
   record.
2. The under-fire window [D]: the Oak Hill artillery on the open
   skirmish line from deployment to retreat.
3. **The 157th NY ledger** [D]: detached leftward against Doles's
   flank (Schurz's order), routed — "But few of this force escaped
   us" (or-27-2-doles, both-sided); return row **307** of ~409
   engaged (or-27-1-union-return p. 183) — the corps' single worst
   regimental cell that day.
4. The 45th NY's July 2 Culp's stone-wall defense share [D]; the
   staff-officer traffic-jam complaint carried as color.
