## EC1 — Identity & command

- Artillery Brigade, XI Corps; Maj. Thomas W. Osborn, 1st New York
  Light Artillery, commanding (report dated July 29, 1863, to Hunt)
  (or-27-1-osborn).
- Battery successions: Capt. Wilkeson (G/4th US) **mw July 1** —
  "Almost at the first fire … carried from the field by 4 of his men.
  The command of the battery now devolved upon Lieutenant Bancroft"
  (or-27-1-osborn p. 747).
- **The hill's command split** [documented]: "General Howard directed
  Colonel Wainwright … to take charge of all the artillery on Cemetery
  Hill east of the Baltimore pike, and myself all of the artillery on
  Cemetery Hill west of the same road. This order was not changed"
  (osborn-weeklytimes-1879, as-quoted in the Barnett PDF fn 25) — the
  "Osborn group" of this dossier is the west-of-pike command; Ricketts/
  Wiedrich on East Cemetery Hill fought under Wainwright ("on this
  p.m. Colonel Wainwright, chief of artillery First Corps, took
  command of his battery", or-27-1-osborn).

## EC2 — Engaged strength

- Guns, his own report: July 1 organic "Total, 26 guns" (Wilkeson's
  G/4th US six 12-pdrs; Dilger's I/1st OH six 12-pdrs; Heckman's K/1st
  OH four 12-pdrs; Wiedrich's I/1st NY six 3-inch; Wheeler's 13th NY
  four 3-inch); July 2 morning, with the six Reserve batteries (Eakin
  H/1st US, Taft 5th NY 20-pdr Parrotts, Hill C/1st WV, Huntington
  H/1st OH, Hall 2nd ME, Edgell 1st NH) and after Heckman's withdrawal
  and one of Wheeler's guns dismounted: "a total of fifty-two guns"
  on the hill (or-27-1-osborn pp. 747-749). The seminar essay's July 3
  figure for his own command: 36 guns (npshistory seminar 4, essay
  statement) — the corpus's "~39" secondary figure is retired in
  favor of the report's arithmetic; men-engaged figure still not
  located (gap).
- The corpus's "~1,600 rounds into Pettigrew's left": **his report
  explicitly declines the figure** — "I am unable to give any definite
  estimate of the amount of ammunition expended" (or-27-1-osborn
  p. 751) — the circulating number is downgraded to unsourced;
  verified negative carried.

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 1, afternoon → July 4 — Cemetery Hill (west of the Baltimore
  pike)** [E multi-day window]: continuous hold from the July 1
  retreat. Layout documented (p. 749): "Dilger had the right, resting
  next the Baltimore road … on his left, and in order, were Bancroft,
  Eakin, Wheeler, Hill and Hall … and across the Baltimore road I
  placed Taft in rear of and perpendicular to Bancroft; also
  Huntington in rea[r] of and perpendicular to Wheeler … I commanded
  with a reputable number of guns every point on which the enemy
  could place artillery commanding Cemetery Hill."

## EC4 — Movement legs

- Group attested-static July 3; the July 2 Reserve reinforcements
  (six batteries up) and Heckman's July 1 withdrawal are the
  battery-grain legs (pass-2 detail); ammunition resupply legs

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **July 1**: covering the retreat and the hill's first defense
  (Wilkeson mw at Barlow Knoll with his battery, or-27-1-osborn).
2. **July 3, cannonade — under the convergence, firing deliberately**
  [A window vs CA-J3A-1; his stated start "About 2 p. m." is the
  ~14:00 nominal cluster, rule 4]: "they opened along our whole front
  with an unbroken line of artillery, and also heavily on our right
  flank … the guns of not less than one-half mile of this front were
  concentrated on our position, besides several batteries on our
  right, which enfiladed our position" (p. 750); his 1879 account:
  "The shells must have reached us at the rate of one hundred and
  upwards a minute at the least" and the hill was "the best target
  for artillery practice the enemy had during the war"
  (osborn-weeklytimes-1879, as-quoted). Meade on the hill
  mid-cannonade: "He said the result of the fight depended on our
  holding the hill" (same, as-quoted).
3. **The cease — CA-J3A-4/5's Cemetery Hill event, contemporary
  wording** [D within the slackening window]: "No battery even
  s[how]ed a disposition to retire, and several times during the
  cannonading w[e] silenced several of their batteries, but at a
  moment's cessation on our part they would reopen upon us … **but as
  no satisfactory results were obtained, I ordered all our guns to
  cease firing, and the men to lie down to await developments. At the
  same time the artillery of our entire front ceased firing, and a
  few moments later the infantry of the enemy broke over the crest
  from where their artillery had been playing, and made their grand
  char[g]e across the plain upon our lines**" (or-27-1-osborn
  p. 750). Note the coupling his report makes: cease → "a few moments
  later" the infantry — the tightest Union-side cease-to-step-off
  interval statement in the corpus.
4. **The repulse fire** [D between CA-J3A-6 and CA-J3A-10]: "The whole
  force of our artillery was brought to bear upon this column, and
  the havoc produced upon their ranks was truly surprising … I
  insisted that the artillery fire should be turned intensely upon
  the infantry, and no notice wh[a]tever was to be taken of their
  artillery" (p. 750); 1879 account on effect: "Each solid shot …
  which struck either line cut out two men, but when a shell exploded
  immediately in front of either line it cut out four, six, eight or
  even more men" (as-quoted). Range keeps the hill out of canister
  radius — its fire attests range/geometry (spec EC5 principle).

## Conflicts & negative evidence

1. **Cease-fire motive, contemporary vs postwar — first-class**: the
  OR report (July 29, 1863) gives "as no satisfactory results were
  obtained"; the postwar tradition (his 1879 article as paraphrased,
  the padresteve/secondary layer, and Hunt's B&L "mistaken
  impression" effect-statement) frames a deliberate lure-the-assault
  ruse proposed on the hill. **His own ruse wording could NOT be
  verbatim-verified through any reachable channel** (verified absence
  in the reachable reproductions; the Crumb reprint is print-only).
  ED-33 candidate carries the adoption question.
2. His opening clock "About 2 p. m." — the ~14:00 nominal cluster
  (now four reports: Longstreet, Smyth, Stannard, Osborn); profiled,
  rule 4.
3. The ~1,600-round figure: report-declined (EC2) — downgraded.
4. Heckman's July 3 role unattested (flag stands); Taft's 1st CT
  Heavy companies "not engaged" (attested negative, corpus layer).
5. Men-casualty detail sits in the revised statement p. 183 (unfetched
  — open item).
