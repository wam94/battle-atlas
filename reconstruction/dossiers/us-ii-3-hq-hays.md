## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **The ridge statement** [C]: July 2 ~08:00 "assigned a position on a
   ridge nearly parallel with the [Taneytown] road, facing westward. A
   stone wall just below the crest of the hill gave much strength to
   the position, and an open space of half a mile in our front afforded
   the artillery posted on the right and left flanks a fair field"
   (or-27-1-hays-div) — the Ziegler's-Grove-to-Bryan front the pass-2
   Smyth/Willard dossiers occupy.
2. Sheet reads: j3-02 HAYS' DIV label ~local (4517, 5093) ±120 m
   (08:00–11:00); j3-03 label ~(4338, 5028) amid the breaking assault
   bars (13:00–17:00 drawn state).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. Division static July 2–3 (attested by the front's continuous-wall
   language); Willard's brigade detached to the left July 2 evening
   (pass-2 dossier; "The loss of this brigade amounts to one-half the
   casualties in the division", or-27-1-hays-div).
2. **The Bliss-buildings order** [C via or-27-1-hays-div −55]:
   "About 11 a.m. an entire lull occurred ... Anticipating the movement
   of the enemy, I caused the house and barn in our front, which
   interrupted the fire of our artillery, to be burned" — the division
   commander's own record of the front-field clearance (executed on
   the skirmish front the 8th Ohio/14th Conn line held; corrected to
   ~10:00–10:30 canonical by his profile).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. July 2: "desultory fire from their artillery ... frequently
   shifting"; skirmishing continuous (or-27-1-hays-div).
2. **The cannonade** [C −55]: "the most terrific and uninterrupted fire
   from artillery. I cannot believe there were less than eighty pieces
   bearing on us within good range" (or-27-1-hays-div) — an
   eighty-gun receiving-side count for the gun-census ledger (reads
   against the pass-2/3 subtraction records).
3. **The repulse, verbatim**: the column's "march was as steady as if
   impelled by machinery"; "When within 100 yards of our line of
   infantry, the fire of our men could no longer be restrained. FOUR
   LINES ROSE FROM BEHIND OUR STONE WALL, and before the smoke of our
   first volley had cleared away, the enemy, in dismay and
   consternation, were seeking safety in flight" (or-27-1-hays-div) —
   the four-deep firing line is a rendering-relevant formation
   statement for the Smyth front (interleaved ranks, pass-2
   Smyth/Willard interleaving corroborated at division grain).
4. Capture ledger: fifteen battle-flags turned in (more "surreptitiously
   disposed of"); Lt. Potter's 2,500 stand of arms collected + ~1,000
   estimated left; prisoners "cannot be less than 1,500"
   (or-27-1-hays-div) — a receiving-side mass for the ED-49
   missing-column cross-check (pass-4 report §4).
