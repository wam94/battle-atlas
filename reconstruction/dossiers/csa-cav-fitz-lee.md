# Dossier: Fitzhugh Lee's Brigade, Stuart's Cavalry Division (reg-csa-cav-fitz)

Pass 12 (the cavalry theater opener) · Cast status: not-yet-cast ·
Achieved T-level: **T3 (East Cavalry Field grain)** · 2026-07-12

Chain role: Hampton's charge partner at CA-ECF-3; the brigade whose
own marker records the field's clearest strength CAVEAT (one
battalion detached, not present).

## EC1 — Identity & command

- Fitzhugh Lee's Brigade, Stuart's Cavalry Division: 1st, 2nd, 3rd,
  4th, 5th Virginia Cav.; the 1st Maryland Battalion Cav. WAS on the
  brigade's roster but NOT present (below, EC1 negative). **Brig. Gen.
  Fitzhugh Lee** throughout.
- **Negative evidence, first-class** [D]: "The Battalion [1st Md.]
  being on duty with Ewell's Corps the Brigade brought only five
  regiments to this field" (brigade marker, fetched this pass) — an
  attested detachment, the cavalry theater's cleanest EC1 subtraction
  fact.

## EC2 — Engaged strength

- **B&M-repro (hop, addressing-gettysburg-oob): 1,913** — a brigade
  total; whether this figure already excludes the detached 1st Md.
  Battalion was not determined this pass (flagged, not assumed).

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **Cress Ridge woods, "soon after midday"** [C ~12:00-13:00,
   marker-adjudicated]: "the Brigade... arrived soon after midday and
   took position on the left of Hampton's Brigade on the edge of the
   neighboring woods" (brigade marker) — one of the four independent
   CSA arrival-clock agreements feeding CA-ECF-1; the "left of
   Hampton's" placement is the theater's clearest CSA line-order
   statement (Fitz Lee left, Hampton right, of the woods position).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. [C ~12:00-13:00] March to and deployment on Cress Ridge, left of
   Hampton's brigade (above).
2. [D] The charge leg itself is not separately described for this
   brigade beyond the co-credit on McIntosh's Union-side tablet
   ("Brig. Gen. Hampton's and Brig. Gen. Fitzhugh Lee's Brigades...
   emerged from the woods in front and charged but were repulsed") —
   no CSA-side leg detail specific to Fitz Lee's regiments was found
   this pass.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **"It participated actively in the conflict which ensued"**
   (brigade marker, fetched this pass) — the marker's own summary
   phrase; no regiment-level or fire-window detail found this pass.
2. Co-credited on the Union side (McIntosh's tablet) as one of the two
   named brigades in the ~15:00 charge (CA-ECF-3).

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Brigade marker total: Killed 5, Wounded 16, Missing 29, Total
  50** (gettysburg.stonesentinels.com). The lowest of the four
  engaged CSA brigade totals — consistent with (but not proof of) a
  brigade fighting one battalion light per EC1's negative evidence.

## Conflicts & negative evidence

1. Same marker-provenance caveat as csa-cav-ham-hampton.md — GNMP-era
   position marker, not a War Department tablet; treated at
   monument-adjacent tier.
2. No primary report specific to Fitzhugh Lee himself was located
   this pass; the civilwarcycling.com index attributes OR 27/2
   pp. 737-741 (Report No. 574) to "Fitzhugh Lee's Brigade (commanded
   by Thomas T. Munford)" — an apparent index mismap (Munford
   commanded the 2nd Virginia and, at points, acted as a subordinate
   brigade officer, not brigade command at Gettysburg on the evidence
   in hand) that this pass did NOT resolve; a targeted fetch of
   pp. 737-741 is a pass-13 item, recorded as a standing gap rather
   than silently attributed either way.
