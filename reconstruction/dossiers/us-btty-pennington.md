## EC1 — Identity & command

- Battery M, 2nd US Artillery, 1st Brigade Horse Artillery, Cavalry
  Corps. **Lieut. Alexander C. M. Pennington Jr.** commanding
  throughout (gettysburg-stonesentinels-2usarty-m, fetch-verified this
  pass) — 24 years old, USMA 1860, in his first battle as battery
  commander (secondary-literature detail, attributed grade, not
  independently page-verified this pass).
- Armament (tablet, verbatim): "Six 3 inch Rifles" — six guns, the
  standard horse-artillery armament (no discrepancy, unlike Randol's
  dossier's flagged "12 Pounders" reading).
- Parent brigade: Capt. James M. Robertson (1st Brigade, Horse
  Artillery), whose own report (No. 368, OR 27/1 pp. 1020-1022,
  fetch-verified this pass at pp. 1020-1022) records the brigade's
  July pursuit itinerary in detail but — like Tidball's report for
  Randol's battery — does NOT narrate Battery M's July 3 action at
  East Cavalry Field beyond noting it was "detached… to report to the
  Third Division, Cavalry Corps" (i.e., to Custer's/Kilpatrick's
  command) — the narrative burden again falls to the receiving
  division/brigade's own report (Custer's, already on file).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **July 2, Hunterstown** [C, tablet]: "Engaged with the Confederates
   at Hunterstown" — the battery's first action, matching Hampton's
   brigade's own EC3.2 Hunterstown note (`csa-cav-ham-hampton.md`) —
   the two units may have faced each other in both actions (Custer's
   brigade both days), though this pass does not adopt that as a
   confirmed cross-reference (Cobb's Legion led the CSA attack per
   Hampton's marker; the tablet does not name which Union battery
   specifically dueled it).
2. **July 3, the general engagement** [C ~14:00, CA-ECF-2 basis]:
   tablet: "Engaged in Brig. General Custer's Brigade with Major
   General J. E. B. Stuart's Confederate Cavalry on the right of the
   Union Army" — matching Custer's own report (already on file,
   `us-cav-3-2-custer.md` EC5.3, or-27-1-custer p. 998): "Took up a
   position on the right of the line at Gettysburg, and was engaged
   with Stuart's cavalry; battery engaged all day." The REPORT and the
   TABLET independently agree on the "all day" / general-engagement
   framing — a report=tablet corroborating pair, the same class of
   agreement Merritt's dossier flagged as "the theater's cleanest such
   pair" (this is the second instance this pass finds, not the
   first — recorded as a genuine pattern, not a one-off).
3. **Secondary-literature performance note** [attributed, NOT a cited
   primary]: "Pennington replied with astonishing effect… the opposing
   artillerists were unable to silence a single Union gun" (Emerging
   Civil War web summary, accessed this pass) — carried at attributed
   grade only, consistent with the dossier discipline applied to
   Hampton's wounding mechanism and Farnsworth's death narrative in
   sibling pass-12 dossiers.
