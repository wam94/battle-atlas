# Dossier: Farnsworth's Brigade, 3rd Cavalry Division (reg-us-cav-3-1)

(`us-cav-farnsworth`) · Achieved T-level: **T3 (South Cavalry Field

Chain role: the CA-SCF-2 executor — Farnsworth's death pin, the

## EC1 — Identity & command

- 1st Brigade, 3rd Cavalry Division, Cavalry Corps: 5th New York Cav.,
  18th Pennsylvania Cav., 1st Vermont Cav., 1st West Virginia Cav.
  (or-27-oob). **Brig. Gen. Elon J. Farnsworth**, killed in the July 3
  charge (below); **Col. Nathaniel P. Richmond** succeeding.
- **Officer pin [D]**: Farnsworth killed, per the brigade's own tablet
  and multiple accounts (below, EC5/EC6) — the theater's most
  extensively documented single death.

## EC2 — Engaged strength

- **B&M-repro sum (hop, addressing-gettysburg-oob), per-regiment**:
  5th NY 420 + 18th PA 509 + 1st VT 600 + 1st WV (B-H, L-N) 395 =
  **1,924**.

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. [C ~13:00] March/arrival on the Gettysburg-area roads toward the
   Confederate right and rear (above).
2. [C ~17:30, triple-tablet agreement — see CA-SCF below]: the charge
   itself: "the First and Reserve Brigades advanced[;] Gen. Farnsworth
   with the 1st West Virginia and 18th Pennsylvania [charged] through
   woods and across stone walls occupied by superior forces of
   Confederate infantry and artillery" (3rd Division tablet); the
   brigade's own tablet narrows the charging regiments further: "the
   18th Pa. 1st Vt. and 1st West Virginia charged the Confederate left
   through the woods" — a THREE-of-four-regiment charge (the 5th NY
   not named in either charge sentence, consistent with a
   skirmish/support role rather than the mounted charge itself,
   though this pass does not adopt that as a ruling).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The charge and repulse** [C ~17:30]: "was repulsed with heavy
   loss and Gen. Farnsworth killed" (3rd Division tablet, verbatim).
2. **Farnsworth's death, the primary account** [D, retrospective]:
   Capt. Henry C. Parsons (1st Vermont), writing in *Battles and
   Leaders* (1887-8, ~24-year retrospective — the same evidentiary
   distance class as Alexander's memoir), records Farnsworth's protest
   before the charge — "General, do you mean it? Shall I throw my
   handful of men over rough ground, through timber, against a
   brigade of infantry? The First Vermont has already been fought half
   to pieces" — and that Farnsworth was shot five times and refused to
   surrender before dying on the field (web research this pass,
   Parsons's B&L account NOT independently page-verified against the
   original text — attributed grade, flagged for pass-13
   verification).
3. **The contested death circumstance**: a Confederate account
   (attributed to Col. William C. Oates) claims Farnsworth was
   surrounded and took his own life to avoid capture; this reading is
   "disputed by other witnesses and discounted by most historians"
   per the secondary survey consulted this pass. **Not adopted either
   way this pass** — recorded as a first-class litigated-memory
   conflict in the ED-39 §3 pattern (the 72nd PA monument-suit
   exemplar), pending a page-verified primary check of both Parsons
   and Oates.

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Brigade tablet: Killed 3 officers + 18 men, Wounded 6 officers +
  28 men, Captured or Missing 1 officer + 42 men, Total 98**
  (gettysburg.stonesentinels.com).
- **Division tablet: Total 355** for the "First and Reserve Brigades"
  combined July 3 action — NOT simply Farnsworth's 98 plus Merritt's
  total (see us-cav-1-3-merritt.md EC6 for the Fairfield-pooling
  conflict); the two totals are not directly additive without
  resolving that composition question, carried as an open EC6
  reconciliation item rather than silently summed.
