## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **"Immediately in front of the enemy, in support of Pegram's
   batteries"** [C, report-primary, verbatim]: Mahone's own report:
   "was posted in line of battle immediately in front of the enemy,
   and in support of Pegram's batteries" — a direct cross-reference to
   Pegram's battalion (already dossiered, `csa-3c-bn-pegram.md`),
   naming the artillery this brigade covered for two full days without
   itself advancing.
2. **July 2, arrival "in the forenoon"** [C ~forenoon, tablet]:
   "Arrived and took position here in the forenoon under orders to
   support the artillery" — the brigade's static position was assumed
   HOURS before Wright's/Posey's afternoon advance requests for
   support (below, Conflicts), a materially useful timing fact for
   any future rendering of the "why didn't he move" question (Mahone
   was already in a covering position, not marching up late).
3. **July 3-4, unchanged** [C, tablet]: "Remained here in support of
   the artillery… In line here all day. At dark began the march to
   Hagerstown" — the same ground held three full days.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The skirmish line, both days [C, report + tablet, corroborating]**:
   report: "The brigade took no special or active part in the actions
   of that battle beyond that which fell to the lot of its line of
   skirmishers" and "the skirmishers... were quite constantly engaged,
   and inflicted much loss upon the enemy," maintaining their line
   after the July 3 repulse; tablet: "A strong skirmish line was sent
   out which was constantly engaged and did effective service" (July
   2) / "Took no active part in the battle except by skirmishers"
   (July 3) — report and tablet independently agree almost verbatim on
   the SAME two-part characterization (skirmish-only, but the
   skirmishing itself real and costly).
2. **Under bombardment, both days [C, report-primary]**: "During the
   2nd and 3d, the brigade was exposed to a large share of the
   terrific shelling of those days, and from which its loss was mainly
   sustained" — Mahone's OWN account of where his casualties came from
   (artillery fire while stationary), not from any advance.
3. **THE NON-ADVANCE ITSELF [D, negative evidence, first-class]**:
   Mahone's report offers **NO explanation** for why the brigade did
   not advance when Wright's and Posey's brigades (both already
   dossiered) requested support during the ~19:00 Cemetery Ridge
   assault (CA-J2A-10) — a verified NEGATIVE in the report itself (the
   report simply does not address the question), not a gap in this
   pass's research. Secondary literature (web research this pass,
   attributed grade, NOT independently page-verified) attributes to
   Mahone the claim that he believed he was under a standing order not
   to advance without a direct order from Anderson, and that Anderson
   himself "took little active part in directing his division in
   battle" that evening — carried as attributed context for why the
   controversy exists, not as a cited primary fact.

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Report AND tablet, digit-exact agreement**: report "Killed, 8 men;
  wounded, 2 officers and 53 men; missing, 39 men. Total, 102" =
  tablet "Killed 8 Wounded 55 Missing 39 Total 102" (55 = 2 officers +
  53 men, same figure differently split) — a clean, fully-reconciled
  EC6, unusual for its precision given the brigade's otherwise sparse
  activity record.
- Cause mix [C]: "mainly sustained" from artillery fire while
  stationary (EC5.2), not from close combat — consistent with a
  bombardment-dominant casualty shape (the shelling-not-charging
  class), a useful contrast case to the assault brigades' close-combat
  shape already on file (Armistead's, Garnett's, etc.).

## Conflicts & negative evidence

1. **THE CENTRAL CONTROVERSY**: Mahone's brigade did not advance to
   support Wright's and Posey's brigades on July 2 despite (per
   secondary literature, attributed grade, not independently
   page-verified this pass) direct requests for support from both and
   an order from Anderson to advance; Mahone's own report is SILENT on
   the refusal, addressing only the skirmish-line/bombardment
   narrative. This dossier carries the negative (what the report does
   NOT say) as a first-class fact per the truth-spec's "explicitly
   open" discipline — the controversy itself is attested by its
   ABSENCE from the primary, not resolved by this pass's research.
2. **No cross-verification of Wright's/Posey's own support requests
   against Mahone's brigade** was performed this pass from Mahone's
   side — `csa-3c-and-3-wright.md` (already dossiered) should be
   checked for its own account of requesting Mahone's support; not
   re-opened this pass.
