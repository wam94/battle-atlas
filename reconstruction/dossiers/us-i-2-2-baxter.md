## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **Arrival** [C via or-27-1-baxter ~0]: "arriving near the front
   where the battle was raging at 11 a.m." — agreeing with Coulter's
   11 a.m. massing W of the railroad embankment (or-27-1-coulter).
2. The first front [D]: two regiments (11th Pa, 97th NY) forward on
   Wadsworth's right; then the brigade "changed front by filing to
   the right", covering the right flank — **"a division of the
   Eleventh Corps being on our right at least 400 yards"** (the gap
   primary; Robinson's addenda says "at no time less than half a
   mile" — within-command variance, both carried).
3. **The crest line** [D]: the left-flank change "moved forward to
   the crest of the hill" — the Oak Ridge wall position (the pass-10
   j1-09 drawn "BAXTER'S BRIGADE" bar at the wall (4007, 8424),
   already registered, is this line — drawn=report agreement).
4. Post-relief [D]: to Stewart's battery support; the retreat through
   town "to Cemetery Hill".
5. July 1 evening → July 2 [C ~0]: "About 5 o'clock… from Cemetery
   Hill to the left and forward, near and parallel with the
   Emmitsburg road" with temporary breastworks; relieved by **Webb's
   brigade about 10 o'clock July 2 morning** (both-sided with
   us-ii-2-2-webb's arc); then the day of battery supports (~16:00
   XI Corps battery; ~18:00 to the III Corps front, no enemy found;
   dark, back right).
6. July 3 [C ~0]: ~09:00 right/rear of Cemetery Hill (XII Corps
   support); **~13:00 right and front of Cemetery Hill "sustaining a
   heavy fire from the enemy's batteries for nearly two hours"** —
   the CA-J3A-1→5 bombardment window from a I Corps support unit;
   then left to Hays's right "under one of the most galling fires of
   artillery ever witnessed", breastworks, the skirmish push (12th
   Mass + 90th Pa detachment).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The Iverson repulse, receiving side** [D, bracketed by CA-J1P-1/2]:
   "the brigade opened on the advancing foes a most deadly fire, soon
   causing them to recoil and give way"; repeated lines repulsed; the
   charge (97th NY + 83rd NY + 88th Pa) "capturing many prisoners,
   the Eighty-eighth Pennsylvania taking two battle-flags and the
   Ninety-seventh New York one"; **"The Twelfth Massachusetts had a
   galling fire on the flank of this brigade at this time, which I
   think had a great influence upon its surrender"** — the surrender
   mass attested from the direct receiving echelon (pairs Cutler's
   pass-10 record, Iverson's own, and Ewell's "the greater part of
   three regiments had fallen where they stood in line of battle").
2. Coulter's grain [C ~0]: "about 12.30 p.m. the firing became
   general along the entire line"; the sally capturing ~500; the
   division ledger "three flags and about 1,000 prisoners"
   (or-27-1-robinson).
3. Duration primaries: Baxter "engaged over two hours… expended our
   ammunition"; Robinson "from about noon until nearly 5 p.m." at
   division grain (the rear-guard reading — see conflicts).
4. July 3: the two-hour bombardment endurance + the evening skirmish
   clear (12th Mass/90th Pa detachment, "moving steadily forward…
   though not without considerable loss").
5. The Northrup conduct record (97th NY, July 4 ~01:00 picket
   withdrawal without pressure) — unfavorable-report class, carried
   [D].

## Conflicts & negative evidence

1. The 400-yards (Baxter) vs half-a-mile (Robinson) XI Corps gap —
   within-command, both carried.
2. Robinson's "until nearly 5 p.m." withdrawal vs the I Corps tablet
   "At 4 P.M. the Corps retired" (CA-J1P-5): semantically consistent
   (the division was the ordered rear guard, "last to leave the
   field", orders received "not… until all other troops… had
   commenced moving"); recorded as the anchor's late tail, not a
   competing time — feeds the pass-11 ED-70 candidate.
3. Baxter's own report carries NO clock between 11:00 and 17:00 —
   the interior fight is timed by Coulter's 12:30, the >2-hour
   duration, and the ~15:00 relief [D lattice].
4. The Robinson addenda is a grievance document (protesting Meade's
   report) — engagement-duration "nearly four hours" there vs "noon
   until nearly 5" in the main report; both his, both carried.
