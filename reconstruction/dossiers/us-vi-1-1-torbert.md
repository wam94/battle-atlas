# Dossier: Torbert's Brigade (New Jersey Brigade), 1st Division, VI Corps (reg-us-vi-1-1)

Pass 14 (the VI Corps brigade batch, census item 3) · Cast status:
not-yet-cast · Achieved T-level: **T3 (own-report + tablet, physics-
timed march leg)** · 2026-07-14

Chain role: the first of the VI Corps's seven remaining brigades
(Nevin's, pass 7, is the corps's only prior dossier); substantiates
the corps HQ dossier's (`us-vi-hq-sedgwick.md`) "attested-static
majority" framing with a first brigade-grain primary.

## EC1 — Identity & command

- 1st Brigade, 1st Division, VI Corps: 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 15th New Jersey
  Volunteers (or-27-oob; brigade tablet, gettysburg.stonesentinels.com,
  "1st Brigade, 1st Division, 6th Corps," fetch-verified this pass).
  **Brig. Gen. Alfred T. A. Torbert** throughout; own report dated
  Camp near Warrenton, Va., August 3, 1863 (No. 225, OR 27/1 pp.
  668-670, civilwar.com transcription, fetch-verified this pass —
  PRIMARY, own-report; a second, later report also survives, per the
  civilwar.com page's own note of a "second report" dated July 17,
  1863, not separately quoted here).
- **Strength [C, report-primary]**: "1st New Jersey 28 officers, 328
  enlisted men (356 total); 2nd New Jersey 30/418 (448); 3rd New
  Jersey 27/340 (367); 15th New Jersey 21/471 (492) — Total 106
  officers, 1,557 enlisted men, 1,663 all ranks" (Torbert's report,
  paraphrase-verified this pass) — the campaign-period figure (not
  scoped explicitly to July 3 alone).

## EC2 — Engaged strength

- **1,663** (Torbert's own report, EC1) — a rare case in this corpus
  where the brigade-grain EC2 figure comes directly from the
  commander's own itemized regimental table rather than a B&M-repro
  hop.

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2, arrival ~4 P.M.** [C ~16:00, report=tablet near-exact
   agreement]: "Arrived at 4 P. M. from Manchester Md. A distance by
   the route taken of about 35 miles having halted an hour only"
   (brigade tablet, verbatim); Torbert's own report independently
   states the brigade "marched approximately 35 miles from near
   Manchester, Maryland, arriving by 4 p.m." and rested two hours
   before taking position on the left of the line near dark "with
   only 25 men absent" — a report=tablet agreement on both the
   distance and the arrival clock, plus a straggling figure (25 of
   1,663) the tablet does not carry.
2. **July 2 night position** [C, tablet]: "After sunset moved to the
   east slope of the north side of Little Round Top and arriving
   there at dark was held in reserve" (tablet, verbatim).
3. **July 3** [C, report=tablet agreement]: "Moved to a position
   southeast of the Weikert House and remained until the close of the
   battle. Not engaged except on the skirmish line" (tablet, verbatim)
   — Torbert's report independently places the brigade "at the center
   of the line under Major-General Newton's command" on the morning
   of July 3, consistent with the tablet's Weikert-farm-area siting
   (the Weikert farm sits behind the II/III Corps center-left).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **The forced march** [B: 35 mi in "about" the interval between the
   prior night's start and the stated 4 P.M. arrival, halting only
   once for an hour — the same physics-timeable class as Nevin's
   34-mile/17-hour leg, `us-vi-3-3-nevin.md` EC4.1, though no start
   time is stated here to close the interval independently].
2. [D] Sunset move to the Little Round Top reserve position (EC3.2) —
   no distance/timing beyond "after sunset."
3. [D] July 3 move to the Weikert-House-area center position (EC3.3).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **Attested-static reserve, July 2 night through July 3** [D,
   report=tablet agreement]: "Not engaged except on the skirmish
   line" (tablet); Torbert's report independently confirms the
   brigade "remained in reserve, actively engaged only on the picket
   line," crediting "Lieutenant-Colonel Wiebecke, Second Regiment New
   Jersey Volunteers" and "Lieutenant [Howard H.] Goldsmith,
   additional aide-de-camp" for picket-line management — a named
   command credit the tablet does not carry.
2. **The picket-line casualty event, July 3** [C, report-primary]:
   "11 enlisted men wounded" on the picket line July 3 (Torbert's
   report, verbatim figure) — this IS the brigade's only combat
   contact this pass's sources document.

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Brigade tablet: Wounded 11 Men** (gettysburg.stonesentinels.com,
  verbatim) — matching Torbert's own report's July-3 picket-line
  figure (EC5.2) EXACTLY, a clean report=tablet agreement scoped to
  Gettysburg alone.
- **CONFLICT, scope class (report vs. tablet, campaign-period vs.
  battle-day)**: Torbert's report's SECOND, broader casualty line —
  "1 killed, 17 wounded across multiple engagements (Gettysburg,
  Funkstown, Hagerstown, and Fairfield, Pennsylvania)" — is a
  CAMPAIGN-TOTAL figure, not a Gettysburg-day total; it is NOT a
  disagreement with the tablet's 11 (Gettysburg-scoped) but a
  different-scope superset, per the standing ED-49/ED-52 scope
  discipline (never conflated, never treated as competing totals for
  the same event). Recorded here because the civilwar.com
  transcription surfaces both figures on the same page without
  separating them cleanly — an honest disambiguation, not a new
  conflict.

## Conflicts & negative evidence

1. **The EC6 scope disambiguation** (above) — the 17-wounded figure is
   NOT a Gettysburg-day competing count; carried as a campaign-period
   superset per standing scope discipline.
2. No engagement beyond the picket line is documented by either
   source fetched this pass — an attested-static brigade, consistent
   with the corps HQ dossier's framing (`us-vi-hq-sedgwick.md` EC5.1).
