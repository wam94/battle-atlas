## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **The 19:00 move-away** [C via or-27-1-geary ~0/-30]: "at 7 p. m.
   I received orders to move the division by the right flank, and
   follow the First Division, leaving one brigade" — CA-J2E-1 at
   division grain (vs Greene's stated 6.30 for the same order: the
   30-minute spread is order-propagation-vs-skew, carried on both
   profiles).
2. **The wrong road** [D]: Williams's "By some mistake" — the two
   brigades never reached the left wing and never fought; ended in
   the open fields off the pike (the division's evening is a
   NEGATIVE: marched, missed, returned).
3. **Kane's return** [C via or-27-1-geary, 21:00 order]: "At 9 p. m.
   I ordered Kane's brigade to return... within 200 yards of the
   breastworks, Kane's brigade was met by a sharp fire, which in the
   midst of the surrounding darkness, was at first supposed to be
   from General Greene's troops. Without replying, the brigade was
   withdrawn to the turnpike, taken in past the rear of Greene's
   brigade and past Greene's right, when it was again met with a
   volley, thus proving that the enemy still occupied the ground" —
   the two-volley discovery leg, ending on Greene's right ~22:00
   (Greene's "About 10 o'clock... General Kane... returning").

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The Latimer counter-duel** [C via or-27-1-geary, 16:00; ~30
   min]: "a battery of the enemy posted on a hill across Rock Creek
   was enfilading the lines of the First and Eleventh Corps, I
   ordered a section of Knap's (Pennsylvania) battery and one of
   Battery K, Fifth U. S. Artillery, to take position on the eminence
   at the left of Greene's brigade and to silence the enemy's
   battery. This they did after a hot artillery duel of about thirty
   minutes" — BOTH-SIDED with Johnson's "4 p. m... I ordered Major
   Latimer to open" (or-27-2-johnson) and the Ewell-wing dossier's
   Latimer record; section markers mon-kinzie-culps (5788-class) +
   mon-knap-culps. The 60th NY's gun-in-the-line detail (Godard,
   p. 860: gunners replaced by infantrymen) rides this episode.
2. Greene's fight at division grain [D]: "four separate and furious
   charges... two and a half hours"; the 355/400 reinforcement
   ledger repeated; "discontinued their attack at about 10 p. m."
3. July 3 [C]: the seven-hour morning defense; the enemy attacking
   first at the 15-minute artillery preparation's end (Williams).
   **PASS-9 UPGRADE — the wrong-road column's return clock pair at
   brigade/regiment grain**: Candy "Remained in position until about
   12 p. m." (midnight) and the 28th PA's "12.30 a. m. July 3…
   moved forward to retake the position left the morning before"
   (or-27-1-candy; p. 845) bracket the division's night
   reoccupation; Kane's discovery leg (or-27-1-kane) and

   the division's three-column night state — the astray-and-return
