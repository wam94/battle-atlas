# Dossier: Carroll's Brigade (Gibraltar Brigade), 3rd Division, II Corps — with the 8th Ohio flank action as a first-class element (reg-us-ii-3-1)

Pass 4 (Union center command batch) · Cast status: in-build as TWO units
— `us-carroll` (the three regiments, "minus the 8th Ohio") and `us-8oh`
(the detached regiment) · Achieved T-level: **T4 full-arc** (both build
units) · 2026-07-11

Command-batch role: the audit's strangest brigade arc — three regiments
spent July 3 a mile from their division ON EAST CEMETERY HILL (sent
there in the July 2 night crisis and never returned), while the fourth,
the 8th Ohio, alone on an advanced picket line, executed the flank
attack that broke the assault column's left. The 8th Ohio element is the
documented counterpart of Brockenbrough's collapse
(csa-3c-het-2-brockenbrough.md) and is treated first-class throughout.

## EC1 — Identity & command

- Col. Samuel S. Carroll (8th Ohio's own colonel by origin), commanding;

- No brigade-command casualty; regimental pins below. Carroll's report
  is dated July 5 AT Two Taverns — the earliest-written Union brigade

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **The night rush to East Cemetery Hill** [D vs CA-J2E-3]: ordered
   "immediately to the assistance of part of the Eleventh Corps
   supporting batteries on Cemetery Hill, as they were being driven
   back"; moved with three regiments, 14th Indiana leading, "guided by
   an aide of General Howard's"; the 7th WV changed front in the fight
   (or-27-1-carroll; the 14th Ind had been supporting the 8th Ohio's
   line at 18:00, or-27-1-coons). Trigger side: Harry Hays's CSA
   brigade attacked "A little before 8 p.m." (or-27-2-hays, the
   CA-J2E-3 anchor source) — order + counter-order dressed to the same
   event from both sides.
2. **The 8th Ohio's change of front (first-class)** [D]: "I changed
   front forward on the left company, thus presenting our front to the
   left flank of the advancing rebel column" (or-27-1-sawyer) — a
   parade-ground evolution executed by a single, already-worn
   regiment against a division front; the geometric counterpart of Brockenbrough's break and the
   enfilade every CSA left-wing report describes ("a column of the
   enemy's infantry was thrown forward in that direction, which
   enfiladed my whole line", or-27-2-lane-jh).
3. No return leg July 3 for either element (both held their ground;
   the three regiments came off the hill July 5).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. July 2 skirmishing: four companies of the 4th Ohio out from ~08:00,
   "a brisk" engagement (or-27-1-carroll); 14th Ind under artillery
   fire "At 4.30 p.m. ... heavy and continuous", ordered to support the
   8th Ohio at 6 o'clock (or-27-1-coons).
2. The East Cemetery Hill night fight [D vs CA-J2E-3]: retook the
   battery line among Wiedrich's/Ricketts's guns (Carroll's narrative;
   receiving side of or-27-2-hays's "two lines of infantry" storming
   record — cross-referenced, us-i-arty-wainwright.md carries the
   ground from pass 2).
3. **July 3 on the hill** [A window]: "exposed to a great deal of
   cross-firing during the heavy cannonading of the 3d"
   (or-27-1-carroll) — under-fire-without-reply, an ED-44-class state
   for the three regiments.
4. **The 8th Ohio under the cannonade** [C via or-27-1-sawyer −55]:
   "Soon after 2 p.m. the enemy opened a terrific fire from sixty-four
   pieces of artillery, in a semicircle which inclosed my position" —
   a sixty-four-gun count from INSIDE the pocket, the picket line's
   own gun census.
5. **The flank fire** [D]: "Our fire was poured into their flank with
   terrible effect for a few minutes BEFORE the Second Brigade at the
   battery opened, but almost instantly on the fire from the front,
   together with the concentrated fire from our batteries, the whole
   mass gave way" (or-27-1-sawyer) — sequence bracket: the 8th's flank
   fire PRECEDED Smyth's front volley (a tier-D ordering across the
   pass-2 Smyth dossier's climax).
6. Capture ledgers: 8th Ohio ~200 prisoners + three battle-flags (34th
   NC [Lowrance's] and 38th Va [Armistead's] identified — collected in
   the general collapse, so the colors attest the POCKET's mixing, not
   a Brockenbrough-only kill) (or-27-1-sawyer); brigade: 252 prisoners,
   113 enemy wounded cared for, 37 buried, 349 stand of arms
   (or-27-1-carroll).

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- Official return (three-day): brigade 211 — 14th Ind 31, 4th Oh 31,
  **8th Ohio 102**, 7th WV 47 (or-27-1-union-return p. 176).
- **The 8th Ohio's split is primary and unusual** [C/D]: "4 of my men
  having been killed, and 1 captain, 1 lieutenant, the sergeant-major,
  and 38 men wounded" BY NOON JULY 3 on the picket line — i.e. ~45 of
  the 102 fell BEFORE the charge; the report total (101 k+w + 1 m =
  102) matches the return exactly (or-27-1-sawyer). The flank action
  itself cost less than the 40-hour picket tour — a casualty-shape
  inversion the build's decay should reflect (flat attrition then
  moderate spike, NOT a single climax spike).
- Three-regiment element: losses dominated by the July 2 night fight
  and July 3 cross-fire (no per-day primary split; open).
