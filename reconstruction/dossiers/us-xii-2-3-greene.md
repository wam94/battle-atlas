Chain role: **the ED-29 chain's Union spine** — the one brigade left on
Culp's Hill when the corps went to the left (CA-J2E-1), whose report

  (or-27-oob; or-27-1-greene). Regimental commanders named in the
  report: Godard (60th), Hammerstein (78th), Lane (102nd, **severely w

  (us-xii-2-hq-geary.md); Williams the corps, Slocum the right wing
  (us-xii-hq-slocum-williams.md).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The four-charge defense** [A vs CA-J2E-2, "a few minutes before
   7 p. m."; duration D]: "we were attacked on the whole of our front
   by a large force... which were effectually resisted" — Geary's
   division grain adds "four separate and furious charges" over
   "two and a half hours" (or-27-1-geary p. 827), i.e. ~19:00-21:30.
   Opposite-side agreement: Nicholls "7 p. m." forward order and
   four-hours fire (or-27-2-williams-nicholls); Jones's darkness
   climb (or-27-2-jones-jm).
2. **The 20:00 flank crisis** [C via or-27-1-greene ~0]: "About 8
   p. m. the enemy appeared on our right flank" in Williams-division
   works = Steuart's 23rd VA taking the perpendicular breastwork
   (csa-2c-joh-1-steuart.md, both-sided at regiment grain).
3. **The relief rotation** [D]: reinforcing regiments "sent into the
   trenches to relieve our regiments as their ammunition was
   exhausted"; July 3 version codified ("relieved from thirty to
   ninety minutes by others with fresh ammunition... the fire was
   kept up constantly") — the fire-continuity mechanism a cast should
   render instead of a static line blazing for seven hours.
4. **The 71st PA episode** [D, conflict-flagged]: "the California
   Regiment, Colonel Smith, reported to assist me... They soon fell
   back and were withdrawn — the commanding officer saying that he
   had received orders from his commanding general to retire — leaving
   our right in a very critical position" (carried as Greene's view;
   the 71st's side is a pass-9+ item on the II Corps record).
5. Kane fired on while returning ~22:00 [C ~0]; Candy in support
   01:30 [C]; July 3 04:00 artillery opening → 10:30 die-off [C]
   (CA-J3M frame, full-arc capture).
6. **The enemy-loss survey** [untimed]: 391 dead counted in front +
   ~150 across the creek; 2,000 muskets picked up; 130 prisoners;
   Maj. B. W. Leigh (Johnson's chief of staff) found dead in front —
   the same body Johnson's report mourns (both-sided at officer
   grain, or-27-2-johnson p. 505).
