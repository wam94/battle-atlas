## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 1 — Seminary Ridge** [C]: the Seminary line ("Gettysburg
  Seminary is situated on a ridge about a quarter of a mile from the
  town, the ridge running nearly north and south and parallel with
  the Emmitsburg pike", or-27-1-wainwright p. 355) and the fighting
  withdrawal — the right section covering from "some 75 yards to the
  rear", the last piece's horses all shot, Wadsworth's "peremptory
  order" to the heights.
2. **July 1 dusk → July 4 — EAST Cemetery Hill, layout VERBATIM** [C]:
  "Four guns of Battery B, Fourth U. S. Artillery, across the road so
  as to command the approaches from the town ... along the north
  front of the hill; four guns of Battery I, First New York Artillery
  [Wiedrich's, an XI Corps battery under his layout] ... on the left;
  next Cooper's battery and then Reynolds'" — thirteen 3-inch guns on
  the north front; "The Fifth Maine battery was posted to the right
  and some 50 yards in front of this line, on a small knoll"
  (pp. 357) — the McKnight's-Knoll relation. This is the pass's
  third primary left-to-right gun-line roster (after McGilvery's and
  the Bachelder low-ground list) — the East-Hill frame pass 3 places
  Ricketts/Wiedrich/Stewart against.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **July 1** [C]: the Seminary fight (canister at the charge; the
  83-casualty day).
2. **July 2 evening — the wheat-field battery duel** [C]: a CSA
  battery "(four 20-pounders and six 10-pounder Parrotts) in a
  wheat-field on our immediate front, at about 1,300 yards ... the
  most accurate fire"; his thirteen 3-inch guns silenced it in ~90
  min, "twenty-eight dead horses" left on its ground — the Benner's
  Hill duel from the receiving side (Latimer's battalion,
  csa-2c-ewell-wing.md EC5).
3. **July 2 dusk — the hill assault repulsed by the guns** [C]:
  "fifteen guns in their front and six on their left flank";
  Wiedrich's and Ricketts's cannoneers fighting "with fence-rails and
  stones"; his judgment: "this attack was almost entirely repelled by
  the artillery" (p. 358).
4. **July 3** [C]: morning "occasional shots at bodies of the enemy's
  troops"; afternoon participation in the general engagement — the
  report gives NO brigade rounds total (negative); component returns
