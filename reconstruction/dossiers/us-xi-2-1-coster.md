## EC3 — Position anchors

1. Cemetery Hill first [C via or-27-1-vonsteinwehr ~14:00]: "I placed
   the First Brigade, Col. Charles R. Coster, on the northeast end of
   the hill, in support of Wiedrich's battery" — with one regiment
   skirmished forward and **one "into a large stone church and the
   surrounding houses in town"** (the sharpshooter screen).
2. **The commitment** [D]: Schurz's second reinforcement call →
   "I ordered Colonel Coster to advance with his brigade through the
   town, to report to General Schurz… Colonel Coster met General
   Schurz in town, who ordered him to take a position north and east
   of Gettysburg, and to check the advance of the enemy"
   (or-27-1-vonsteinwehr; Schurz's own account concurs, pass 10) —
   the brickyard line NE of town (Kuhn's brickyard; the 73rd Pa
   stayed on the hill per the division's two-regiment backfill note).
3. The fall-back [D]: "not strong enough to restore the battle… again
   took up his position on Cemetery Hill, leaving one regiment to
   occupy the nearest brick houses of the town, which successfully
   prevented the farther advance of the enemy" — the brick-house
   stopper is the brigade's second delaying artifact.
4. July 2-3 [E]: the hill's NE front (division fronting north);
   under the July 2 ~16:00-18:00 concentric bombardment
   (or-27-1-vonsteinwehr's nine-battery passage) and adjacent to the
   ECH July 2 evening fight (the pass-8 record).
