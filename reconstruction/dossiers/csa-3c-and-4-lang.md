## EC4 — Movement legs

1. ~07:00: up with Wilcox's left to the foot of the gun hill [C/D].
2. **The supporting advance** [D, after the repulse]: "Soon after
   General Pickett's troops retired behind our position, General Wilcox
   began to advance, and, in accordance with previous orders to conform
   to his movements, I moved forward also, under a heavy fire from
   artillery, but without encountering any infantry until coming to the
   skirt of woods at the foot of the heights" (or-27-2-lang) — trigger
   class: standing conformity order (no new order arrived, by design).
3. **The flank collapse and retreat**: "a heavy body of infantry
   advanced upon my left flank ... The noise ... so deafening that it
   was impossible to make the voice heard ... To remain ... was certain
   annihilation"; the retreat order failed to reach men firing from the
   rocks, "and remained there until captured"; "not in time to save a
   large number of the Second Florida Infantry, together with their
   colors, from being cut off and captured by the flanking force on the
   left" (or-27-2-lang) — the 16th Vermont's action from the receiving
   side (us-i-3-3-stannard.md EC5: "2nd FL colors taken", veazey-or).
