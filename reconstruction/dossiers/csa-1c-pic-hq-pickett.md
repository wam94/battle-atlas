## EC1 — Identity & command

- Maj. Gen. George E. Pickett, commanding Pickett's Division, First Corps
  (or-27-oob). Three brigades present (Garnett, Kemper, Armistead);
  **Corse's and Jenkins's brigades absent** (detached in Virginia,
  or-27-oob) — the division fought at three-fifths establishment.
- Staff pins: Maj. Charles Pickett, AAG (Peyton's report addressee,
  peyton-or-1863). Division artillery: Dearing's battalion
  (csa-1c-bn-dearing.md, pass 2).
- The freshness fact (structural, drives EC6 scope): "two-thirds of the
  troops to be engaged in the assault had been in a severe battle two days
  previous, Pickett's division alone being fresh" (or-27-2-longstreet).
- **Command-record negative evidence, primary**: Pickett's division report
  was written, and Lee ordered it destroyed — "You and your men have
  crowned yourselves with glory; but we have the enemy to fight, and must
  carefully, at this critical moment, guard against dissensions which the
  reflections in your report would create. I will, therefore, suggest that
  you destroy both copy and original, substituting one confined to
  casualties merely." (or-27-3-lee-pickett, p. 1075, fetch-verified
  verbatim). No substitute report appears in the OR; the division's only
  surviving reports are Peyton's (No. 441) and Dearing's (No. 442).

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2, late afternoon–dusk — arrival near the field** [C/D]: the
   division closed on the Marsh Creek/Bream's vicinity west of the field
   after its Chambersburg march; Longstreet's report treats it as not
   available for July 2 action (or-27-2-longstreet); the Pendleton
   arrival chain (pass 1) has the division artillery "arrived since dusk."
2. **July 3, ~09:00 — on the field** [C via peyton clockProfile]:
   "reached the field about 9 a.m." (peyton-or-1863).
3. **July 3, ~12:00 — the waiting position** [C/E]: "At about 12 m. we
   were ordered to take position behind the crest of the hill on which
   the artillery, under Colonel Alexander, was planted" (peyton-or-1863);
   Longstreet: Pickett was "charged with the duty of arranging the lines
   behind our batteries" and "About 2 p.m. [−53 clock] ... reported that
   the troops were in order and on the most sheltered ground"
   (or-27-2-longstreet). Geometry at brigade grain on the brigade
   dossiers; the Virginia Memorial (mon-va-mem, local (3175.5, 5020.3))
   is a **dedication point, not a unit position** (register semantics
   field, ED-39 §2) and is cited only as the start-line vicinity marker.
4. **Step-off through repulse**: the Angle-cast T5 window governs
   (angle.claims.json; ED-1/ED-2/ED-8 frames). Not re-litigated here.

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. July 2: Chambersburg pike march (~35 km over July 2; road-column
   class) — endpoint only attested; leg interior open.
2. July 3 morning: camp → behind Seminary Ridge crest, in position by
   ~12:00 (Peyton) [C].
3. **The order chain (CA-J3A-6 executor record)**: Alexander's 13:25 and
   13:40 notes → Pickett refers to Longstreet, who "bowed his head" /
   gave no verbal reply → "I am going to move forward, sir" → Pickett
   orders the advance **in person**, "repeated by General Garnett with
   promptness" (alexander-1907 ch. 19; peyton-or-1863; the T5 corpus).
   Trigger class: written note from the artillery clock-bearer, assented
   by corps command in person.
4. Repulse/rally: survivors reformed toward the Emmitsburg road rise and
   Seminary Ridge; Longstreet ordered Wright "to rally and collect the
   scattered troops behind Anderson's division" (or-27-2-longstreet) —
   the division's rally is attested only through corps-level actors
   (division-grain record open).

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Division total, official return**: 232 k / 1,157 w / 1,490 m =
  **2,888** (or-27-2-anv-return p. 339), with the return's own headnote:
  "many of the 'missing' were killed or wounded. Especially is this the
  case with Pickett's division" (p. 338 verbatim) — the missing-scope
  caution is FIRST-CLASS and inherited by all three brigade dossiers
  (ED-49 candidate).
- Episode split at division grain: bombardment share attested at ~20 k&w
  (Garnett, Peyton) + "suffered severely" (Kemper, per Wilcox) + no
  Armistead figure — the division's bombardment ordeal is real but
  quantified only per-brigade; advance/works/retreat splits live on the
  brigade dossiers.
- All three brigadiers were casualties: Garnett k, Kemper w&c (recovered
  by CSA), Armistead mw&c (d. July 5) — pins on the brigade dossiers.
