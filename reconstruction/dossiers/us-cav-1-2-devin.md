## EC3 — Position anchors

1. June 30 [E]: "marched by Emmitsburg to Gettysburg, Pa., and
   encamped" (or-27-1-devin).
2. **The four-road screen** [D, geometry primary]: formed "on the
   crest of the hill on the right of the First Brigade... my right
   resting on the road to Mummasburg", vedettes to the pickets "on
   the three roads on the right leading toward Carlisle, thus
   establishing a continuous line from the York road, on the extreme
   right, to the left of the First Brigade, on the Cashtown road"
   (or-27-1-devin) — Buford's "four roads" made explicit
   (Mummasburg, Carlisle, Harrisburg/Heidlersburg, York). OSM
   Devin-HQ marker registered (mon-devin-hq, 3574.4, 8446.8 — the
   Carlisle-road front).
3. The Heidlersburg front hold [D]: the 9th NY + dismounted line
   against "the advance of the enemy's line of battle, coming from
   the direction of Heidlersburg" (Rodes's approach axis).
4. The York-road post → the town → the Emmitsburg road [D]: massed
   "on the right of the York road"; shelled out by a Cemetery Hill
   Union battery; reformed "in rear of the batteries of the division,
   with its right flank resting on the town".
5. Night [E]: "ordered to the extreme left, where it bivouacked."

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **The withdrawal-by-regiment drill** [D]: "This I effected in
   successive formations in line to the rear by regiment, in the face
   of the enemy" — a formation-class primary (successive lines,
   textbook cavalry delay).
2. The York-road picket push [D]: pickets "advanced three-quarters of
   a mile" before the shelling.
3. The through-town displacement under friendly shells [D]: "the
   column being shelled the whole distance."

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The two-hour hold** [D, duration-class]: "succeeded in holding
   the rebel line in check for two hours, until relieved by the
   arrival of the Eleventh Corps" — pairs with Schurz's ~13:30-14:00
   deployment window from the relieving side (his 2 p.m.
   deployment-complete clock bounds the relief).
2. **THE FRIENDLY-FIRE RECORD** [D, first-class]: "a heavy fire of
   shells was opened on us from one of our own batteries on Cemetery
   Hill, immediately in my rear... many of the shells bursting among
   us"; after his withdrawal "the battery turned its attention to my
   pickets on the road, and shelled them out" — the day's one
   Union-on-Union artillery record (the Wiedrich-hill guns firing on
   the York road; carried on both this row and the XI-arty frame,
   unresolved which battery).
3. **The town counter-charge** [D]: the 9th NY squadron dismounted
   "who, with their carbines, drove them some distance into the town,
   punishing them severely" — the last Union offensive act north of
   town on July 1 (against Early's entering skirmishers).
4. July 2 morning [D]: reconnaissance "in rear of the enemy's right";
   two squadrons dismounted supporting Berdan's sharpshooters —
   BOTH-SIDED with the Pitzer Woods record (us-iii Berdan frame /
   Wilcox's July 2 arc, pass 3/7); relieved by III Corps arrival,
   ordered to Taneytown (the division's July 2 withdrawal — the
   Gamble/Devin absence from July 2-3 the register notes).
