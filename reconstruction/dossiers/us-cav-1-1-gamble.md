## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **June 30 night–July 1 dawn** [E]: "in camp at the seminary
   building" (or-27-1-gamble); division pickets out the western
   roads (or-27-1-buford).
2. **The morning delay line** [C via or-27-1-gamble ~0 + drawn]:
   "placed in line of battle about 1 mile in front of the seminary,
   the right resting on the railroad track and the left near the
   Middletown or Fairfax [Fairfield] road, the Cashtown road being a
   little to the right of the center, at right angles with the line"
   — a fully-specified line geometry primary. Drawn: j1-03 "8'Ill
   Cav" bar read (3362, 7266) with the relieving I Corps columns
   arriving; **the OSM Gamble-HQ marker (mon-gamble-hq, 3264.6,
   7327.0) reads 115 m from the drawn bar — HQ-on-the-line**.
   Buford division HQ marker beside it (mon-buford-hq); Calef pike
   pair (mon-calef-a2us).
3. **The one fall-back** [D]: "compelled to fall back about 200 yards
   to the next ridge, and there make a stand" — the only ground
   yielded before relief (agreeing with Buford's "literally dragged
   back a few hundred yards").
4. **The afternoon stand** [D + drawn]: "deployed in line on the
   ridge of woods, with the seminary on our right", half the command
   dismounted "behind a portion of a stone wall and under cover of
   trees" (or-27-1-gamble) — **drawn on j1-13 (4 p.m. sheet): the
   "GAMBLE" bar at (4479 px → local 3714, 6488), ~520 m south of the
   drawn seminary building, seminary on the line's right exactly as
   reported**.
5. Night [E]: withdrew "to the next ridge, on the left of the town";
   division "bivouacked that night on the left of our position, with
   pickets extending almost to Fairfield" (or-27-1-buford).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. Camp → the delay line [D]: on the ~8:00 picket warning; skirmish
   squadrons pushed forward to the picket line, guns split one

   (or-27-1-gamble).
2. The 200-yard fall-back under artillery [D].
3. **Relief hand-off** [D, CA-J1M-4/5 adjacency]: "our infantry

   brigade" — both-sided with Wadsworth/Morrow (the cavalry "hotly

   (Tidball's battery called up by Wadsworth after relief).
4. **The afternoon trot south** [D]: "ordered my brigade forward at a
   trot" to the left ridge; the dismount; the mounted withdrawal when
   nearly enveloped (or-27-1-gamble).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The morning fight** [C ~08:00 → relief]: skirmisher line + the
   six rifles "opened on the enemy's advancing column, doing good
   execution"; two CSA batteries in reply; "After checking and
   retarding the advance of the enemy several hours" —
   duration-class, agreeing with Buford's "held its own for more than
   two hours". His "opposed by three divisions of Hill's corps" is an
   overcount (Heth's four brigades led) — carried as stated, flagged.
2. **The afternoon carbine fire** [D]: "we opened a sharp and rapid
   carbine fire, which killed and wounded so many of the first line
   of the enemy that it fell back upon the second line" (Lane's
   brigade front, per the CSA side's account of cavalry on Pender's
   right — see conflicts); his claim: "prevented our left flank from
   being turned, and saved a division of our infantry."
3. The closer, verbatim: "This brigade had the honor to commence the
   fight in the morning and close it in the evening."
