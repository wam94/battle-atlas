## EC5 — Activity record

- **July 3, 13:07–~15:00** [A window vs CA-J3A-1/5]: under the cannonade
  at the line's north shoulder; Napoleons largely outranged in the
  counter-battery duel — the battery's hour was the assault.
- **July 3 forenoon — the eight engagements** [C, new this pass]: "quiet
  until about 8 o'clock, when the enemy suddenly opened fire … Little
  reply was ma[de], save by Light Company I, First U. S. Artillery,
  which battery during the forenoon had eight separate engagements with
  the enemy" (or-27-1-hazard, read at live ehistory) — the ONLY II Corps
  battery active through the morning; a documented pre-cannonade
  activity record no other Union battery on the front has.
- **~15:16–15:30 — canister into Pettigrew's left** [D between CA-J3A-7
  and CA-J3A-10]: with Osborn's Cemetery Hill guns enfilading from the
  north, Woodruff's canister broke up the Brockenbrough/Davis end of the
  advancing front short of the wall (full-cast survey §3.3; the 8th
  Ohio's flanking fire worked the same ground). As the copse-sector
  batteries were pulled out, "Woodruff still remained in the grove, and
  poured death and destruction into the rebel lines … [he] fell,
  mortally wounded, at the very moment of victory" (or-27-1-hazard) —
  the line's last battery in action.
- Rounds expended: no battery figure (negative).
