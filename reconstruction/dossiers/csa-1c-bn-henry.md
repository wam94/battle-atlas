# Dossier: Henry's Artillery Battalion, Hood's Division (reg-csa-1c-bn-henry)

Pass 13 (the first of the two orphaned CSA battalions) · Cast status:
`csa-bn-henry` (battalion) — two of its four batteries already
in-build (Bachman's, Reilly's) with zero prior dossier coverage ·
Achieved T-level: **T4 (battalion grain, ED-36 pattern)** · 2026-07-13

Chain role: the CSA reference frame for Hood's Division's own guns —
the South Cavalry Field flank's artillery arc, closing one of the two
battalion-dossier holes pass 12's triage named in an otherwise-complete
First Corps battalion set (Alexander/Eshleman/Dearing/Cabell already
dossiered; Henry's was the gap).

## EC1 — Identity & command

- Henry's Artillery Battalion, Hood's Division, Longstreet's (First)
  Corps: Branch (N.C.) Artillery (Latham's), German (S.C.) Artillery
  (Bachman's), Palmetto (S.C.) Light Artillery (Garden's), Rowan
  (N.C.) Artillery (Reilly's) (or-27-oob; battalion marker,
  fetch-verified this pass). **Maj. Mathis W. Henry** commanding
  throughout; own report dated Fredericksburg, Aug. 23, 1863
  (or-27-2-henry, No. 463, pp. 427-428, fetch-verified this pass).
- Armament (battalion tablet, verbatim): "Eleven Napoleons, Four 10
  Pounder Parrotts, Two 3 Inch Rifles, One 12 Pounder Howitzer, One 6
  Pounder Bronze Gun" = 19 guns across four batteries.
- **Equipment-loss/replacement fact [C, report-primary]**: "one
  3-inch rifle belonging to Captain Reilly's battery burst" and the
  battalion "captured and brought off the field this night three
  10-pounder Parrott rifles" (Henry's report) — corroborating the
  battalion tablet's "the Howitzer, the Bronze gun, and one 3-inch
  Rifle were disabled, and three captured 10-pounder Parrotts were
  substituted" almost verbatim (report=tablet digit-exact agreement
  on the replacement count).

## EC2 — Engaged strength

- No brigade/battalion-total strength figure was located this pass
  (neither the battalion tablet nor Henry's own report states a
  personnel total) — EC2 stays explicitly open. Gun count (19, above)
  is the only quantified EC1/EC2-adjacent figure.

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2, the right of the line, afternoon** [C, report-primary]:
   "took position in the afternoon on the right of our line, and
   engaged the enemy's artillery with two batteries, Captains
   [James] Reilly and [A. C.] Latham" (Henry's own report, verbatim,
   fetch-verified this pass) — ONLY two of the four batteries engaged
   at the July 2 opening; Bachman's and Garden's join later (below).
2. **Latham's (Branch) battery, July 2** [C 16:00, marker-adjudicated]:
   "Took position here 4 P. M. and actively engaged until night"
   (Branch/Latham marker, fetch-verified this pass) — the battalion's
   only stated clock this pass, a tier-C tablet reading.
3. **Garden's (Palmetto) battery, July 2** [D, marker]: "In reserve
   near here but not engaged" — the fourth battery's own attested-
   static July 2 window, matching Henry's report's "with two
   batteries" (Reilly/Latham only) by omission — Garden's and, by
   inference, Bachman's were NOT among the two engaged.
4. **July 3, whole-battalion engagement** [C, report-primary]:
   "Engaged the enemy again in the same position with the whole
   battalion" (Henry's report) — all four batteries in line July 3,
   consistent with each battery's own marker independently claiming
   July 3 activity (below, EC5).
5. **South Cavalry Field flank, ~17:00-17:30** [C ~17:00, TWO
   independent marker agreements]: both Latham's and Garden's
   batteries' markers carry IDENTICAL closing language: "About 5 P.M.
   aided in repelling cavalry under Brig. Gen. Farnsworth which had
   charged into the valley between this point and Round Top" — a
   two-battery CSA-side corroboration of the CA-SCF-2 anchor (ADOPTED
   ED-72, ~17:30 envelope 17:00-18:00), independent of the Union-side
   tablet triple-agreement already on file (`us-cav-3-1-farnsworth.md`,
   `us-cav-1-3-merritt.md`).
6. **July 4, withdrawal** [C, marker]: "Occupied position near by and
   west of this until 6 P. M. Then withdrew from the field" (both
   battery markers, verbatim-identical wording — likely a shared
   template phrase across the battalion's markers, not independent
   attestation; recorded as such, not double-counted as corroboration).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. [C ~16:00] Reilly's and Latham's batteries into line, July 2
   afternoon (EC3.1/3.2).
2. [D] Bachman's and Garden's batteries join the line by July 3
   morning at latest (EC3.4) — no leg timing stated.
3. [D] July 4 withdrawal leg, ~18:00 start, destination unstated this
   pass.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **July 2 duel** [C 16:00-dark]: Reilly's and Latham's batteries
   "engaged the enemy's artillery" from the 4 P.M. position.
2. **July 3, whole-battalion fire** [C, report + tablet]: "Engaged in
   firing upon the Union lines within range" (both battery markers,
   near-verbatim) — a bombardment/counter-battery role, not a
   participant in Pickett's Charge's own cannonade (South Cavalry
   Field's guns are geometrically distinct from the Peach
   Orchard/Seminary Ridge arc the main CA-J3A chain covers).
3. **The Farnsworth-repulse fire** [C ~17:00, EC3.5]: both Latham's
   and Garden's batteries credit themselves with "aided in repelling"
   the charge — direct-fire canister-class engagement against cavalry
   at close range, the same activity class as the Union horse-
   artillery batteries on the OTHER side of this same action
   (`us-btty-randol.md`/`us-btty-pennington.md` at East Cavalry Field
   are the theater's mirror case, not this same field, but the same
   activity CLASS per ED-34).
4. **ROUNDS EXPENDED (report-primary): "about 1,500 rounds"** across
   the two days (Henry's report, matching the battalion tablet's
   figure exactly — a report=tablet digit-exact agreement) — [B basis:
   1,500 ÷ 19 guns ≈ 79 rds/gun over two engaged days, though only
   2 of 4 batteries fired July 2, so the per-gun rate is uneven across
   the battalion's own guns; no battery-level breakdown found].

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Report AND tablet, digit-exact agreement: "lost 4 men killed and
  23 wounded"** (Henry's report) = "Losses Killed 4 Wounded 23"
  (battalion tablet) — a clean report=tablet match, the battalion's
  strongest EC6 fact. No missing/captured figure given by either
  source (explicitly not stated, not assumed zero).
- **Garden's battery, its own marker**: "Losses not reported in
  detail" — a first-class negative at the individual-battery grain,
  consistent with (not contradicting) the battalion total above.
- No per-battery breakdown of the 4/23 total across the four batteries
  was found this pass.

## Conflicts & negative evidence

1. **EC2 is fully open** — no personnel-strength figure (report,
   tablet, or B&M-repro hop) was located for this battalion this pass;
   recorded as a genuine gap, not silently filled by inference from
   gun count.
2. **The July 4 withdrawal phrase is template-identical across two
   battery markers** — recorded as a shared-boilerplate caution (EC3.6),
   not double independent corroboration.
3. **No per-battery July 2/3 position geometry beyond Latham's stated
   "here" (the marker's own siting) was authored this pass** — Bachman's
   and Reilly's markers (both batteries already in-build) were NOT
   independently fetched this pass; a pass-14 item if either battery
   graduates toward its own dossier (per ED-36, battalion grain is the
   standard; per-battery detail is authored only where an anchor
   exists, and none beyond Latham's/Garden's was found this pass).

  the pass's best cross-theater find: the ~17:30 CA-SCF-2 envelope now
  has FIVE independent sources across both sides (3 Union + 2 CSA).
