## EC1 — Identity & command

- Designation: 3rd Brigade, 3rd Division (Doubleday), I Corps — the "2nd
  Vermont Brigade": 12th, 13th, 14th, 15th, 16th Vermont, nine-month
  regiments (or-27-oob; wikipedia-oob-union).
- Commander: Brig. Gen. George J. Stannard, **wounded (right thigh) July 3**
  while directing the flank attack; remained on the field to the end of the
  action; Col. Francis V. Randall (13th VT) succeeded to brigade command
  (or-27-oob; stone-sentinels, brigade HQ tablet; oob-strengths survey).
  Timing of the wound: [D] during the second (Wilcox/Lang) flank movement,
  after the CA-J3A-8 wheel — see EC6 officer pins.
- Regimental commanders July 3: Randall (13th), Col. William T. Nichols
  (14th), Col. Wheelock G. Veazey (16th) (or-27-oob).
- Note on the arm: fresh, large, and untried — mustered fall 1862, first
  battle; the brigade joined the I Corps on the march (see EC4) and its
  nine-month terms expired within a fortnight of the battle.

## EC3 — Position anchors

Battlefield-local coordinates are the traced/authored build frame (metres);
coordinate inferences are marked.

1. **July 1, ~17:00–nightfall — Cemetery Hill approach** [E window]: the
  brigade (less 12th/15th) reached the field the evening of July 1 after a
  forced march from the Emmitsburg direction, taking position with I Corps
  on Cemetery Hill/left of the cemetery (or-27-1-stannard; benedict-1888).
  No coordinate adopted this pass (reference-frame layer work).
2. **July 2, evening — left-centre of Cemetery Ridge** [D, before the
  twilight counterstroke]: posted on Doubleday's front, from which the 13th
  VT advanced against Wright's lodgment (see EC4/EC5). Uncertainty:
  regimental, ~150 m.
3. **July 3, all morning → 15:20 — the advanced line** [E for the window;
  position documented]: "the advanced position south of the main line",
  regiment bars legible on the Bachelder third-day sheet — brigade centroid
  (4300, 4470), 13th VT (4279, 4587), 14th VT (4300, 4470), 16th VT (4321,
  4353), facing ~260°, uncertainty ~50 m (claim-stannard-position;
  bachelder-third-day-1876 sheet 3). This stands ~100 m WEST (forward) of
  the main II Corps wall line — the geometry that made CA-J3A-8 possible.
4. **~15:20–15:28 — wheeled flank position** [A vs CA-J3A-8]: 13th VT
  terminal anchor (4326, 4764) facing ~15°, 16th VT on its left (4240,
  4770) — perpendicular to and ~60 yards from Kemper's passing flank
  ("saw in their immediate front not sixty yards away General Pickett's
  command", sturtevant-1910 pp. 304-305; vt13-monument "advanced 200
  yards"). Coordinate basis: build keyframes t=8700 (inference from the
  monument's 200-yard statement + Bachelder start position — marked
  inferred, ±40 m).
5. **~15:45–16:00 — southward front against Wilcox/Lang** [D after
  CA-J3A-9]: 16th VT reversed, terminal anchor (4180, 4360) facing ~200°
  (build t=10380, documented-grade via veazey-or); 14th VT refused its
  front in place.
6. **July 3 evening → July 4 — original line** [E]: brigade returns to the
  advanced line / main line; leaves the field with I Corps July 5-7 (the
  nine-month muster-outs: 13th VT July 21, 14th July 30, 16th August 10,
  1863 — EC1 note; benedict-1888).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **March to the field, June 25–July 1** [E]: attached from the Washington
  defenses to I Corps on the march; final leg reached Cemetery Hill the
  evening of July 1 (or-27-1-stannard; benedict-1888). Route class: road
  column; no leg timing adopted this pass.
2. **July 2 twilight — 13th VT advance to the guns** [D: after Wright
  crested the ridge, before dark]: front ~one regiment, ~400–600 m out and
  back, line; trigger: Doubleday/Hancock orders during the crisis
  (or-27-1-stannard; benedict-1888; sturtevant-1910). Recovered guns —
  see EC5.
3. **July 3, ~15:20 — the change of front** [A vs CA-J3A-8, adopted ~15:20,
  envelope 15:18–15:24]: "Change front forward on first company" — 13th VT
  pivots from line facing W to line facing N, advances ~200 yards
  (vt13-monument); 16th VT conforms on its left after withdrawing from the
  skirmish line [D immediately after the 13th] (veazey-or;
  claim-stannard-wheel). Physics check [B: 200 yd ≈ 180 m at ~1.4 m/s
  advance + wheel ≈ 3–4 min]: the leg self-times to ~15:20–15:24 from a
  15:16–15:18 road crossing (CA-J3A-7) — consistent with the D-bracket.
  Formation: line, company-front pivot; trigger: Stannard's brigade order
  (Sturtevant's "famous and now historic order").
4. **July 3, ~15:40–15:50 — the reversal** [D: after the Angle repulse,
  as Wilcox/Lang advanced]: 16th VT about-faces/reforms facing S, joined by
  the 14th's refused fire, striking Lang's left (veazey-or; build t=9900–
  10380). Leg length ~300 m; [B: ~4 min at advance pace] inside the
  15:30–16:00 CA-J3A-10 window.
5. **Return to the line, ~16:00+** [E]: both wheeled regiments re-form on
  the advanced line (reconstruction in build; no primary leg record —
  negative evidence noted below).

## EC5 — Activity record

- **July 2, twilight**: 13th VT (5 cos. under Randall) advanced, retook
  Weir's Battery C 5th US guns lost in the Wright/Perry lodgment, took
  ~80 prisoners (or-27-1-stannard; benedict-1888 — figures to be verified
  at the page; carried at report grade).
- **July 3, 13:07–15:00 — under the cannonade** [A window vs CA-J3A-1/5]:
  prone on the advanced line, under the overshooting fire; skirmish
  companies forward (16th VT held the skirmish line until recalled for the
  wheel — veazey-or).
- **July 3, ~15:16–15:20 — front fire** [D between CA-J3A-7 and CA-J3A-8]:
  as Kemper's front passed obliquely north across the brigade's front, the
  advanced line opened at flank-effective range; the oblique it punished is
  documented from inside Kemper's line (claim-kemper-oblique,
  martin-smith-shsp32-1904 pp. 189-195).
- **July 3, ~15:20–15:28 — the flank fire** [A vs CA-J3A-8]: 13th + 16th
  from the wheeled position "at once opened a deadly fire on their flank"
  (sturtevant-1910 pp. 304-305); 13th VT monument: "attacking the
  Confederate right flank … capturing 243 prisoners" (vt13-monument).
- **July 3, ~15:45–16:00 — the second flank fire** [D after CA-J3A-9]:
  16th VT (with 14th) into Wilcox/Lang's left; the 16th took the 2nd
  Florida's colors and prisoners (veazey-or; benedict-1888).
- Munition/range note: musketry only (infantry); the sixty-yards statement
  (sturtevant-1910) attests the wheel's closing range — a position-grade
  fact per the spec's canister principle applied to musketry.

## Conflicts & negative evidence

1. **Strength**: 1,950 (brigade total) vs 1,788 (sum of engaged regiments)
  — unexplained ~160-man gap; both carried (oob-strengths #3).
2. **Credit dispute**: Sturtevant (1910) ignores the Stannard-vs-
  Doubleday/Hancock credit dispute over who ordered the flank attack
  (sturtevant-1910 editionNote). Hancock's later accounts claim a role;
  the dossier keeps the order with Stannard per the brigade's own record
  and flags the dispute.
3. **No clock exists for the wheel** (verified negative, R1 + this pass):
  Sturtevant, the monument, and Veazey's report are sequence-only. CA-J3A-8
  stays D-promoted-to-B bracket arithmetic; any future "15:20" print
  citation should be checked against this negative before adoption.
4. **Return-to-line legs are unattested** (negative): the build's return
  keyframes (t=9600/10800) are reconstruction; no source narrates the
  re-forming.
5. 12th/15th VT non-engagement is attested (train guard), not silence.
