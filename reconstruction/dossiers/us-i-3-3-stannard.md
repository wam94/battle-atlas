## EC1 — Identity & command

- 2nd Vermont Brigade = 3rd Brigade, 3rd Division (Doubleday), I Corps:
  12th VT (Col. Blunt), 13th (Col. Francis V. Randall; Lt. Col. Munson),
  14th (Col. William T. Nichols; Lt. Col. Rose), 15th (Col. Proctor),
  16th (Col. Wheelock G. Veazey). Nine-month regiments, first battle
  ("Officers and men behaved like veterans, although it was for most of
  them their first battle", or-27-1-stannard p. 350) (or-27-oob;
  stone-sentinels brigade tablet).
- Brig. Gen. George J. Stannard, **wounded July 3** — succession to
  Randall: "General Stannard was also wounded soon after [Hancock], and
  compelled reluctantly to leave the field, since which time I have been
  in command of the brigade" (or-27-1-randall p. 353). But Benedict has
  him refusing to leave until the action closed ("though urged to do so
  by his aids and others, he refused to leave the field", benedict-1888
  p. 505) — succession effective at the battle's close [D].
- Staff (named in the report, p. 350): Capt. Hill AAG; Lts. Hooker and
  **G. G. Benedict** ADC (the brigade's later historian was Stannard's
  own aide — source-independence caveat on benedict-1888), Clark,
  Prentiss.
- Report chain: Stannard's No. 69 (written Gettysburg, July 4);
  Randall's No. 70 (Middletown, July 10); Veazey's in the appendix
  (Berlin, July 17).

- 14th VT: "647 men brought to the field" (stone-sentinels 14th VT page).

- 12th/15th VT detached: "detailed, by order of General Reynolds, as

  15th rejoined briefly on the morning of July 2 "but was again ordered
  back on the same duty about noon" (or-27-1-stannard p. 349).

## EC3 — Position anchors

Battlefield-local coordinates are the traced/authored build frame
(metres); coordinate inferences marked.

1. **July 1, evening (~sunset/dusk) — front line with III Corps** [C
  via or-27-1-randall, ~17:00 stated; corroborated "nearly sunset"
  benedict-1888 p. 470, "at dusk" tablet]: "my tired troops upon their
  arrival were placed in position in column by regiments on the front
  line, in connection with the Third Army Corps" (or-27-1-stannard
  p. 349); tablet: "took position on right of Third Corps". Route in:
  "country roads west of Marsh Creek … struck across Willoughby Run to
  the Emmittsburg road" (benedict-1888 p. 470).
2. **July 2, morning — rear of the left of Cemetery Hill** [E]: "were
  allowed to join the First Army Corps, and reported to Major-General
  Doubleday … placed in the rear of the left of Cemetery Hill"
  (or-27-1-stannard p. 349).
3. **July 2 evening → July 3 end — the advanced line** [E window;
  documented, now primary]: "I selected a position to occupy, if
  attacked with infantry, some distance in front of the main line …
  The front thus established was held by my brigade for twenty-six
  hours" (or-27-1-stannard p. 349) — the 26-hour statement is the
  brigade's own negative evidence of stillness between the moves.
  Geometry: brigade centroid (4300, 4470) facing ~260°, 13th VT (4279,
  4587), 14th (4300, 4470), 16th (4321, 4353), ±50 m (bachelder-third-
  day-1876 sheet 3; claim-stannard-position). Field fortification:
  "In the morning 100 men advanced 45 yards under the fire of
  sharpshooters and placed a line of rail" (vt13-monument) — rails, not
  rifle pits. 14th VT's own forward step: "I moved the Fourteenth …
  to the front of the main line about 75 yards … at double-quick in
  good order" (or-27-1-stannard p. 349, during the ~04:00 artillery).
4. **~15:20–15:28 — the wheeled flank position** [A vs CA-J3A-8]:
  "the Thirteenth changed front forward on first company; the
  Sixteenth, after deploying, performed the same, and formed on the
  left of the Thirteenth, **at right angles to the main line**"
  (or-27-1-stannard p. 350). Terminal anchors: 13th VT (4326, 4764)
  facing ~15°, 16th on its left (4240, 4770) (build t=8700; inference
  from the monument's 200 yards + Benedict's rods, ±40 m). Range at
  contact: "hardly a dozen rods [~60 yd] from the enemy's flank"
  (benedict-1888 pp. 496-497; sturtevant-1910 "not sixty yards away").
  Codori-thicket cross-bearing: the 16th's original monument stood
  "south 58 degrees, west 1000 feet … near the northerly end of the
  Codori thicket" (stone-sentinels 16th VT page) — a documented
  survey-grade bearing for the second-charge ground.
5. **~15:45–16:00 — southward front vs Wilcox/Lang** [D after
  CA-J3A-9]: the 16th "after moving by the flank to the right for some
  fifty rods, made an oblique change of front" then faced the second
  column whose "left flank [was] not more than thirty or forty rods
  distant" (benedict-1888 pp. 497, 502); build terminal (4180, 4360)
  facing ~200° (t=10380). 16th VT monument: "assaulted his flank to
  the right 400 yards – then changing front charged left flank of
  Wilcox's and Perry's brigades."
6. **July 3 evening → July 5-18 — departure by muster-out ladder** [E]:
  Stannard evacuated to Baltimore "on the Sunday following" (July 5,
  benedict-1888 p. 518); the regiments left in echelon; muster-outs:
  12th July 14, 13th July 21, 14th July 30, 15th Aug 5, 16th Aug 10
  (benedict-1888 p. 521).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **March to the field, June 25–July 1** [E, now report-grade]:
  "marched from the line of the Defenses of Washington, upon the
  Occoquan, on the 25th ultimo … The distance marched averaged about
  18 miles per day", joining I Corps "on the evening of July 1, after
  an exhausting march of seven day's duration" (or-27-1-stannard
  p. 348); Randall's clock: "arriving there on July 1, at about 5 p.m."
  [C report-nominal].
2. **July 2 twilight — 13th VT (right wing, 5 cos.) to the guns** [D:
  after Wright's break, before dark]: trigger documented as an on-spot
  grant: "upon reaching the break in the line, was granted by
  Major-General Hancock, commanding upon the spot, the privilege of
  making the effort to retake the guns of Company C, regular battery"
  (or-27-1-stannard p. 349); pursuit "about half way to the Emmitsburg
  road" then one company sent "under cover of the road, dug way, and
  fences" to the two rebel pieces shelling them from the south
  (or-27-1-randall p. 352; vt13-monument "charged to the Rogers House
  on the Emmitsburg Road, captured 83 prisoners and recaptured 4
  guns"). Return: "In pursuance of orders from General Hancock, we
  [now: sic 'no'] slowly fell back to the main line" (or-27-1-randall).
3. **July 3, ~15:20 — the change of front (CA-J3A-8)** [A vs CA-J3A-8,
  adopted ~15:20, envelope 15:18–15:24]: trigger — the column's
  divergence: "The charge was aimed directly upon my command … the
  enemy diverged midway, and came upon the line on my right"
  (or-27-1-stannard p. 349); the flank-march it punished: "the enemy's
  lines suddenly changed direction and marched by the flank to the
  north across its front for some sixty rods" (benedict-1888 p. 495).
  Leg geometry: "The Thirteenth moved first, **in column of fours, for
  thirty or forty rods** toward the enemy" then wheeled (benedict-1888
  p. 496) ≈ 150–200 m, matching the monument's "advanced 200 yards".
  Physics [B: 180 m in column of fours at ~1.4 m/s + deploy ≈ 3–4 min]
  — self-times to ~15:20–15:24 from a 15:16–15:18 road crossing
  (CA-J3A-7), consistent with the D-bracket. **Command-wording
  conflict, first-class**: Stannard "changed front forward on first
  company" vs Randall "I changed front forward on my right company"
  (both verified) — same maneuver, different remembered pivot; carried,
  not reconciled.
4. **The 16th's approach leg** [D immediately after the 13th]: recalled
  from picket, "PASSED BACK AND ALONG BY THE REAR OF THE FOURTEENTH AND
  MOVED TO THE LEFT OF THE THIRTEENTH" (veazey-or p. 1042); "after
  moving by the flank to the right for some fifty rods [~250 m], made
  an oblique change of front" (benedict-1888 p. 496).
5. **July 3, ~15:40–15:50 — the reversal vs Wilcox/Lang** [D after the
  first charge]: trigger: "I saw another rebel column charging
  immediately upon our left. Colonel Veazey, of the Sixteenth, was at
  once ordered to attack it in its turn upon the flank"
  (or-27-1-stannard p. 350); execution: "I IMMEDIATELY RECEIVED AN
  ORDER TO MARCH BACK AND GET INTO POSITION TO OPPOSE THIS NEW LINE …
  I RECEIVED PERMISSION TO CHARGE" (veazey-or p. 1042); support: "four
  companies of the Fourteenth, under … Lieutenant-Colonel Rose"
  (Stannard p. 350). The 13th also followed and was recalled: "General
  Stannard sent orders to me to bring my regiment back to the main
  line" (or-27-1-randall p. 353).
6. **Return to the line** [D]: the Randall recall order above is the
  one documented return leg; the 16th's return is unnarrated (negative
  carried; the build's return keyframes remain reconstruction).

## EC5 — Activity record

- **July 2, ~14:00** [C report-nominal]: five companies of the 13th
  advanced "to support a battery on our front" (or-27-1-randall
  p. 351) — the brigade's first fire exposure.
- **July 2, twilight — the counterattack**: "We received one volley
  from them … when my men sprang forward with the bayonet"; four of
  Weir's guns retaken (identified by Randall: "the Fifth U. S.
  Regulars, Lieutenant Weir. There were four of them"), two rebel
  pieces and "the rebel picket reserve, consisting of 3 officers and
  80 men" captured (or-27-1-randall p. 352; or-27-1-stannard p. 349:
  "about 80 prisoners … by five companies of the Thirteenth … in this
  single charge"; Randall's horse shot under him). Benedict p. 484
  corroborates Weir's state ("The gunners had abandoned three of his
  guns").
- **July 2 night**: 16th VT entire on picket "extending to the left of
  our immediate front" (Stannard p. 349; veazey-or p. 1041).
- **July 3, ~04:00 — under the morning artillery** [C report-nominal]:
  "At about 4 o'clock on the morning of the 3d, the enemy commenced a
  vigorous artillery attack" (Stannard p. 349); Veazey's picket clock
  "AT A QUARTER BEFORE 4 O'CLOCK, THE ENEMY ENGAGED THE LEFT OF THE
  PICKET LINE" (p. 1042).
- **13:07–~15:00 — under the cannonade** [A window vs CA-J3A-1/5]:
  Stannard's own duration: "After subjecting us for one and one-half
  hours to the severest cannonade of the whole battle, from one
  hundred guns or more" (p. 349) — his stated start "At about 2 p. m."
  is the ~14:00 nominal-cluster reading (clockProfile, rule 4); the
  duration corroborates CA-J3A-4's envelope.
- **~15:16–15:20 — the front fire** [D between CA-J3A-7 and CA-J3A-8]:
  fire discipline documented: "Our men were directed to withhold their
  fire" until the column was "about half way from the Emmitsburg road
  to our position … when the two regiments rose up and poured in a
  volley that seemed to level their front rank and all mounted
  officers" — then the column "commenced to move by their left flank
  toward a position more nearly in front of the cemetery"
  (or-27-1-randall p. 353); the 14th opened "at about sixty rods'
  distance" (benedict-1888 p. 495). This is the executor-side record
  of Kemper's oblique (claim-kemper-oblique).
- **~15:20–15:28 — the flank fire (CA-J3A-8)** [A]: "opened a
  destructive fire at short range, which the enemy sustained but a
  very few moments before the larger portion of them surrendered"
  (Stannard p. 350); **expenditure documented: "We had fired about 10
  rounds per man when they seemed to be in utter confusion"**
  (or-27-1-randall p. 353) → [B: 10 rounds ÷ ~2.5 rds/min drill rate ≈
  4 min of flank fire] — the first physics input for the CA-J3A-8 →
  surrender interval. Coordination: "General Doubleday at this time
  rode up … he ordered the regiments to my right to cease firing and
  allow me to pass in front of their line" (Randall p. 353). Prisoners:
  "capturing 243 prisoners" (vt13-monument); "I had apparently more
  than there were men in my regiment" (Randall).
- **~15:45–16:00 — the second flank fire** [D]: the 16th (+ 4 cos.
  14th) into Wilcox/Perry's left at "thirty or forty rods"; colors
  taken: "the regimental colors of the Second Florida and Eighth
  Virginia Regiments, and the battle-flag of another regiment"
  (Stannard p. 350; veazey-or names the two brought in; brigade tablet
  "captured three flags").
- Conditions: "executed in the open field, under a very heavy fire of
  shell, grape, and musketry" (Stannard p. 350).

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Official return (OR 27/1 p. 174, fetched this pass)** — primary
  figures, now the adopted basis: Staff 2 officers w; 13th VT 10 k /
  103 w (4 off + 99) / 10 m = 123; 14th VT 19 k (1 off + 18) / 67 w
  (1 off + 66) / 21 m = 107; 16th VT 16 k / 102 w (5 off + 97) / 1 m
  = 119. **Brigade 45 k (1 off + 44) / 274 w (12 off + 262) / 32 m =
  351.**
- Disagreements carried verbatim: Stannard "There were 350 killed,
  wounded, and missing from my three regiments engaged; of the missing,
  only 1 is known to have been taken prisoner" (p. 350) — 350 vs the
  return's 351, and his missing-note vs the return's 32; Randall "8
  killed, 89 wounded and 26 missing" (p. 353, "as near as I can now
  ascertain") vs the return's 123 aggregate vs the monument's "22
  killed and mortally wounded, 80 other wounded" (the k-vs-mw
  accounting difference explains most of the 13th's spread).
- Episode distribution [D structure, upgraded]: July 2 losses light
  ("one volley … which did us very little injury", Randall); July 3
  dominated by (a) the cannonade window on the advanced line, (b) the
  two flank actions under "shell, grape, and musketry". A per-episode
  split is still not stated primary — the build curve remains
  reconstruction, now bracketed by these statements.
- Officer pins:
  - Brig. Gen. Stannard, w — "an iron shrapnel ball, which passed down
    for three inches into the muscles on the inside of the thigh"
    (benedict-1888 p. 505); sequence pin [D]: after Hancock's wound
    ("General Stannard was also wounded soon after", or-27-1-randall
    p. 353) = during/just after ordering the second flank movement;
    refused to leave the field (Benedict).
  - Lt. John F. Sinnott (13th) mw — "During the flank attack of July
    3d he was struck in the forehead by a piece of a shell"
    (benedict-1888 p. 515) [A vs CA-J3A-8 window].
  - Lt. C. B. Lawton (16th, Co. F) mw — "NEAR THE CLOSE OF THE BATTLE"
    (veazey-or p. 1042) [D late in the second action].
  - Lt. W. H. Hamilton (14th) mw (benedict-1888 p. 515) [E].
