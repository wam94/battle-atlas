# Dossier: Biddle's Brigade (1st Bde, 3rd Div, I Corps — Gates's demi-brigade at the July 3 line) (reg-us-i-3-1)

Pass 4 (Union center command batch — the deferred Doubleday pieces) ·
Cast status: in-build (`us-biddle`) · Achieved T-level: **T3 full-arc**
(honestly bounded — the July 3 record is strong but the casualty ledger
cannot be split by day at primary grade) · 2026-07-11

Command-batch role: the I Corps brigade wrecked on July 1 whose
two-regiment remnant (80th NY + 151st Pa under Col. Gates) stood in the
front line just south of the Copse on July 3 and slid north into the
climax — the second line's documented participation in the repulse,
alongside Stannard (pass 1) on one flank and Harrow/Hall (pass 2) on
the other.

## EC1 — Identity & command

- Command chain: Col. Chapman Biddle (July 1, w); Brig. Gen. Thomas A.
  Rowley commanded the division July 1 (Doubleday at corps) and the
  brigade thereafter in the register's reading; **Col. Theodore B.
  Gates (20th NYSM/80th NY) commanded the two-regiment front-line
  element July 3** (or-27-oob; or-27-1-gates, signature "Colonel,
  Commanding" reporting direct to Doubleday).
- Regiments: 80th NY (20th NYSM), 121st Pa, 142nd Pa, 151st Pa
  (or-27-oob).
- Officer pins: Biddle w July 1; Col. Langhorne Wister / Lt. Col.
  McFarland losses belong to Stone's/151st's July 1 records; no
  July 3 field-officer casualty in the fetched primaries (negative).

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. July 1 night [C]: "posted in line along the Taneytown road"
   (or-27-1-gates).
2. **July 2 ~17:00 — to the left center** [C]: "the brigade was ordered
   to the left center, to support the Second Corps"; position "halted
   on the last and lowest of the ridges running nearly north and south
   between the Taneytown and Emmitsburg roads" (or-27-1-gates) — the
   low ground south of the Copse, in Doubleday's second line.
3. **July 3 front line** [C/E]: "The Vermonters, with the Twentieth New
   York and One hundred and fifty-first Pennsylvania Volunteers, held
   the front line during the remainder of the action"
   (or-27-1-doubleday p. 258). **Sheet reads (new)**: j3-02
   (08:00–11:00) draws "BIDDLE'S Brig" at ~local (4482, 4569) with a
   second "BIDDLE." bar at (4625, 4515) ±120 m; j3-03 (13:00–17:00)
   draws BIDDLE'S BRIG at ~(4591, 4559) just south of RORTY — the
   drawn climax state south of the Copse sector.
4. Climax endpoint [D]: "up to the foot of the bluff ... between the
   enemy and the object of his enterprise"; "Two regiments advanced to
   the fence" (or-27-1-gates) — the fence/crest line at the Copse's
   south shoulder.

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. July 1: the McPherson Ridge fight and retreat through town (July 1
   record carried at register grain; this dossier's commissioned window
   is the July 2-3 frame).
2. July 2 ~17:00: Taneytown road → the left-center low ridge [C]
   (or-27-1-gates).
3. **July 3 — the lateral slide north (the climax leg)** [D]: "I moved
   my command by the right flank up to the foot of the bluff,
   delivering our fire as we marched, and keeping between the enemy and
   the object of his enterprise" (or-27-1-gates) — a firing flank-march
   toward the Copse, ~200-400 m by the sheet geometry (build's track
   should be audited against it); then the two-regiment advance to the
   fence and the short-range volley.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. July 3 dawn [C]: "About 5 a.m. ... the enemy opened with artillery,
   and for some time kept up a brisk fire" (or-27-1-gates — the Culp's
   Hill morning fight heard from the center; CA-J3M frame witness).
2. The cannonade [E window]: under the 100+-gun fire (or-27-1-gates's
   near-three-hour framing rides his unprofiled clock; window-level
   only).
3. **The repulse fire** [D]: fire delivered on the march (EC4.3); "when
   within 200 feet ... opened a warm fire"; the fence volley "at very
   short range, who now completely broke"; "took a large number of
   prisoners"; "Two colors were left upon the ground by the enemy"
   (or-27-1-gates).

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- Official return (three-day): brigade **898** — 80th NY 170, 121st Pa
  179, 142nd Pa 211, 151st Pa 337 (or-27-1-union-return p. 174).
- **The day split cannot be made at primary grade** (the T4 blocker,
  recorded honestly): the return is three-day; Gates's stated 170 (3
  off k, 15 off w, 1 off m; 32 men k, 96 w, 23 m) equals the 80th NY's
  return row EXACTLY, i.e. his report's figure is also a battle total,
  not a July 3 figure. Narrative shape [D]: July 1 dominant (the
  brigade broke on McPherson's Ridge; 151st Pa's 337 is a famous
  July 1 loss), July 3 light-to-moderate (a firing advance and a
  short-range volley against a breaking enemy).
- Pins: Biddle w (July 1).
