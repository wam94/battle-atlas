## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 1, reserve then McMillan's Woods** [C, tablet + attributed
   report]: "In reserve north of Chambersburg Pike on left of the
   Division. At sunset moved to position in McMillan's Woods" (tablet,
   verbatim) — matching the attributed report summary's "formed in
   line of battle on the left of the road leading to Gettysburg…
   advanced to within about 1 mile of Gettysburg in readiness to
   support… Heth's division, then moved to a position assigned by…
   Hill, and near sunset took position near Gettysburg on the right of
   the town in support of artillery" (web research, attributed grade —
   the "right of the town" phrasing and the tablet's "McMillan's
   Woods" are not independently reconciled this pass, a recorded
   ambiguity).
2. **July 2, artillery support, then Long Lane** [C ~22:00, tablet,
   verbatim]: "On duty in support of artillery. At 10 P. M. advancing
   took position in Long Lane with left flank in touch with McGowan's
   Brigade and the right near the Bliss House and Barn" — TWO named
   adjacencies: McGowan's brigade (= Perrin's, already dossiered,
   `csa-3c-pen-1-perrin.md`) on the left, and the Bliss farm buildings
   on the right — the SAME burned barn/house Posey's brigade's own
   report names one division-front over (`csa-3c-and-5-posey.md`
   EC3.2), a genuinely new cross-brigade geographic tie this pass
   surfaces (not previously on record in either dossier).
3. **July 3, the skirmish line** [C, tablet]: "Engaged most of the day
   in severe skirmishing and exposed to a heavy fire of artillery.
   After dark retired to this Ridge" — NOT the quiet-flank case the
   task's framing implied; this brigade's July 3 was materially more
   active than Mahone's entire battle.

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Tablet (fetch-verified this pass): "Killed 34 Wounded 179 Missing
  57 Total 270"** — the HIGHEST total of the three brigades this
  pass's batch covers (Mahone's 102, Posey's 83, Thomas's 270),
  directly contradicting any assumption that "quiet flank" brigades
  share a uniformly light casualty shape — recorded as a genuine,
  pass-level finding: skirmishing "most of the day" under artillery
  fire is a materially different (and here, costlier) activity class
  than either Mahone's stationary-under-shellfire posture or Posey's
  brief piecemeal advance.
