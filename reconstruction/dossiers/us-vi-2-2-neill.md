# Dossier: Neill's Brigade, 2nd Division, VI Corps (reg-us-vi-2-2)

Pass 14 (the VI Corps brigade batch, census item 3) · Cast status:
not-yet-cast · Achieved T-level: **T3 (tablet-primary; own-report
located but its Gettysburg-day content is a single terse paragraph
appended to a June-campaign narrative)** · 2026-07-14

Chain role: the corps HQ dossier's "attested-static majority" class,
but the LEAST static of the seven brigades this pass researches —
detached from the corps entirely on July 2, and moved to the extreme
right flank (cavalry-picket contact) on July 3.

## EC1 — Identity & command

- 3rd Brigade, 2nd Division, VI Corps: 7th Maine (6 companies), 43rd,
  49th, 77th New York, 61st Pennsylvania (or-27-oob; brigade tablet,
  gettysburg.stonesentinels.com, "3rd Brigade, 2nd Division, 6th
  Corps," fetch-verified this pass). **Brig. Gen. Thomas H. Neill**
  throughout; own report dated Headquarters Third Brig., Second Div.,
  Sixth Corps, Camp near Berlin, Md., July 17, 1863 (No. 231, OR 27/1
  pp. 678-680, civilwar.com transcription, fetch-verified this pass —
  PRIMARY, own-report, though its fetched pages are dominated by a
  June 9-12 narrative of Col. McIntosh's cavalry brigade cooperating
  with Neill's command in the immediate post-Gettysburg pursuit, not a
  Gettysburg-day battle narrative).

## EC2 — Engaged strength

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2, arrival, then DETACHED** [C, tablet-primary]: "Arrived
   after a march of 33 miles from Manchester Md. and about 6 P. M.
   was detached from the Corps and ordered by Major Gen. G. G. Meade
   to hold Powers Hill" (tablet, verbatim) — Powers Hill is the XII
   Corps/army reserve-artillery ground (already the ground of
   `us-xii-hq-slocum-williams.md` and `us-xii-arty-muhlenberg.md`,
   pass 8-9), giving Neill's brigade a genuinely different July 2-3
   role than the other six VI Corps brigades this pass covers: NOT
   corps reserve, but an army-level detachment to a named,
   already-dossiered piece of ground.
2. **July 3, the extreme right** [C, tablet-primary]: "The Brigade by
   order of Major Gen. Slocum crossed Rock Creek and took position on
   the extreme right of the Army making connection with the Cavalry
   pickets" (tablet, verbatim) — Slocum (XII Corps/right-wing
   commander) directly ordering a VI Corps brigade is itself notable;
   Rock Creek is the same watercourse Culp's Hill's defenders (Greene,
   the Johnson-division brigades) fought along, placing Neill's
   brigade on the army's far right flank alongside the cavalry
   picket line — a genuinely new geographic anchor for this corner of
   the field this pass's other dossiers do not cover.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The Powers Hill hold, July 2 night** [D]: no combat activity
   stated — an army-reserve/ground-security role, not a firing
   position.
2. **The Rock Creek right-flank connection, July 3** [D]: "making
   connection with the Cavalry pickets" (tablet, verbatim) — a
   screening/liaison role, not a documented firing episode; consistent
   with the brigade's small casualty total (EC6) relative to its
   distinctive movement.
3. **Neill's own report's actual narrative content** [C, report-
   primary, but NOT dated to Gettysburg's battle days]: the fetched
   pages (678-679) describe operations "assum[ing] command of Colonel
   McIntosh's cavalry brigade" with artillery support during the
   POST-Gettysburg pursuit (June 9-12 dating on the visible fetched
   text is itself likely a transcription/OCR artifact of a
   multi-letter bundle, same pattern as Grant's dossier this pass;
   the substantive content — a fight at "Zeigler's Mills, near
   Chewsville" where McIntosh's cavalry "drove them in full retreat
   across the Antietam, silencing their battery," 4 wounded 1
   mortally, 300-400 prisoners/deserters sent to Gen. Couch — belongs
   to the Maryland pursuit phase AFTER Gettysburg, not the July 1-3
   battle itself). Neill explicitly notes his detailed submission was
   delayed by "subsequent marching and battle formations with the
   Sixth Corps" — an author's own explanation for why a cleaner
   Gettysburg-day narrative was not filed separately.

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Brigade tablet: Killed 1 Officer + 1 Man, Wounded 11 Men,
  Captured or Missing 2 Men, Total 15** (gettysburg.stonesentinels.com,
  verbatim) — a genuine, if small, combat-casualty record (including
  an officer killed), distinguishing this brigade from the wholly
  attested-static ones this pass (Torbert's, Russell's) despite no
  documented firing episode (EC5.2) — the officer/enlisted killed
  figure most plausibly belongs to the Powers Hill/Rock Creek
  detachment duty (picket contact) rather than a set-piece action, but
  this pass does NOT adopt that attribution as a ruling — no source
  fetched states a cause.

## Conflicts & negative evidence

1. **Neill's own report's Gettysburg-day content is thin relative to
   its post-Gettysburg pursuit narrative** — recorded honestly (EC5.3),
   not treated as if a full battle-day account exists.
2. The officer-killed figure (EC6) has no attested cause in either
   source fetched this pass — an open gap, not silently attributed to
   combat drama it may not have had.
