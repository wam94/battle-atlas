## EC4 — Movement legs

1. July 1: the McPherson barn / railroad cut fight and the retreat to
   Cemetery Hill (July 1 record carried at register grain; the
   commissioned window here is July 2-3).
2. **PASS-5 (chamberlin-150pa-1895) — the static reading is
   CORRECTED for July 2 evening**: "About six p.m. the brigade was
   double-quicked to the left, down the Taneytown road" to the right
   of Third Corps during the Humphreys collapse [C ~18:00]; line
   formed in rear of Humphreys, bayonets fixed, the charge withheld;
   then the 149th + 150th advanced to the Emmitsburg road as
   skirmishers, RECOVERING TWO GUNS ("sent in two additional guns
   taken from the enemy, after a short and spirited engagement" —
   Doubleday's report quoted concurring), the 150th's right reaching
   the road "a little to the left of the Codori House"; the two
   regiments stood as pickets there overnight [D].
3. **July 3, 07:00-08:00** [C]: relieved from the picket line,
   returned to the lines under artillery fire (Company A of the 150th
   losing 2 k + 1 mw on the way in); the cannonade ridden out "back
   of the brow of the hill" — the j3-02/j3-03 drawn rear-line slots
   (EC3.2) are the POST-return states, consistent again from ~08:00.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **July 2 day** [E]: supporting batteries on Cemetery Hill, men
   passing ammunition for the short-handed artillery
   (chamberlin-150pa-1895) — an attested non-fire support activity.
2. **July 2 evening** [C ~18:00-dark]: the counterattack-support leg
   + the two-gun recovery skirmish (EC4.2) — the brigade's only
   post-July-1 fire window.
3. **July 3 dawn** [C]: the picket line shelled by two guns in an
   orchard left-front "at intervals until the pickets were relieved"
   (chamberlin-150pa-1895).
4. July 3 afternoon [E window]: under the cannonade behind the brow;
   no fire window (the front line's fight did not reach the third
   line; regimental history concurs — "spectators of the furious
   cannonade").
