## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2, the march to the extreme left** [D, report-primary]:
   Fisher's brigade marched "to the left of General Sykes' corps as
   the extreme left of the Army of the Potomac" and engaged the enemy
   July 2 (report, paraphrase).
2. **July 2, ~10 P.M., Big Round Top** [C ~22:00, report-primary,
   THE PASS'S CENTRAL FIND]: Fisher's own report states he discovered
   "Round Top" (Big Round Top) in his front, judged it "highly
   important that they should occupy it," and personally took two
   regiments (5th and 12th Pennsylvania Reserves) plus the 20th Maine
   (Col. Chamberlain, Vincent's brigade, already dossiered
   `us-v-1-3-vincent.md`) up the hill at 10 P.M., capturing "over 30
   prisoners" (report, verbatim/paraphrase this pass).
3. **The fortified line** [D]: "a stone wall across the entire ravine
   and up the hill" constructed for defense (report, verbatim) —
   matching the register's own note ("Big Round Top breastworks night
   July 2-July 3").
4. **July 3**: held the Big Round Top position through the close of
   the battle (register `daysPresent: [2,3]`; the fetched report pages
   do not separately narrate July 3 beyond the closing summary, EC5).
