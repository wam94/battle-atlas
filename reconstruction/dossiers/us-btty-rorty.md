# Dossier: Battery B, 1st New York Light Artillery (14th NY Battery attached) — Rorty's (reg-us-ii-b1)

Pass 1 (anchor-executors) · Cast status: in-build (`us-btty-rorty`) ·
Achieved T-level: **T4** · 2026-07-10

Chain role: CA-J3A-4/5 counterpoint — a II Corps battery that did NOT
slacken by order, fought the cannonade to wreckage, and met Kemper's front
with canister and handspikes.

## EC1 — Identity & command

- Battery B, 1st New York Light Artillery, with the 14th New York Battery
  attached; II Corps Artillery Brigade (Hazard) (or-27-oob).
- Capt. James McKay Rorty, **killed July 3** (see EC6 pin); Lt. Albert S.
  Sheldon **wounded July 3**; Lt. Robert E. Rogers succeeded (or-27-oob;
  stone-sentinels 1st NY Battery B page).
- Command context: Rorty had taken the battery only on July 1-2 (posted
  from ordnance duty) — first and last battle in command.
- Guns: **four 10-pounder Parrott rifles** (reduced from a larger
  complement by July 2 losses) (stone-sentinels, via 2026-06-13 OOB pass).

## EC2 — Engaged strength

- **114 men** brought to the field (stone-sentinels; single-source figure,
  flagged).

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2, evening — left of Gibbon's front** [E]: engaged in the
  twilight fight (guns served against Wright's advance; the battery lost
  heavily). No coordinate adopted this pass.
2. **July 3, morning–repulse — south end of Hazard's line** [E window;
  position documented]: (4435, 4570) facing 262°, ~250 m south of the
  Copse, in front of the prone 19th Massachusetts (bachelder-third-day-
  1876; waitt-1906 pp. 234-237: "Rorty's battery was in a position in
  front of the regiment"). Uncertainty ~40 m.
3. Attested-static July 3 (negative evidence: no movement narrated; the
  battery died in place — Waitt's account has guns wrecked successively in
  position).

## EC4 — Movement legs

- None on July 3 until the close: the wrecked battery was withdrawn
  "[t]oward the close of this struggle" with Arnold's and Cushing's, its
  place filled by fresh Reserve batteries (or-27-1-hunt, verified this
  pass); post-battle it was consolidated away (us-ii-arty-hazard EC4).
  July 2 posting leg not researched this pass (reference-frame layer).

## EC5 — Activity record

- **July 3, 13:07–~15:00 — under the cannonade, firing** [A window vs
  CA-J3A-1/5]: the battery took the bombardment at its worst — from the
  infantry eyewitness directly behind it: "Five horses and the drivers of
  the leading gun fell among the prostrate infantry men. Soon the third gun
  rolled helpless from its wheels. With but two guns left, heroic Rorty
  continued to fire. Then a caisson burst. Immediately his left piece was
  struck and shattered…" (waitt-1906 pp. 234-237, repo-verified
  2026-06-13). Sequence [D]: 4 guns → 3 → 2 → 1 inside the cannonade
  window — the most granular gun-attrition record on Hazard's line.
- **~15:16–15:30 — canister and the overrun** [D between CA-J3A-7 and
  CA-J3A-10]: the remaining gun(s) fired into Kemper's front; the position
  was briefly overrun — Rorty killed and ~9 men with him in hand-to-hand
  fighting, Sheldon wounded (stone-sentinels; waitt-1906). Guns recovered
  as Kemper's men were driven off [D] (build t=9000, reconstruction).
- Ammunition: Parrott long-range fire through the cannonade; canister at
  the close (rounds-expended figure not located in the corpus — see
  us-ii-arty-hazard for the brigade-level expenditure basis; flagged
  thin pending a battery-level OR return).

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- Battle totals: **10 killed, 16 wounded** (= 26) of 114 (stone-sentinels).
- Episode distribution [D]: concentrated July 3, in two spikes — the
  cannonade (the Waitt gun-by-gun sequence implies steady crew loss
  13:07–15:00) and the overrun (~10 dead in minutes at ~15:25). This is
  the inverse of most Union units' bombardment-light pattern and should
  shape any authored casualty curve (rising through the cannonade, spike
  at the melee).
- **July 2 share now primary**: "1 man killed, 8 men wounded, and 13
  horses disabled" (or-27-1-hazard, verbatim) — so ~9 of the 26 fell
  July 2; the July 3 remainder splits between the cannonade attrition
  and the overrun.
- Officer pins: Capt. Rorty **k** [D at the overrun/crisis window] —
  "had enjoyed his new position but one day, having assumed command …
  on July 2" (or-27-1-hazard); Lt. Sheldon **w** same action [D]; both
  confirmed at army grain in Hunt's July 3 officer-loss list
  (or-27-1-hunt).

## Conflicts & negative evidence

1. Strength (114) and battle-total casualties (10/16) remain
  monument-committee figures (inferred tier), but Hazard's verified
  report now corroborates the state ("entirely exhausted; its
  ammunition expended; its horses and men killed and disabled") and
  supplies the July 2 split; no battery OR return EXISTS — the
  civilwarcycling OR index lists none for any of the five II Corps
  batteries (verified negative, this pass).
2. No battery-level rounds-expended record (negative; Hazard gives the
  qualitative "ammunition expended" only).
2a. Gun-attrition variant: Stone Sentinels reads "bombardment disabled
  three cannons before the assault … Rorty personally worked the
  remaining gun and enlisted approximately twenty men from the 19th
  Massachusetts" — one gun left BEFORE the assault, vs Waitt's
  two-then-one sequence continuing into it; carried, and the 19th MA
  crew-relief detail joins Waitt's account as corroborated context.
3. The overrun is attested (Rorty's death in hand-to-hand fighting), but
  its depth (did Kemper's men cross the crest line?) is not narrated in
  the corpus — the build models the position held/retaken within ~5 min;
  reconstruction, flagged.
