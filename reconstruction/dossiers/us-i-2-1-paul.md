Pass 11 (residual sweep) · Cast status: in-build · Achieved

Robinson division's fight) and the seminary entrenchment's builders;
carries the division's most severe command cascade and the 16th
Maine's rear-guard sacrifice order verbatim.

## EC1 — Identity & command

- 1st Brigade, 2nd Division, I Corps: 16th Me, 13th Mass, 94th NY,
  104th NY, 107th Pa (+ 11th Pa transferred in with Coulter on
  Cemetery Hill) (or-27-oob; or-27-1-coulter).
- **THE CASCADE** [D]: Brig. Gen. Gabriel R. Paul "fell, severely
  wounded, while gallantly directing and encouraging his command"
  (or-27-1-robinson; shot through both eyes — the wound's nature is
  literature-grade, not in the OR text, flagged); command passed
  through Col. Leonard (13th Mass, w) and Col. Root (94th NY, w) to
  Col. Coulter (11th Pa) by Robinson's assignment on Cemetery Hill
  ("Just before we moved from this point, Colonel Coulter was
  assigned to the command of the First Brigade, taking his regiment
  with him", or-27-1-baxter); Coulter himself w July 3 ("But see"
  his report No. 44 second part) — the brigade had five commanders
  across the battle. Three of Paul's staff officers reported missing
  (or-27-1-coulter).

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. Reserve near the seminary [D]: "the First Brigade, under
   Brigadier-General Paul, was set at work to intrench the ridge"
   (or-27-1-robinson) — **the seminary rail-barricade the CSA final
   push later stormed** (Perrin: "a breastwork of rails behind
   [which] the enemy was posted" — both-sided across four hours,
   or-27-2-perrin).
2. The Oak Ridge relief positions [D]: "part of it in the position
   first occupied by Baxter's brigade, and the remaining battalions
   as a support to his second position" (or-27-1-robinson);
   Coulter's ~15:00 relief clock (or-27-1-coulter).
3. **The 16th Maine hill** [D]: "ordered, alone, by General Robinson,
   to take possession of a hill which commanded the road, and hold
   the same as long as there was a man left" (or-27-1-farnham-16me —
   the sacrifice order verbatim from the executing regiment); the
   fall-backs (hollow → woods → the failed retreat, "not in time to
   reach the main body").
4. Cemetery Hill; then the Baxter-parallel July 2-3 support ladder
   (the two brigades moved together under Robinson — the Baxter
   anchor set 5-6 applies at division grain).

3. July 2: battery support on Cemetery Hill; the dark gauntlet run
