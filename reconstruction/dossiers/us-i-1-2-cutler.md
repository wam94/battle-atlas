## EC1 — Identity & command

- 2nd Brigade, 1st Division, I Corps: 7th Indiana, 76th NY, 84th NY
  (14th Brooklyn), 95th NY, 147th NY, 56th Pa (or-27-oob). **Brig.
  Gen. Lysander Cutler** throughout.
- Officer pins [D]: Maj. Grover (76th NY) k "early in the action";
  Lt. Col. Miller (147th NY) w "at the moment of receiving" the
  fall-back order — the wounding that CAUSED the regiment's

- Detachment: **7th Indiana "on duty in the rear" July 1** — rejoined
  at Cemetery Hill that evening (EC2 detachment primary,
  or-27-1-cutler).

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **The opening line north of the pike** [B vs CA-J1M-4/5 + drawn]:
   across the railroad with 76th NY / 147th NY / 56th Pa, "engaged
   with a vastly superior force... in front and on my right flank";
   **95th NY + 14th Brooklyn detached left BY GENERAL REYNOLDS's
   order to support Hall** (or-27-1-cutler) — Reynolds's last
   tactical act in a brigade primary. Drawn: j1-03 "CUTLER'S Brig."
   (3173, 7397), "147 N.Y" bar (3170, 7528), "HALL 2'ME" (3260,
   7630); Cutler HQ marker mon-cutler-hq (3519.6, 7788.0); 84th NY
   markers (mon-84ny-rrcut).
2. The fall-back to the woods, the re-advance to "the crest of the
   ridge" for "half to three-quarters of an hour" [D].
3. **The changed front right** [C via or-27-1-cutler ~0]: "having no
   support on either my right or left until 2 o'clock, when a brigade
   from the Second Division formed on my right, and the Eleventh
   Corps came in on the right of them" — the Baxter/XI sync clock
   (CA-J1P-1's receiving preamble).
4. The railroad-embankment retreat line → through town → Cemetery
   Hill → the hill on the right (Culp's) [D]: the 7th Ind sent by
   Wadsworth "to hold the crest of a hill to the right" — **the
   Culp's Hill first-occupation pin** (both-sided with the pass-8/9
   Greene frame).
5. July 2-3 [E]: "between the First Brigade and General Greene's
   brigade of the Twelfth Corps... mostly in the trenches"
   (or-27-1-cutler).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. March: "moved from camp early... being the leading brigade of the
   corps"; obliqued left across the fields to the seminary ridge
   [D].
2. The withdrawal by Wadsworth's order "to the woods on the next
   ridge"; the 147th's stranded stand (order undelivered) [D].
3. The 2-o'clock change of front to the right; the flank push
   through the woods onto the column attacking Robinson [D].
4. **The embankment retreat** [D, formation-class]: "I moved off on
   the railroad embankment, and, although exposed to the enemy's
   fire on both flanks, the men marched with perfect steadiness and
   no excitement. Their steadiness had the effect to bring the enemy
   to a halt" (or-27-1-cutler).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The opening fight** [C 10:00→]: two-line frontal+flank assault
   received at short range (Davis's 42nd Miss / 2nd Miss / 55th NC —
   both-sided); the 147th NY holding after the others fell back
   "until the enemy were in possession of the railroad cut on his
   left" — released by the 6th Wis / 95th NY / 14th Brooklyn charge
   (or-27-1-cutler; or-27-1-dawes-6wi; or-27-2-davis-jr).
2. **The afternoon flank volley** [D]: "came in on their flank, and
   opened so hot a fire on them that one regiment threw down their
   arms and surrendered" (or-27-1-cutler) — matching the
   Iverson-front capture mass from the Union side (Cutler was on
   Robinson's left as Iverson's line died at the wall; the
   surrendered regiment unnamed — carried, not identified).
3. The ammunition exhaustion and relief; the twenty-minute wait at
   the railroad; the three-regiment detachment to the seminary [D].
4. Sgt. Hubbard's 18-man provost guard fighting in the line, 12 lost
   (or-27-1-cutler — a sub-unit accounting rarity).
