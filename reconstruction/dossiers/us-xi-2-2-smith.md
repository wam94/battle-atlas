  "Second Brigade, Second Division, Eleventh Army Corps," matching

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **The approach march, June 12 – July 1** [C, report-primary]:
   departed Brooke's Station June 12; through Hartwood Church,
   Weaverville, Blackburn's Ford, Centreville (June 12-17); Goose
   Creek picket/reconnaissance duty (June 17-24); Edwards Ferry,
   Jefferson, Middletown, Boonsborough Gap, Frederick (June 24-29);
   Emmitsburg (June 29 – July 1). Smith notes the brigade's shoes
   "began to fail, thus leaving many men to march barefooted," and
   that it covered 38 miles from Boonsborough Gap to Emmitsburg in
   "twenty-four and one-half hours," with "scarcely an instance of
   straggling" (report, verbatim).
2. **July 1, ~2 P.M. arrival, Cemetery Hill** [C ~14:00, report-
   primary + tablet]: "brought up the rear of the entire corps;
   consequently it was the last to arrive at the scene of action,
   which had been commenced earlier in the day by the First Corps"
   (report, verbatim), operating under Steinwehr's orders — the
   brigade formed "in line of battle by battalions in mass in rear of
   Cemetery Hill" then moved "through the front of the hill overlooking
   the town," passing retreating Union forces (report, paraphrase).
   Went into position "in line behind stone walls along Emmitsburg and
   Taneytown Roads facing northwest, supporting Battery I, 1st New
   York" (tablet, verbatim).
3. **Regimental deployment along the wall** [E]: 55th Ohio on the
   extreme right; 73rd Ohio center; 136th New York left, "connecting
   with Second Corps"; 33rd Massachusetts on the northeast side of
   Cemetery Hill (report, paraphrase).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **July 1-3, continuous skirmishing** [D]: "sharpshooting was kept
   up all day by the Union troops from stone walls and by the
   Confederates from houses in the town with considerable loss"
   (tablet, verbatim, July 2); "sharp skirmishing continued with
   artillery firing from Confederate batteries east of the town"
   (tablet, verbatim, July 3).
2. **The night of July 2** [C, report-primary]: "the corps' right wing"
   faced a strong attack, but "no direct assault" fell on this brigade
   specifically (report, paraphrase) — consistent with the East
   Cemetery Hill assault landing on Harris's/von Gilsa's/Ames's front
   (already dossiered), not Smith's own position.
3. **July 3, the cannonade and Pickett's Charge** [D]: "during the
   final assault, enemy direction initially suggested an attack on the
   brigade, but a directional change concentrated the assault on the
   Second Corps" (report, paraphrase) — Smith's brigade held its
   Emmitsburg/Taneytown Road wall position throughout without being
   directly struck.
4. **Discipline under fire, verbatim [D]**: "never a man faltered"
   despite exposure to fire from multiple directions (report,
   paraphrase/verbatim blend).

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Tablet total: Killed 51 men, Wounded 5 officers + 273 men,
  Captured/Missing 2 officers + 17 men, Total 348** (gettysburg.
  stonesentinels.com, verbatim).
- **Smith's own report total: "Killed, 62 enlisted men, 4 officers;
  Wounded, 257 enlisted men, 2 officers; Missing, 20 enlisted men, 6
  officers. Total, 345"** (report, verbatim, fetch-verified this
  pass) — a REPORT-VS-TABLET CONFLICT: the two sources disagree on
  every single category (killed 51 vs 66, wounded 278 vs 259, missing
  19 vs 26) though the grand totals are close (348 vs 345). Recorded
  as a genuine, first-class report-vs-tablet casualty conflict per
  this corpus's standing discipline (ED-71/ED-74 class) — NOT
  adjudicated this pass; both readings preserved.
