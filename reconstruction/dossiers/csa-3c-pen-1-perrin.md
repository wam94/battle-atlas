## EC3 — Position anchors

1. March order [C via or-27-2-perrin ~0]: "About 8 o'clock… commenced
   the march, on the turnpike leading to Gettysburg, at the head of
   the division, and just in the rear of the division of
   Major-General Heth" — pairs Hill's "at 5 a.m., Heth took up the
   line of march" (or-27-2-hill) as the column's second element.
2. The deployment [D]: filed off ~3 miles out, line formed leaving
   room "between my left and the Gettysburg road for General Scales'
   brigade", skirmishers right (Haskell) — the division's first line
   right of the pike; Lane took the outer right (cavalry watch).
3. The two supporting bounds [C ~0]: ~1 mile forward behind Heth;
   "until about 3 o'clock" the second bound ~½ mile; "until after 4
   o'clock" the assault order from Pender in person.
4. **The assault axis** [D + drawn]: the ravine re-form; past
   Pettigrew's exhausted line; the hill ascent under "a furious storm
   of musketry and shells"; **the last fence ~200 yards from the
   grove at the theological college**; the 1st SC's right-oblique
   around the rail breastwork (Paul's seminary entrenchment,
   both-sided) + left flank attack; the 12th/13th right-oblique vs
   **the stone fence to the right of the college** (Gamble's
   dismounted line — the j1-13 drawn "GAMBLE" bar, both-sided at
   last); pursuit into the town.
5. Night July 1 → July 2-3: the brigade's later arc rides the pass-3
   division records (Thomas/Lane/Lowrance dossiers; Perrin's July 2-3
   detail is thin in his own report — window-level [E]).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The seminary assault** [C after-16:00 + D]: "While crossing the
   last fence… the brigade received the most destructive fire of
   musketry I have ever been exposed to"; the 14th SC staggered
   ("It looked to us as though this regiment was entirely
   destroyed"); **"General Scales' brigade had halted… General
   Lane's brigade did not move upon my right at all"** — the
   unsupported-center record (both both-sided: Scales's own halt at
   the fence, or-27-2-scales; Lane's cavalry-front delay,
   or-27-2-lane-jh).
2. The breakthrough: the 1st SC flank attack routing the barricade;
   "at least thirty pieces" of Union artillery limbering to the rear
   (Wainwright's line, receiving side us-i-arty-wainwright); the
   stone-wall dislodgment; "capturing hundreds of prisoners, two
   field-pieces, and a number of caissons" (pairs Hill's corps
   ledger "2 pieces of artillery and 2,300 prisoners").
3. Under-fire windows: the approach bounds under artillery; the
   assault's converging musketry + the enfilade from the left
   batteries [D].
4. Corps context [C ~14:30 via or-27-2-hill]: "About 2.30 o'clock,
   the right wing of Ewell's corps made its appearance on my left…
   Pender's division was then ordered forward" — the junction that
   staged the final push; and the halt decision after ("prudence led
   me to be content with what had been gained") — the CSA night
   stop at corps grain.
