# Dossier: Lane's Brigade, Pender's Division (reg-csa-3c-pen-2)

Pass 3 (first command batch) · Cast status: in-build (`csa-lane`) ·
Achieved T-level: **T4 full-arc** · 2026-07-11

Command-batch role: the third line's left — the brigade whose left was "in
the air" (no second line behind Heth's left, Lane's own structural
statement) and whose commander held the division twice in the battle. The
loss record carries the batch's sharpest return-vs-report conflict (389 vs
660, ED-47 candidate).

## EC1 — Identity & command

- Brig. Gen. James H. Lane; brigade under **Col. C. M. Avery (33d NC)
  during the intervals Lane commanded the division** (July 2 evening –
  morning July 3, and again after Trimble fell) (or-27-2-lane-jh;
  or-27-2-engelhard; or-27-oob).
- Regiments: 7th, 18th, 28th, 33d, 37th North Carolina; July 1 order
  7th-37th-28th-18th-33d, right on the road (or-27-2-lane-jh).
- Officer pins: the two left regimental commanders in the assault —
  Cols. Avery and Barry — brought their regiments back "under direct
  orders" after the line broke (or-27-2-engelhard); no field-officer
  casualty catalogue survives for July 3 (open).

## EC2 — Engaged strength

- **PRIMARY: "an effective total of 1,355, including ambulance corps and
  rear guard"** (or-27-2-lane-jh) — the build's 1,355 matches. Loss "on
  the 1st and 2nd being but slight" makes this a serviceable July 3
  step-off basis (unlike the July-1-scope figures elsewhere in the wing).

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. July 1 endpoint: the McMillan orchard/stone fence, ordered back a few
   yards for shelter from Cemetery Hill fire (or-27-2-lane-jh).
2. **July 2** [D]: same ground; 33d + 18th sheltered "to the left of
   Lieutenant-Colonel Garnett's battalion of artillery"
   (or-27-2-lane-jh — a direct frame link to csa-3c-bn-garnett.md);
   skirmishers took the road in front that afternoon.
3. **July 3 morning** [D]: moved right by Hill's order, formed "in rear
   of the right of Heth's division" with Lowrance on the right, behind
   the Poague/Pegram gun line (or-27-2-lane-jh; or-27-2-lowrance);
   sheet read: "LANE. N.C." with second-line bars at ~local (3050, 5750)
   ±120 on j3-02 — the PRE-move state (08:00–11:00 window brackets the
   move; drawn-state note).
4. Farthest advance: "to within a few yards of the stone wall"
   (or-27-2-lane-jh) on the Bryan-barn front; reform point in rear of
   the artillery (or-27-2-engelhard).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. July 3 morning: division second line (left sector) → rear of Heth's
   right [D; ~1.2 km lateral, B: 15–20 min at route pace].
2. **The advance** [A vs CA-J3A-6 via the division trigger]: "through
   the woods in which our batteries were planted, and through an open
   field about a mile"; the left uncovered and checked (Trimble's
   order); "As soon as Pettigrew's command gave back, Lowrance's brigade
   and my own, without ever having halted, took position on the left of
   the troops which were still contesting the ground"
   (or-27-2-lane-jh) — the third line PASSED THROUGH the collapsing
   front without halting.
3. The withdrawal: forced by the left-flank infantry column ("enfiladed
   my whole line"), "the troops on my right having already done so";
   reformed immediately in rear of the artillery (or-27-2-lane-jh).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **Bombardment** [A window]: "the two hours' exposure"
   (or-27-2-engelhard); no brigade figure (open).
2. **The fire window** [D, climax]: fire reserved "until within good
   range ... then opened with telling effect, repeatedly driving the
   cannoneers from their pieces, completely silencing the guns in our
   immediate front, and breaking the line of infantry which was formed
   on the crest of the hill" (or-27-2-lane-jh) — the claim that the
   third line silenced the guns on its front reads against the
   Woodruff/Arnold wreckage records (both batteries WERE effectively
   out by the climax — pass-1 dossiers corroborate the observation's
   substance).
3. Received: "a heavy raking artillery fire from the right" (the II
   Corps/McGilvery arcs) and the left-flank musketry column (the 8th
   Ohio front, pass-2 frame) (or-27-2-lane-jh).

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **The conflict (ED-47 candidate)**: the return prints 41 k / 348 w /
  — = **389** with its own footnote "+General Lane reports his entire
  loss at 660" (or-27-2-anv-return p. 344); Lane: "Our great loss tells
  but too sadly ... **660 out of an effective total of 1,355** ... our
  loss on the 1st and 2nd being but slight" (or-27-2-lane-jh). The
  ~271 delta is the brigade's captured/missing, absent from the return's
  k+w scope — the two figures are different MEASURES, not competing
  counts.
- Regiment k+w (return): 7th NC 89, 18th NC 45, 28th NC 104, 33d NC 63,
  37th NC 88.
- Episode split: July 1–2 "but slight"; the mass falls July 3, shaped
  by the fire narrative — moderate on the mile approach, spike at the
  crest fire window and the flank collapse [D].
- Campaign postscript: total trans-Potomac loss 731 incl. Falling
  Waters/stragglers (or-27-2-lane-jh).
