## EC1 — Identity & command

- Artillery Brigade, II Corps; Capt. John G. Hazard, 1st Rhode Island
  Light Artillery (report dated Aug. 1, 1863, to Lt. Col. Morgan, II
  Corps chief of staff) (or-27-1-hazard).
- Batteries: Rorty's B/1st NY (+14th NY attached), Arnold's A/1st RI,
  Brown's B/1st RI (Perrin July 3), Cushing's A/4th US, Woodruff's
  I/1st US (or-27-oob; or-27-1-hazard). Individual dossiers:
  us-btty-rorty / -arnold / -brown / us-btty-cushing (T5 Angle cast) /
  -woodruff.
- **Commander attrition, the worst of any Union artillery brigade**
  (or-27-1-hazard, all verbatim): Rorty k ("had enjoyed his new
  position but one day, having assumed command … on July 2"); Cushing
  "fell on July 3, mortally wounded by a musket-shot" (k); Woodruff mw
  "expired on July 4, and, at his own request, was buried on the
  field"; Milne "musket-shot through the lungs … breathed his last at
  Gettysburg on July 10"; Sheldon w; Brown w July 2 ("severely wounded
  in the neck by a musket-shot"); Lt. Canby (A/4th US) w July 2 in the
  hand; of Cushing's battery: "Every officer in this battery was
  either killed or wounded." Field promotion: 1st Sgt. Frederick Fuger
  "obliged to assume command of the company … most earnestly
  recommended for promotion."
- Command conflict: fired through the cannonade while Hunt's policy
  was economy — but with nuance now verbatim: see EC5.1
  (us-arty-hq-hunt EC1; hunt-bl-3 "under the orders of their corps
  commander").

## EC3 — Position anchors

- **July 2 line (Hazard's own layout, verbatim)** [E]: "On the right,
  in a grove, Light Company I [six light 12-pounders]; 150 yards to
  the left, Battery A, First Rhode Island Light Artillery, and Battery
  A, Fourth U. S. Artillery [both six 3-inch batteries]. Upon their
  left was placed Battery B, First Rhode Island Light Artillery [six
  light 12-pounders], while to the extreme left, and operating with
  the First Division of the corps, was placed Battery B, First New
  York Artillery [four 10-pounder Parrotts]" (or-27-1-hazard) — the
  150-yard grove-to-Arnold relation is a primary spacing datum for the
  reference-frame pass.
- July 3 build coordinates per battery (documented, Bachelder):
  Woodruff (4570, 5095) · Arnold (4425, 4955) · Cushing (4398, 4880) ·
  Brown (4445, 4785; withdrawn) · Rorty (4435, 4570). Frontage ~530 m
  = the convergence target square.

## EC4 — Movement legs

- July 2: the two left batteries conformed to the infantry's movement
  and fought back to the crest ("At 6 p. m. the enemy advanced in
  force, and, after a sharp contest, our lines were pushed back
  several hundred yards, the two batteries on the left … conforming
  their movement to that of the infantry. Upon gaining a more
  commanding position upon the crest of the hill, a rapid fire was
  opened … causing great slaughter", or-27-1-hazard) [C stated 18:00].
- July 3: static except Brown's withdrawal (battery dossier), the
  close-of-action withdrawals (Rorty's, Arnold's, Cushing's — Hunt OR
  "toward the close"), and the post-repulse consolidation: "it was
  found necessary to consolidate Light Company I …, Battery A, Fourth
  U. S. …, and Batteries A and B, First Rhode Island …, thus reducing
  the five batteries that entered the fight to three"
  (or-27-1-hazard).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **July 3, ~08:00 — the morning exchanges** [C]: "quiet until about
  8 o'clock, when the enemy suddenly opened fire upon our position,
  exploding three limbers of Battery A, Fourth U. S. …" and
  "Light Company I … during the forenoon had eight separate
  engagements with the enemy" (or-27-1-hazard) — the brigade's day
  did not start at 13:07.
2. **13:07–~14:22 — the cannonade, with the reply nuance verbatim**
  [A start vs CA-J3A-1; duration C]: "At 1 p. m. the artillery of the
  enemy opened along the whole line, and for an hour and a quarter we
  were subjected to a very warm artillery fire. **The batteries did
  not at first reply, till the fire of the enemy becoming too
  terrible, they returned it till all their ammunition, excepting
  canister, had been expended; they then waited for the anticipated
  infantry attack**" (or-27-1-hazard). The nuance matters for
  CA-J3A-4: the II Corps line DID withhold initially (per Hunt's
  standing instruction), then burned its long-range ammunition — the
  Hunt/Hancock conflict is visible inside Hazard's own sentence.
3. **Attrition in place** [D]: Rorty's battery "entirely exhausted;
  its ammunition expended; its horses and men killed and disabled"
  with Rorty k and Sheldon w; "The other batteries were in similar
  condition; still, they bided the attack" (or-27-1-hazard).
4. **The assault — the withhold and the canister** [D between
  CA-J3A-7 and CA-J3A-10, verbatim]: "The rebel lines advanced slowly
  but surely; **half the valley had been passed over by them before
  the guns dared expend a round of the precious ammunition
  remaining** … when within deadly range, canister was thrown with
  terrible effect into their ranks. Battery A, First Rhode Island …
  had expended every round, and the lines of the enemy still
  advanced. Cushing was killed; Milne had fallen … their battery was
  exhausted … the two batteries were taken away; but Woodruff still
  remained in the grove, and poured death and destruction into the
  rebel lines" (or-27-1-hazard).
5. Rounds expended: no numbers in the report (verified negative);
  the qualitative record ("all their ammunition, excepting canister";
  "expended every round") is the brigade's material arc.

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- Brigade totals, two verified readings carried: "28 men killed, 119
  wounded, and three missing" (Iron Brigader text of the report's
  return note) vs the brigade tablet "Killed 3 Officers 24 Men
  Wounded 5 Officers 114 Men Captured or Missing 3 Men Total 149"
  (stone-sentinels) — consistent within officer/man accounting; the
  report itself defers to the revised statement p. 177 (unfetched —
  open). "Of whom one-third were either killed or wounded"
  (or-27-1-hazard) against ~600 engaged.
- July 2 split now verbatim: Brown's B "1 man killed, 7 men wounded,
  and 2 missing … 24 horses were killed and 6 disabled, and it became
  necessary to send two guns to the rear"; Rorty's "1 man killed, 8
  men wounded, and 13 horses disabled" (or-27-1-hazard) — the July 2
  vs July 3 apportionment for two of five batteries is primary.
- Episode shape [D]: morning exchanges (limber explosions) →
  continuous cannonade attrition → the repulse melee spikes. The
  brigade's *flat-then-spike* shape vs the McGilvery/Osborn
  *silent-then-fire* shape stands (ED-34).
- Officer pins: EC1 list, all verbatim-grounded; commendation
  negatives (Arnold unwounded, "gallantly fighting his own battery
  and saving it, also withdrew the battery of A, Fourth U. S. …,
  Cushing and Milne having fallen").
