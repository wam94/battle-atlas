# Dossier: Artillery Brigade, II Corps — Hazard's (reg-us-ii-arty)

Pass 1 (anchor-executors) · Cast status: not-yet-cast as a command row (all
five batteries in-build individually) · Achieved T-level: **T4 (brigade
grain)** · 2026-07-10

Chain role: **CA-J3A-4's counterpoint** — the one Union artillery line that
did NOT slacken by policy. Its continued fire under Hancock's counter-order,
and the long-range-ammunition exhaustion that resulted, shape both the
slackening anchor's sectoral reading and the canister-only fight at the
walls.

  (or-27-1-hazard, Iron Brigader reproduction, verified 2026-06-13).

  (see us-arty-hq-hunt EC1/EC5.3; 2026-06-13 library "mixed" ruling).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **13:07–~15:00 — fought the cannonade throughout** [A window vs
  CA-J3A-1/5]: under Hancock's order the line returned fire continuously
  — the corps commander's own report frames the duration: "After an hour
  and forty-five minutes, the fire of the enemy became less furious"
  (or-27-1-hancock, fetch-verified 2026-07-10). Consequence, documented
  at brigade grain: **long-range ammunition effectively exhausted by the
  cannonade's end** (or-27-1-hazard) — the material fact behind ED-16's
  wrecked-battery staging and the canister-only repulse.
2. **Attrition in place** [D within the window]: Rorty's 4→1 gun
  sequence (waitt-1906); Cushing's battery wrecked to two serviceable
  guns run to the wall (T5 corpus, ED-16); Brown withdrawn (~14:40).
3. **~15:16–15:30 — canister** [D between CA-J3A-7 and CA-J3A-10]:
  every surviving gun met the assault with canister (double/treble at
  the close — fuger-jmsi-1907 for Cushing's sector; claim-arnold-
  canister; the overrun at Rorty's).
