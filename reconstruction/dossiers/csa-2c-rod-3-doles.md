Chain role: Rodes's left-flank brigade in the plain — the CA-J1P-1
attack's left hinge, the Barlow Knoll fight's second axis (with
Gordon, CA-J1P-3), the 157th NY destruction's CSA side, and a July 2

## EC1 — Identity & command

- Doles's (Georgia) brigade, Rodes's division, Second Corps: 4th, 12th,
  21st, 44th Georgia (or-27-oob; or-27-2-doles). **Brig. Gen. George
  Doles** throughout — the one Rodes brigade whose commander was
  neither killed, wounded, nor superseded.
- Regimental commanders in the report's credit list: Willis + Hardeman
  (12th), Mercer + Hooper + Glover (21st), W. H. Willis (4th), Peebles
  (44th) (or-27-2-doles).
- Officer pins [D]: Lt. Col. Winn (4th Ga) k July 1; Lt. Col. Lumpkin
  (44th Ga) severely w July 1, leg since amputated — both "while
  gallantly leading their respective regiments in a charge"
  (or-27-2-doles).

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **The formation** [C via or-27-2-doles ~0]: "formed into line of
   battle about 1 p.m. July 1, in front of Gettysburg… We occupied the
   left of Major-General Rodes' division" — in the plain per Ewell
   ("Doles on the left, in the plain", or-27-2-ewell), the 5th Ala
   guarding the wide gap to O'Neal on the ridge.
2. **The skirmisher hill** [C ~0]: cavalry pickets driven, "we occupied
   his position (a hill to our left), about 3.30 p.m." — wait, the
   clock reads the RE-occupation after the first dislodgment; see
   conflicts (the report's one sequence ambiguity).
3. The knoll attack line [D]: moved by the left flank to meet the
   left/rear threat; Gordon's conjunction on his left
   (or-27-2-doles = or-27-2-gordon both-sided); the rock-fence
   position carried.
4. The change of front vs the right-flank force (the 157th NY episode)
   [D]; pursuit "across the plain in front of Gettysburg."
5. The through-town halt [D]: "as far as the outer edge of town…
   form line of battle in the street running east and west through
   the town." Night July 1-2 in that line.
6. **July 2 evening** [C ~0]: "about 8 p.m. July 2… advancing toward
   the enemy's position on Cemetery Hill… within 100 yards of the
   enemy's line", then back to "a dirt road some 300 yards to the
   rear" — held until **1 a.m. July 4** [C ~0], then the college
   heights.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. July 1 skirmisher fight for the hill; the main attack with Gordon
   ("driven from behind a rock fence, with heavy loss… a large number
   of prisoners"); "We suffered severely from the enemy's batteries
   and musketry in this attack" — under-fire window [D].
2. **The 157th NY destruction, CSA side** [D]: "a strong force…
   appeared on my right flank and rear. We changed our front… attacked
   and routed him, pursuing him across the plain… But few of this
   force escaped us" — pairs the Union return's 157th NY 307-of-~409
   row (or-27-1-union-return p. 183) and Schurz's division record.
3. **The friendly-fire appendix (first-class)**: "my line was
   subjected to and did receive a severe fire from one of our own
   batteries… a two-gun battery (brass pieces) stationed on the side
   of the hill where General Rodes' headquarters were at the opening
   of the engagement" — losses "several men killed and wounded"
   (or-27-2-doles, appendix of July 19).
4. July 2-4: "actively engaged in heavy skirmishing during July 2, 3,
   and 4" [E]; the July 2 evening probe (above); one flag captured by
   the 12th Ga, "We lost no colors."
5. Ewell's corps-grain companion [D]: the O'Neal-gap counterattack
   repulsed by Doles "changing front with his two right regiments"
   (or-27-2-ewell) — the corps report singles the maneuver out.

## Conflicts & negative evidence

1. **The 3.30 clock's referent**: the report reads "After a short
   engagement, he was driven from his position, when we occupied his
   position (a hill to our left), about 3.30 p.m." — but the next
   sentence has the enemy re-taking the hill and the main attack
   following (with Gordon, whose own clock is "About 3 p.m."). Either
   the 3.30 stamps the FIRST occupation (making the main knoll attack
   ~3.30-4.00, inside CA-J1P-3's late half) or the narrative is
   compressed. Carried as recorded; CA-J1P-3's 15:00-15:30 adoption
   is not moved (Gordon=Schurz two-primary controls).
2. Doles's "about 1 p.m." formation sits between the Rodes tablet's
   "about noon" Oak Ridge arrival and CA-J1P-1's 14:00 attack — a
   consistent intermediate, recorded as part of the 13:00-14:00
   pre-attack cluster (see the pass-11 ED-69 candidate).
3. No July 3 content beyond "heavy skirmishing" — attested-thin, the
   brigade's July 3 is window-level only [E].
