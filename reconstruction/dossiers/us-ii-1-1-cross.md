Wheatfield (CA-J2A-8, wave 3 of the occupation sequence) — first in,
first out of ammunition, and the wave's first commander death.

## EC1 — Identity & command

- 1st Brigade, 1st Division, II Corps: 5th NH, 61st NY, 81st PA,
  148th PA. Col. Edward E. Cross → Col. H. Boyd McKeen (81st PA).
- **Officer pin: Cross mw "early in this engagement"** [D, bracket
  ~18:15-18:30 = wave-3 opening] — Caldwell: "Brigadier-General Zook

- Episode split [D, strong]: overwhelmingly wave 3 (~18:15-18:45

## Conflicts & negative evidence

1. Report-vs-return composition drift (59/259/12 vs 57/260/13; total
  identical 330) — carried, return adopted per ED-52.
2. The brigade advanced with NO time stated in its own report; the
  division's clocks (Kelly's "about 5 p.m.", the tablet's "between 5
  and 6 P.M.") run ~+60-70 early against CA-J2A-8 (18:00-18:15) —
  the Union side of the southern-field early-clock class (ED-58
  candidate, pass-6 report §5).
3. Cross's exact fall position is tradition-fixed (5th NH ground),
  not stated in the OR reports (negative) — the pin is [D] +
  monument-check class, not document-fixed.
