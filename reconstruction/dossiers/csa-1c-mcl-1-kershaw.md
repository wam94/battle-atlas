## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **The countermarch + flank approach** [D]: rode with the division's
  concealed circuitous march (the Lee map-order route,
  mclaws-shsp7-1878; "a good deal of delay on the march, which was
  quite circuitous", oates-1905) — the leg whose physics (3-4 miles
  of column at ~2.5 mph with the countermarch reversal) explains the
  15:00-15:30 arrival the reports state.
2. **The step-off** [trigger, first-class]: **Cabell's guns paused
  and fired THREE GUNS IN RAPID SUCCESSION as the signal**; the men
  leaped the wall and were aligned by company officers
  (alexander-1907 quoting Kershaw's B&L) — the batch's cleanest
  step-off trigger record. Longstreet accompanied Kershaw on foot to
  the Emmitsburg road (same source).
3. **The advance to the stony hill** [B: ~900 m with obstacle
  catalogue — "numerous fences ... the stone building and barn, and
  the morass, and a raking fire of grape and canister" ≈ 15-20 min]:
  center on the stony hill, 2nd SC ordered against the battery, 8th
  SC + James's battalion left toward the orchard flank
  (or-27-2-kershaw).
4. **The Barksdale interval** [D, first-person]: "When we were about
  the Emmitsburg road I heard Barksdale's drums beat the assembly and
  knew then that I should have no immediate support on my left"
  (Kershaw B&L via alexander-1907) — the ladder's third-to-fourth
  interval attested from inside; Alexander times it "at least 20
  minutes".
5. **The Wheatfield oscillation** [D]: right wing refused under
  Bland; Semmes brought up; the fall-back to the stone wall 200 yd
  right-rear when the flanks doubled; the Wofford-led resweep "up to
  the foot of the mountain" (or-27-2-kershaw).
6. **Withdrawal near nightfall** [D] to the Rose ground → Peach
  Orchard after dark (report).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **Under the counter-bombardment before the advance** [C]: the
  deployment took casualties in line (division record,
  mclaws-shsp7-1878 — limbs cut from trees, men killed in formation).
2. **The stony hill fight ~17:30-18:30** [D bracket]: "the fighting
  was general and desperate" at 30 paces; the two-line Union
  counterattack (Caldwell) lapping both flanks (or-27-2-kershaw).
3. **The resweep and the mountain's foot** [D]: with Wofford until
  the recall.
4. **July 3** [E]: skirmishing at the Peach Orchard through noon;
  supported artillery there (the ED-36/Cabell frame's infantry
  anchor); no assault participation (division tablet: "not engaged"
  July 3 beyond skirmishing).
