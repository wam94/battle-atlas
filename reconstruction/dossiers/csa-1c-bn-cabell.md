Frame role: the Peach Orchard arc's fourth battalion (with Alexander,
Eshleman, Henry) and Dearing's attested left-rear neighbor. Verified
layer: Cabell's OR report (or-27-2-cabell, pp. 374-378), battalion

## EC1 — Identity & command

- Cabell's Battalion, McLaws's Division artillery; Col. Henry C.
  Cabell. Batteries: Manly's A/1st NC, Fraser's Pulaski (GA),
  McCarthy's 1st Richmond Howitzers, Carlton's Troup (GA)
  (or-27-oob; tablet).
- Armament (tablet): four Napoleons, four 10-pdr Parrotts, six 3-inch
  rifles, two 12-pdr howitzers = 16 guns.
- Officer pins: **Capt. Fraser "dangerously wounded by the bursting
  of a shell"** (which killed two sergeants and a man) **July 2**;
  Lt. R. H. Couper w July 2; **Capt. Carlton w July 3** during the
  cannonade; Lt. Henry Jennings w July 3 (or-27-2-cabell).

## EC2 — Engaged strength

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2 — the Peach Orchard duel line** [C]: "on the edge of the
  wood, the right resting near the road leading from Gettysburg to
  Emmitsburg", facing the Union guns across "600-700 yards" of open
  ground at the peach orchard (or-27-2-cabell) — the 500-yard
  Alexander relation's southern extension (csa-1c-bn-alexander.md
  EC3.2).
2. **July 3 — consolidated, north along the road** [C/D]: Fraser's
  depleted command consolidated; "six guns near a brick house" on
  the Gettysburg-Emmitsburg road; Carlton's battery + a McCarthy
  section "slightly en echelon" BEFORE Pickett's division
  (or-27-2-cabell) — the before-Pickett relation matching Dearing's
  "on my left and rear was Colonel Cabell." The brick house is the
  Codori-area landmark class; radius ~100 m pending a named-house
  adjudication (flagged).
3. **July 3 morning forward probe** [C]: McCarthy "advanced 300-400
  yards ahead of skirmishers that morning, firing twenty rounds"
  before the consolidation (or-27-2-cabell) — a clocked-by-sequence
  pre-cannonade advance with a round count.
4. **Post-assault forward line** [C ~16:00 stated]: McCarthy's and
  Motes's guns forward "immediately on the road", sections under
  Anderson/Payne/Furlong; ground "about 700 yards from the Cemetery
  Hill" held on orders "till night" (or-27-2-cabell) — with the
  j3-03 "McCARTY" label at ~(3500,4670) (read 2026-07-11) the
  battalion's forward state is drawn on the sheet.
5. **Withdrawal** [D]: after dark July 3; then the retreat ("a most
  distressing march" to Monterey Springs).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **July 3 pre-dawn/morning** [D]: Peach Orchard line → the
  consolidated brick-house line (~800-1,200 m north along the road).
2. **July 3, post-repulse** [C stated ~16:00]: the forward move onto
  the road; one Furlong gun to the rear (ammunition out), the other
  "about 300 yards on the left of his previous position."
3. **Night** [D]: withdrawal after dark.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **July 2** [C]: the Peach Orchard duel — "most effective fire",
  losses "very heavy" (or-27-2-cabell).
2. **July 3 cannonade** [C +7]: "about 1 p. m. ... For over two hours
  the cannonading on both sides was almost continuous and incessant,
  far, very far, exceeding any cannonading I have ever before
  witnessed" (or-27-2-cabell) — a clean +7-class CSA clock, the
  chain-agreeing counterweight to Longstreet's −53.
3. **Post-assault covering fire** [C]: the road-line guns holding to
  night.
4. **ROUNDS EXPENDED (tablet): "expended about 3300 rounds"**
  July 2-3 (stone-sentinels tablet extract) — [B basis: ~3,300 ÷ 16
  guns ≈ 206 rds/gun over two engaged days; against the July 2
  duel's 2-3 hours + the July 3 cannonade's ~2 hours, a sustained
  rate of ~0.8-1.2 rds/gun/min — inside the deliberate-fire class
  Dearing states]. The report itself gives no total (tablet-only
  figure; hop = War Dept commission, flagged).
