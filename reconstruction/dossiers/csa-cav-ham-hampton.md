# Dossier: Hampton's Brigade, Stuart's Cavalry Division (reg-csa-cav-ham)

Pass 12 (the cavalry theater opener) · Cast status: not-yet-cast ·
Achieved T-level: **T3 (East Cavalry Field grain)** · 2026-07-12

Chain role: the CSA reserve brigade whose commitment IS the CA-ECF-3
charge; Hampton's wounding is the theater's one CSA officer pin.

## EC1 — Identity & command

- Hampton's Brigade, Stuart's Cavalry Division: 1st North Carolina
  Cav., 1st South Carolina Cav., 2nd South Carolina Cav., Cobb's (Ga.)
  Legion, Jeff Davis (Miss.) Legion, Phillips's (Ga.) Legion
  (or-27-oob). **Brig. Gen. Wade Hampton** through his wounding July 3;
  **Col. Laurence S. Baker** thereafter (division marker page,
  "Stuart's Cavalry Division," fetched this pass).
- **Officer pin [D]**: Hampton "severely wounded" in the hand-to-hand
  action (below, EC5/EC6) — the theater's clearest single-officer
  casualty event, though NO clock accompanies it in the sources
  fetched this pass (see Conflicts).

## EC2 — Engaged strength

- **B&M-repro (hop, addressing-gettysburg-oob): 1,751** — a brigade
  total, not itemized by regiment in the fetch this pass.

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **Cress Ridge, ~noon** [C ~12:00, marker-adjudicated]: the brigade's
   own marker: "The Brigade arrived here about noon and skirmished
   with Union sharpshooters" (gettysburg.stonesentinels.com,
   "Hampton's Cavalry Brigade"). This agrees independently with
   Chambliss's ("reached here about noon"), Fitz Lee's ("arrived soon
   after midday"), and Jenkins's ("about noon") markers — FOUR
   independent CSA brigade accounts converging on the same hour, the
   cavalry theater's best-corroborated single clock (see the ED-72
   candidate, CA-ECF-1).
2. **July 2, Hunterstown** [D]: "Engaged in the evening with 3rd
   Division Cavalry Corps [Custer's, then still under Kilpatrick] near
   Hunterstown. Cobb's Legion led the attack and lost a number of
   officers and men killed and wounded" — the brigade's first contact
   with the same opposing brigade (Custer's) it would face again July 3.
3. **The wounding ground** [D]: the hand-to-hand action described as
   occurring while the brigade moved "to their support" of the 1st NC
   and Jeff Davis Legion — geometrically inside the same Rummel-farm
   frame as EC3 in the Union dossiers (±75 m ECF georeference class,
   `ecf-base` tie point).
4. **PASS-13 SHEET-CROP PATCH (ED-73 class):** sheet `ecf-j3-mid`
   draws "HAMPTON'S BR[IGADE]" at local (10,077, 7,468), directly
   NORTH of the Rummel farm buildings (local 10,044, 6,847) — a
   ~620 m gap, with the brigade's own regiments (Cobb's Legion,
   Phillips's Legion, Jeff Davis Legion, 1st/2nd SC, 1st NC) drawn as
   a dense block astride Cress Ridge, Chambliss's brigade to its west
   and Fitzhugh Lee's to its east. Radius ~77 m. This directly
   substantiates the marker's "arrived here about noon" line with a
   drawn position, not just a clock phrase.

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. [C ~12:00] March to Cress Ridge and deployment (above).
2. [D] The support advance: "In the afternoon the 1st North Carolina
   and Jeff Davis' Legion[,] advancing in support of Chambliss'
   Brigade[,] drove the Union cavalry but met their reserve and were
   in a critical position when the Brigade went to their support and
   a hand to hand fight ensued" (brigade marker, fetched this pass) —
   this is the SAME action McIntosh's tablet calls the "reserves...
   concealed on the Stallsmith Farm" charge (CA-ECF-3), read from the
   CSA side: Hampton's brigade was committed as the RELIEF for an
   already-engaged CSA advance (1st NC + Jeff Davis Legion), not as
   the theater's opening move.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The relief-and-melee sequence** [D]: two CSA regiments
   (1st NC, Jeff Davis Legion) advance and drive the Union line, are
   themselves met by the Union reserve and pinned, and Hampton
   commits the rest of the brigade — "a hand to hand fight ensued in
   which Brig. Gen. Wade Hampton was severely wounded" (brigade
   marker). This is the CSA-side narrative of the same ~15:00 charge
   McIntosh's and the 2nd Division's tablets describe as "repulsed
   with the aid of Artillery."
2. **Hampton's wound**: two saber cuts to the head (one through to the
   skull) plus a shrapnel wound to the body, per secondary literature
   surveyed this pass (Warfare History Network; not independently
   page-verified against a primary this pass — carried at attributed
   grade, NOT promoted to a cited-primary EC1/EC6 fact). The brigade
   marker's own wording ("severely wounded") is the adopted primary
   phrasing; the wound-mechanism detail rides as an unverified
   secondary note.

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Brigade marker total: Killed 17, Wounded 58, Missing 16, Total
  91** (gettysburg.stonesentinels.com). No report-side figure to
  cross-check this pass — Hampton's own report was not located in
  the OR 27/2 index consulted this pass (the wounding may explain a
  missing personal report; not confirmed).

## Conflicts & negative evidence

1. **Marker provenance, recorded honestly**: the CSA cavalry-brigade
   pages on gettysburg.stonesentinels.com ("confederate-headquarters"
   URL path) are position/narrative MARKERS, not the 1900s War
   Department troop tablets ED-39 profiles — no erection date or
   adjudicating body was found on the fetched pages this pass. They
   read as GNMP-era interpretive markers (the CSA cavalry brigades
   have no War Department tablets — ED-39 §4's asymmetry-by-side rule
   applies in its strongest form here: the ENTIRE CSA cavalry
   evidence base at this grain is these markers plus the OR reports'
   thin chronological-letter layer). Treated at monument-adjacent tier
   (±30 min class per event, no anchor-defining authority alone) until
   a primary corroborates a specific claim — this is a NEW marker
   subclass, proposed for the record (see ED candidates below).
2. **No clock accompanies the wounding** — it rides inside the ~15:00
   CA-ECF-3 window by sequence only [D], not independently timed.
3. **Stuart's own report** (OR 27/2 pp. 679-720, No. 565) — **PASS-13
   RE-ATTEMPT**: two targeted civilwar.com fetches this pass (p. 693:
   June 26-27 movements, Hampton/Fitz Lee named but pre-Gettysburg;
   p. 712: mid-June Middleburg/Upperville casualty tables and a
   Congressional thanks resolution, NOT the East Cavalry Field
   narrative) confirm the report's chronological span but did NOT
   locate the July 3 section. The gap is real, not merely unattempted
   — three passes running (11, 12, 13) have now failed to locate the
   exact page; recorded as a standing fetch gap, not papered over.
