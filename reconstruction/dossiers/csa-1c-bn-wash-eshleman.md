## EC1 — Identity & command

- Battalion Washington Artillery of New Orleans, First Corps reserve
  artillery; Maj. Benjamin F. Eshleman commanding; report addressed to
  "Colonel J. B. WALTON, Chief of Artillery, First Corps" (dated Aug. 11,
  1863) (or-27-2-eshleman; or-27-oob).
- Companies: 1st (Squires — one gun, operated with Miller), 2nd
  (Richardson, with Lt. Hawes), 3rd (Miller), 4th (Norcom — **w July 3
  "early in the day"**, Lt. Battles) (or-27-2-eshleman; or-27-oob).
- **The Walton/Alexander divided command** [order-chain context, D]:
  Walton's own status claim: "I had been for more than a year before,
  was during the battle and after the battle of Gettysburg, Chief of
  Artillery of the First Army Corps" (walton-shsp5-1877 p. 47);
  Longstreet's report: "Colonel Walton, chief of artillery of First
  Corps, and Colonel Alexander had posted our batteries and agreed with
  the artillery officers of the other corps upon the signal … Colonel
  Walton was ordered to open the batteries" (or-27-2-longstreet) —
  while Eshleman took his POSITION orders from Alexander ("About
  midnight, I receive[d] orders from Colonel Alexander, commanding
  reserve artillery, to take position on the field before daylight",
  or-27-2-eshleman). The signal order-path and the tactical-direction
  path were genuinely split; both are documented, and the Walton-vs-
  Alexander postwar controversy (walton-shsp5-1877 is a polemic in it)
  rides the command row.

## EC2 — Engaged strength

- **338 men, 10 guns** (addressing-gettysburg-oob, B&M-type figures,
  secondary hop flagged; a "340 men" variant exists at SNIPPET grade).
  Per company: 1st — 77 men, 1 Napoleon; 2nd — 80, 2 Napoleons + 1
  howitzer; 3rd — 92, 3 Napoleons; 4th — 80, 2 Napoleons + 1 howitzer.
- Armament, tablet + OR concurring: "Eight Napoleons … Two 12 Pounder
  Howitzers" (stone-sentinels battalion tablet); "eight Napoleon guns
  and two 12-pounder howitzers" (or-27-2-eshleman). Build adoption 240
  now carries a verified counter-figure (338) — conflict recorded, ED
  candidate below.

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **March and approach** [E]: from Culpeper C.H. June 15; "crossed the
  South Mountain at Cashtown Gap on July 1, and arrived on the 2d near
  the battle-field"; parked "within about 3 miles of the battlefield"
  by Walton's direction (or-27-2-eshleman). July 1 order verbatim:
  "headquarters, near Gettysburg, Pa., July 1st, 5:30 P. M., 1863.
  Colonel: The Commanding-General desires you to come on tonight…
  you will be wanted for to-morrow's battle" (Sorrel to Walton,
  walton-shsp5-1877 p. 49) — a timestamped document pin for the march
  trigger.
2. **July 3, before daylight → dark — the Peach Orchard line** [D
  placed before ~04:30; layout documented per company]: "Three
  Napoleons (Third Company) … about 100 yards to the left of the peach
  orchard, and on the immediate left of Captain Taylor's battery, of
  Alexander's battalion; … two Napoleons (Fourth Company) … on Captain
  Miller's left, and two Napoleons (Second Company) … on the left of
  Captain Norcom. The two howitzers — one of the Second and one of the
  Fourth Company — were held in reserve" (or-27-2-eshleman). Line
  context: "Alexander's was next, in front of the peach orchard. Then
  came the Washington Artillery Battalion, under Major Eshleman, and
  Dearing's battalion on his left, these two having arrived since dusk
  of the day before … making nearly sixty guns for that wing"
  (or-27-2-pendleton). Build centroid (3220, 3550) f55; frontage ~300 m
  NE from the orchard corner.
3. **Post-repulse** [E]: withdrawn "by order of Colonel Alexander …
  went into park near the old school-house, and bivouacked for the
  night" (or-27-2-eshleman).
4. **July 4** [C tablet clock]: "At 9 A. M. ordered to Cashtown to
  reinforce the cavalry escorting the wagon train" (stone-sentinels
  battalion tablet; the OR transcription did not surface this sentence
  — tablet-only, flagged).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **Night march onto the field, July 2/3** [D: trigger the 5:30 P.M.
  July 1 Sorrel order + Alexander's midnight positioning order]: Owen's
  account as quoted in Walton's letter: "At about 10:30 to 11 o'clock,
  at night … A long delay then occurred in starting, on account of an
  immense wagon-train passing" (walton-shsp5-1877 p. 49, Owen-as-quoted
  — the only Owen verbatim verified this pass); in position "before
  daylight" (or-27-2-eshleman).
2. **In-window sub-unit moves** [D]: Miller + Battles advanced 400–500
  yards supporting the charge (Miller dossier EC4.2); Norcom's
  "Napoleons were moved several hundred yards to the left but soon
  disabled and sent to the rear. The Howitzer was brought forward and
  did effective service until withdrawn after dark" (stone-sentinels
  Norcom marker) — the reserve howitzers entered the line as the guns
  wrecked.
3. Withdrawal to park at dark; July 4 Cashtown leg (EC3.3/3.4).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **CA-J3A-1 — the signal guns, 13:07** [A]: "you ordered me to give
  the signal for opening along the entire line. Two guns in quick
  succession were fired from Captain Miller's battery, and were
  immediately followed by all the battalions along the line opening
  simultaneously" (or-27-2-eshleman). Eshleman's stated time "Between
  1 and 2 p. m."; Pendleton's "At length, about 1 p. m., on the
  concerted signal, our guns in position, nearly one hundred and
  fifty, opened fire along the entire line" (or-27-2-pendleton).
  Adopted clock stays Jacobs's 13:07 (ED-24).
2. **13:07–~14:45 — bombardment** [A window]: "a most terrific
  artillery duel ensued … my men stood bravely to th[eir] work"
  (or-27-2-eshleman); ten guns + the captured piece (below).
3. **The captured Union 3-inch rifle** [D]: "A Union 3 inch rifle left
  the day before between the lines was brought in under a heavy fire"
  and served by Richardson's 2nd Company in the cannonade until,
  "after having fired away all its ammunition, [it] was struck on the
  ax[le] by a solid shot and disabled" (stone-sentinels Richardson
  marker; or-27-2-eshleman) — an eleventh tube, documented.
4. **The infantry-advance statement** [conflict-flagged]: "About thirty
  minutes after the signal guns had been fired, our infantry moved
  forward over the plateau in our front" (or-27-2-eshleman) — reading
  ambiguous (assault step-off vs infantry moving up); carried on the
  source record, rule 4.
5. **The supporting advance + howitzer phase** [D]: EC4.2.
6. Rounds expended: **verified negative** — no figures in the report.

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Battalion return (or-27-2-eshleman, verbatim)**: "Wounded, 3
  officers. Killed, 3; wounded, 23, and missing, 16, non-commissioned
  officers and privates; 37 horses killed and disabled; 3 guns
  disabled; 1 limber blown up."
- Tablet folding: "Killed 3 Wounded 26 Missing 16 Total 45 / Horses
  killed and disabled 37 / Guns disabled 3" (stone-sentinels) — the
  tablet's 26 wounded = 23 men + 3 officers; both carried.
- Episode distribution [D]: all July 3 (the battalion arrived July 2
  night — clean single-day apportionment); within the day: Norcom hit
  "early in the day", the gun/horse wreckage split between the
  counter-battery duel and Miller's advanced-position phase.
- Officer pins: Capt. Norcom w "early in the day" [D within the
  morning/cannonade]; the other two wounded officers unnamed in the
  transcription (open).
