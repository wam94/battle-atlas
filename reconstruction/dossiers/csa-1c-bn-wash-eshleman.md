Chain role: **CA-J3A-1's parent body and order chain.** The signal guns
were the 3rd Company's (see csa-1c-wsh-3-miller.md); the order ran
Longstreet → Col. Walton (nominal chief of the corps artillery, with the
battalion) → Miller. Register command-context row: reg-csa-1c-arty-cmd.

## EC1 — Identity & command

- Battalion Washington Artillery of New Orleans, First Corps reserve
  artillery; Maj. Benjamin F. Eshleman commanding (or-27-oob).
- Companies: 1st (Squires), 2nd (Richardson), 3rd (Miller), 4th (Norcom —
  **w July 3**, Lt. H. A. Battles) (or-27-oob).
- **The Walton context** [order-chain fact, D]: Col. J. B. Walton, the
  battalion's longtime colonel, was nominal Chief of Artillery, First
  Corps; Longstreet's signal order went to him — "Colonel Walton was
  ordered to open the batteries. The signal guns were fired"
  (or-27-2-longstreet, NPS reproduction, verified) — while Alexander held
  the *tactical* direction of the corps' guns by Longstreet's separate
  arrangement. The divided command is first-class context for why the
  chain's opening event has both a Walton order-path and an Alexander
  clock-path (alexander-1907; ED-24).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **CA-J3A-1 — the signal guns, 13:07** [A — this battalion's company
  fired the chain's defining event]: two guns of Miller's 3rd Company,
  in sequence, on Longstreet's order via Walton. Adopted 13:07 on
  Jacobs's clock reading (jacobs-1864, re-verified full text: "At seven
  minutes past 1 p. m., the awful and portentous silence was broken");

  Longstreet's report-nominal "About 2 p.m." is the recorded outlier
