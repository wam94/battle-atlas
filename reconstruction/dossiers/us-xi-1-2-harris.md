## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **The base wall** [E, July 1 night → July 2 ~20:00]: "a stone
   wall on the right, and nearly at right angles with the Baltimore
   road" (Harris), skirmishers into the town edge; monuments
   mon-25-75oh **(5065.9, 5977.3)** and
   mon-17ct **(5099.2, 5909.9)** trace the wall line below the
   batteries; von Gilsa's line to their right (mon-41ny
   (5174.1, 5763.2), tab-us-xi-1-1-hq).
2. **The vacated space** [D, ~sundown]: the 17th CT moved "to the
   extreme right and front", its wall slot "left unoccupied,
   excepting by a few of the Twenty-fifth Ohio" — the gap primary.
3. The re-formed line [D, after the repulse]: "the stone wall in the
   rear of and parallel with the one occupied the previous day, my
   left resting on the Baltimore road."
4. July 3 [E]: right-shifted to join the 1st Brigade before day;
   skirmish fire all day. July 4: first into the town.

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. The near-sundown regimental re-shuffle [D]: the 17th CT to the
   right/front — the leg whose vacancy became the breach (both-sided
   with Hays's second-line stone wall passage).
2. **The break and fall-back** [D, ~20:00]: "the attack of the enemy
   on Cemetery Hill was made, and I was forced back by superiority
   of numbers, with heavy loss" (Harris); at regiment grain the 75th
   OH was enveloped "in front and on the flank and rear at nearly
   the same time... but few escaped, and those only in the darkness
   and smoke" — the same darkness Hays credits for HIS cover,
   claimed as cover by BOTH sides (the twilight physics cuts both
   ways; ED-63-candidate exhibit).
3. Von Gilsa's line [frame, via the 41st NY]: right wing held; left
   (68th/54th NY) broke, "the enemy... move[d] on toward the
   batteries"; Theinhardt's stone-wall stand until "a regiment of
   the Second Corps arrived."
