  Series I Vol. XXVII Pt. I p. 1028, No. 372, fetch-verified this
  pass — PRIMARY, but a forwarding cover letter, not a first-hand
  operational narrative — see EC3/EC5).
- **THE REPORT'S OWN CHARACTER, stated by Tidball himself [C,
  report-primary]**: his report forwards and summarizes Lt. Calef's
  and Lt. Fuller's own reports rather than narrating the brigade's
  movements directly; he names and commends "Second Lieutenants Calef
  and Roder" (Battery A) and "First Lieutenants Fuller, Meinell, and
  Kelly" plus "Second Lieutenant Lancaster" (Battery C), and notes
  that Graham's (Battery K) and Randol's (Battery E) reports "were to
  follow separately" (i.e., filed independently, not folded into his
  own account) — a clean, self-attested EXPLANATION for why this
  dossier stays thin at command grain: the brigade's OWN operational
  detail lives on its constituent batteries' reports, two of which
  (Randol's, and by inference Graham's and Calef's) are the actual
  primary sources for the brigade's July 1-3 activity.
- **The brigade's own generalization [C, report-primary, verbatim]**:
  Tidball on the horse-artillery arm generally: cavalry batteries were
  "constantly detached and frequently engaged with the enemy,"
  requiring exceptional officer leadership given the uncertainty of
  "position, force, and other circumstances" during reconnaissance
  operations — a first-hand command characterization of exactly the
  dispersed-battery pattern this pass's companion dossier
  (`us-ha-1-robertson.md`) finds independently on the other
  horse-artillery brigade.
