## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **The ridge east of town, "distant... nearly 1,400 yards" from
   Cemetery Hill** [C, report-primary]: Lane's own report, verbatim:
   "a ridge east of the town of Gettysburg, fronting the enemy's guns
   on Cemetery Hill, and distant therefrom nearly 1,400 yards" — a
   named, measured geometric relation, the strongest position
   statement of any dossier this pass (a self-reported range figure,
   not an inferred one).
2. **Patterson's battery DETACHED to Wilcox's brigade, July 2 morning**
   [C, report-primary, cross-corps link]: "Early on the morning of
   July 2, in compliance with an order, I sent Captain [G. M.]
   Patterson's battery… to report to Brigadier-General Wilcox" (Lane's
   report) — a direct, named attachment to Wilcox's brigade (already
   dossiered, `csa-3c-and-1-wilcox.md`), the battalion's clearest
   cross-reference to the main Anderson-division narrative. Patterson's
   own battery marker independently confirms the consequence: "engaged
   Union positions near Peach Orchard; advanced beyond Emmitsburg Road
   at 6 P.M., fighting until dark" — geometrically and temporally
   consistent with the CA-J2A-10 anchor (Wright reaches Cemetery Ridge,
   ~19:00-19:15 "shortly before sunset," ADOPTED ED-28/ED-53) — the
   SAME evening advance corridor a supporting Anderson-division brigade
   used, now with an artillery battery's own attested presence in it.
3. **Ross's and Wingfield's batteries, remaining on the ridge (EC3.1)**
   throughout July 2-3 — Ross's marker: "Five of the guns were in
   position here"; Wingfield's marker: "In position here."
4. **July 3, the cannonade** [C, TWO independent battery markers]:
   Ross's marker: "Remained here and participated in all the
   operations of the artillery including the cannonade preceding
   Longstreet's assault"; Wingfield's marker: "took part in all
   artillery conflicts of the day including that which preceded
   Longstreet's assault" — near-identical wording across two
   independently-fetched battery pages, both explicitly tying this
   battalion's fire to **CA-J3A-1** (the 13:07 signal-gun cannonade,
   ADOPTED ED-24) from a sector (east of town, Anderson's front) the
   standing chain's own sources (Peach Orchard/Seminary Ridge
   batteries) do not cover — a genuinely NEW geographic witness to the
   cannonade's extent, not a duplicate of an existing one.
5. **July 3, Patterson's battery, explicit negative** [D, marker]:
   "Occupied a position near here in reserve and did not take part in
   the active operations of the day" — Patterson's battery, having
   fought hard July 2 (EC3.2), sat out July 3 entirely; a clean
   attested-static/negative fact, not a gap.
6. **July 4 withdrawal** [C ~sunset, both markers]: "Withdrew about
   sunset and began the march to Hagerstown" (battalion + battery
   markers, consistent).

3. **ROUNDS EXPENDED — a DIGIT-EXACT three-way cross-check, the pass's

   expended 1082 rounds" (fetch-verified); summing the three
   INDEPENDENTLY fetched battery markers — Ross's "506 rounds" +
   Patterson's "170 rounds" + Wingfield's "406 rounds" = **1,082,
   EXACT** — a clean battalion-total-equals-sum-of-parts match across
   four independently fetched pages (one battalion marker, three
   battery markers). Lane's own report additionally itemizes
   Ross's-battery fire by projectile type ("78 rounds Napoleon shell,
   332 rounds 10-pounder Parrott shell, 96 rounds 3-inch navy Parrott
   shell" = 506, matching Ross's marker total exactly as well) — a
   FOUR-way agreement on Ross's battery alone (report's own itemized
   sum = report's stated total = marker total = battalion-tablet
   contribution).

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Battalion tablet: "Killed 3 Wounded 21 Missing 6 Total 30"**
  (fetch-verified).
- **Battery-marker sum, INDEPENDENT digit-exact match**: Ross's
  (K1/W7/M2) + Patterson's (K2/W5/M2) + Wingfield's (K0 stated/W9/M2)
  = K3/W21/M6 = **30, EXACT match to the battalion tablet** — the
  SAME three-way-sums-to-total pattern as the rounds-expended finding
  (EC5.3), doubly corroborating the tablet's arithmetic from
  independently fetched components.
- **CONFLICT, per ED-71-class discipline (printed figures disagreeing
  across channels)**: Lane's OWN REPORT gives a DIFFERENT total —
  "Men killed, 3; wounded seriously, 2; severely, 7; slightly, 13;
  missing, 4; total loss of men, 29" — i.e., report wounded
  (2+7+13=22) vs tablet/battery-sum wounded (21, off by ONE), and
  report missing (4) vs tablet/battery-sum missing (6, off by TWO),
  yielding report total 29 vs tablet/battery-sum total 30 (off by
  ONE). The report's own internal arithmetic is self-consistent
  (3+22+4=29); it simply does NOT match the tablet/battery-marker
  figure. **Recorded as a first-class conflict, NOT adopted either
  way** — this is a genuinely new case for the ED-71 pattern (a
  report's own total disagreeing with an independently-cross-checked
  tablet/battery-marker consensus, rather than a single table failing
  its own arithmetic) — see ED candidates below.
