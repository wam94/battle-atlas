## EC4 — Movement legs

1. July 2 evening: rear → the first line behind the artillery [D].
2. July 3 ~09:00: quarter-mile left shift, same order of battle [C].
3. **Step-off** [A vs CA-J3A-5/6]: the standing order — "when the
   artillery in our front ceased firing, the division would attack the
   enemy's batteries, keeping dressed to the right, and moving in line
   with Major-General Pickett's division ... and march obliquely to the
   left. The artillery ceased firing at 3 o'clock, and the order to move
   forward was given and promptly obeyed" (or-27-2-davis-jr) — trigger
   class: pre-arranged cease-fire condition + order.
4. The advance: ~3/4 mile open ground, fences deranging alignment and
   "in each case promptly rectified"; the right reached the wall first
   ("the conformation of the ridge ... a shorter distance to pass over")
   (or-27-2-davis-jr).
5. **Retreat endpoint** [C +5]: "About 4 p.m. the division reached the
   line held in the morning" (or-27-2-davis-jr) — the batch's cleanest
   retreat clock; [B: ~1.2 km at fall-back pace ≈ 12–20 min from a
   ~15:30–15:40 collapse, consistent].

## EC5 — Activity record

1. Early morning: enemy shells at the artillery caused "a few casualties
   ... in one of the brigades" [D] (or-27-2-davis-jr).
2. **The bombardment, division-grain** [A window]: "About 1 p.m. the
   artillery along our entire line opened ... For two hours the fire was
   heavy and incessant. Being immediately in the rear of our batteries,
   and having had no time to prepare means for protection, we suffered
   some losses" (or-27-2-davis-jr) — his TWO-HOUR duration statement is
   the long-duration counterpart to Peyton's one hour (both carried on
   the chain as duration records).
3. The advance under fire: "Not a gun was fired at us until we reached a
   strong post and rail fence about three-quarters of a mile from the
   enemy's position, when we were met by a heavy fire of grape, canister,
   and shell" (or-27-2-davis-jr) — the division-grain range relation the
   pass-2 canister ladders cross-check (Osborn/Woodruff long-range
   openings).
4. The collapse: the left gave way first (Brockenbrough/Davis fronts,
   brigade dossiers); "any further effort to carry the position was
   hopeless" (or-27-2-davis-jr).

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Division total, official return**: 411 k / 1,905 w / 534 m =
  **2,850** (or-27-2-anv-return p. 344) — covering July 1 AND July 3
  (the division fought both days; per-day split lives on the brigade
  dossiers where primaries exist).
- Officer devastation, July 3 (division-grain primary): "in Archer's
  brigade but two field officers escaped, in Pettigrew's but one, and in
  Davis' all were killed or wounded" (or-27-2-davis-jr); "After this
  day's fight, but one field officer was left in the brigade [Marshall's].
  Regiments that went in with colonels came out commanded by lieutenants"
  (or-27-2-jones-26nc).
- Command pins: Fry w&c, Marshall k-or-w&c (conflict on his dossier),
  Pettigrew hand + horse.
