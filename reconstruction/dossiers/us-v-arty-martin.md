# Dossier: Artillery Brigade, V Corps — Martin's (reg-us-v-arty)

Pass 13 (the pass-12-recommended Union corps-arty group) · Cast status:
not-yet-cast as a command row (Rittenhouse's battery, formerly
Hazlett's, is separately in-build; the other four batteries are
not-yet-cast) · Achieved T-level: **T4 (brigade grain)** · 2026-07-13

**Why V Corps over III/VI**: pass 12's census left all three Union
corps-artillery groups uncovered at battery grain. This pass picked
V Corps because its own report (Martin's, No. 221, OR 27/1 pp.
659-661+) is a substantive multi-page narrative with a named officer
death, a named recapture episode, and a full casualty table — the
richest of the three on a quick source check (III/VI Corps artillery
reports were not fetched this pass to confirm the comparison directly,
so this is a reasoned choice from search-stage signals, not an
exhaustive three-way read-off; recorded honestly, see Conflicts).
V Corps's own fight (Little Round Top, the Wheatfield) also gives this
brigade's guns the battle's second-most-documented infantry-support
role after II Corps's, already dossiered at T4 (`us-ii-arty-hazard.md`,
the explicit model for this dossier's grain).

## EC1 — Identity & command

- Artillery Brigade, V Corps; **Capt. Augustus P. Martin** (3rd
  Massachusetts Battery) commanding, report dated July 31, 1863 to V
  Corps headquarters (or-27-1-martin, fetch-verified this pass).
- Five batteries (or-27-1-martin; or-27-oob): Battery D, 5th US
  (**1st Lt. Charles E. Hazlett**, k July 2; **Lt. Benjamin F.
  Rittenhouse** succeeding — already dossiered, `us-btty-rittenhouse.md`);
  Battery C, 3rd Massachusetts (Lt. Aaron F. Walcott); Battery I, 5th
  US (Lt. Malbone F. Watson); Battery L, 1st Ohio (Capt. Frank C.
  Gibbs); Battery C, 1st New York (Capt. Almont Barnes).
- **Officer pin [C, report-primary]**: Hazlett — "The battery had been
  engaged only about an hour, when First Lieutenant Charles E.
  Hazlett… was shot in the head" and died at 8 p.m. (Martin's report,
  verbatim, fetch-verified this pass) — a materially fuller account
  than the corpus's existing note on `us-btty-rittenhouse.md` (which
  carries only the tablet's "mortally wounded" and a library note
  about the Weed connection); this pass's fetch UPGRADES that pin to a
  report-primary with an explicit time of death.
- **Second officer episode [C, report-primary]**: Battery I (Watson's)
  was "without support of any kind" and its guns were "abandoned"
  after heavy losses, then "recaptured by the bravery and
  determination of Second Lieutenant Samuel Peeples" with help from
  the Garibaldi Guards (39th NY Infantry) — a named battery-loss-and-
  recapture episode, distinct in kind from any activity record
  elsewhere in the corpus this pass (guns actually left the line and
  were retaken, not merely under fire).

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **Arrival, "between 4 and 5 p. m." July 2** [C, report-primary]:
   Martin's report states the brigade entered the field in this
   window — the corps-level trigger for every battery's individual
   deployment leg that afternoon.
2. **Battery D (Hazlett's/Rittenhouse's), Little Round Top summit**
   [C ~16:30, tablet — already dossiered]: cross-referenced, not
   re-authored (`us-btty-rittenhouse.md` EC3).
3. **Battery I (Watson's), an exposed forward position** [D,
   report-primary]: "without support of any kind" — the position's
   geometry is not separately stated this pass beyond the general
   corps arrival window; the exposure itself is the attested fact.
4. **Batteries C (Massachusetts, Walcott's) and I "not to be found" at
   their assigned positions** [C, report-primary, first-class
   COMMAND-CONFUSION fact]: Martin's report states these units had
   been REASSIGNED by staff officers of General Sickles (III Corps) —
   i.e., a V Corps battery was moved by a DIFFERENT corps's staff
   without (implicitly) Martin's own brigade-level knowledge of where.
   This is a rare explicit command-friction fact, the cross-corps
   mirror of the Hunt/Hancock tension already on file in
   `us-ii-arty-hazard.md` EC5.2 — recorded, not adopted as blame
   either way.

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Brigade total (report-primary, verbatim): "Killed, 9; wounded, 33,
  and missing, 2. Total, 44"** (Martin's report, fetch-verified this
  pass) — a REPORT-grade figure, not a tablet hop; the strongest EC6
  basis of any dossier this pass.
- **Battery D's component, already report-primary**: "1 officer and 6
  men" killed, "6 men" wounded (Martin's report) — 7 killed + 6
  wounded = 13 of the brigade's 44; this NARROWS (does not replace)
  the existing figure on `us-btty-rittenhouse.md`, which should be
  cross-checked against this report quote in a future pass (flagged,
  not silently merged this pass since that dossier's own EC6 was not
  re-opened here).
- Ammunition: Martin's report itself notes "the report of ammunition
  expended is not included in the report of the battery commandant"
  for at least one battery — an explicit, report-stated EC5 negative
  (not this dossier's own omission).
