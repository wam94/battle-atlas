# Dossier: Scales's Brigade (Lowrance's), Pender's Division (reg-csa-3c-pen-4)

Pass 3 (first command batch) · Cast status: in-build (`csa-lowrance`) ·
Achieved T-level: **T4 full-arc** · 2026-07-11

Command-batch role: the third line's right — a brigade that made the July 3
assault at ~500 muskets, three days after losing every field officer but
one. Its two reports (Scales for July 1, Lowrance from July 1 evening)
plus Engelhard give the batch's most complete wrecked-formation arc.

## EC1 — Identity & command

- Brig. Gen. Alfred M. Scales (w July 1, shell fragment, at the ~75-yard
  bottom before the seminary) → **Col. William Lee J. Lowrance (34th NC)**
  from July 1 evening (himself already w July 1; w again July 3 per the
  register) (or-27-2-scales; or-27-2-lowrance; or-27-oob).
- Regiments: 13th, 16th, 22d, 34th, 38th North Carolina (or-27-oob).
- Officer pins: July 1 — "Every field officer of the brigade save one
  had been disabled" (or-27-2-scales; the exception Lt. Col. G. T.
  Gordon); Scales w + AAAG Riddick w; Pender personally rallied the
  brigade (or-27-2-engelhard). July 3 — Lowrance w (register; his own
  report is silent on it — flag).

## EC2 — Engaged strength

- **PRIMARY: "about 500 men, without any field officers, excepting
  Lieutenant-Colonel Gordon and myself ... In this depressed,
  dilapidated, and almost unorganized condition, I took command"**
  (July 1 evening, or-27-2-lowrance) — the July 3 step-off basis, minus
  small July 2 skirmish wear. Build start 1,250 is the pre-July-1
  strength; the build's decay must cross this 500-man documented
  waypoint (authoring note).
- Climax bound: the combined two-brigade "800 guns" statement at the
  works (or-27-2-lowrance).

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. July 1 night [D]: moved to the extreme right at ~01:00 ("It was then
   1 o'clock" — a rare CSA night clock) (or-27-2-lowrance).
2. **July 2** [D]: dawn to "a position on the right of and on line with
   the artillery ... we its only guard", half-mile skirmish line under
   Lt. A. J. Brown (38th NC); relieved 1 p.m. by Anderson's division;
   rejoined the division second line, right of Lane's brigade
   (or-27-2-lowrance).
3. **July 3 morning** [D]: with Lane to the right, under Trimble,
   "formed on the second line, in rear of Major Poague's battalion of
   artillery" (or-27-2-lowrance) — the Poague-frame anchor (ED-36 line;
   Poague's battalion itself remains un-dossier'd, noted on the Trimble
   record). Sheet j3-02 note: a "SCALES N.C." label reads at ~local
   (3300, 6150) ±150 — the pre-move left-sector state (drawn-state
   caution; the 08:00–11:00 window brackets the move right).
4. Farthest advance: "the right of the brigade touched the enemy's line
   of breastworks" (or-27-2-lowrance) — the Bryan/Angle seam with the
   front line's survivors.

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. July 3 morning: left sector → rear of Poague, right of the support
   line [D; B: ~1.2 km lateral].
2. **The advance** [division trigger]: "over a wide, hot, and already
   crimson plain ... at a distance of 1 mile"; at two-thirds of the way
   "troops from the front came tearing through our ranks, which caused
   many of our men to break, but with the remaining few we went forward"
   (or-27-2-lowrance) — the front line's collapse WASHING BACK through
   the support, the batch's only inside record of that mechanism.
3. The retreat: "without orders, the brigade retreated, leaving many on
   the field"; rallied "on the same line where it was first formed"
   (or-27-2-lowrance).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **Bombardment** [A window + B duration]: "here we remained at least
   one hour, under a most galling fire of artillery, which ... the men
   endured with the coolness and determined spirit of veterans"
   (or-27-2-lowrance; Engelhard's "two hours' exposure" is the wider
   division reading — both carried).
2. The advance under the full arc ("their whole line of artillery was
   playing upon us"); the works touched on the right; "the pieces in
   our front were all silenced" at the climax (or-27-2-lowrance,
   concurring with Lane's silenced-guns observation against the
   Woodruff/Arnold wreckage records).

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Return (July 1–3)**: 102 k / 323 w / 110 m = **535**; regiment k+w:
  13th NC 126, 16th NC 66, 22d NC 89, 34th NC 64, 38th NC 79
  (or-27-2-anv-return p. 345).
- **CONFLICT: Scales's own July 1 report table prints 545 total (48 k /
  381 w / 116 m; officers 9/45/1)** — a July-1-scoped table LARGER than
  the compiled three-day total (or-27-2-scales) — carried, never
  averaged; the return's approximative-headnote applies (ED-49).
- Episode split [D]: July 1 catastrophic (~500 of ~1,250 by Lowrance's
  count — his "one-half of its remaining numbers" reading in Engelhard);
  July 2 slight (skirmish line); July 3 the difference — the brigade's
  July 3 loss is bounded by the 500-basis and the ~99-survivor squads
  implied by the 800-guns statement, i.e. very heavy in proportion.
- Build decay 599 vs these primaries: reconciliation flagged (authoring).
