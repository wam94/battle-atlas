## EC1 — Identity & command

- 2nd Brigade, 1st Division, VI Corps: 5th Maine, 121st New York,
  95th, 96th Pennsylvania (or-27-oob; brigade tablet,
  gettysburg.stonesentinels.com, "2nd Brigade, 1st Division, 6th
  Corps," fetch-verified this pass). **Brig. Gen. Joseph J. Bartlett**
  throughout; own report dated August 6, 1863 (No. 226, OR 27/1 pp.
  671-673, civilwar.com transcription, fetch-verified this pass —
  PRIMARY, own-report).
- **THE COMPOSITE-COMMAND FACT [C, report-primary]**: on July 3,
  Bartlett was "ordered by Major-General Sedgwick to command Third
  Brigade" (i.e., Nevin's/Wheaton's 3rd Brigade, 3rd Division, VI
  Corps — `reg-us-vi-3-3`, `us-vi-3-3-nevin.md`) "and advance against
  Hood's division" — a temporary composite command distinct from
  Bartlett's own 2nd Brigade. This directly resolves Nevin's dossier's
  own EC1 note ("July 3 the brigade 'was assigned to the command of
  Brig. Gen. J. J. Bartlett'") and its Conflicts §4 ("a rendering-
  metadata hazard"): the Bartlett-command episode is now
  cross-substantiated from BOTH sides (Bartlett's own report here;
  Nevin's tablet there) and dated to July 3, distinctly from the July
  2 evening W5 charge Nevin's own brigade fought under its own colors.

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2, arrival "late in the day"** [D, report=tablet agreement]:
   "The Brigade arrived late in the day and was formed in two lines to
   support Fifth Corps of which the troops in front were giving
   ground" (tablet, verbatim); Torbert's report independently confirms
   "a little after 5 p.m." for the brigade's own arrival.
2. **July 3, the Wheatfield-area advance** [C, TRIPLE cross-reference —
   this dossier, Nevin's, and McCandless's]: "Late in the day the Third
   Brigade Third Division in a second line at an interval of 200 yards
   supported First Brigade Third Division Fifth Corps [McCandless's,
   `reg-us-v-3-1`] in an advance through the Wheatfield and the woods
   on the south but soon after being engaged the Third Brigade Third
   Division advanced to the front and the combined forces captured
   about 200 prisoners of Brig. Gen. Benning's Brigade and the colors
   of the 15th Georgia" (tablet, verbatim) — this is the SAME episode
   McCandless's own report documents from the V Corps side ("At 5
   o'clock on the 3d... completely routing them, taking over 200
   prisoners, one stand of colors belonging to the Fifteenth Georgia,"
   `us-v-3-1-mccandless.md` source register) and Nevin's own tablet
   documents as its own brigade's action (6 p.m. "recapturing
   artillery," `us-vi-3-3-nevin.md` EC5.2) — a genuinely new
   THREE-DOSSIER cross-confirmation this pass assembles for the first
   time, though the identity of "First Brigade Third Division Fifth
   Corps" on THIS tablet as McCandless's brigade specifically (not a
   different V Corps unit) is inferred from the matching narrative
   detail (200 prisoners, 15th Georgia colors), not independently
   labeled on this tablet.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **July 2**: support only, "2 privates wounded" (Torbert's — i.e.
   Bartlett's — report, verbatim; EC6).
2. **July 3, the composite-command charge** [C]: Bartlett's report:
   "loss in killed and wounded in my command was between 20 and 30"
   and "about 200 prisoners" captured by the joint command (report,
   verbatim) — a genuinely engaged action, the VI Corps brigade
   batch's clearest combat episode besides Shaler's (this pass,
   `us-vi-3-1-shaler.md`).
3. **July 4**: a reconnaissance advance limited to "500 yards" with
   "no loss" (report, verbatim).

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Brigade tablet (Bartlett's OWN 2nd Brigade, not the composite
  command): Killed 1 Man, Wounded 4 Men, Total 5**
  (gettysburg.stonesentinels.com, verbatim) — this figure is
  explicitly Bartlett's OWN brigade's loss (July 2's "2 privates
  wounded" plus a small July-3 remainder), NOT the composite
  Bartlett-commanded Third Brigade's loss.
- **The composite-command loss, separately stated [C, report-
  primary]**: "between 20 and 30" killed and wounded (Bartlett's
  report) for the JULY 3 advance under his temporary command of
  Nevin's brigade — a materially larger figure than his own brigade's
  tablet total, consistent with (not contradicting) the two being
  DIFFERENT commands: Nevin's brigade's own EC6 (`us-vi-3-3-nevin.md`,
  tablet=return exact, 53 total across its full arc) is the more
  granular source for that body's casualties; Bartlett's "20 to 30"
  is his own in-the-field estimate for the same July 3 action,
  roughly consistent in order of magnitude with a sub-total of
  Nevin's 53 (July 2 W5 charge + July 3 combined).
