## EC1 — Identity & command

- 3rd Brigade, 2nd Division, II Corps; Col. Norman J. Hall (7th MI)
  commanding, unwounded. Regiments: 19th MA, 20th MA (Col. Revere
  **mw July 3**), 7th MI (Lt. Col. Steele **k**), 42nd NY, 59th NY

  approximately 190 miles" before arriving July 2 (or-27-1-hall p. 435).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **July 2, ~sunset** [D]: 42nd NY + 19th MA detached "to support
  General Humphreys" on the left, returning after the repulse of
  Wright (or-27-1-hall p. 436).
2. **July 3, ~15:20-25 — the by-the-flank rush to Webb** [A vs
  CA-J3A-9]: "I was forced to order my own brigade back from the line,
  and move it by the flank"; "the head of the line, still slowly moving
  by the flank, was crowded closer to the enemy" (or-27-1-hall p. 439)
  — ~150-250 m along the crest under fire [B: 3-5 min at crowd pace];
  the OR editors' footnote on p. 438 ("*Should be Arnold's") flags
  Hall's battery naming on his right (battery-identity conflict slot,
  see us-btty-cowan.md EC3.3).
3. **The ten-minute melee** [B: stated duration]: "The line remained in
  this way for about ten minutes, rather giving way than advancing"
  (p. 440) — a primary DURATION for the Copse fight's stalemate phase.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **July 2 evening** [C ~16:30-19:00]: "Artillery and picket fire
  continued until approximately 4.30 p. m."; then the general
  engagement; "the men, lying down, poured into him so well-directed a
  fire that he halted, fell back, and finally broke in great disorder";
  "[Barksdale] was mortally wounded and two colors left on the ground
  within 20 yards of the line of the Seventh Michigan" (or-27-1-hall
  p. 436) — NOTE: the Barksdale attribution is Hall's own (Barksdale
  fell on the Plum Run axis; identity conflict carried).
  **PASS-5 UPGRADE (the July 2 arc closed both-sided)**: the attacker
  now has its own dossier (csa-1c-mcl-3-barksdale.md) — the j2-04
  drawn state puts Barksdale's main body at Plum Run (4053, 4080)
  with Hall's front several hundred meters northeast, and the
  tablet's Plum-Run fall statement and Hall's 20-yards statement are
  COMPATIBLE at ~100 m grain (adjoining fronts, one running fight);
  Hall's July 2 evening fire is the brigade's second engaged window
  in its own right. The exact brigade-front attribution (Barksdale's
  left regiments vs Wright's/Wilcox's echelon on that frontage)
  stays a recorded conflict per the pass-2 Willard record.
2. **July 3 cannonade** [C +5]: "At 1 o'clock the enemy opened with
  artillery" — ~150 pieces on the line "between the cemetery and
  Fifth Corps"; his praise of Cushing's battery riding the same page
  (p. 437).
3. **The repulse fire discipline** [A window]: 7th MI and 20th MA
  "open[ed] fire at about 200 yards ... some regiments waiting even
  until but 50 paces intervened"; "The deadly aim of the former
  regiment was attested by the line of slain within its range"; "The
  enemy halted to deliver his fire, wavered, and fled" (p. 439) — the
  range ladder for the south-of-Copse musketry that pass-3 fire
  windows can compile.
4. **Aftermath** [D]: "Between 1,500 and 2,000 prisoners were captured
  at the point of attack, where the First, Second, and Third Brigades
  were equally present"; "Twenty battle-flags were captured in a space
  of 100 yards square" (p. 440).
