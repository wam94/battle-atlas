## EC5 — Activity record

1. **Under the bombardment overs** [A window vs CA-J3A-1..5]: lay under
   "a most terrific cannonading ... kept up without intermission for one
   hour" (peyton-or-1863 — his ONE-HOUR duration statement is a primary
   short-duration reading, carried on the chain as a conflict record, not
   a clock).
2. **The mountain-battery enfilade** [D, in-advance]: "one [battery]
   posted on the mountain, about 1 mile to our right, which enfiladed
   nearly our entire line with fearful effect, sometimes as many as 10
   men being killed and wounded by the bursting of a single shell"
   (peyton-or-1863) — the receiving-side record of Rittenhouse's D/5th US
   (cross: us-btty-rittenhouse.md EC5, upgraded this pass).
3. Fire discipline: "loading and firing as it advanced" after routing
   the first line (peyton-or-1863); take_canister at the wall (T5).
4. The works fight: "hand to hand, and of the most desperate character;
   but more than half having already fallen, our line was found too weak
   to rout the enemy"; men "climbing over the wall, and fighting the
   enemy in his own trenches until entirely surrounded" (peyton-or-1863).
