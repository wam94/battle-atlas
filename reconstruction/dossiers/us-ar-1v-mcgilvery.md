## EC5 — Activity record

1. **July 3, opening — now clock-fixed by his own report** [C via
  clockProfile]: "At about 12.30 o'clock the enemy opened a terrific
  fire upon our lines with at least one hundred and forty guns. This
  fire was very rapid and inaccurate, most of the projectiles passing
  from 20 to 100 feet over our lines" (p. 883) — his stated 12:30 is
  the corpus's one EARLY opening clock (+37 against the chain;
  profiled, rule 4). The overshoot statement is first-class evidence
  for the low-ground line's low casualties.
2. **The ordered silence** [A window vs CA-J3A-1/5]: pre-arranged —
  "the commanders of batteries were repeatedly ordered that, in the
  event of the enemy opening a cannonading upon our lines, to cover
  their men as much as possible, and not return the fire until
  ordered" (p. 883); the breach put down: "About one-half hour after
  the commencement, some general commanding the infantry line ordered
  three of the batteries to return the fire. After the discharge of a
  few rounds, I ordered the fire to cease and the men to be covered"
  (p. 884) — the unnamed "some general" is the Hunt-vs-infantry-
  command conflict inside his own line (identity open; carried).
3. **The deliberate reply** [C]: "After the enemy had fired about one
  hour and a half, and expended at least 10,000 rounds of ammunition,
  with but comparatively little damage to our immediate line, a slow,
  well-directed fire from all the guns under my command was
  concentrated upon single batteries of the enemy of those best in
  view, and several badly broken up and successively driven from
  their position to the rear" (p. 884) — economy → deliberate
  counter-battery, Hunt's 10:00 instruction executed to the letter
  (us-arty-hq-hunt EC5.1).
4. **The repulse fires** [C + A-window]: "At about 3 p. m. a line of
  battle of about 3,000 or 4,000 men appeared, advancing directly
  upon our front, which was completely broken up and scattered by our
  fire before coming within musket range of our lines. Immediately
  after, appeared three extended lines of battle … presented an
  oblique front to the guns under my command, and by training the
  whole line of guns obliquely to the right, we had a raking fire
  through all three of these lines … instead of a well-ordered line
  of battle, there were broken and confused masses, and fugitives
  fleeing in every direction. This ended the operations of the
  batteries under my command at the battle of Gettysburg" (p. 884).
  Phillips's monument adds the battery-grain repulse of the Florida
  brigade (stone-sentinels, corpus layer).
5. Rounds expended (own line): still no figure (the "10,000 rounds"
  is his estimate of the ENEMY's fire) — flagged.
