# Dossier: Posey's Brigade, Anderson's Division (reg-csa-3c-and-5)

Chain role: the PARTIAL-advance case between Mahone's clean
non-participation and Wright's/Lang's full advances — regiments went
forward piecemeal, as skirmishers, and never joined the main assault;
Posey's own report gives the clearest first-person account of HOW an
Anderson-division brigade's advance order dissolved into fragments.

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **An open field, woods on both flanks** [C, report-primary,
   verbatim]: "an open field, with a woods on my right and left
   flanks" positioned facing the enemy's lines — Posey's own
   description, geometrically unplaced beyond this pass's fetch
   (no named landmark or coordinate).
2. **The advance, "200 or 300 yards beyond the barn and house, which
   were burned"** [C, report-primary]: the 48th and 19th Mississippi,
   sent forward as skirmishers, "advanced some 200 or 300 yards
   beyond the barn and house, which were burned" — this is the BLISS
   FARM (the burned barn/house match the Bliss farm buildings' known
   fate July 2-3, a landmark shared with `csa-3c-pen-3-thomas.md`'s
   own July-3 "near the Bliss House and Barn" line, below), a
   genuinely useful cross-brigade geographic tie this pass surfaces
   for the first time in the corpus.
3. **The withdrawal, "rear of Pegram's battery"** [C, report-primary]:
   "fall back to my original position, in the rear of Pegram's
   battery" — a second Anderson-division brigade this pass ties
   directly to Pegram's battalion (`csa-3c-bn-pegram.md`), alongside
   Mahone's brigade (`csa-3c-and-2-mahone.md` EC3.1) — TWO brigades
   independently naming the same artillery unit as their reference
   point, a small but genuine corroboration of Pegram's battalion's
   own position.
4. **July 3, reserve** [C, both report and tablet]: "in reserve to
   support the battery in my front" (report) / "held in reserve here
   supporting artillery in its front" (tablet) — near-verbatim
   agreement.

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. [D] Initial deployment to the open field (EC3.1) — no timing
   stated.
2. [C, report-primary] The piecemeal advance: "Following orders to
   advance with only two regiments deployed as skirmishers" first (the
   48th and 19th), THEN the 16th, THEN "subsequently the 12th…after
   learning of threats to his flanks" — a FOUR-STAGE staggered
   commitment, not a single leg, explicitly attested by the brigade
   commander himself as the direct CAUSE of the advance's failure to
   cohere ("through some misunderstanding of orders instead of the
   Brigade advancing in compact ranks… the regiments were ordered
   forward at different times" — tablet, near-verbatim to the report).
3. [C, nightfall] The withdrawal leg to the rear-of-Pegram's-battery
   position (EC3.3), triggered by "orders" as darkness fell.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The skirmish advance** [C, report-primary]: "Deployed as
   skirmishers and fighting in detachments they pushed back the
   Union outposts and drove some artillerists for awhile from their
   guns but did not join in the attack upon the Union position on
   Cemetery Ridge" (tablet, matching the report's own framing) — a
   REAL tactical success at the skirmish grain (Union pickets pushed
   back, a gun crew driven off temporarily) that nonetheless never
   became the brigade-grain assault the day's plan called for.
2. **Opposition identified [D, attributed, secondary]**: web research
   this pass (NOT independently verified) attributes the check on
   Posey's advance to Ohio regiments of Brig. Gen. Alexander Hays's
   3rd Division, II Corps — a plausible but unconfirmed opposing-unit
   identification, consistent with the Bliss farm's known role as a
   contested position between II Corps and Third Corps CSA brigades.
3. **July 3, reserve/artillery support only** [D]: no active combat
   role stated beyond the static support posture (EC3.4).
