- 3rd Brigade, 2nd Division (Ayres), V Corps: 140th New York
  (O'Rorke), 146th New York (Garrard), 91st Pennsylvania (Sinex),
  155th Pennsylvania (Cain). **Brig. Gen. Stephen H. Weed mw July 2**
  "on the summit, near the battery" (or-27-1-garrard) → **Col. Kenner
  Garrard (146th NY)** (or-27-oob).
- Officer pins [D]: **O'Rorke mw "at the head of his regiment while
  leading it into action"** (or-27-1-garrard; k on the crest in the
  140th's counter-rush, norton-1913/Farley); **Weed mw on the summit
  near Hazlett's guns**, dying requests recorded by Farley — "I'm as
  dead a man as Julius Caesar" — **died at about nine o'clock that
  evening** [C ~21:00] (norton-1913 pp. ~138-139); **Hazlett mw

  us-btty-rittenhouse.md).
- Warren connection: Warren w by musket ball on the summit "while
  talking with Lieutenant Hazlett" (norton-1913 ch. VII, his own
  letter) — three pins within yards of one another.

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2 late afternoon — the vacated-and-reoccupied ground** [D]:
  the brigade was posted toward the left, found moving off toward the
  Peach Orchard "By order of General Sickles", and ordered back by
  Sykes "at the double-quick step" (or-27-1-sykes — the cross-order
  incident; it is why only the 140th made the first crisis).
2. **The 140th NY at the summit crisis** [D]: detached from the
  column personally by Warren ("I took the responsibility to detach
  Colonel O'Rorke, the head of whose regiment I struck", norton-1913
  ch. VII; Garrard's report names Warren) — over the crest into the
  16th MI breach without loading (norton-1913/Farley).
3. **The brigade on the summit line** [E → drawn]: 91st + 155th PA
  "positioned on Round Top ridge to secure and hold" with the two NY
  regiments (or-27-1-garrard); j2-03 draws "WEED" as a bar at
  **(4169, 2640)** ±62 m on the summit with the signal-station circle
  (read 2026-07-11 — this read also REFINES the register's LRT
  summit eyeball: the pass-2 note's (4430,2600) ±50 sits ~260 m east
  of the drawn summit bar; the tie-point geometry supports the
  ~(4170-4250, 2500-2650) placement).
4. **The night fortification** [D]: "a stone wall about half way down
  the slope, wherever the rocks offered no protection"
  (or-27-1-garrard) — a built-feature record for the July 3 state.
5. **July 3** [E]: held the summit line (j2-05 draws "WEED'S BRIG"
  and "AYRES DIV" continuing; the battery's July 3 fire window is the
  paired dossier's record).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **The approach** [B: ~1.5 miles advanced under fire,
  or-27-1-garrard].
2. **The Sickles diversion + Sykes recall** [D]: out toward the Peach
  Orchard road and back at the double-quick (or-27-1-sykes).
3. **The 140th's summit rush** [B: ~300-400 m up the north slope at
  the double ≈ 3-5 min]: O'Rorke swung the column up on Warren's
  word; the regiment went in by companies off the march, bayonets
  only (norton-1913/Farley: no time "to load their guns or fix
  bayonets" — O'Rorke's men went in as they came).
4. **The rest of the brigade following** [D]: Weed brought the 91st,
  146th, 155th onto the ridge behind the 140th (or-27-1-garrard).
