## EC5 — Activity record — THE NEGATIVE, VERBATIM

1. **July 2** [C]: the rifles "opened fire upon the enemy's batteries
  opposite, which they kept up without cessation until about thirty
  minutes before sunset" — the battalion's ONE sustained fire episode.
2. **July 3 — the attested silence, first-class**: Richardson
  "placed his guns in position under fire at this point, but **did
  not fire a single shot, having received orders to that effect**"
  (or-27-2-garnett-bn); the six smoothbores "**bore no part in these
  actions, although they were upon the field in readiness whenever
  they should be called upon**." Tablet concurs: "The Napoleons and
  Howitzers were in reserve and not actively engaged at any time"
  (stone-sentinels). The silence was ORDERED — presumably ammunition
  economy on the rifle side and range on the smoothbore side (motive
  not stated; inference flagged, not encoded).
3. Under-fire window [D]: "in position under fire" through the
  cannonade — the battalion takes the counter-battery weather
  without contributing outbound fire; its casualty exposure class is
  Garnett-specific (present-under-fire-silent).
