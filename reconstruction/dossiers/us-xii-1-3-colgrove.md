## EC1 — Identity & command

- 3rd Brigade, 1st Division, XII Corps: 27th Indiana, 2nd
  Massachusetts, 13th New Jersey, 107th New York, 3rd Wisconsin
  (or-27-oob). **Brig. Gen. Thomas H. Ruger** commanded until the
  morning of July 2, when he took the division (Williams up to the
  corps, Slocum to the wing) and **Col. Silas Colgrove** (27th IN)
  took the brigade (or-27-1-colgrove: "I assumed command of the
  Third Brigade" morning July 2); Lieut. Col. Fesler commanded the
  27th IN, Lieut. Col. Mudge the 2nd MA.
- **THE MUDGE PIN**: Lieut. Col. Charles R. Mudge k leading the 2nd
  MA's charge — Morse: "Colonel Mudge gave the order, 'Forward!'…
  I now took command of the regiment, Colonel Mudge having been
  killed" (or-27-1-morse-2ma p. 817); Colgrove: "fell while gallantly
  leading his men in the charge" (p. 814). The famous
  refusal-and-obedience exchange ("Are you sure that is the order?…
  it is murder, but it's an order") is monument-page/tradition class
  (stone-sentinels 2nd MA page), NOT in either OR report — carried at
  that grade, never promoted.
- Succession: Morse to the 2nd MA mid-charge; Col. William Cogswell's
  supplementary (p. 818) postdates the battle.

## EC2 — Engaged strength

- **27th IN PRIMARY: "339 officers and men"** carried into action
  (or-27-1-fesler-27in) — matched EXACTLY by the monument's "Number
  engaged 339" (mon-27in).
- **2nd MA: 294 enlisted men** taken into action July 3 (Cogswell's
  supplementary, p. 818) **+ 22 officers** per the monument
  (mon-2ma: "22 officers + 294 enlisted") — 316 all ranks.
- Other three regiments: no strength primary in the fetched pages
  (open; the brigade fought July 3 with the 13th NJ/107th NY detached
  to Gregg from ~17:00).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **The left-wing round trip** [C, July 2 ~sundown → ~22:00]:
   ~1.5 miles out, 40-60 min at the far end, back — the brigade
   nearly missed the fight for its own works (matching Ruger's
   division record and McDougall's parallel column).
2. **THE CHARGE** [the CA-J3M-3 two-pole event; route physics B]:
   from the works over the meadow — Morse: "The men jumped over the
   breastworks with a cheer, and went forward on the double-quick";
   Colgrove's tactical statement: skirmishers impossible ("a line of
   skirmishers would be cut down before they could fairly gain the
   open ground"), so "carry his position by storming it"; the 2nd MA
   straight at the woods, the 27th IN obliquing right with "nearly
   double the distance to traverse". ~100-yard meadow at double-quick
   (≤2.7 m/s, R-linear-connective-motion class) ≈ 40-70 s in the
   open; the 27th's oblique ~2×. Withdrawal by order, covered by the
   supports' fire; the enemy's counter-rush over the works "quickly
   driven back by the two regiments, who turned and opened fire"
   (or-27-1-ruger p. 781) — charge, repulse, counter-repulse in one
   leg cluster.
3. Detachment leg [C ~17:00 July 3]: 13th NJ + 107th NY to Gregg's
   cavalry (returned ~9 a.m. July 4); 27th IN's ~16:00
   reconnaissance (Fesler).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The order as received** [D; the order-corruption pair]:
   Colgrove verbatim — "The general directs that you advance your
   line immediately," delivered VERBALLY by Lieut. Snow — vs Ruger's
   stated intent: "advance skirmishers against the enemy at that
   point, and, if not found in too great force, to advance two
   regiments, and dislodge him" with his own attribution "From
   mistake of the staff officer, or misunderstanding on the part of
   Colonel Colgrove" (or-27-1-ruger). BOTH echelons' wordings
   carried; no adjudication of whose error.
2. **The charge itself** [CA-J3M-3; the time dispute]: Morse "Firing
   was kept up until 5.30 o'clock, when the regiment was ordered to
   charge the woods in front of us"; Fesler occupied the 3rd WI works
   "between 5 and 6 o'clock… I was then ordered by you to charge";
   the 27th IN advance marker "charge at 6 a.m."; VS Ruger "until
   about 10 a. m.… At this time I received orders to try the
   enemy… with two regiments" and the j3 sheet pair drawing the
   charge in the 8–11 window. **The full two-pole conflict record
   lives on the ED-64 candidate (pass-9 report §5); nothing adopted
   here.**
3. Fire support [D]: the 3rd WI + supports covering the withdrawal;
   "at every volley of the enemy, gaps were being cut through its
   ranks" (Colgrove, on the 27th's oblique); ~100 prisoners taken in
   the counter-repulse (Ruger).
4. Under the afternoon cannonade [E]: "shell, shot, and missiles
   bursting over them, around them, and among them for hours"
   (Colgrove) — the Culp's Hill line's share of the 13:07+ program.
5. July 1 [E]: 2nd MA Co. F skirmish toward the cavalry-held crest
   (Morse) — the brigade's first contact.

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Return (p. 184): brigade 2+47 k / 20+205 w / 0+5 m = 279**;
  regiment rows: **27th IN 110** / **2nd MA 136** / 13th NJ 21 /
  107th NY 2 / 3rd WI 10 — the charge pair carries 246 of 279 (88%).
- **The 27th IN component cluster** (all carried): Fesler's itemized
  episode split — "In the charge… killed, 15; wounded, including 7
  commissioned officers, 83. …in works, 3 enlisted men killed;
  wounded, 10… Total… 111" — vs the return's 110 vs Colgrove's "112
  in killed and wounded" vs the monument's "Killed and wounded 110.
  Missing, one." **Fesler's charge-vs-works split is the episode
  apportionment primary** (98 of 111 in the charge); the advance
  marker adds the color-guard toll ("Four color bearers were killed
  and four were wounded").
- **The 2nd MA cluster** (all carried): Morse's enumeration (2 o. k,
  2 o. mw, 6 o. w; 21 m k, 102 w, 3 m ≈ 136) vs return 136 vs the
  monument's 4+41 k&mw / 6+84 w vs Colgrove's "about 130 killed and
  wounded" — return=Morse agreement at aggregate.
- Officer pins: Mudge k (in-charge, the hard pin); the 27th IN's 8
  officers among the wounded (7 in the charge, 1 in the works).
