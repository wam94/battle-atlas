## EC1 — Identity & command

- Brig. Gen. John Gibbon, commanding 2nd Division; commanded II Corps
  in the intervals when Hancock held the left-center wing (his report
  states the July 3 shuffle: Hancock resumed corps command at the
  cannonade's approach, Gibbon returned to the division)
  (or-27-1-gibbon; or-27-1-hancock).
- **Wounding pin** [D, sequence]: "Toward the close of the main
  contest, I had the misfortune to lose the valuable services of ...
  Brigadier General John Gibbon, commanding Second Division ...
  severely wounded. A short time afterward I was myself wounded"
  (or-27-1-hancock) — Gibbon w BEFORE Hancock w, both toward the
  close; Gibbon's own laconic record: "I was wounded and left the
  field", with the context sentence placing him toward the division's
  LEFT ("the right of the enemy's line did not extend as far as the
  left of my division") (or-27-1-gibbon). Command passed (division) to
  Harrow (or-27-oob).
- Staff pin: Lt. F. A. Haskell "slightly wounded, but refused to quit
  the field" (or-27-1-gibbon) — the clock-bearer stayed.

## EC2 — Engaged strength

- **Division primary (new)**: ~**3,800** engaged July 3, losing "in
  killed and wounded over 1,600" and capturing "more prisoners than it
  had men on the ground at the end of the conflict" (or-27-1-gibbon) —
  a division-grade EC2+EC6 statement in one sentence, the best-attested
  Union division figure in the audit so far.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **Cannonade duration statement** [A vs CA-J3A-1/5]: "At 1 o'clock
   ... the enemy opened with his artillery all along his line, and for
   two hours the most terrific shower of shot and shell continued, ably
   responded to by our batteries" (or-27-1-gibbon) — a TWO-HOUR primary
   (13:00→15:00 on his clock), the report twin of Haskell's watch
   arithmetic and the long-duration school's second II-Corps statement
   (with Hancock's 1h45m).
2. Fire discipline order: the division's hold-until-close-range
   ("met with such a withering fire of canister and musketry as soon
   melted it away", or-27-1-gibbon) — the command act behind the
   brigade-level 200-yard/50-pace statements (or-27-1-hall, pass 2).
3. The repulse and recovery: "turned again, drove him back over the
   wall, capturing a large number of prisoners and many colors"
   (or-27-1-gibbon).
