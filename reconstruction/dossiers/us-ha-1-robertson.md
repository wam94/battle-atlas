## EC1 — Identity & command

- 1st Brigade, Horse Artillery, Cavalry Corps: 9th Michigan Battery
  (Capt. Jabez J. Daniels), 6th New York Battery (Capt. Joseph W.
  Martin), Batteries B & L, 2nd U.S. (Lt. Edward Heaton), Battery M,
  2nd U.S. (Lt. A. C. M. Pennington Jr. — already dossiered,
  `us-btty-pennington.md`, T3 ECF grain), Battery E, 4th U.S. (Lt.
  Samuel S. Elder) (or-27-oob; brigade tablet, gettysburg.stonesentinels.com,
  "Horse Artillery First Brigade," fetch-verified this pass). **Capt.
  James M. Robertson**, 2nd U.S. Artillery, commanding throughout; own
  report dated Camp near Warrenton, Va., August 22, 1863, addressed to
  Capt. L. Siebert, A.A.G. (civilwar.com OR transcription, Series I
  Vol. XXVII Pt. I, pp. 1020-1022, No. 368, fetch-verified this pass —
  PRIMARY, own-report).
- **THE JULY 3 SUCCESSION FACT [C, report-primary]**: "Moved to the
  front... assumed command of the reserve artillery" after "General
  Tyler['s]" incapacitation by sunstroke (Robertson's report,
  paraphrase-verified this pass via the civilwar.com transcription) —
  matching the register's own commander field for `reg-us-ar-hq`
  ("Brig. Gen. Robert O. Tyler; Capt. James M. Robertson (after
  Tyler's July 3 incapacitation)") but sourced here from Robertson's
  OWN account rather than inferred from the register alone — a
  genuinely new primary confirmation, not previously cited on either

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **The approach march** [C, report-primary]: June 28 report at
   Frederick; June 29 "at daylight" Pennington's and Elder's batteries
   detached to the 3rd Cavalry Division; Robertson departed 4 p.m.
   with the remaining two batteries, Daniels's 9th Michigan joining
   en route; June 30, 2 a.m. arrived Middleburg, Md., rested to 9 a.m.;
   June 30, 4:30 p.m. reached Taneytown, remaining until July 1,
   11:30 p.m. (Robertson's report, dates verbatim per the fetch).
2. **July 2, 5:30 a.m., arrival near Gettysburg** [C, report-primary]:
   held in reserve; moved back 2 miles on the Baltimore Pike at dark
   — an attested-static reserve posture for the brigade's remaining
   two batteries (Daniels's, Martin's/Heaton's) through July 2, while
   Pennington's and Elder's (already detached, EC3.1) fought their own
   theaters (East Cavalry Field and South Cavalry Field respectively —
   see EC3.4).
3. **July 3, moved to the front** [C, report-primary]: Robertson's own
   command moved up and he personally assumed the Artillery Reserve
   command (EC1) — the brigade HQ's position for the balance of the
   day is therefore the Artillery Reserve's own ground (Powers
   Hill/Taneytown Road area, per `us-ar-hq-tyler-park.md`), not a
   separately-anchored horse-artillery position.
4. **The brigade's batteries were geographically split across THREE
   theaters July 3** [C/D, cross-dossier]: Pennington's at East
   Cavalry Field (`us-btty-pennington.md`, dossiered pass 13); Elder's
   at South Cavalry Field, "with First Brigade Third Division on the
   left" per the brigade's own tablet (gettysburg.stonesentinels.com,
   verbatim) — independently CONFIRMED this pass by the EC3
   sheet-crop exercise (see `us-cav-3-1-farnsworth.md` EC3, below):
   "ELDER'S BAT." is drawn on Bachelder sheet j3-03 (No. 8, 1-5 P.M.
   July 3) directly beside Farnsworth's Brigade block, local
   (2728, 1351) — the same South Cavalry Field ground; Daniels's 9th
   Michigan fought under Newton's direction on Cemetery Ridge (EC5).
   No single "brigade position" exists for July 3 — a genuine
   dispersed-command finding, the horse-artillery counterpart of the
   infantry corps' detachment patterns already on record elsewhere in
   this corpus.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **Daniels's 9th Michigan Battery, July 3** [C, report-primary]:
   engaged under Maj. Gen. Newton's direction, "doing good execution,
   until the close of the battle" (Robertson's report, paraphrase this
   pass) — matching the brigade tablet's "Engaged on Cemetery Ridge
   south of Pleasonton Avenue subject to the orders of Major Gen. J.
   Newton" (verbatim). Held its position on the battlefield overnight.
2. **July 4**: Daniels rejoined Robertson's command after encamping on
   the Baltimore Pike (report).
3. Martin's (6th NY) and Heaton's (2nd US B&L) batteries carry **NO
   activity statement beyond the tablet's own "In Reserve"** for both
   — an attested-static/negative record, not a research gap (both
   tablets independently agree: "In Reserve").
4. Pennington's and Elder's activity records live on their own
   dossiers/register notes (EC3.4) — not re-derived here.

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Brigade tablet total: Killed 2 Men, Wounded 1 Officer + 5 Men,
  Total 8** (gettysburg.stonesentinels.com, verbatim).
- **Daniels's 9th Michigan Battery, component figure [C, report-
  primary]**: "1 killed, 4 wounded... 23 horses killed" (Robertson's
  report, paraphrase this pass) — accounts for 5 of the brigade's 8
  human casualties, leaving 3 (the tablet's remaining wounded) to the
  other batteries; no further per-battery breakdown found this pass.
