## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2, arrival ~4 P.M.** [C ~16:00, report=tablet agreement]:
   "Arrived about 4 P. M. from Manchester Md. and moved to the east
   slope of the northern side of Little Round Top. Arriving at dark
   and held in reserve until morning" (tablet, verbatim); Russell's
   own report independently states "This brigade reached Gettysburg on
   the evening of July 2" — consistent, though less precisely timed
   than the tablet.
2. **July 3 morning, the extreme left** [C, report=tablet agreement]:
   "Moved to the extreme left and on the east slope of Round Top and
   remained until late in the afternoon then went into position on
   the left centre in support of Fifth Corps. Not engaged" (tablet,
   verbatim); Russell's report: "On July 3, early in the morning, it
   was put in position on the extreme left of our line of battle, and
   there held until late in the afternoon, when it was brought up to
   aid in opposing an anticipated attack on the center of our line" —
   a report=tablet match on both the position and its timing, and the
   report adds the CAUSE for the afternoon shift (an anticipated
   attack on the center — i.e., the cannonade/charge crisis, CA-J3A)
   the tablet's terser text omits.
3. **July 4, relief of the V Corps** [C, report-primary]: "On the
   morning of July 4, this brigade relieved a portion of the Fifth
   Corps in the position held by a part of that corps, on the steep
   hill to the left of the center of our line" (report, verbatim) —
   i.e., Little Round Top itself; a clean geographic identification
   ("the steep hill") the report makes explicit.

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Brigade tablet: Wounded 2 Men** (gettysburg.stonesentinels.com,
  verbatim).
- **CONFLICT (report vs. tablet)**: Russell's own report states "There
  were no casualties in this command during any of the engagements on
  July 2, 3, and 4" (verbatim) — directly disagreeing with the
  tablet's 2 wounded. Neither figure is adopted over the other this
  pass: the report is a commander's own count filed weeks after the
  battle (August 3), the tablet a later War-Department-commission
  compilation (ED-39 class); a 2-man discrepancy on an otherwise
  wholly attested-static brigade is a small, honestly recorded
  conflict, not investigated further this pass (no obvious tie-
  breaking evidence — e.g. a regimental return — was fetched).
