  2nd Cavalry Division, Cavalry Corps: 1st Maine Cav., 10th New York
  Cav., 4th Pennsylvania Cav., 16th Pennsylvania Cav. (or-27-oob).
  **Col. J. Irvin Gregg** throughout (D. McM. Gregg's cousin, not to
  be confused with the division commander).

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2, Brinkerhoff's Ridge** [D]: with McIntosh's brigade
   (before Kilpatrick detached it — see us-cav-2-1-mcintosh.md), the
   brigade "took and held Brinkerhoff Ridge after a sharp skirmish
   with the 2nd Virginia Infantry [the Stonewall Brigade's 2nd Va.]...
   about 10 P.M. the two Brigades withdrew and bivouacked... on the
   Baltimore Pike a mile East of Rock Creek Bridge" (web research
   summarizing the division tablet, this pass; NOT independently
   page-verified against the tablet's own text — carried at
   attributed grade).
2. **July 3, the Baltimore Pike / reserve** [D]: the brigade was held
   at or near the Baltimore Pike through the day's main action,
   per the same secondary survey.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **July 2 Brinkerhoff's Ridge skirmish** [D]: against Confederate
   infantry (2nd Virginia), not cavalry — a distinct engagement from
   East Cavalry Field, fought the day before.

   lost several men, wounded, nevertheless" (web research, this pass)
   — an attested reserve posture with SOME casualties, the exact
   ED-44 present-but-mostly-not-engaged rendering class.
