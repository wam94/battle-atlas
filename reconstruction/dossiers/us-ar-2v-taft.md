# Dossier: 2nd Volunteer Brigade, Artillery Reserve (reg-us-ar-2v)

Pass 14 (the four remaining Artillery Reserve brigade HQs, census item
5) · Cast status: not-yet-cast (command echelon; none of its four
batteries previously dossiered) · Achieved T-level: **T3 (own-report
primary, but scoped to the commander's OWN battery — same pattern as
Ransom's brigade, this pass, though with actual battle-day content)**
· 2026-07-14

Chain role: East Cemetery Hill's Artillery Reserve reinforcement —
Taft's own 5th New York Battery reported directly to Howard (XI Corps)
on July 2, tying this brigade to the East Cemetery Hill fight already
well documented from the infantry/XI-Corps-artillery side
(`us-xi-arty-osborn.md`, `us-btty-wiedrich.md`, `us-btty-ricketts.md`).

## EC1 — Identity & command

- 2nd Volunteer Brigade, Artillery Reserve: Battery B, 1st Connecticut
  Heavy (Capt. Albert F. Brooker), Battery M, 1st Connecticut Heavy
  (Capt. Franklin A. Pratt), 2nd Connecticut Light Battery (Capt. John
  W. Sterling), 5th New York Battery (Capt. Elijah D. Taft) (or-27-oob;
  brigade tablet, gettysburg.stonesentinels.com, "Artillery Reserve
  Second Volunteer Brigade," fetch-verified this pass). **Capt. Elijah
  D. Taft** commanding, doubling as his own battery's captain; own
  report dated July 19, 1863, Camp of the Artillery Reserve (OR 27/1
  pp. 891-892, No. 325, civilwar.com transcription, fetch-verified
  this pass — PRIMARY, but scoped to his OWN battery's action, not a
  brigade-wide narrative — the same pattern this pass's Ransom dossier
  documents for the 1st Regular Brigade, though here the commander's
  own report at least covers his own constituent battery in full).
- **Officer/enlisted pins [C, report-primary, unusually dense]**:
  Pvt. John C. Begg, mortally wounded July 2 by an "accidental caisson
  limber explosion" approaching the field, died July 7; **Lt. Clinton
  Thalheimer**, wounded in the abdomen by a bullet at 9 A.M. July 3,
  died at midnight the same day; Sgt. Henry Dillon, slightly wounded
  in the neck ~10 A.M. July 3; Pvt. Adolph Wittenberg, severely
  wounded through the leg ~2 P.M. July 3 — FOUR individually-timed
  casualties in one battery's report, an EC1/EC6 pin density this
  corpus rarely finds outside its most-worked dossiers.

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2, the march and East Cemetery Hill arrival** [C, report-
   primary]: marched from Taneytown July 2, arriving near Gettysburg
   ~10:30 A.M.; by 4 P.M. reached the cemetery and reported to
   **Maj. Gen. Howard** (XI Corps commander), taking position at 5
   P.M. (Taft's report, paraphrase-verified this pass) — a direct,
   named cross-corps reporting chain (Artillery Reserve battery
   attaching to XI Corps command), tying this brigade's own battery
   into the East Cemetery Hill defense this corpus already documents
   from Osborn's, Wiedrich's, and Ricketts's dossiers.
2. **July 3, sustained position** [D, report-primary]: held position
   through the day, firing 8 A.M. and again after 1:30 P.M. (EC5).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **The march to the field** [B: Taneytown to near Gettysburg,
   ~10:30 A.M. arrival; then 4 P.M. cemetery arrival, 5 P.M. into
   position — the interval implies a staged approach, not a single
   continuous march].
2. [D ~10:00 A.M. July 5] Return march to the Artillery Reserve camp,
   2 miles from Gettysburg (report, verbatim).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **July 2, counter-battery + infantry fire** [C]: engaged CSA
   batteries north and west of the cemetery with four and two guns
   respectively, and fired on CSA infantry (report, paraphrase).
2. **July 3, sustained fire and a gun-burst casualty** [C ~08:00 and
   ~13:30+]: "returned enemy fire beginning around 8 a.m. and again
   after 1:30 p.m."; one 20-pounder Parrott gun burst at the muzzle
   during the action (report, verbatim/paraphrase) — an equipment-
   failure event distinct from combat loss, recorded as such; firing
   ceased ~6 P.M.
3. **Rounds expended [C, report-primary, itemized]**: "80 Schenkl
   percussion shell, 63 Schenkl combination shrapnel, 32 Parrott
   time-fuse shell, 382 Parrott time-fuse shrapnel, and 557
   cartridges" — a genuinely itemized-by-projectile-type expenditure
   record, the ED-42 "expenditure ledger" class (never conflated with
   an issue figure — none was found this pass).

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Brigade tablet total: Killed 1 Man, Wounded 5 Men, Captured or
  Missing 2 Men, Total 8** (gettysburg.stonesentinels.com, verbatim).
- **Taft's own battery, component figures [C, report-primary]**: the
  four individually-timed casualties (EC1) — Begg (mortally wounded,
  died July 7), Thalheimer (mortally wounded, died same night),
  Dillon (slight), Wittenberg (severe) — sum to at least 2 mortal + 2
  wounded for this ONE battery alone, consistent with (a substantial
  share of, though not independently reconciled against) the
  brigade's own 8-total tablet figure. No reconciliation is attempted
  this pass (Begg and Thalheimer both die of wounds AFTER the
  battle-day window the tablet's "Killed"/"Wounded" categories may or
  may not separately capture — a scope caution, not a resolved
  conflict).

## Conflicts & negative evidence

1. **This dossier's own-report primary is scoped to Taft's OWN
   battery**, not the brigade as a whole (EC1) — the same "commander
   doubles as battery captain, files only his own battery's report"
   pattern as Ransom's brigade this pass, though here WITH substantial
   content rather than none.
2. **The mortally-wounded-later casualties (Begg, Thalheimer) create a
   scope caution against the tablet's day-level Killed/Wounded
   columns** (EC6) — not adjudicated, per the standing ED-43 tablet-
   scope discipline (tablet casualty lines are day/scope-limited until
   corroborated).
3. No independent primary was fetched for Brooker's, Pratt's, or
   Sterling's batteries this pass — their activity/position record
   rests on the brigade tablet alone (Sterling's has a further note,
   see the tablet quote already on file from the pass-13 census
   fetch: "July 2. Reinforced Third Corps line and late in the day
   retired and formed line under Lieut. Col. F. McGilvery on left of
   Second Corps" — a direct tie to `us-ar-1v-mcgilvery.md`'s composite
   line, not independently re-verified this pass).
