  Michigan Cavalry (or-27-oob). **Brig. Gen. George A. Custer**
  throughout — newly commissioned (June 1863), his first field command
  at brigade grain.

  Michigan) killed is attested only via a secondary channel this pass

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **Two Taverns → the right** [C ~08:00]: the brigade's own tablet:
   "Marched to Two Taverns arriving at daylight and at 8 A. M. moved to
   the right under orders to report to" Gregg (gettysburg.stonesentinels.com,
   "2nd Brigade, 3rd Division, Cavalry Corps" marker). This is the
   brigade's OWN clock for the march that put it on Gregg's front
   BEFORE Kilpatrick's recall order (below) — recorded as the
   deployment leg's start.
2. **The Rummel farm ground**, same ±75 m ECF georeference class as
   McIntosh's dossier (EC3.1) — no separate tie point authored for
   Custer's brigade this pass; the frame is shared with the division.
3. **PASS-13 SHEET-CROP PATCH (ED-73 class):** sheet `ecf-j3-mid`
   draws "CUSTER'S BRIG. 2' Brig. 3' Div." at local (9,570, 5,333),
   with the 5th/6th/7th/1st Michigan regiments drawn as separate
   sub-blocks stretching south along the Low Dutch Road (6th MI:
   "all day"). The `ecf-j3-am` sheet (4–11 a.m.) draws "9 A.M.–12 M.
   6th MICH. CAPT. WEBBER Two companies" (local 10,451, 7,533) and a
   "Skirmish Line CAPT. MAXWELL 1' MICH." running south toward local
   (10,267, 6,437) — a picket/skirmish presence pushed well forward of
   the brigade's main block BEFORE the ~14:00 CA-ECF-2 engagement,
   read for the first time this pass. Radius ~77 m (sheet
   `estAbsUncertaintyM`); Pennington's battery (Battery M, 2nd US) is
   drawn immediately south of Custer's block at local (10,072, 5,237)
   — see `us-ha-1-4-pennington.md`.

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. [C ~08:00] Two Taverns → the Hanover Road right (above).
2. [C ~14:00, cross-line with McIntosh's tablet]: Kilpatrick ordered
   Custer's brigade recalled to rejoin the 3rd Division on the Union
   left (South Cavalry Field, CA-SCF below); Gregg refused to release
   it given the observed Confederate force — the 2nd Division tablet's
   phrasing ("ordered Second Brigade Third Division to return" [i.e.,
   Gregg *countermanding* the return]) and Gregg's own report both
   attest the retention. Gregg's report (p. 957, fetch-verified this
   pass): a division commander OVERRODE another division's summons of
   an attached brigade — a first-class command-decision fact,
   independent of any clock.
3. [D] The mounted charge sequence itself (below, EC5) has no
   leg-by-leg timing in the sources fetched this pass — the famous
   7th Michigan repulse-then-1st-Michigan-countercharge sequence is
   NOT separately clocked here (honest gap; see Conflicts and the
   pass-12 report §4 EC honesty).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The general engagement** [C ~14:00]: "about 2 P. M. [the brigade]
   was immediately engaged with the Confederate forces which were
   repulsed and forced from the field" (brigade tablet — the tablet's
   own summary compresses the whole afternoon's back-and-forth into
   one sentence; the "repulsed and forced from the field" line reads
   as the DAY'S outcome, not a single event, per the tablet-adjudicated
   caution of ED-39).
2. **The 7th Michigan's charge, checked** [D, report-primary]: the
   regiment's own itinerary line in Custer's report: "Charged the
   advance in [sic] of the enemy's skirmishers at Gettysburg. Held the
   field until the advance of the First Michigan Cavalry"
   (or-27-1-custer, p. 1000, fetch-verified this pass) — read alongside
   Gregg's "but not successfully" line (p. 957: "The charge of this
   column was met by the Seventh Michigan Cavalry ... but not
   successfully") the two primaries together attest a checked first
   charge followed by the 1st Michigan's advance — the shape, not the
   clock, of the action.
3. **Battery M, 2nd US Artillery** (Pennington's, attached this
   sector): "Took up a position on the right of the line at
   Gettysburg, and was engaged with Stuart's cavalry; battery engaged
   all day" (or-27-1-custer, p. 998) — the artillery duel's own-side
   attestation, all-day duration, no rounds figure this pass.

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Brigade tablet total: Killed 1 officer + 31 men, Wounded 13
  officers + 134 men, Captured or Missing 78 men = Total 257**
  (gettysburg.stonesentinels.com marker).
- **Conflict, per ED-43 (tablet casualty lines are day-scoped until
  corroborated)**: the July-3-only OR Return of Casualties row (p.
  958, fetch-verified this pass) reads "Custer's brigade[:] 1 [officer]
  and 28 [enlisted] killed[,] July 3" — a component figure 3 BELOW the
  tablet's 31 enlisted killed. The brigade fought at Hunterstown July 2
  as well as East Cavalry Field July 3; the tablet's 257 total is not
  explicitly day-scoped on its face, and the July-3-only return row is
  the narrower, corroborating measure. **Recorded as an ED-43-class
  conflict, not adopted either way** — the return row is the safer
  July-3-only floor; the tablet total likely pools both days.

## Conflicts & negative evidence

1. **Custer's own OR report (No. 361, pp. 997-1004) is diary/itinerary
   form, regiment by regiment**, confirmed independently this pass
   (fetch-verified, both the report's own framing sentence — "takes
   the form of a diary" — and its content, which is terse
   engagement-line entries per regiment rather than a brigade
   narrative). This matches the standing secondary-literature note
   (Custer's OR report is often described as thin; a separate, longer
   "lost report" manuscript is not in this corpus and was NOT
   consulted this pass — recorded as a standing gap, not fetched).
2. **Maj. Noah Ferry's death** (5th Michigan, dismounted skirmish line)
   surfaced via the OR itinerary line "The regiment was dismounted to
   fight on foot on the left of the brigade. Major Ferry was killed"
   (or-27-1-custer, p. 998, fetch-verified) — this IS a primary
   (report-embedded), carried here as EC1 material rather than a
   dedicated pin, since no clock accompanies it.
3. **No leg-by-leg clock for the charge sequence** — see EC4.3. The
   ECF's clock problem is genuinely thin at this grain: the two
   independent tablets (McIntosh's, Custer's) agree on ~14:00 for the
   general engagement and the 2nd Division tablet + McIntosh's tablet
   agree on ~15:00 for the mounted charge/repulse, but nothing in the
   sources fetched this pass clocks the 7th Michigan's checked charge
   or the 1st Michigan's advance to a finer grain than "during the
   afternoon."
