# Dossier: Poague's Artillery Battalion (Pender's Division, Third Corps) (reg-csa-3c-bn-poague)

Pass 4 (CSA frame completion) · Cast status: in-build (`csa-bn-poague`)
· Achieved T-level: **T4 full-arc** · 2026-07-11

Frame role: the last un-dossier'd battalion of the ED-36 Third Corps
arc — the battalion Lowrance's anchor cites ("formed on the second
line, in rear of Major Poague's battalion of artillery",
or-27-2-lowrance), i.e. the third line's own reference frame. Its
report (No. 562, transcribed in full this pass) carries an attested
July 1-2 NON-participation, a split-position July 3 geometry, and the
howitzer negative that quantifies ED-44's subtraction ledger; its
tablet carries a 657-round expenditure return matching the ANV return's
casualty row exactly.

  Graham's Charlotte (NC — joined June 21, "reported to me for duty",
  or-27-2-poague), Ward's Madison (Miss), Brooke's (Va)
  (or-27-2-poague; or-27-oob).

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. July 1-2: **off the line** — detached at Cashtown July 1 morning,
   ordered up ~11:00 but "took no part in the engagement of July 1 and
   2" (or-27-2-poague) [C].
2. **July 2 late evening — the ten-gun position** [C]: "three rifles
   and two Napoleons were posted on the left of Anderson's division,
   and not far from Pegram's battalion, and on the right of these and
   in front of Anderson's left, at the distance of 400 yards, five
   Napoleons"; "about 1,400 yards from the enemy's batteries";
   "Immediately on my right were the batteries of the First Corps";
   six howitzers "a short distance in rear, as no place could be found
   from which they could be used with advantage" (or-27-2-poague).
3. **Sheet reads (new)**: j3-03 (13:00–17:00) draws the group
   separately — "WYATT" at ~local **(3427, 5220)** and "GRAHAM" at
   ~(3489, 5325) ±120 m on the north (Bliss-front) axis near Pegram's
   batteries, and the "POAGUE'S BATT." bar at ~**(3467, 4894)** ±120 m
   in front of Anderson's left (the Napoleon group) — the report's
   400-yard two-position split drawn as ~350-450 m of separation on

4. Tablet line: West Confederate Ave, ~335 ft south of the Tennessee
   monument; the howitzer marker ~900 ft further south

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. June 15 → July 1: the march arc (Fredericksburg → Culpeper →
   Berryville [Graham joins] → Cashtown) (or-27-2-poague; register
   grain).
2. **July 2 late evening**: Cashtown-side reserve → the two-position
   line ("at last succeeded in getting ten of my guns into position" —
   an effortful night emplacement) [C/D].
3. July 3 post-repulse: "I ordered up the howitzers" (anticipating a
   Union counter-advance) — a leg INTO the line that produced no fire
   window (below) [D].
4. July 4 ~dusk: withdrawal toward Hagerstown (or-27-2-poague; tablet
   concurs) [C].

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **July 1-2: attested non-participation** (ED-44 class — the
   battalion subtracts from every gun-census claim for both days)
   (or-27-2-poague).
2. **July 3 ~07:00 — the unordered morning affair** [C]: Wyatt's five
   guns opened on Hill's order (unknown to Poague, who "directed the
   firing to cease" against "a very unequal" contest); several Union
   batteries concentrated in reply; Wyatt lost 8 horses; "A caisson of
   the enemy was exploded" (or-27-2-poague) — a morning fire window
   the build should NOT smooth into the cannonade.
3. **The cannonade** [A window vs CA-J3A-1/5]: "In the general
   engagement that occurred about the middle of the day, the battalion
   participated" (or-27-2-poague) — the ten guns fired in the 13:07
   bombardment (Lane's/Lowrance's advances passed "through the woods
   in which our batteries were planted", or-27-2-lane-jh).
4. **The howitzer negative, verbatim**: "The enemy, however, failed to
   follow up his advantage, and I got no service out of those useless
   guns" (or-27-2-poague) — six tubes present-but-never-firing across
   the whole battle (ED-44 rendering state; with Garnett's battalion,
   the Third Corps' subtraction ledger now has two attested classes).
5. **Expenditure return (tablet)**: "Ammunition expended 657 rounds.
   Horses killed or disabled 17" (stone-sentinels) — battery-side
   ledger (ED-42); at drill-manual sustained rates for ten guns, 657
   rounds ≈ 60-90 minutes of deliberate fire [B: 657 ÷ 10 guns ÷
   ~1 rd/gun/min] — consistent with cannonade participation plus the
   morning affair, NOT with continuous all-day fire.
