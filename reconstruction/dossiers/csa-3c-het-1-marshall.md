Command-batch role: the left wing's right-center and its heaviest-loss
brigade — the 26th North Carolina's brigade, whose farthest-advance marker
at the wall is registered (mon-26nc-1). The arc is unusually well
documented: Jones's brigade report, Young's July 4 letter, and the
regiment-grain return.

## EC1 — Identity & command

- Brig. Gen. J. Johnston Pettigrew (to division command July 1 when Heth
  fell) → **Col. James K. Marshall (52d NC)** July 2–3 → Maj. John T.
  Jones (26th NC, the report author) (or-27-oob; or-27-2-jones-26nc).
- Regiments: 11th, 26th, 47th, 52d North Carolina (or-27-oob). July 1
  line right-to-left: 52d — 47th — 11th — 26th (or-27-2-jones-26nc).
- Officer pins: **Col. Marshall fell July 3 — CONFLICT: Davis's division
  report has him "wounded and taken prisoners while gallantly leading",
  Young's July 4 letter has "Colonel Marshall and Major Richardson, of
  the Fifty-second, supposed to be killed", and the register carries
  k July 3** (or-27-2-davis-jr; or-27-2-young-26nc; or-27-oob) — all
  three readings recorded, the register's k stands as adopted. July 1
  pins: Col. H. K. Burgwyn, Jr. (26th) k "shot through both lungs",
  Lt. Col. J. R. Lane (26th) w "through the neck, jaw, and mouth" — both
  with the colors; Col. C. Leventhorpe (11th) w&c, Maj. E. A. Ross (11th)
  k (or-27-2-jones-26nc; or-27-2-young-26nc). July 3: Capt. S. P. Wagg
  (26th) k by grape, Lt. G. Broughton k (or-27-2-young-26nc); "After
  this day's fight, but one field officer was left in the brigade"
  (or-27-2-jones-26nc).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. July 2 evening: ~1 mile right into the artillery's rear [D].
2. July 3 ~09:00: the division shift [C].
3. **Step-off** [C via jones profile +65: "About 2 o'clock we were
   ordered to advance"]: initial confusion — "ordered to close in on the
   right on Pickett's division, while that command gave way to the left.
   This was soon corrected" (or-27-2-jones-26nc) — the two converging
   axes meeting badly at launch, then the ~3/4-mile advance dressed
   right (or-27-2-davis-jr).
4. The final rush and the flank collapse: "many had reached the wall,
   when we received a deadly volley from the left. The whole line on the
   left had given way, and we were being rapidly flanked ... We therefore
   fell back to our original position in rear of the batteries"
   (or-27-2-jones-26nc) — retreat endpoint = the morning position, by
   ~16:00 canonical [C via davis-jr].
