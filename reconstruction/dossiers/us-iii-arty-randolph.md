# Dossier: Artillery Brigade, III Corps (reg-us-iii-arty)

Chain role: the reference-frame command for the Peach Orchard/
Emmitsburg-Road salient's artillery — closes the Union corps-artillery
class this corpus has been building since pass 1/2 (Hazard's II Corps,
Wainwright's I Corps, Osborn's XI Corps, Muhlenberg's XII Corps

## EC1 — Identity & command

- Artillery Brigade, III Corps: Battery B, 1st New Jersey Light /
  "2nd NJ Battery" (Capt. A. Judson Clark; Lt. Robert Sims), Battery D,
  1st New York Light (Capt. George B. Winslow — already dossiered,
  `us-btty-winslow.md`, T4), 4th New York Independent Battery (Capt.
  James E. Smith — already dossiered, `us-btty-smith.md`, T4), Battery
  E, 1st Rhode Island Light (Lt. John K. Bucklyn, wounded — see EC1
  pin; Lt. Benjamin Freeborn succeeding), Battery K, 4th U.S. (Lt.
  Francis W. Seeley, wounded — see EC1 pin; Lt. Robert James
  succeeding) (or-27-oob; brigade tablet, gettysburg.stonesentinels.com,
  "Artillery Brigade, 3rd Corps," fetch-verified this pass — TWO
  physical monuments carry the identical text, Sickles Avenue and the
  Peach Orchard, both fetch-verified). **Capt. George E. Randolph**
  commanding, WOUNDED July 2 ("a wound that I received in the
  shoulder early in the action prevented my being as active on the
  field as I desired," Randolph's own report, verbatim); **Capt. A.
  Judson Clark** succeeding. Randolph's own report dated September 2,
  1863, covering June 28 – July 3 (No. 181, OR 27/1 pp. 581-585,
  civilwar.com transcription, fetch-verified this pass — PRIMARY);
  Clark's own supplementary report (No. 182, OR 27/1 pp. 585-586,
  dated August 14, 1863, from near Beverly Ford, Va., fetch-verified
  this pass — PRIMARY, own-report, Battery B's own July 2 action).
- **Officer pins [C, report-primary]**: Randolph himself wounded in the
  shoulder, early in the July 2 action; Lt. Bucklyn (Battery E) wounded
  while recovering a caisson; Lt. Seeley (Battery K) "badly wounded"
  around 5:30 P.M., Lt. Robert James assuming command — THREE named
  officer casualties across the brigade's own command chain in one
  afternoon, an unusually dense command-attrition record for a single
  dossier this corpus has seen at brigade-artillery grain.

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **The approach march, June 28 – July 1** [C, report-primary]:
   June 28, most batteries encamped near Middletown, Md. (Clark's
   battery near Crampton's Pass with infantry, joining later); the
   corps moved to Woodville that afternoon; June 29 to Taneytown; June
   30 to Bridgeport (~2 mi from Taneytown); July 1, three batteries
   (Randolph's own F/E, Clark's B, Seeley's K) marched to Gettysburg,
   encamping left of town near the Taneytown road (Randolph's report,
   verbatim-paraphrased this pass).
2. **July 2, the Emmitsburg Road salient line** [C, report-primary]:
   Randolph's and Clark's batteries placed "on the line held by
   General Birney's division, running from near the left of the
   Second Corps to the base of Signal or Round Top Mountain" — a
   position the report itself characterizes as "low, unprotected, and
   commanded by the ridge" along the Emmitsburg-Gettysburg road (i.e.
   the ground Sickles's advance created, the salient this corpus's
   Graham/Carr/Ward/de Trobriand dossiers already document from the
   infantry side).
3. **Clark's own siting, July 2, 9:30 A.M.** [C ~09:30, report-
   primary]: "placed in line on the rise of ground midway between
   General Sickles' headquarters and the peach orchard, on the
   Emmitsburg road" (Clark's own report, verbatim) — opening fire on
   CSA infantry columns crossing the Emmitsburg road at ~1,400 yards,
   "6 or 7 rounds" before the columns dispersed — a genuinely early,
   long-range engagement well before the general afternoon action.
4. **The Sickles repositioning notice, 1:00-1:30 P.M. July 2** [C,
   report-primary]: Sickles personally notified the artillery
   commander of the impending line change in this window (Randolph's
   report) — a command-chain timing fact this corpus's own CA-J2A
   chain does not currently name at this grain.
5. **The extended defense line, ~3 P.M. to dark, July 2** [C, report-
   primary]: batteries named in sequence along the line — Smith's,
   Winslow's, Bigelow's [`reg-us-ar-1v-2`, Artillery Reserve, already
   a chain executor per pass 8], Clark's, Ames' [`reg-us-ar-4v-4`],
   Randolph's own, Seeley's — a mixed III-Corps/Artillery-Reserve
   gun line (matching McGilvery's own composite-line dossier,
   `us-ar-1v-mcgilvery.md`, and Hunt's cross-corps consolidation
   pattern already well documented in this corpus).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **Solid shot, then case, then canister** [C, report-primary]:
   "Use of solid shot against enemy in woods... transition to case
   shot and canister fire" as the range closed — a textbook
   range-narrowing ammunition sequence, report-attested rather than
   inferred.
2. **The infantry assault, "shortly after 3 P.M."** [C ~15:00+,
   report-primary]: "the attack was made by the enemy's infantry.
   Beginning... on the left, near Smith's position, it extended to the
   right, and brought the whole line under a destructive fire of
   musketry" (Randolph's report, verbatim) — a left-to-right rolling
   assault the brigade's own guns received directly, corroborating the
   infantry-side accounts of the Devil's Den/Wheatfield/Peach Orchard
   collapse already dossiered.
3. **Battery-by-piece withdrawal** [C, report-primary]: batteries
   "withdrawal 'by piece' under heavy fire" as the line gave way —
   the standard artillery-arm retrograde-under-pressure class already
   named in this corpus's activity vocabulary (ED-34's classes;
   closest to `fought-to-exhaustion`, though this brigade's line was
   overrun by infantry rather than simply firing out its ammunition).
4. **Seeley's crisis, ~5:30 P.M.** [C ~17:30, report-primary]: Seeley
   "badly wounded," Lt. James assuming command, with Humphreys's
   (2nd) Division infantry supporting the battery directly (EC1) — a
   battery fighting IN infantry support contact, not from a rear gun
   line.

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Brigade tablet (both monuments, identical text): Killed 8 Men,
  Wounded 3 Officers + 78 Men, Captured or Missing 17 Men, Total 106**
  (gettysburg.stonesentinels.com, verbatim) — the "3 Officers"
  wounded figure is directly consistent with the THREE named officer
  pins this dossier documents (Randolph, Bucklyn, Seeley — EC1), a
  clean tablet-to-report officer-count agreement, though no
  per-battery breakdown of the 106 total was located this pass.

## Conflicts & negative evidence

1. **EC2 (strength) is fully open** — no personnel total located this
   pass at brigade grain.
2. **No per-battery casualty breakdown** was found this pass beyond
   the brigade aggregate (EC6) — Winslow's and Smith's own dossiers
   may carry battery-grain figures already; not cross-checked against
   the brigade total this pass (a reconciliation item for a future
   pass).
3. Randolph's own wound (EC1) explains, in his own words, why his
   report reads as somewhat compressed for the climax of the action —
   a self-attested limitation on the report's own completeness, not a
   gap this pass introduced.
