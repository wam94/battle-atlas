- Brig. Gen. Henry J. Hunt, Chief of Artillery, Army of the Potomac
  (or-27-oob).
- **Authority context (first-class conflict)**: Hunt's command over
  corps-attached artillery was contested — on the II Corps front Hancock
  claimed and exercised tactical control, ordering Hazard's batteries to
  keep firing during the cannonade for infantry morale while Hunt's policy
  was economy for the assault. The two orders coexisted on the same line;
  the II Corps batteries followed Hancock, the Reserve/XI Corps lines
  followed Hunt (2026-06-13 library "mixed" ruling; hunt-bl-3;
  or-27-1-hancock's "less furious" framing). This dispute is why
  CA-J3A-4's slackening was partial and sectoral — the anchor's shape IS
  the command conflict.

## EC5 — Activity record (the orders as events)

1. **The pre-cannonade withhold order** [C via hunt-bl-3; ~11:00–12:30]:
  instruction to the artillery commanders that on any bombardment they
  should withhold reply 15–20 minutes, then fire deliberately at visible
  batteries only, conserving ammunition for the assault. Delivered in
  person along the line (EC3.1). Executor record: Phillips's 5th MA
  monument "About 1:30 by order of Brig. Gen. H.J. Hunt fired on the
  Confederate batteries" (stone-sentinels, verified 2026-07-02) shows the
  authorized deliberate counter-battery phase.
2. **The slackening/cease order — CA-J3A-4's event** [C, ~14:30, envelope
  14:00–15:00 per ED-24]: Hunt ordered fire ceased/slackened to save
  ammunition and (with Osborn's proposal, see us-xi-arty-osborn) to feign
  silenced guns and invite the assault. The mixed nature is ruled in the
  corpus: "Not purely a ruse: encode as mixed" (2026-06-13 library —
  some copse-sector guns were genuinely wrecked/withdrawn: Brown's
  battery, Cushing's losses).
3. **The counter-order conflict** [D simultaneous]: Hancock ordered the
  II Corps line to keep firing; Hazard's batteries burned their
  long-range ammunition — the material cause of their canister-only
  assault fight (or-27-1-hazard; us-ii-arty-hazard dossier).
4. **Reinforcement orders** [D repulse window]: fresh batteries ordered
  to the crest (Cowan, Fitzhugh, Parsons, Weir, Wheeler — full-cast
  survey §repulse fires).
- **The ammunition arithmetic** [B inputs]: Hunt's stated reserve-economy
  reasoning rides his account; the Reserve train's ability to refit the
  line by evening is the physics behind the policy (pass-2 material).
