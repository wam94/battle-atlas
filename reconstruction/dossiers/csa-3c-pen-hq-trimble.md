## EC1 — Identity & command

- **The succession chain (all primary)**: Maj. Gen. W. D. Pender mw
  July 2 ~sunset ("a severe wound in the leg from a fragment of a
  shell", riding his right during Longstreet's attack; d. July 18) →
  **Brig. Gen. James H. Lane commanded the division from July 2 evening**
  ("About sunset, I was informed by Captain Norwood ... that General
  Pender had been wounded and that I must take command of the division",
  or-27-2-lane-jh) → **morning July 3, Maj. Gen. Isaac R. Trimble
  relieved Lane** of command of the two-brigade support column ("General
  Lane was relieved of the command by Major General I. R. Trimble, who
  acted under the same orders given to General Lane",
  or-27-2-engelhard) → **Trimble w&c July 3** → Lane resumed
  (or-27-2-lane-jh) (or-27-oob).
- Trimble commanded ONLY Lane's and Lowrance's (Scales's) brigades in
  the assault — Thomas and Perrin stayed on the skirmish line
  (or-27-2-engelhard; or-27-2-lane-jh).
- **Trimble pin**: "severely wounded near the enemy's works, in the leg,
  which necessitated its amputation, and in the retreat to the Potomac
  ... fell into the hands of the enemy" [D, at the works, ≈CA-J3A-9
  window]; one staff officer k, 2 w (or-27-2-engelhard).

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 3 morning — the ordered position** [D]: Hill's order via Capt.
   F. T. Hill: report to Longstreet as support to Pettigrew; Longstreet:
   "form in rear of the right of Heth's division"; executed with
   **Lowrance on the right**; Lowrance's own anchor: "formed on the
   second line, in rear of Major Poague's battalion of artillery"
   (or-27-2-lane-jh; or-27-2-engelhard; or-27-2-lowrance) — EC3 via the
   Poague frame (ED-36 battalion line).
2. **The structural gap (primary)**: "Heth's division was much larger
   than Lowrance's brigade and my own, which were its only support, and
   there was consequently no second line in rear of its left"
   (or-27-2-lane-jh) — the column's left-rear void, load-bearing for the
   left-wing collapse rendering.
3. Advance axis: ~1 mile through the woods where the batteries stood
   into the open (both brigade reports).
4. Reform point: "just in rear of the artillery, and upon the same
   ground where it had rested before making the attack"
   (or-27-2-engelhard, by Trimble's own order through Lane).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. Morning: from the division's second line (left sector) to the right,
   by Hill's order [D, pre-bombardment].
2. **The advance** [A vs CA-J3A-6, division-record trigger]: "advanced
   in close supporting distance of Pettigrew's line, General Trimble,
   with portions of his own and General Pender's staff, being with and
   taking immediate command of the movement" (or-27-2-engelhard);
   Trimble's one recorded in-advance command act: Lane's left, uncovered
   by the front line's rightward drift, "advance[d] more rapidly than
   the rest of the line, which was checked by an order from General
   Trimble" (or-27-2-engelhard).
3. The works and the fall-back: the right "had reached the works"; the
   two left regiments of Lane's fell back last, "under direct orders"
   (or-27-2-engelhard).
4. Reform on the morning ground; Lane resumed division command that
   evening (or-27-2-lane-jh).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **Bombardment** [A window]: the two brigades "suffering no little
   from the two hours' exposure to the heavy artillery fire which
   preceded the attack" (or-27-2-engelhard) — qualitative only (no
   number; open).
2. The advance: reserved fire "until within easy range, and then opening
   with telling effect, driving the artillerists from their guns,
   completely silencing them" (or-27-2-engelhard; or-27-2-lane-jh
   concurs) — the third line's fire window opens at the crest.
3. Received: "a murderous artillery and infantry fire in front, a severe
   artillery fire from the right, and an enfilade fire of musketry from
   the left" (or-27-2-engelhard) — the three-sided fire statement,
   matching the pass-1/2 frames on both flanks.
