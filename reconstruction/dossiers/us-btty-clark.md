## EC1 — Identity & command

- Battery B, 1st New Jersey Light ("2nd NJ Battery"); **Capt. A.
  Judson Clark** commanding throughout (assumed acting command of the
  whole III Corps Artillery Brigade after Randolph's wounding, per
  `us-iii-arty-randolph.md` EC1). Guns: **six 10-pounder Parrott
  rifles** (tablet, gettysburg.stonesentinels.com, "New Jersey Battery

  this pass).

## EC2 — Engaged strength

- **143 men** (tablet, verbatim, fetch-verified this pass).

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2, ~9:30 A.M.** [C ~09:30, report-primary, via
   `us-iii-arty-randolph.md`]: "placed in line on the rise of ground
   midway between General Sickles' headquarters and the peach orchard,
   on the Emmitsburg road" (Clark's own report, verbatim).
2. **July 2, 2 P.M. – 7 P.M.** [D, tablet]: "fought here from 2 until
   7 o'clock on July 2, 1863" (tablet, verbatim) — the Sickles Avenue
   monument's own stated engagement window, six hours longer than the
   ~09:30 opening long-range fire alone would suggest; carried as the
   tablet's own scope (likely spans the whole afternoon action, not
   continuous fire).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. [C ~09:30 July 2] Into the salient line, opening fire on CSA
   infantry columns crossing the Emmitsburg road at ~1,400 yards, "6
   or 7 rounds" before the columns dispersed (`us-iii-arty-randolph.md`
   EC3.3, cross-referenced).
2. [D] Battery-by-piece withdrawal as the line gave way in the
   afternoon collapse (`us-iii-arty-randolph.md` EC5.3, cross-
   referenced — not independently re-timed for this battery alone).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The long-range opening fire** [C ~09:30]: EC3.1/EC4.1.
2. **The extended defense line, ~3 P.M. to dark** [C, report-primary,
   via Randolph's dossier]: Clark's battery named in sequence among
   the mixed III-Corps/Artillery-Reserve gun line (Smith's, Winslow's,
   Bigelow's, Clark's, Ames's, Randolph's own, Seeley's).
3. **Rounds expended, tablet [E]**: "1300 rounds of ammunition" over
   the 2-7 P.M. window (tablet, verbatim) — this pass's largest single
   ammunition-expenditure figure among the III Corps battery batch.
