## EC1 — Identity & command

- 3rd Brigade, 3rd Division, II Corps; the war's hardest command
  attrition on this front: **Col. George Willard k July 2** at the Plum
  Run charge ("pursuant to the order of Colonel Willard, given just
  before his death", or-27-1-bull); **Col. Eliakim Sherrill (126th NY)
  commanded until his death, "about 4 p. m." July 3** (or-27-1-bull —
  the stated clock rides the source profile, ~30-40 min late against
  the corrected repulse window; conflict carried); then Lt. Col. James
  M. Bull (126th NY). Hays: "The history of this brigade's operations
  is written in blood" (p. 453 fetch).
- Regiments: 39th NY (4 cos, Maj. Hildebrandt w), 111th NY (Col.
  MacDougall w), 125th NY, 126th NY (or-27-oob; p. 453).

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2 morning** [C via or-27-1-bull]: "by battalion in mass near
  a barn a little south of ... Cemetery Hill" (Bryan barn area); tablet:
  "along Cemetery Ridge at right of the Angle."
2. **July 2, ~15:00 →** [C]: "At about 3 p. m. ... moved from its
  position by the left flank about a quarter of a mile toward the left
  of the line, where it was formed in line of battle"; then the evening
  charge on "Barksdale's Brigade in the wooded swale at the head of
  Plum Run forcing it back" (tablet) — the Plum Run/Trostle low ground
  fronting McGilvery's improvised line.
3. **July 2 night → July 3 — return to the ridge; the slope east of
  the wall** [C/D]: regiments "so placed as supports to the artillery"
  on "the slope of the hill occupied by us" (or-27-1-bull); tablet
  (July 3): "At 3 P. M. after a terrific cannonade of two hours the
  Brigade was moved up to the line of the Second Brigade and assisted
  in repulsing Longstreet's assault."
4. **Monument row** (ED-39, climax semantics): 39th NY (4467,4988) ·

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **July 2, ~15:00** [C]: quarter-mile move left by the left flank
  (Bull) — endpoint the massed position behind the ridge.
2. **July 2 evening** [D, ordered by Willard just before his death]:
  the Plum Run charge and return.
3. **July 3, cannonade end** [A vs CA-J3A-5, tablet-adjudicated 15:00]:
  "moved up to the line of the Second Brigade" — a short (~100-200 m)
  support-to-wall leg [B: 2-4 min at advance pace]; the brigade's one
  in-window leg.
4. July 4: skirmish details only ("sending out details to skirmish on
  the flat", or-27-1-bull).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **July 2 skirmish (39th NY)** [C]: Hildebrandt's regiment sent
  forward; "lost 26 enlisted men killed and wounded, and 2 commissioned
  officers wounded" (or-27-1-bull) — a per-episode casualty pin.
2. **July 2 evening — the Plum Run charge** [D]: Barksdale's brigade
  forced back, "capturing many prisoners" (tablet); Willard k; the
  recapture axis included Watson's battery ground (39th NY marker
  mon-39ny-1 at (4212,3738) near the Trostle farm).
3. **July 3 cannonade** [C +7]: "About 1 p. m. the enemy opened from
  his batteries ... a terrific cannonade ensued, which continued about
  two hours" (or-27-1-bull) — a clean +7-class report clock agreeing
  with the chain.
4. **The repulse** [A window]: "The enemy, advancing in four lines
  across the flat, were subjected to a murderous fire of musketry and
  artillery, and were driven back in confusion, after an engagement of
  about an hour" (or-27-1-bull) — the four-lines reading (vs Smyth's
  three) carried as a formation-count conflict; Sherrill fell in this
  fight [D, stated 16:00 riding the profile].

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Brigade report: attested-unreported** — "a severe loss was
  sustained by the brigade in killed and wounded, the precise number of
  which I am unable to report" (or-27-1-bull; first-class negative).
- **Tablet total: 714** ("Killed 11 officers 128 men, wounded 26
  officers 516 men, captured/missing 33 men").
- Episode distribution [D, unusually well bracketed]: 39th NY July 2
  skirmish = 28 exactly (EC5.1); July 2 Plum Run charge (Willard k) and
  July 3 repulse (Sherrill mw, MacDougall w, Hildebrandt w) split the
  remainder — Hays: "The loss of this brigade amounts to one-half the
  casualties in the division" (p. 453).
- Officer pins: Willard **k** [D July 2 evening charge]; Sherrill
  **mw** [C stated ~16:00, profile-corrected toward the repulse
  window]; MacDougall, Hildebrandt **w**.
