## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **The echelon trigger** [D, division primary]: Anderson's "about
   noon" briefing — "each brigade of the division would begin the
   attack as soon as the brigade on its immediate right commenced
   the movement" (report; = or-27-2-anderson-rh's division record) —
   Wright moves on Perry's movement, Perry on Wilcox's, Wilcox on
   Barksdale's front (the ladder's northern extension).
2. **The advance, ~18:30–18:45 canonical** [A vs CA-J2A-10 via the
   echelon ladder; stated "about 5 p.m." profiled +100 ED-58 class]:
   "more than a mile" over the open plain under artillery, taking
   the Emmitsburg-road line near the Codori house (several guns +
   prisoners there — the 15th MA/82nd NY collapse, both-sided with
   us-ii-2-1-harrow.md), then up the west slope to the gun line
   [B: ~1,700 m total at 1.3–1.5 m/s under fire with the road fight
   ≈ 22–30 min].
3. **The cut-out** [D]: both supports failed — Posey "had not
   advanced" (left), the right "had actually given way" (Lang's own
   withdrawal record concurs from inside, csa-3c-and-4-lang.md);
   "a strong body of infantry under cover of a high ledge of rocks
   … came upon my right and rear"; "we abandoned our captured guns,
   faced about, and prepared to cut our way through the closing
   lines in our rear" — recrossed the road under converging fire
   (Wasden k there) [B: the return leg ≈ 15–20 min broken-order].
4. July 3 cover move [D]: the 500–600-yard forward move at Pickett's
   repulse — an ED-44-adjacent present-but-covering state.
