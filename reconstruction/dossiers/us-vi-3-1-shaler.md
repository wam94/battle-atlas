## EC1 — Identity & command

- 1st Brigade, 3rd Division, VI Corps: 65th, 67th, 122nd New York,
  23rd, 82nd Pennsylvania (or-27-oob; brigade tablet,
  gettysburg.stonesentinels.com, "1st Brigade, 3rd Division, 6th
  Corps," fetch-verified this pass). **Brig. Gen. Alexander Shaler**
  throughout; own reports — a battle narrative dated July 21, 1863 to
  Capt. A. J. Smith, AND a second, separate letter dated November 20,
  1863 correcting a published misattribution (below) — both on OR
  27/1 pp. 680-683 (No. 232, civilwar.com transcription,
  fetch-verified this pass — PRIMARY, own-report, double-dated).
- **THE NAMING-CORRECTION FACT [C, report-primary, verbatim]**:
  Shaler's second (Nov. 1863) letter explicitly protests published
  reports crediting "Wheaton's brigade" with assisting the Twelfth
  Corps: "The First Brigade, and the First alone, was directed, and
  did report to General Geary" on the morning of July 3, acting under
  Shaler's own command by Geary's direction — a commander correcting
  the historical record in his own lifetime, a genuinely rare
  first-person editorial-dispute document in this corpus (compare
  ED-39 §3's litigated-memory class, though this is a report-era
  correction, not a postwar monument dispute).

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2, arrival ~2 P.M., then reserve** [C ~14:00, report=tablet
   agreement]: "Arrived about 2 P. M. from Manchester Md. and late in
   the day moved to the northeast slope of Little Round Top and held
   in reserve" (tablet, verbatim); Shaler's own report opens mid-
   narrative "At 7 p.m. on the 2nd instant, by direction of Brigadier-
   General Wheaton, commanding the division, this brigade was formed
   in line of battle on the left of the position held by the army, in
   rear of the Second Brigade" — a report=tablet-compatible, more
   precisely timed account (7 P.M. formation, naming Wheaton as
   division commander and Eustis's brigade, `us-vi-2-3-eustis.md`
   [sic — 3rd Division 2nd Brigade], as the unit in front).
2. **July 3, 8 A.M., detached to Culp's Hill** [C ~08:00, report=
   tablet agreement — THE FLAGGED FACT, now closed]: "Ordered to the
   left and at 8 A. M. to the right to the support of Second Division
   Twelfth Corps. Took position in rear of woods on Culp's Hill beyond
   which action was progressing and was engaged under command of Brig.
   Gen. J. W. Geary from 9 until 11 A. M." (tablet, verbatim); Shaler's
   own report independently: "reported to Brigadier-General Geary at
   8 a.m. on July 3rd, positioning themselves 'in rear of a piece of
   woods, beyond which the action was then progressing'" — a
   report=tablet exact match on both the 8 A.M. order-clock and the
   Culp's-Hill-woods siting.
3. **The regiment-grain timeline, July 3** [C, report-primary,
   verbatim per the fetch]: "9:00 a.m. — 122nd NY directed to relieve
   111th PA [a XII Corps regiment]; 9:20 a.m. — 23rd PA positioned 150
   yards in rear; ~3 hours later — five companies detached to support
   Second Division, Twelfth Corps; 11:30 a.m. — 122nd NY relieved by
   82nd PA" — a genuinely fine-grained, sub-hour ladder inside the
   CA-J3M-2/CA-J3M-4 window (ADOPTED ED-30, ~04:30-11:00), the
   VI Corps's only contribution to that chain's clock resolution to
   date.
4. **3:30 P.M., withdrawal from Culp's Hill** [C ~15:30, report-
   primary]: "under General Wheaton's direction, the brigade moved
   under artillery fire to report to General Newton. Subsequently,
   General Meade ordered the brigade to remain in reserve behind the
   Third Corps position" — placing the brigade on the II/III Corps
   center-rear ground for the July 3 afternoon cannonade/charge
   window, the same reserve-under-fire class as Vincent's/Rice's
   brigade (ED-54) and several other "famous elsewhere, reserve here"
   units this corpus has already documented.
5. **July 4, 6 A.M.**: "rejoined the division, by an order received
   direct from General Meade" (report, verbatim).

1. **THE CULP'S HILL ENGAGEMENT, 9-11 A.M. July 3** [C, report=tablet
   agreement]: "engaged under command of Brig. Gen. J. W. Geary from 9
   until 11 A. M." (tablet, verbatim) — the regiment-grain ladder
   (EC3.3) is this dossier's evidentiary spine for HOW that two-hour

   RECEIVING side, a Union reinforcement the standing chain proposal

2. **"Under a severe fire" / "galling fire of musketry"** [D, report,

   role, distinguishing this brigade sharply from Torbert's, Russell's,
   and Grant's (this pass) attested-static record.
