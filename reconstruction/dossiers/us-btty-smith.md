# Dossier: 4th New York Independent Battery (Smith's) (reg-us-iii-b3)

Pass 6 (Devil's Den receiving side; the guns cross-credit resolution)
· Cast status: not-yet-cast · Achieved T-level: **T4** · 2026-07-11

Chain role: the battery whose three lost 10-pdr Parrotts are the July
2 southern field's most-claimed trophy — Law's 44th/48th AL (tablet +
regimentals), Robertson's 1st TX (report), and Benning's brigade
(report + tablet) all credit the same three guns. This dossier closes
the standing pass-5 conflict item from the UNION side and proposes the
ruling (ED-56).

## EC1 — Identity & command

- 4th New York Independent Battery, III Corps artillery brigade
  (Randolph): six 10-pdr Parrott rifles. Capt. James E. Smith,
  unwounded.

## EC2 — Engaged strength

- Six guns in two elements (four forward, two rear); personnel
  compilation-tier (~126 class, hop flagged; no primary).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **The forward guns never displace** [D]: Smith kept them firing
  and left them rather than signal retreat to the infantry — "the
  terrain made rapid removal difficult" (or-27-1-smith-4ny); an
  attested-static loss, not a withdrawal leg.
2. **The captain's shift to the rear section** [B: ~150 yd on foot
  ≈ 2-3 min] at the hill's fall (~17:30, CA-J2A-5).

## Conflicts & negative evidence — THE GUNS CROSS-CREDIT, BOTH-SIDED

1. **The Union primary fixes the object**: exactly THREE 10-pdr
  Parrotts + carriages abandoned on the hill; the fourth forward gun
  had been disabled/withdrawn earlier and the rear section saved —
  matching Benning's "three front guns ... 10-pounder Parrotts"
  while "the enemy managed to withdraw three rear guns"
  (or-27-2-benning): the COUNT and TYPE agree across the lines.
2. **The claimants**: Law's tablet credits the 44th/48th AL "+ 3
  guns of the 4th NY"; Robertson: the 1st TX "took the battery hill
  (3 of 6 guns)" (or-27-2-robertson); Benning: "we captured ...
  three front guns" with the 1st TX already at the base when his
  line arrived and his regiments intermixed with it
  (or-27-2-benning). Smith's own report credits NO unit (he believed
  supports would retake them, and understood — wrongly per the CSA
  record — that they were later "retrieved").
3. **Proposed ruling (ED-56, NOT self-adopted)**: convergent joint
  capture — the hill fell to an intermixed force (1st TX + Benning's
  GA regiments, with the 44th/48th AL fighting up the gorge on
  their right); all three unit-level credits are honest views of one
  event; no exclusive credit is encodable without inventing
  precedence the primaries refuse to state. Consequence: any cast
  renders the three guns changing hands ONCE (~17:30, CA-J2A-5) with
  a three-way provenance note, and Benning's brigade holds the
  ground (and the guns) to July 3 evening (or-27-2-benning).
4. Smith's "Twelfth Corps supposedly retrieved them" belief is
  contradicted by Benning's holding-and-removal record — carried as
  the loser's honest misinformation, first-class.
