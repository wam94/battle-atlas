Chain role: **the chain's clock-bearer's own battalion.** CA-J3A-2/3 (the
two notes to Pickett) were written from this battalion's frontage; its
positions and ammunition state are the notes' physical context. Verified

## EC1 — Identity & command

- Alexander's Battalion, First Corps reserve artillery; Col. E. Porter
  Alexander — on July 3 directing the whole corps artillery preparation
  by Longstreet's arrangement, with **Maj. Frank Huger** at the battalion
  (or-27-oob; the July 2 precedent in his own OR: "about 4 p. m. I
  placed four batteries … the whole commanded by Major Frank Huger",
  or-27-2-alexander). Alexander's commendation: "I very respectfully
  recommend for special merit and gallantry, Majors Huger and Dearing"
  (or-27-2-alexander).
- Six batteries (or-27-oob; tablet): Woolfolk's Ashland (VA) — Capt.
  Pichegru Woolfolk Jr. **w July 2**, Lt. James Woolfolk; Jordan's
  Bedford (VA); Rhett's Brooks (SC) — Lt. S. Capers Gilbert commanding
  (Gilbert also wounded — SNIPPET-grade, open); Moody's Madison (LA);
  Parker's (VA); Taylor's (VA, Bath).
- The Walton/Alexander divided command: carried on
  csa-1c-bn-wash-eshleman.md EC1 and reg-csa-1c-arty-cmd.

## EC2 — Engaged strength

- **576 men, 24 guns** (addressing-gettysburg-oob, B&M-type, secondary
  hop flagged) — matches the build adoption (576). Per battery: Moody
  135 (4×24-pdr howitzers), Woolfolk 103 (2 Napoleons + 2 Parrotts),
  Taylor 90 (4 Napoleons), Parker 90 (3×3-in rifles + 1×10-pdr
  Parrott), Jordan 78 (4×3-in rifles), Gilbert 71 (4×12-pdr howitzers).

  Parrott / Seven 3 Inch Rifles Six Napoleons / Four 24 Pounder

- **Armament conflict carried**: Ashland's Parrotts are 20-pdr on the

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2, 09:00 — arrival** [C via or-27-2-alexander,
  report-nominal]: "arrived at 9 a. m. on July 2" via Winchester,
  Greencastle, Chambersburg; then ordered by Longstreet to accompany
  McLaws/Hood against the left.
2. **July 2, ~16:00–19:30 — the Peach Orchard duel line** [C]: "about
  4 p. m. I placed four batteries … in action against a heavy artillery
  and infantry force of the enemy, about 500 yards distant, in a peach
  orchard on the Emmitsburg pike"; after "a spirited engagement of a
  half hour" the position fell and the battalion "occupied the enemy's
  original position, in time to seriously annoy their retreat"
  (or-27-2-alexander). SHSP concurs: "We were so engaged probably for
  an hour, when McLaws charged and carried the Peach Orchard, my
  batteries following him closely" (alexander-shsp4-1877). Night:
  "Sleeping on the field that night, and replacing ammunition"
  (or-27-2-alexander).
3. **July 3, dawn → withdrawal — the ridge line from the Peach Orchard
  NE** [tablet + OR]: "at dawn I again placed the whole battalion in
  position for the attack upon the enemy's new line" (or-27-2-
  alexander); tablet: "In the line on ridge from Peach Orchard to N. E.
  corner of Spangler's Woods. Aided in the cannonade and supported
  Longstreet's assault." Build centroid (3170, 4150) f78; frontage
  ~400–500 m; battery-point example: Taylor "Took position 3 A. M. in
  main artillery line near Smith House northeast of Sherfy House on
  Emmitsburg Road and held it all day" (stone-sentinels Taylor marker)
  [C tablet clock 03:00]. Alexander's own command post sat on this
  frontage — the CA-J3A-2/3 notes' origin.
4. **July 4** [C tablet]: "until 4 P. M. Then withdrew to Marsh Creek
  on Fairfield Road" (tablet) — the battalion covered the field a full
  day after the repulse.

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **July 2 night / July 3 pre-dawn — into the bombardment line** [D
  before the 03:00 Taylor tablet clock]: redeployment NE along the
  ridge from the captured Peach Orchard ground.
2. **In-window forward displacement — NOW ATTESTED, with an internal
  conflict**: SHSP: "I galloped along my line of guns, ordering those
  that had over 20 rounds left to limber up and follow Pickett, and
  those that had less to maintain their fire from where they were"
  (alexander-shsp4-1877) [D at/after CA-J3A-6] — vs his own OR:
  "During the afternoon, the batteries all maintained their respective
  positions … driving off the enemy's sharpshooters with canister"
  (or-27-2-alexander). The 1863 report and the 1877 letter cannot both
  be exact; carried as the battalion's EC4 conflict (the build's

3. Withdrawal: "They were withdrawn from the field only when it was
  entirely abandoned by our infantry" (or-27-2-alexander) [D July 4
  per the tablet clock].

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **July 2 — the Peach Orchard duel** [C ~16:00–17:00]: four batteries
  under Huger at ~500 yards; the half-hour silencing; pursuit fire into
  the retreat (or-27-2-alexander).
2. **July 3, 13:07–~14:45 — core of the cannonade** [A start vs
  CA-J3A-1]: "about 75 guns which I then had in line" (alexander-
  shsp4-1877 — his own count of the wing he directed); the battalion's
  24 tubes at its centre. His watch: "It was 1 P. M. by my watch when
  the signal guns were fired" (SHSP; identical in the 1907 memoir;
  clockProfile +7).
3. **CA-J3A-2 — first note** [C, stated 13:30 (SHSP) / 13:25 (1907) →
  ~13:32-37 corrected]: "If you are coming at all you must come
  immediately or I cannot give you proper support; but the enemy's
  fire has not slackened materially, and at least 18 guns are still
  firing from the Cemetery itself" (alexander-shsp4-1877 — note the
  5-min internal variant between his two retrospectives, carried).
4. **CA-J3A-3 — second note** [C, stated 13:40 → 13:47]: "For God's
  sake come quick; the 18 guns are gone" (SHSP wording; 1907 memoir
  wording differs slightly — both carried, per the corpus rule).
5. **The ammunition famine — the notes' physical cause, now verbatim**:
  the trains held "about 100 rounds per gun additional" (which he
  doubted: "[I] don't believe they had over 60 rounds to a gun");
  by the step-off "not over 20 rounds to the gun were left"
  (alexander-shsp4-1877). The pre-cannonade Longstreet exchange
  ("If the artillery fire does not have the effect…" / "If, as I infer
  from your note, there is any alternative to this attack, it should
  be carefully considered before opening our fire") is the command
  frame of CA-J3A-0.
6. **Support fire during the charge** [D]: the >20-rounds guns follow
  Pickett (SHSP) vs canister-on-sharpshooters from position (OR) —
  the EC4.2 conflict; either way the battalion fired through the
  repulse window.
7. Rounds expended: **verified negative in the OR report** ("No
  specific round-expended figures"); the 20-rounds-left statements are
  the only quantitative ammunition record → [B potential: (initial
  chest load ~130-150/gun − 20) ≈ 110-130 rounds/gun fired July 2–3
  combined — NOT adopted, inputs too soft; recorded as a pass-2
  derivation candidate against the ordnance returns].

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Battalion return (or-27-2-alexander, verbatim)**: "In the battle
  of Gettysburg, July 2 and 3, killed, 19; wounded, 114; missing, 6;
  total, 139 men … Horses killed and disabled in action, 116."
  Materiel: "Destroyed in action, one 12-pounder howitzer, two
  12-pounder howitzer carriages, and six wheels." Tablet concurs
  exactly (stone-sentinels).
- Episode distribution [D]: the return itself spans July 2-3 jointly;
  the July 2 Peach Orchard duel at 500 yards was the close-range day
  (Woolfolk w July 2); July 3's share came through the counter-battery
  exchange. No primary split — flagged; per-battery detail exists only
  for Taylor ("Killed 2 Wounded 10", marker) with the rest "heavy/
  serious but not reported in detail" (markers).
- Officer pins: Capt. Woolfolk **w July 2** [D during the Peach
  Orchard fight]; Lt. Gilbert w (SNIPPET-grade, open).
