## EC1 — Identity & command

- Alexander's Battalion, First Corps reserve artillery, Army of Northern
  Virginia (or-27-oob). Col. E. Porter Alexander commanding — but on July
  3 **directing the whole corps artillery preparation by Longstreet's
  order**, with Maj. Frank Huger commanding the battalion in the line
  (or-27-oob register note; alexander-1907). The Walton/Alexander divided
  command is the order-chain context of CA-J3A-1 (see
  csa-1c-bn-wash-eshleman.md EC1).

  Woolfolk Jr. **w July 2**, Lt. James Woolfolk; Jordan's Bedford (VA);
  Gilbert's Brooks (SC) — Lt. S. Capers Gilbert; Moody's Madison (LA);
  Parker's (VA); Taylor's (VA, Bath) — Capt. O. B. Taylor.
- Alexander's July 3 command position [A vs CA-J3A-1..6]: at/near the
  battalion's guns opposite the Union centre — the notes chain and the
  Longstreet exchange happened on this frontage (alexander-1907,
  re-verified full text 2026-07-10).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **13:07–~14:45 — the bombardment, core emitter** [A start vs
  CA-J3A-1]: the battalion is the centre of the ~150-gun cannonade's
  First Corps arc ("YES — core of cannonade", full-cast survey battalion
  table, tablet-grade). Alexander's plan rode its ammunition: "I dared
  not presume on using more ammunition than one hour's firing would
  consume… So I determined to send Pickett the order at the very first
  favorable sign and not later than after 30 minutes' firing"
  (alexander-1907, re-verified 2026-07-10) [C via his +7 profile].
2. **CA-J3A-2, first note** [C, stated 13:25 → 13:32 corrected]: "If you
  are to advance at all, you must come at once or we will not be able to
  support you as we ought. But the enemy's fire has not slackened
  materially and there are still 18 guns firing from the cemetery"
  (alexander-1907).
3. **CA-J3A-3, second note** [C, stated 13:40 → 13:47 corrected]: "For
  God's sake come quick. The 18 guns have gone. Come quick or my
  ammunition will not let me support you properly" (alexander-1907).
4. **The support fire during the charge** [D between CA-J3A-6 and
  CA-J3A-10]: batteries with remaining long-range ammunition fired over
  and beside the advancing column; the ammunition famine (the trains'
  displacement, the unfilled chests) is the activity record's defining
  negative — the battalion could NOT deliver the sustained close support
  the assault plan assumed (alexander-1907; verification §2.3).
5. Ammunition: rounds-expended figures not yet page-fixed (OR battalion
  report is the upgrade path; flagged).

  chain (13:25/13:40 stated) vs Haskell/Hancock/McGilvery/Jacobs's
