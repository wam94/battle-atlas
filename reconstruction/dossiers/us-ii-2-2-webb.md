## EC4 — Movement legs

1. July 2 06:30: into the Granite Ridge line [C].
2. **July 2 ~18:30 — the counterattack** [C via or-27-1-webb]: Wright's
   line "advanced beyond one gun of Brown's battery, receiving at that
   point the fire of the Sixty-ninth; also that of the One hundred and
   sixth and Seventy-second Pennsylvania Volunteers"; it "halted,
   wavered, and fell back"; pursuing regiments took ~250 prisoners
   (1 colonel, 5 captains, 15 lieutenants) (or-27-1-webb; the
   Gibbon-division twin: "came quite through a vacancy in our line",
   or-27-1-gibbon).
3. July 2 "a little before dark": 106th detached to Cemetery Hill
   (above) — a leg OUT of the brigade.
4. July 3 morning [E]: "The Seventy-first ... advanced to the wall on
   the right of the Sixty-ninth"; 72nd "held in reserve under the crest"
   (or-27-1-webb) — the two-line disposition Hancock's report confirms
   ("disposed in two lines", or-27-1-hancock).
5. July 3 crisis [T5 window, held]: the 72nd ordered up to the crest
   ("advanced to within 40 paces"); the 69th/71st wall fight; recovery
   of the wall (claims corpus; or-27-1-webb; or-27-1-hancock).
