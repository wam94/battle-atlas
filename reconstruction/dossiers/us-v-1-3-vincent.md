## EC1 — Identity & command

- 3rd Brigade, 1st Division (Barnes), V Corps: 20th Maine
  (Chamberlain), 16th Michigan (Welch), 44th New York (Rice), 83rd
  Pennsylvania (Woodward). **Col. Strong Vincent mw July 2** rallying
  the breaking 16th MI right ("Colonel Vincent fell 'mortally
  wounded' during the attack", or-27-1-rice-vincent; died July 7,
  norton-1913 biographical) → **Col. James C. Rice (44th NY)**
  commanded through the battle's end (or-27-oob).
- Officer pins [D]: Vincent mw at the 16th MI crisis (multiple
  witnesses via norton-1913; the sheet labels the ground "VINCENT'S
  SPUR"); Lt. Linscott (20th ME) mw on Great Round Top ~21:00
  (or-27-1-chamberlain); captains/lieutenants of the 44th NY, 20th
  ME, 16th MI killed, listed by name in Rice's report.

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **The diversion to the hill** [D trigger + B: ~700 m at the
  double ≈ 6-9 min]: Warren's messenger to Barnes; Vincent took the
  responsibility and moved without waiting ("If I detached Vincent's
  brigade I don't recollect it" — Warren's own 1872 honesty,
  norton-1913 ch. VII; Sykes: Vincent "had seized the rocky height,
  closely followed by Weed's brigade", or-27-1-sykes); "thrown
  forward at the pas de course" (norton-1913).
2. **The 20th ME left-refuse** [D]: the flank extension bent "at
  right angles" when Oates's envelopment showed
  (or-27-1-chamberlain; Rice's report corroborates the maneuver).
3. **The bayonet charge** [D]: the extended right wheel sweeping the
  valley (or-27-1-chamberlain; Oates's report is the mirror).
4. **The Great Round Top night ascent** [C 21:00]: ~200 men, fixed
  bayonets, no firing on the climb (or-27-1-chamberlain; ordered by
  Rice "about nine o'clock", norton-1913).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The defense, ~16:45-19:00** [A vs CA-J2A-6]: Hood's attack "along
  the entire line" in three columns without skirmishers; the
  44th NY/83rd PA front charged "again and again within a few yards";
  the 16th MI right broke and was saved by the 140th NY's arrival;
  the 20th ME's half-hour crisis and five repulsed charges (both
  sides' counts agree — or-27-1-rice-vincent, or-27-1-chamberlain,
  or-27-2-oates-15al).
2. **The ammunition crisis** [D]: multiple regimental commanders out
  of ammunition at the height; "every officer and man in the rear"
  pressed into cartridge supply (or-27-1-rice-vincent) — the material
  precondition of Chamberlain's bayonet decision, brigade-attested.
3. **Repulse complete** [C]: "It was now 8 o clock in the evening"
  (or-27-1-rice-vincent — the phase's best Union primary end-clock);
  full possession before 21:00.
4. **Trophies**: 500+ prisoners (2 colonels, 15 officers), 1,000+
  muskets collected (Rice, brigade grain incl. the 20th ME's ~400 and
  the night's 25).
5. **July 3** [E]: reserve at the center; no fire returned (ED-44
  class).

## Conflicts & negative evidence
