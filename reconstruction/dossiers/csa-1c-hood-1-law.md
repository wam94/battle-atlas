## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **The New Guilford approach march** [B: 25 miles ÷ 2.5-3 mph with
  halts ≈ 8.5-10 h]: ordered ready 3 A.M. (tablet "Left New Guilford
  25 miles distant at 3 A.M."); "near 4 o'clock when the brigade was
  put in motion" (oates-1905). Arrival readings, three-way: "a little
  before twelve" (Stine via norton-1913), "about 2 o'clock P.M."
  within sight of the field (oates-1905), "arriving at 3:30 p.m." at
  the deployment area (or-27-2-scruggs-4al) — read as one march:
  march-column head near the field ~12:00-14:00, deployment on the
  division right ~15:30 after the flank movement; the physics
  brackets the set (03:30-04:00 start + 8.5-10 h = 12:00-14:00 ✓).
  The tablet's "Arrived and formed line ... about 4 P.M." is the
  formed-line endpoint.
2. **The assault leg, step-off → Devil's Den/Round Top foot**
  [A vs CA-J2A-3; B: ~1,300-1,600 m broken ground ≤1.5 m/s ≈
  20-30 min]: "Advancing rapidly across the valley ... all the time
  under a heavy fire from the batteries" (law-bl-1884); Law obliqued
  right hoping to turn the flank, opening the gap toward Robertson
  that pulled the 4th/5th TX to him (alexander-1907; law-bl-1884).
  Law's duration pin: "In less than an hour from the time we advanced
  ... the hill by Devil's Den ... was taken" [B input].
3. **The 15th/47th AL Round Top excursion** [D]: Oates pursued the
  sharpshooters UP Great Round Top, halted on the summit, argued to
  hold it, was ordered on by Law's AAG Terrell (who brought word of
  Hood's fall), then descended to attack the spur (oates-1905) — the
  detour that put his attack ~30+ min behind the first contact.
4. **The withdrawal** [D]: 15th AL back over Great Round Top at dusk
  ("like a herd of wild cattle", oates-1905; trigger: flanked right,
  two regiments — Fisher's arrivals — moving on his rear 200 yd off,
  or-27-2-oates-15al); the brigade line settled at the west foot
  (EC3.3).
