  2nd Rhode Island (or-27-oob; brigade tablet,
  gettysburg.stonesentinels.com, "2nd Brigade, 3rd Division, 6th
  Corps," fetch-verified this pass). **Col. Henry L. Eustis**
  throughout.
- **THE NO-REPORT FINDING [C, page-checked this pass]**: OR 27/1's
  Report No. 233 (the numbered slot between Shaler's No. 232 and
  Nevin's No. 234, confirmed by the civilwar.com transcription's own
  page sequence — Nevin's report opens directly at the top of p. 684)

  "the severest engagement of the war" (civilwar.com transcription,

  pass 11, "no-report class... third XI Corps case") and Godwin's/

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2, arrival ~2 P.M.** [C ~14:00, tablet-primary]: "Arrived
   about 2 P. M. from Manchester Md. and late in the day moved to the
   northeast slope of Little Round Top and held in reserve.
   Bivouacked for the night with First Brigade in the rear" (tablet,
   verbatim) — "First Brigade" here is Shaler's (`us-vi-3-1-shaler.md`,
   this pass), whose own report independently confirms "in rear of the
   Second Brigade" at 7 P.M. — a clean cross-tablet/report agreement
   on the two brigades' relative siting.
2. **July 3** [C, tablet-primary]: "Moved to the right centre and
   reported to Major Gen. J. Newton and was held in reserve during the
   battle. Not engaged but subject to artillery fire" (tablet,
   verbatim) — the same II/III Corps-center-rear reserve ground
   Torbert's brigade also occupied under Newton (`us-vi-1-1-torbert.md`
   EC3.3), and the same present-but-not-firing (ED-44) class as
   Grant's Vermont Brigade this pass.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **Attested-static, artillery-fire-only, July 3** [D, tablet]: "Not
   engaged but subject to artillery fire" (verbatim) — ED-44 class.
2. **Rogers's 2nd Rhode Island, its own component narrative** [D,
   report-primary, the only first-hand narrative this dossier has]:
   arrival "on the afternoon of July 2"; July 3 characterized in the
   regiment's own words as "the hottest of the battle" and "the
   severest engagement of the war" — a strikingly strong characterization
   for a brigade whose tablet says "not engaged," most plausibly
   describing the EXPERIENCE of sitting under the July 3 cannonade
   (the same phenomenon Nevin's brigade's own report independently
   describes as "the enemy shelled my whole line and the hill to our
   rear," `us-vi-3-3-nevin.md` EC5.2) rather than a firefight the
   brigade's own tablet does not otherwise attest — this pass does
   NOT adopt an interpretation, recording both readings.

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Brigade tablet: Killed 3 Men, Wounded 2 Officers + 39 Men,
  Captured or Missing 25 Men, Total 69** (gettysburg.stonesentinels.com,
  verbatim) — a substantial total for a brigade whose own tablet says
  "not engaged," and the LARGEST captured-or-missing figure (25) of
  any VI Corps brigade this pass researches by a wide margin. No
  per-regiment breakdown or explanation for the missing figure was
  located this pass (no report exists to supply one, EC1) — an open,
  honestly flagged EC6 puzzle: a reserve brigade "subject to artillery
  fire" alone would not typically generate 25 captured/missing; this
  pass records the figure without inventing a cause (compare the
  Fairfield-pooling discipline on `us-cav-1-3-merritt.md`, a DIFFERENT
  mechanism — no analogous detached-action explanation was found here).
