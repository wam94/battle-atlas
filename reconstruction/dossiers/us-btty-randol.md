## EC1 — Identity & command

- Batteries E & G (consolidated), 1st US Artillery, 2nd Brigade Horse
  Artillery, Cavalry Corps. **Capt. Alanson M. Randol** commanding
  throughout (gettysburg-stonesentinels-1usarty-eg, fetch-verified
  this pass).
- **Split command, July 3** (tablet, verbatim): "With First Brigade
  Second Cavalry Division. Not engaged" July 1-2; July 3, "one section
  under Lieut. James Chester was ordered to Second Brigade Third
  Cavalry Division" (i.e., attached to Custer's brigade) while "the
  other section under Lieut. Ernest L. Kinney remained near the
  Hanover Road" — a battery-level split, not a whole-unit deployment,
  the same "not one position" discipline Beckham's dossier applies to
  the CSA horse artillery (csa-cav-bn-ha-beckham.md EC3).
- Armament note (tablet, flagged as printed): "Four 12 Pounders" — the
  tablet's own text; standing corpus note (from the tablet fetch
  itself) that Federal horse artillery at Gettysburg uniformly used
  3-inch Ordnance Rifles, not 12-pounders — carried as a printed-tablet
  discrepancy, NOT silently corrected (ED-71-adjacent class: a printed
  reading that conflicts with known unit-type convention; here
  recorded as a flag, not adopted either way since no independent
  ordnance-return primary was fetched this pass to arbitrate).
- Parent brigade: Capt. John C. Tidball (2nd Brigade, Horse Artillery),
  whose own report (No. 372, OR 27/1 p. 1028, fetch-verified this
  pass) confirms the detachment structure: "Horse Battery... E, First
  U. S. Artillery (Randol's)... [is] still detached with the First
  and Second Cavalry Divisions" — i.e., Tidball's own report explicitly
  defers Randol's Gettysburg-day narrative to the cavalry division
  commanders' reports (Gregg's), rather than narrating it himself.
