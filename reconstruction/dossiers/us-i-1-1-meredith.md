# Dossier: Meredith's Iron Brigade, 1st Division, I Corps (reg-us-i-1-1)

Pass 10 (July 1 batch) · Cast status: in-build (`us-meredith`) ·
Achieved T-level: **T4 full-arc** · 2026-07-12

Chain role: **the CA-J1M-4/5 receiving executor** — the brigade whose
counter-charge captured Archer (the war's first Lee-army general
taken) minutes after Reynolds fell on its front; the heaviest-losing
Union brigade of the battle (1,153).

## EC1 — Identity & command

- 1st Brigade, 1st Division, I Corps: 2nd, 6th, 7th Wisconsin, 19th
  Indiana, 24th Michigan (or-27-oob). **Brig. Gen. Solomon Meredith
  (w July 1) → Col. William W. Robinson (7th Wis)** — succession
  pinned in Dawes's report ("reported for duty to Col. W. W.
  Robinson, now commanding the brigade", on Culp's Hill that
  evening).
- Deployment order R-to-L (Doubleday): 2nd Wis, 7th Wis, 19th Ind,
  24th Mich; **6th Wis + the 100-man brigade guard detached as corps
  reserve by Doubleday's order** (or-27-1-doubleday;
  or-27-1-dawes-6wi).
- Officer pins [D]: Morrow (24th Mich) w scalp & c in town; Lt. Col.
  Flanigan leg, Maj. Wight eye (24th Mich); Capt. Ticknor and Lt.
  Chapman (6th Wis) k at the cut; 24th Mich officer ledger 8 k / 14 w
  (or-27-1-morrow-24mi; or-27-1-dawes-6wi).

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. March leg [C via or-27-1-morrow-24mi]: Marsh Creek → the field,
   "6 or 7 miles", early start, "about 9 a.m. we came near the town"
   — pace-consistent with Wadsworth's 8:00 start / 10:00 arrival pair
   (the 9:00 near-town reading runs early; both carried).
2. **The unloaded double-quick charge** [D, drill-class]: deployed
   "on the double-quick... no order being given or time allowed for
   loading our guns" (staff override recorded), over the crest into
   the ravine (or-27-1-morrow-24mi); Doubleday: "deployed en echelon
   without a moment's hesitation, charged with the utmost steadiness
   and fury, hurled the enemy back into the run."
3. The withdrawal to the east bank, then the woods line [D].
4. The barricade stand → through town → Culp's Hill [D]
   (or-27-1-morrow-24mi "orders... to fall back, given, I believe, by
   Major-General Doubleday"; Dawes's faced-by-the-rear-rank retreat
   with the through-town crush).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The Archer capture** [D, CA-J1M-4's climax]: "we captured a
   large number of prisoners, being a part of General Archer's
   brigade" (Morrow); "captured... nearly 1,000 prisoners — all from
   Archer's brigade... to [the 2nd Wis] also belongs the honor of
   capturing General Archer himself. He was brought in by Private
   Patrick Maloney, of Company G" (k later that day)
   (or-27-1-doubleday) — both-sided with Shepard's "some 75 of the
   brigade were unable to make their escape, General Archer among
   the rest" (the two capture counts CONFLICT; both carried, see
   Conflicts).
2. **The railroad-cut charge (detached 6th Wis)** [D]: the fence
   fire-by-file, the charge, "the rebels began throwing down their
   arms"; Maj. Blair's 2nd Miss surrender to Dawes; 7 officers +
   ~225 men to the cavalry guard; the 2nd Miss flag (Waller, MoH);
   the 2nd Maine gun recaptured (or-27-1-dawes-6wi) — both-sided
   with Davis's "rallied near the railroad... he fled in great
   disorder" and the 147th NY's release (or-27-1-cutler).
3. The woods defense [D]: skirmishers "at once engaged"; "We had
   inflicted severe loss on the enemy, but their numbers were so
   overpowering..." (or-27-1-morrow-24mi) — both-sided with the 26th
   NC record (or-27-2-jones-26nc, pass 3: "lost more than half its
   men" in this fight).
4. The seminary barricade fight [D]: with Biddle/Stone remnants and
   Stewart's/Cooper's/Stevens's guns (or-27-1-doubleday; drawn
   j1-13).

## Conflicts & negative evidence

1. **The Archer-brigade capture count**: Doubleday "nearly 1,000
   prisoners — all from Archer's brigade" vs Shepard "some 75...
   unable to make their escape" (morning fight) — an order-of-
   magnitude conflict; the ANV return's 517 c/m for the brigade is
   battle-total (July 3 works-heavy). Both readings carried; neither
   adopted (candidate resolution: Doubleday's figure includes
   railroad-cut and non-Archer prisoners — NOT ruled).
2. Morrow's "about 9 a.m." vs Wadsworth's 10:00 road-strike — the
   approach clocks differ by ~30-60 min; both carried (Morrow's is
   "near the town", Wadsworth's the deployment point).
3. Meredith's own wounding: no report by him; succession attested by
   Dawes; the wounding moment untimed (open).
4. Brigade strength primary open (only the 24th's 496 fetched).
