## EC1 — Identity & command

- 5th New York Battery; **Capt. Elijah D. Taft** commanding throughout,
  doubling as the 2nd Volunteer Brigade's commander (or-27-oob;
  `us-ar-2v-taft.md` EC1). Own report dated July 19, 1863, Camp of the
  Artillery Reserve (OR 27/1 pp. 891-892, No. 325, civilwar.com
  transcription, fetch-verified pass 14 — PRIMARY, own-report).
- **FOUR individually-timed casualties [C, report-primary, via the
  parent dossier]**: Pvt. John C. Begg, mortally wounded July 2 by an
  accidental caisson-limber explosion approaching the field, died July
  7; Lt. Clinton Thalheimer, wounded in the abdomen by a bullet at 9
  A.M. July 3, died at midnight the same day; Sgt. Henry Dillon,
  slightly wounded in the neck ~10 A.M. July 3; Pvt. Adolph Wittenberg,
  severely wounded through the leg ~2 P.M. July 3 — a pin density this
  corpus rarely finds outside its most-worked dossiers.

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2, the march and East Cemetery Hill arrival** [C, report-
   primary]: marched from Taneytown July 2, arriving near Gettysburg
   ~10:30 A.M.; by 4 P.M. reached the cemetery and reported directly
   to **Maj. Gen. Howard** (XI Corps commander), taking position at 5
   P.M. — a direct, named cross-corps reporting chain tying this
   battery into the East Cemetery Hill defense already documented from
   Osborn's, Wiedrich's, and Ricketts's dossiers.
2. **July 3, sustained position** [D]: held position through the day.
3. **The return march** [D ~10:00 A.M. July 5]: to the Artillery
   Reserve camp, 2 miles from Gettysburg.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **July 2, counter-battery + infantry fire** [C]: engaged CSA
   batteries north and west of the cemetery with four and two guns
   respectively; fired on CSA infantry.
2. **July 3, sustained fire and a gun-burst casualty** [C ~08:00 and
   ~13:30+]: returned enemy fire beginning ~8 A.M. and again after
   1:30 P.M.; one 20-pounder Parrott gun burst at the muzzle during
   the action — an equipment-failure event distinct from combat loss;
   firing ceased ~6 P.M.
3. **Rounds expended, itemized by projectile type [C, report-primary]**:
   "80 Schenkl percussion shell, 63 Schenkl combination shrapnel, 32
   Parrott time-fuse shell, 382 Parrott time-fuse shrapnel, and 557
   cartridges" — the ED-42 "expenditure ledger" class.
