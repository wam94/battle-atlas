## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 2, ~8 A.M. arrival from Taneytown** [C ~08:00, report-
   primary]: "Arriving on the field from Taneytown about 8 a.m. July
   2" (Fitzhugh's report, verbatim) — the earliest-stated arrival
   time of any Artillery Reserve brigade fetched this pass.
2. **The dispersal, per Tyler's own order [C, report-primary]**:
   "Brig. Gen. R. O. Tyler ordered him to position two batteries on
   the Baltimore turnpike near General Slocum's line" — a direct,
   named order from the Reserve's commander himself (`us-ar-hq-tyler-
   park.md`, T3), the clearest command-chain citation any brigade
   dossier this pass has for Tyler's own hand in Reserve battery
   placement.
3. **Battery-by-battery dispersal, stated by the brigade commander
   himself [C, report-primary]**: "G, First New York" (Ames's) and
   "the Sixth Maine Battery" (Dow's) detached under **Lt. Col.
   McGilvery** (`us-ar-1v-mcgilvery.md`'s own composite line — a
   direct brigade-commander-level confirmation of McGilvery's
   cross-brigade command, corroborating what McGilvery's own dossier
   already documents from the other side); Battery A, 1st Maryland
   (Rigby's) assigned to the **Twelfth Corps** (i.e., Powers Hill,
   the same ground Neill's brigade occupied July 2-3, `us-vi-2-2-neill.md`
   this pass, and `us-xii-arty-muhlenberg.md`'s own frame).
4. **July 3, 1 P.M., the crisis reinforcement** [C ~13:00, report-
   primary]: **Gen. Hunt** personally ordered Fitzhugh to position his
   remaining batteries (Parsons's, and his own Battery K) "near the
   stone fence before General Webb's division of the Second Corps" —
   "their infantry having charged up to the stone fence near a small
   wooded knoll about 75 yards on my right" — a direct, commander-
   level account of the SAME crisis-reinforcement class already named
   in this corpus (ED-41), now sourced from the brigade commander's
   own report rather than only the individual battery reports
   `us-btty-parsons.md`/`us-btty-fitzhugh.md` already carry.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The Webb's-fence fire, July 3** [C ~13:00-14:00, report-primary]:
   Parsons's battery "fired approximately 40 rounds of shrapnel into
   the flank of attacking Confederate infantry"; Fitzhugh's own
   Battery K "returned effective fire against Confederate artillery
   positioned about 1,000 yards distant"; the CSA attack repulsed
   within "half or three-quarters of an hour" (report, verbatim) —
   this LAST detail (a stated DURATION for the repulse, from the
   brigade commander's own pen) is a genuinely new timing fact neither
   battery-grain dossier currently carries at this precision.
2. **Rigby's and the detached batteries' activity** [D]: not narrated
   in Fitzhugh's own report beyond the assignment fact (EC3.3) — their
   own tablet lines (already on file from this pass's earlier fetch:
   Rigby "Engaged on Powers Hill"; Dow "With First Volunteer Artillery
   Brigade near left of Second Corps line"; Ames "Engaged in Peach
   Orchard" July 2, "on left of Second Corps line" July 3) remain the
   best source for those three batteries' independent activity.
