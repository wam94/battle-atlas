## EC4 — Movement legs

1. Second-line advance "At 4 p.m." with the division [C, agreeing
   class]: under "a most terrible fire from the enemy's artillery on
   our left, which was most destructive" — the brigade's bleeding
   began on the LEFT-flank artillery axis before any musketry.
2. **The collapse withdrawal** [D trigger]: "the troops on the left
   fell back … the enemy advanced upon us in great force, pouring
   into us a most terrific fire of artillery and musketry, both upon
   our front and left flank"; withdrew "in good order, but with
   terrible loss" (report) — the division leg's second-line copy.
3. **THE RECHARGE** [D, after-sunset class]: Brewster with ~150 men
   of five regiments retook lost ground, "recapturing them
   [the guns], taking from one the colors of the Eighth Florida
   Regiment" — Sgt. Thomas Hogan (3rd Excelsior) the captor; tablet:
   "after sunset again advanced and captured the colors of the 8th
   Florida and thirty prisoners and recaptured guns that had been
   left on the field" [the sunset framing rides CA-J2A-11's pin].
