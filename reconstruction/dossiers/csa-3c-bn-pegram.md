## EC1 — Identity & command

- Pegram's Battalion, Third Corps reserve artillery (Walker); Maj.
  William J. Pegram; report by **Capt. Ervin B. Brunson** (Pegram sick
  during part of the campaign per the register; the report authorship
  itself is the command-state datum). Batteries: Crenshaw's, Marye's
  Fredericksburg, Brander's Letcher, Zimmerman's Pee Dee, McGraw's
  Purcell (or-27-oob; tablet).
- Armament (tablet): ten Napoleons, four 10-pdr Parrotts, four 3-inch
  rifles, two 12-pdr howitzers = 20 guns.
- Officer pin: the battalion's ordnance officer **mw July 1 by solid
  shot** (or-27-2-brunson).

  an eminence about 1 mile west of Gettysburg" with Heth's advance

  "opened on a piece of woods to the left of the pike" — 8-10 rounds

  day" (or-27-2-walker); Brunson: "approximately one mile right of
  the pike, roughly 1,400 yards from" the Union artillery,
  "enfilading their batteries whenever they opened upon the

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. **July 1** [C]: Marye's section forward at 2 miles out; then the
  ten-Napoleon/seven-rifle line into action.
2. **July 1→2** [D]: the one-mile-forward reposting (Walker).
3. **Retreat** [D]: night July 4 on the Fairfield road; ~20 horses
  abandoned for want of shoes; 6-8 men taken prisoner, one howitzer
  caisson abandoned (or-27-2-brunson).

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **July 1** [C]: the line "forced Confederate [sic — Union]
  artillery to limber up and retire their pieces three distinct
  times" (or-27-2-brunson, OCR-side flag recorded); McIntosh's
  railroad-cut enfilade rode the same axis.
2. **July 2** [C]: opportunistic enfilade "whenever they opened upon
  the batteries on our right" — the crossfire component of the
  Peach Orchard duel from the north.
3. **July 3 cannonade** [A window vs CA-J3A-1..5]: "never did men
  behave more heroically"; "one of the most terrific" artillery
  fights; the honest outcome line: "The infantry failed to dislodge
  the enemy from his position" (or-27-2-brunson).
4. **ROUNDS EXPENDED — VERBATIM**: "The battalion expended in the
  three days' fight 3,800 rounds of ammunition" (or-27-2-brunson;
  tablet concurs) — [B basis: 3,800 ÷ 20 guns = 190 rds/gun over
  three days; no per-day split stated — durations derivable only
  against the per-day casualty weights below].

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **PER-DAY SPLIT, PRIMARY — the only one in the pass-2 set**:
  July 1: 2 k / 8 w / 6 horses; July 2: 2 k / 7 w / 25 horses;
  July 3: 10 k / 37 w / 38 horses (or-27-2-brunson) — the July 3
  counter-battery day cost ~3x either other day; exactly the
  episode-distribution shape EC6 wants, stated by the source.
