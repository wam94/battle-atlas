## EC1 — Identity & command

- Batteries F and K, 3rd U.S. Artillery (combined); **1st Lt. John C.
  Turnbull** commanding throughout — 20 years old in 1863, stayed in
  the regular army postwar, retired as a major (tablet, gettysburg.
  stonesentinels.com, "Monuments to Batteries F & K, 3rd United States
  Artillery," fetch-verified this pass, paraphrase). Guns: **six
  12-pounders** (tablet, verbatim).

## EC2 — Engaged strength

- **145 men** (tablet, verbatim, fetch-verified this pass).

## EC3 — Position anchors

1. **July 1** [D, tablet]: "Took position on crest of hill near
   General Meade's Headquarters" (tablet, verbatim).
2. **July 2** [D, tablet]: "Moved to a position on the right of a log
   house on the Emmitsburg road with General A. A. Humphreys' Division
   Third Corps" (tablet, verbatim) — the same log-house ground
   Seeley's battery (`us-btty-seeley.md`, this pass) also occupies,
   placing TWO of this pass's dossiered batteries on the same III
   Corps salient ground from different parent commands (Artillery
   Reserve vs III Corps Artillery Brigade).
3. **July 3** [D, tablet]: "Went into position near Taneytown Road on
   the left of Cemetery Hill" (tablet, verbatim) — a THIRD distinct
   position in three days.

## EC4 — Movement legs

1. [D] July 1: arrival → crest near Meade's HQ (EC3.1).
2. [D] July 2: → the log-house/Emmitsburg road position with
   Humphreys's Division (EC3.2).
3. [D] July 2 → 3: → Taneytown Road, left of Cemetery Hill (EC3.3) —
   a retrograde/relocation following the loss described in EC5.

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **July 2, engaged, THEN COMPELLED TO RETIRE** [D, tablet, verbatim]:
   "Engaged here but was compelled to retire with the loss of 45
   horses killed and 4 guns which were afterwards recaptured" (tablet,
   verbatim) — a genuine gun-loss-and-recapture episode, the
   Artillery-Reserve counterpart of the same activity class this
   corpus already documents for Weir's battery (`us-btty-weir.md`,
   pass 2) and Watson's (`us-btty-watson.md`, pass 8).

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

  this pass).
