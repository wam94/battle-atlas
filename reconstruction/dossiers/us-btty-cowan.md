## EC3 — Position anchors

  Manchester "9 p. m. July 1", arrived near the field "10 p. m. the

2. **July 3, ~10:00 → noon — reserve behind the left centre** [C]:

4. **July 3, crisis — THE BROWN SLOT** [A vs CA-J3A-7..9]: moved "at a

  General Webb's brigade" (or-27-1-cowan) — the position Brown's

  j3-03 labels "COWAN" between "CUSHING" and "BROWN" on the crest bar
  (read 2026-07-11, unit-to-unit class 10-20 m).
5. Battery-naming conflict at this slot: Hall wrote "Cushing's",

## EC5 — Activity record

1. **The cannonade** [C via profile]: in action from his "about noon"
  (profiled +67; rule 4) — counter-battery from the reserve position.
2. **The canister ladder** [A vs CA-J3A-8/9]: "I commenced firing
  canister at 200 yards, and the effect was greater than I could have
  anticipated. My last charge literally swept the enemy from my front,
  being fired at less than 20 yards" (or-27-1-cowan) — the monument
  carves it as "DOUBLE CANISTER AT TEN YARDS" (inscription verbatim;
  the 20-yards-report vs 10-yards-monument variant carried).

## EC6 — Casualty apportionment

- **Report verbatim**: "4 privates killed instantly, and 1 soon after
  died of wounds; 4 enlisted men and 2 officers wounded" + 14 horses,
  8 wheels disabled (or-27-1-cowan). Monument: "four men and fourteen
  horses killed, two lieutenants and six men wounded"; SS page figure
  4 k / 8 w — three accountings, all carried (they differ on the
  died-of-wounds man and the wounded count).
- Episode distribution [D, tight]: all July 3, concentrated in the
  cannonade + crisis window (the battery was not engaged July 1-2) —
  the cleanest single-episode casualty profile in the pass-2 set.
